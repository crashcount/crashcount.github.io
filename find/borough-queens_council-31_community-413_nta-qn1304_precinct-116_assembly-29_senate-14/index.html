      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Cambria Heights?

      Cambria Heights Bleeds While City Hall Sleeps

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      Cambria Heights: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Crashes Keep Coming

      A child on a bike, age nine, struck and bleeding from the head. A four-year-old girl, whiplashed in the back seat. A 54-year-old man, dead behind the wheel. In Cambria Heights, the road does not forgive. In the last twelve months, 149 people have been injured in crashes here. Two were seriously hurt. One was killed. See the NYC crash data.

      The numbers do not rest. Since 2022, there have been 735 crashes. 459 people hurt. One dead. The young are not spared. Twelve children injured in the past year alone. The old are not spared. No one is spared.

      Who Pays the Price

      The machines that do the damage are not hard to name. Cars and SUVs. They break bodies and end lives. In the last three years, not a single bike or motorcycle killed anyone here. The violence comes on four wheels. The violence is relentless.

      What Has Been Done, What Has Not

      The city talks of Vision Zero. The city talks of safer streets. But in Cambria Heights, the blood dries and the engines keep running. No new laws. No new protections. No word from the council. No word from Albany. The silence is heavy. The silence is loud.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is not chance. This is policy.

      If you live here, you know the danger. If you lead here, you know the numbers. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras that never sleep. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Do not wait for another child’s blood on the asphalt. Act now.

      Half Of NYC Taxis Now Wheelchair Accessible
      ▸

      Half the city’s taxis now take wheelchairs. Ten years of pressure forced change. The fleet stands as the most accessible in the nation. Policy, not luck, drove this. Disabled riders gain ground. Streets shift. Access grows.

      amNY reported on June 5, 2025, that New York City’s Taxi and Limousine Commission (TLC) reached a milestone: 50% of active taxis are now wheelchair accessible. TLC Commissioner David Do said, “We made that today. It is the most accessible fleet in the entire country.” This achievement follows a decade-long push by disability advocates and a 2013 settlement requiring half the medallion fleet to be accessible. The transition faced delays, including the COVID-19 pandemic and medallion owner financial struggles. Policy changes, such as alternating accessible vehicles and funding support, drove the shift. The article highlights systemic progress, not individual driver actions, and underscores the impact of sustained advocacy and regulatory pressure.

      Comrie Opposes Idaho Stop Bill Citing Senior Safety Concerns
      ▸

      Victims and advocates marched to Albany. They demanded action for safer streets. Lawmakers split on bills: some backed speed controls, others balked. The Senate killed an anti-congestion bill. The fight for vulnerable lives pressed on, urgent and raw.

      On May 14, 2025, victims of road violence and Families for Safe Streets lobbied at the State Capitol for the SAFE Streets Package. This package includes the 'Idaho stop' bill and the 'Stop Super-Speeders' bill. The event saw support from several Assembly members and a senator for speed controls, while Sen. Anthony Palumbo and Assembly Member Michael Novakhov opposed the super speeder bill, citing government overreach. Sen. Leroy Comrie opposed the Idaho stop bill, citing concerns for seniors.
