      Three Dead, Seventy-Six Hurt: City Stalls While Cunningham Park Bleeds

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations.)

      Cunningham Park: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      Three people are dead. One hundred eighty-five are hurt. This is Cunningham Park since 2022. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care if you are young or old. In the last twelve months alone, 76 people were injured in 100 crashes. No one died this year. But the blood from last year is not dry.

      No children died. But children were hurt. Two under 18. Six aged 75 and older. The road does not care about age. It takes who it wants.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and Speed

      Cars and trucks do the damage. Sedans, SUVs, trucks. They hit, they crush, they kill. Motorcycles crash, but they do not kill here. Bikes are not in the numbers. The street belongs to the heavy and the fast.

      Speed is the constant. Unsafe speed. Unsafe lane changes. Passing too close. The words repeat in the crash logs. The injuries pile up. The dead do not speak.

      Leadership: Action or Silence?

      The city has the power to lower speed limits. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can drop the limit to 20 mph. But the limit stands. The clock ticks. The city can act now. It does not.

      Speed cameras work. They cut speeding by more than half. But the law that keeps them alive is always at risk. The city asks Albany to renew it. Albany waits. The cameras may go dark. The danger will not.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every crash is a choice made by leaders who delay, by laws that wait, by roads that forgive only the fast and the heavy. The dead are not statistics. They are the cost of inaction.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the 20 mph limit. Demand cameras that never sleep. Take action now. The next name on the list could be someone you love.

      Box Truck Rear-Ends Sedan on Long Island Expressway
      ▸

      A box truck struck the rear of a sedan traveling westbound on the Long Island Expressway. Three sedan occupants suffered neck and head contusions. The crash caused left front bumper damage to the truck and rear damage to the sedan.

      According to the police report, at 8:11 AM on the Long Island Expressway, a box truck traveling westbound collided with the center back end of a westbound sedan. The point of impact was the truck's left front bumper striking the sedan's rear. The sedan carried three occupants: a 23-year-old male driver, a 38-year-old female rear passenger, and a 55-year-old male front passenger. All three sustained contusions and bruises to the neck or head but were conscious and not ejected. The report lists no specific contributing factors or driver errors such as failure to yield or speeding. Both drivers held valid New York licenses. Vehicle damage included left front bumper damage to the truck and rear-end damage to the sedan. The report does not attribute fault to the victims or note any victim behaviors.
