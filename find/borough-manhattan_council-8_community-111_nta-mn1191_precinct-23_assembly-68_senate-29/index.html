      Left in the Dark: Randall’s Island Bleeds While City Sleeps

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 14, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      Randall’S Island: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll on Randall’s Island

      No one died on Randall’s Island this year. But the blood still runs. In the last twelve months, there were 29 injuries and 1 serious injury from crashes. No one walks away from a crash the same. The numbers are small, but the pain is not. A shoulder torn. A neck snapped. A back in shock. The wounds are listed in the city’s ledgers, but the lives behind them are not counted.

      The Beating That Broke the Silence

      On May 16, Diana Agudela, a 44-year-old e-bike commuter, was attacked on her way home. She was left with brain injuries, not expected to survive. Her daughter stood in the dark and said, “We need more protection, we need more lights. It is ridiculous that the park that my mom would ride from to go from work to home had no lights and no cameras.” The path was black, empty, and unguarded. The city left her alone.

      Leadership: Promises and Gaps

      No deaths. But no real safety. The city touts Vision Zero and new laws. But on Randall’s Island, the basics are missing. No lights. No cameras. No protection for the people who walk or ride home late. The numbers show a rise: crashes up 291% year over year. Injuries up 300%. Still, the silence from local leaders is louder than the sirens.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. The city can light the paths. The city can install cameras. The city can slow the cars. But only if you force them. Call your council member. Demand action. Do not wait for another mother to be left in the dark.

      Contact your local leaders. Demand lights, cameras, and real protection for every person on Randall’s Island.
