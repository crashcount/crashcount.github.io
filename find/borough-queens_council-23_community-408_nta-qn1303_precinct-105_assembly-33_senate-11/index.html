      Queens Village: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      A man steps into the crosswalk. A truck turns left. The man is 29. He is crossing with the signal. The truck crushes him. He dies at the intersection of Hillside Avenue and 212th Street. The cause: driver inattention. The truck is oversized. The man does not go home. NYC crash data

      In the last twelve months, two people have died on Queens Village streets. Three hundred sixteen have been injured. Five years, five deaths. The numbers do not stop. They do not care about age. Children, elders, workers. The road takes them all.

      The Pattern Is the Policy

      Most injuries come from cars and trucks. Sedans, SUVs, box trucks. They hit, they maim, they kill. In the last three years, not one cyclist killed. But pedestrians are not spared. Trucks and SUVs do not yield. The city counts the bodies. The city moves on.

      A 64-year-old man dies behind the wheel. A 76-year-old woman, too. Both killed by speed and disregard for traffic signals. The stories repeat. The faces change. The pain does not.

      Leadership: Words and Silence

      Local leaders talk about Vision Zero. They talk about safer streets. But the deaths keep coming. The city passed Sammy’s Law. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not done so here. The cameras that catch speeders could go dark if Albany does not act. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program lapsed. No word on renewal.

      Queens District Attorney Melinda Katz called it what it is: “This was a horrific death for the victim… The conduct displayed that evening demonstrates a complete disregard for human life.” Katz called it what it is

      The time for talk is over.

      Act Now: Demand Action

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand streets that put people first.

      Every day of delay is another life at risk. The dead do not get a second chance. The living must fight for the next one.
