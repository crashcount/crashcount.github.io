      Prospect Heights Bleeds—City Hall Waits

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and ensured citation order and deduplication.)

      Prospect Heights: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll in Prospect Heights

      The streets of Prospect Heights do not forgive. In the last twelve months, 111 people were injured in 187 crashes. Two were seriously hurt. No one died this year, but the numbers do not rest. Since 2022, two people have died and nine have been seriously injured on these blocks. The wounds are not just numbers. They are broken hips, crushed bodies, heads split open on the curb.

      Pedestrians and cyclists take the worst of it. Cars and SUVs led the charge, causing most of the pain. Trucks, motorcycles, bikes—they all left their mark. A cyclist’s head hit the pavement on Atlantic Avenue. A pedestrian’s leg was torn open at an intersection. The stories repeat. The faces change. The blood does not.

      Leadership: Words and Waiting

      The city talks about Vision Zero. They promise safer streets. They point to new laws and cameras. But in Prospect Heights, the grind continues. No bold action from local leaders. No sweeping redesign. The speed limit could drop to 20 mph tomorrow. It hasn’t. Cameras could be everywhere. They aren’t. The city says one life lost is too many. The street says otherwise.

      The Machines That Hurt Us

      Cars and SUVs are the main threat. In three years, they caused the most injuries and deaths to people walking and biking. Trucks and motorcycles followed. Bikes hurt too, but not like the rest. The danger is not random. It is built into the road, the law, the silence.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by someone in power. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes. Demand cameras that never sleep. Do not wait for the next siren. Take action now.
