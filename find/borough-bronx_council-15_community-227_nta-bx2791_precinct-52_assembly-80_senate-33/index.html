      Bronx Park Bleeds While City Hall Waits

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Bronx Park: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      Seven dead. Five hundred and four injured. That is the toll in Bronx Park since 2022. One death for every season. One injury for every day and then some. The dead do not speak. The injured carry wounds that do not heal.

      Pedestrians are not spared. A 30-year-old man was killed by a sedan on the Bronx River Parkway. His body was found away from the intersection, crushed and silent. NYC Open Data keeps the record. The street keeps the memory.

      Violence in the Open

      The violence is not hidden. In April 2025, a man was left in the street after a road rage assault. He was struck by an ambulance while lying on the pavement. The news said it plain: “Authorities say a man injured in a road rage assault in the Bronx was also hit by a passing ambulance while lying on the street.”

      Speed is the silent killer. In the last 12 months, 169 crashes. One dead. One seriously hurt. Twenty-two children injured. The numbers do not flinch. They do not forgive.

      Leadership: Promises and the Waiting

      The city has the tools. Sammy’s Law passed. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the limit stands. The council waits. The mayor waits. The dead do not wait.

      Cameras cut speed. Speed cameras slash speeding by more than half. But the law that keeps them on is always at risk. Each delay is another chance for blood on the asphalt.

      The call is simple:

      • Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit.
      • Demand permanent speed cameras.
      • Demand action, not words.

      Contact your council member. Call the mayor. Call the DOT. Take action now.

      The street remembers every delay.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Bronx Park sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, community board Bronx CB27, city council district District 15, assembly district AD 78 and state senate district SD 33.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx Park?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 5 moderate injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 0 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 injuries. (Source: NYC Open Data vehicle rollup)
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The numbers show patterns—speed, reckless driving, and unsafe streets. These are preventable. Every crash is a policy failure.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, redesign streets, and fund enforcement. Delay costs lives.
      ▸ How many people have died or been injured in Bronx Park since 2022?
      Seven people killed. Five hundred and four injured. (Source: NYC Open Data period stats)
