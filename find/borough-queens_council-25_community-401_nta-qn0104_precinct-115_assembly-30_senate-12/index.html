      Kids Broken, City Silent: Lower the Speed, Save Their Lives

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Astoria (East)-Woodside (North): Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      Children Bleed, Cars Roll On

      A 7-year-old girl walked outside her school. A car jumped the curb. She left with a broken femur and a head wound. A 14-year-old girl and a 58-year-old man fell too, their legs battered. The driver did not have a license. Police charged her with reckless endangerment, reckless driving, and driving without a license. The street was quiet again by nightfall, but the blood stayed. “Bah was arrested and charged with reckless endangerment, reckless driving and driving without a license, police sources said.”

      In the last year, 156 people were injured on these streets. Three were hurt so badly they may never walk the same. No one died, but the numbers do not tell the pain. Fifteen children were hurt.

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Since 2022, this corner of Queens saw 1,011 crashes. One person died. 528 were injured. Six suffered serious wounds. Cars and trucks did most of the damage. SUVs, sedans, and trucks together left dozens with broken bodies. Bikes and mopeds hurt a few, but the steel machines did the worst.

      Leaders Speak, Streets Wait

      City Hall says it is fighting for safety. They point to new speed limits, more cameras, and intersection redesigns. But the streets here still run red. Residents beg for speed bumps. They want action, not words.

      The council and the mayor have the power. They can lower speed limits to 20 mph. They can build real protection for children walking to school. They can push Albany to keep speed cameras running. Every day they wait, another family risks losing a child.

      What Now? Demand More

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real street redesigns. Demand cameras that never go dark.

      Do not wait for the next siren. Take action now.
