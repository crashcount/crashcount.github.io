      Sunset Park Bleeds: Demand Safe Streets Now

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed citation formatting, removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, and ensured all quotes and data have proper inline links.)

      Sunset Park (West): Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      Nine dead. Fourteen seriously hurt. In Sunset Park (West), from 2022 to June 2025, the streets have not forgiven. The bodies are not just numbers. They are neighbors, sons, daughters. In the last twelve months, 446 people were injured in 629 crashes. Four were left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same (NYC Open Data).

      No one under 18 died. But eighteen children were hurt. The oldest lost were over 65. The youngest, just 19, killed on a bike. The violence does not discriminate. It finds the young, the old, the careful, the distracted. It finds them on 4th Avenue, on 39th Street, on the Gowanus.

      What Hits Hardest

      Cars and trucks do the most damage. SUVs, sedans, and pickups are the main weapons. They killed two. They seriously hurt four. Trucks and buses left their own mark. Bikes and mopeds are not innocent, but their toll is smaller. The street is a battlefield, but the odds are not even.

      Leadership: Progress and Silence

      The city has tools. Sammy’s Law lets New York lower speed limits to 20 mph. Speed cameras have cut speeding and injuries where installed. But in Sunset Park (West), the carnage continues. Local leaders have the power to act. They can push for more cameras, slower speeds, safer crossings. They can fight for the living instead of counting the dead.

      Every day of delay is another family broken.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. These are not accidents. They are choices—by drivers, by lawmakers, by those who set the rules and walk away. If you live here, you know the danger. If you lead here, you own the outcome.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand more speed cameras. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Take action now.
