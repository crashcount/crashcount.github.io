      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      No More Names: Make 20 MPH the Law, End the Bloodshed

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed citation formatting, and cleaned citations.)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Death Toll Grows, Week After Week

      A man steps into the crosswalk. A car keeps going. The man does not get up. On July 3, a 36-year-old pedestrian was killed at Broadway and East New York Avenue. He was crossing at the intersection. The car was a sedan. The street was not empty. The city was not silent. NYC crash data

      In the last twelve months, 241 people have died in New York City traffic. 700 were seriously injured. The numbers do not flinch. Children, elders, workers, strangers. In the same period, over 48,000 were hurt. The city keeps moving. The bodies keep falling.

      Who Pays the Price

      The dead are not numbers. They are the old woman in the back seat on Cross Island Parkway. The young man on a moped in Brooklyn. The pedestrian at the intersection. The city’s streets are a gauntlet. Cars and SUVs do most of the killing—301 deaths, 509 serious injuries since 2022. Trucks and buses take their share: 93 deaths, 104 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 10 deaths, 44 serious injuries. Bikes: 11 deaths, 54 serious injuries. NYC crash data

      Leadership Moves—But Not Fast Enough

      The city has the power to lower speed limits. It has started to use it, but only in patches. Sammy’s Law lets New York set its own speeds. The city could make 20 mph the rule, not the exception. It has not. Speed cameras are renewed. Some streets are redesigned. But the pace is slow. The carnage is not.

      The council and the mayor hold the keys. They can lower the limit. They can pass the Stop Super Speeders Act. They can end the reign of repeat offenders. Every day they wait, another family gets the call.

      The Call to Action

      Enough delay. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Tell them to set a 20 mph limit citywide. Tell them to pass laws that stop repeat killers behind the wheel. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.
