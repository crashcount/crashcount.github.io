      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      Speed Kills. City Stalls. Blood Flows.

      Written August 7, 2025

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 7, 2025

      Just last week, a driver in Chinatown hit the gas in a stolen car, reaching 100 miles an hour. She struck a man on a bike and a woman sitting on a bench. Both died instantly. Prosecutors said the driver and her passenger tried to run after the crash. Police found alcohol and guns in the car. The judge called it murder.       The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      Since 2022, New York City has seen 1,091 deaths and nearly 192,000 injuries from crashes. In the last year alone, 285 people died. Children, cyclists, and pedestrians keep paying the price.

      Cars and SUVs are the main killers. In three years, they took 328 lives and left thousands more broken. Trucks and buses killed 99. Motorcycles and mopeds, 11. Bikes, 11. The numbers do not stop. The grief does not fade.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      Some leaders have moved. The city legalized jaywalking, ending a law that punished the poor and people of color. The state renewed speed cameras near schools. But the city still drags its feet on lowering speed limits. The law is there. The will is not.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand the Stop Super Speeders Act.

      Every day of delay means another family shattered. The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a choice. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in New York City?
      From 2022 to August 2025, cars and SUVs killed 328 people and caused over 27,000 injuries. Trucks and buses killed 99 and injured over 2,300. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 11 and injured more than 1,100. Bikes killed 11 and injured over 1,500. Cars and SUVs do the most harm.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These deaths and injuries are preventable. Speed, reckless driving, and unsafe streets are choices. Every crash is a policy failure, not fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign streets for safety, expand speed cameras, and pass laws like the Stop Super Speeders Act. They can act now, or let more people die.
      ▸ What has the city done lately to address traffic violence?
      The city legalized jaywalking and renewed speed cameras near schools. But it has not lowered the default speed limit to 20 mph, even though it now has the power.
      ▸ How many people have died or been injured in NYC traffic crashes recently?
      Since 2022, 1,091 people have died and nearly 192,000 have been injured in New York City traffic crashes.
