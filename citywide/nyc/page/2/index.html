      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      Queens nights: three dead on the curb, one gone in the dark

      Written August 25, 2025 (Revised August 25, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 16, 2025

      Speed did its work.

      On South Conduit Avenue at 155th Street, a driver hit a 52-year-old man at 2:30 a.m. and fled. Police said, “The driver sped off without stopping. No arrests have been made.” Gothamist reported the location and time. The Daily News said medics took him to Jamaica Hospital, where he died. City records log the case as a pedestrian killed at 155 St and South Conduit Ave, outside an intersection, at 2:26 a.m. The crash file is here.

      Hours earlier in Astoria, a car hit a food truck. Two men buying food died on the sidewalk. Witnesses told TV a “speeding car slammed into the men.” CBS New York aired the account. The driver also died, city data show, in a four-vehicle wreck at 42nd Street and 19th Avenue; two pedestrians were killed outside an intersection. The city’s dataset lists three deaths at that corner.

      The week did not spare riders either. On the Bronx River Parkway, two young men on mopeds were struck among four vehicles. Both were ejected and died. The file marks one Mercedes, one Volkswagen, and two mopeds. The crash record details the deaths.

      Across the city, the toll mounts.

      • In the last 12 months: 282 deaths and 52,347 injuries in 84,978 crashes, according to city data. See the period stats.
      • This year to date: officials count 182 deaths citywide, down slightly from last year’s pace, but serious injuries are up. The same dataset tracks it.
      • For people on foot, the killers are mostly cars and SUVs. Since 2022, sedans and SUVs account for the bulk of pedestrian deaths and injuries. The city’s rollups show it.

      Wrong-way, full speed, no mercy

      On the Clearview, a man drove into oncoming traffic and hit five cars. Two people were badly hurt. In court he said, “I entered the Clearview Expressway in the wrong direction because I wanted to hurt people and I felt ‘liberated’ by what I had done.” A victim recalled him saying, “You want to fight?” A judge gave him eight years. amNY reported the plea and sentence.

      JFK’s edge, no name, no goodbye

      Police said they were “searching the area Wednesday for surveillance footage.” The man’s name was not released that morning. The driver kept going. The Daily News documented the search. Gothamist counted 17 traffic deaths this year in Queens South through Aug. 10.

      Astoria corner, three lives

      City data say the Volvo SUV made a left. A Toyota sedan struck straight. Two pedestrians died where they stood. An 84-year-old driver also died. The crash entry lists the movements and the dead. TV called it speed. CBS New York used that word.

      Streets built for haste keep taking bodies. Numbers do not grieve. Families do.

      What will end the next one?

      • Lower the default speed limit citywide to 20 mph. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can act. Our guide explains the path and who to call.
      • Force repeat speeders to obey the limit. The Stop Super Speeders Act would require intelligent speed assistance for drivers with heavy ticket histories. Details and contacts are here.
