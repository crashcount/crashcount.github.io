      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      New York City traffic violence: Slow streets, Vision Zero

      Written August 15, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Converted parenthetical sources to descriptive inline links, tightened prose, removed an internal non-cited link, and cleaned/sorted citations.)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 15, 2025

      This week’s deaths

      On Wednesday near JFK, a driver hit a 52-year-old man and drove off. Police said, “The driver sped off without stopping. No arrests have been made.”

      At 2:27 a.m., another outlet reported: “The operator of the vehicle fled the scene after hitting the man.”

      On Tuesday in Queens, a car slammed into a food truck. Two pedestrians died. The driver died too. “Witnesses say a speeding car slammed into the men,” the station reported.

      On Monday, two riders on mopeds were killed on the Bronx River Parkway, according to city crash records.

      In three days, New York lost more neighbors. The tape goes up. The bodies go to Jamaica Hospital or the morgue. The cars roll on.

      The count

      Since January, at least 176 people have been killed and 458 seriously injured on city streets through Aug. 15, according to NYC Open Data. Last year by this point, deaths were higher.

      From 2022 through today, the toll is 1,100 dead and 2,505 seriously hurt, per NYC Open Data.

      Patterns we can see

      In Brooklyn last week, a 29-year-old motorcyclist died. The report lists “Unsafe Speed” and “Passing or Lane Usage Improper.” In the Bronx, a 44-year-old woman was struck off-intersection. The line reads “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” In Brooklyn, a 76-year-old woman was killed in a crosswalk. The field says “Traffic Control Disregarded.”

      These are boxes on a form. They are also funerals.

      Accountability

      Police say the JFK driver fled. No arrests have been made. The investigation remains ongoing.

      Act now

      Call your Council Member. Call the Mayor. Demand slower default speeds and streets that forgive mistakes. Start today and keep calling.
