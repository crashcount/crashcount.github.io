      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      No More Hit-and-Run City: Demand Streets That Don’t Kill

      Written August 4, 2025

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 4, 2025

      The City Bleeds, One Crash at a Time

      Just this week, a cyclist was struck in Washington Heights. The driver made a U-turn, hit her, and ran. Officers checked the victim, then left. The car sat abandoned. The street stayed the same. “No one stops at these stop signs. We see people go through these red lights all the time,” a neighbor said. The intersection is a known hazard, but nothing changes.

      On Saturday, a driver killed a 47-year-old man crossing Broadway in Brooklyn. The driver fled. Police found the body in the street. “A driver struck and killed a 47-year-old pedestrian… then left the scene,” police said. Early reports suggest a garbage truck. The victim’s name is not yet released. The driver is still gone.

      The Numbers: Death and Injury Without End

      In the last 12 months, 279 people died on New York City streets. Another 758 suffered serious injuries. Children, elders, cyclists, pedestrians. The city’s open data tells the story: 1,083 deaths since 2022. Over 191,000 injured. The numbers do not stop. They only grow.

      Cars and SUVs did most of the killing. Trucks, buses, motorcycles, mopeds, bikes—all played their part. But the street is not a fair fight. The most vulnerable lose.

      Leadership: Promises, Delays, and the Fight for Change

      Albany handed New York City the power to lower speed limits. The city can drop the default to 20 mph. It has not. Speed cameras were renewed, but the carnage continues. Residents beg for speed humps. “I really want there to be speed humps because it’s just terrifying,” said Nina Schmidt, a neighbor. The city studies. The city waits. The bodies pile up.

      What Comes Next: Refuse to Wait

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph limit. Demand real street redesigns. Demand action, not condolences. Every day of delay is another life lost. Refuse to let them wait you out.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in New York City?
      Since 2022, cars and SUVs caused 324 pedestrian deaths and 543 serious injuries. Trucks and buses caused 99 deaths and 105 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 11 deaths and 45 serious injuries. Bikes caused 11 deaths and 58 serious injuries. The street is not a fair fight. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These deaths and injuries are not random. They are the result of policy choices—speed limits, street design, enforcement. They can be prevented.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower the citywide speed limit to 20 mph, redesign dangerous streets, and enforce laws against reckless driving. They can act now, or they can wait for another tragedy.
      ▸ What has New York City done lately to address traffic violence?
      Albany gave the city power to lower speed limits. Speed cameras were renewed. But the city has not yet lowered the default speed limit. Residents are still waiting for real change.
