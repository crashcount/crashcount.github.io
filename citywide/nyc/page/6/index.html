      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      City Streets Run Red. Politicians Stall. Kids Pay the Price.

      Written August 6, 2025

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 6, 2025

      The Toll This Week

      Just this week, New York City bled again. A 13-year-old boy on a moped was thrown to the pavement after colliding with an MTA bus in Staten Island. He left the scene in critical condition, his head struck hard. The bus driver and passengers walked away unhurt. Police said, “the moped went through a stop sign without stopping and hit the bus” reported amNY. No arrests. The investigation drags on.

      Elsewhere, two NYPD cruisers collided in Queens while racing to a call. Four officers were sent to the hospital. “Two police vehicles were badly damaged from the collision,” said CBS New York. The cause is still under review. The city’s own enforcers are not immune to the chaos of its streets.

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      In the last 12 months, 284 people died on New York City streets. Over 52,000 were injured. 764 suffered injuries so severe they will never be the same. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians are the first to fall. Fourteen children have died in the past year. Forty-seven people aged 55 to 64. The city counts the bodies. The families count the empty chairs.

      Cars and SUVs do the most damage. 327 deaths and over 27,000 injuries came from these vehicles alone. Trucks and buses killed 99. Motorcycles and mopeds, 11. Bikes, 11. The city is a machine that chews through flesh and bone.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      The city has taken steps. Speed cameras in school zones will stay until 2030. The law now allows lower speed limits, but the default is still too high. The council and mayor have the power to drop it to 20 mph. They have not. Each day they wait, the toll grows.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph limit. Demand action against repeat speeders. The dead cannot speak. The living must shout for them. Take action now.

      Helicopter Breaks Apart Over Hudson River
      ▸

      Six died when a sightseeing helicopter shattered midair over the Hudson. The fuselage, rotor, and tail tore loose. Loud bangs echoed. The craft plunged. No black box. No warning. Only fragments and silence left behind.

      NY Daily News reported on May 7, 2025, that federal officials released images showing a sightseeing helicopter breaking apart in midair before crashing into the Hudson River, killing six. The National Transportation Safety Board’s preliminary report details how the Bell 206L-4 split into three pieces: 'the fuselage, the main rotor system, and the tail boom.' Witnesses heard 'several loud 'bangs'' before the breakup. The helicopter had flown eight tours that day, all with the same pilot, who had less than 50 hours in this model. The aircraft had a prior maintenance issue with its transmission assembly and lacked flight data recorders. The NTSB noted the pilot wore video-capable sunglasses, but they remain missing. The crash highlights gaps in oversight and the risks of repeated tour flights without robust recording or inspection requirements.
