      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      York and 72nd, then the sirens

      Written September 3, 2025 (Revised September 3, 2025)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 3, 2025

      Just before dawn on Aug 30, 2025, on York Ave near E 72 St, 36‑year‑old James Mossetty was hit and killed. Police later arrested a 71‑year‑old driver “for allegedly leaving the scene of an accident that resulted in the death of … James Mossetty.” amNY | NY Daily News

      They are one of 1,115 people killed on New York City streets since Jan 1, 2022. NYC Open Data

      Bold numbers don’t soften broken bones. This year to date, 191 people are dead, compared to 206 at this point last year. Serious injuries have risen to 519 from 502. NYC Open Data

      Brooklyn, a vest and a hit‑and‑run

      An NYPD sergeant directing traffic at Schenectady Ave and Church Ave was struck from behind by a black sedan on Aug 31. “Police are looking for the hit‑and‑run driver.” ABC7

      The uniform didn’t keep him safe. The cones didn’t either. The car kept going. ABC7

      The bridge and the body count

      On the Queensboro Bridge corridor, police say they made an arrest in a fatal crash over Labor Day weekend. The victim was the same man killed on York Avenue. “Police arrested 71‑year‑old Abdul Hakim … for allegedly leaving the scene.” amNY

      It is one story in a ledger that does not close. Citywide since 2022, the toll is 1,115 dead and 196,045 injured. NYC Open Data

      Slow the cars. Stop the repeat offenders.

      The tools exist. New York can lower speeds on city streets and rein in the worst drivers. A citywide 20 MPH default and required speed limiters for repeat speeders are on the table now. See the details and how to push them. /take_action/

      This ends when the speed ends. Start where Mossetty fell. Then keep going. /take_action/

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ How many New Yorkers have been killed since 2022?
      From Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 3, 2025, 1,115 people were killed in traffic crashes citywide, with 196,045 injured. Source: NYC Open Data motor vehicle collisions datasets.
      ▸ What recent incidents does this story cite?
      • The Aug 30, 2025 fatal crash that killed James Mossetty near E 72 St and York Ave, with a subsequent arrest reported by amNY and details from the NY Daily News. - An Aug 31, 2025 hit-and-run that struck an NYPD sergeant directing traffic in East Flatbush, reported by ABC7.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes: h9gi-nx95; Persons: f55k-p6yu; Vehicles: bm4k-52h4). We filtered for crashes occurring in New York City between 2022-01-01 and 2025-09-03, then summed fatalities and injuries citywide. Data were accessed Sep 3, 2025. You can start from the crashes dataset here and apply the same date filters.
      ▸ What can change this?
      Lower speeds and curb repeat offenders. NYC can set a lower default speed limit and Albany can require speed limiters for habitual speeders. See how to press for both on our action page at /take_action/.
