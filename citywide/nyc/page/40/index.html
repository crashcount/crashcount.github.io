      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      Just before 1 PM at 30th and 39th, another walker didn’t make it home

      Written September 4, 2025 (Revised September 4, 2025)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 4, 2025

      Just before 1 PM on Aug 31, at 30 St and 39 Ave, a 38‑year‑old woman in a marked crosswalk was struck and killed by a right‑turning Ford SUV. The cited factor: failure to yield. Source.

      They were one of 1,116 people killed on New York City streets since Jan 1, 2022. Source.

      This Week

      • Aug 30: A pedestrian was killed at York Ave and E 72 St in Manhattan. The vehicle was a taxi. Source.
      • Aug 23: A pedestrian was killed in Manhattan (United Nations Ave S). The vehicle was a sedan. Source.

      The count keeps climbing

      Year to date, the city records 192 deaths and 34,448 injuries; last year by this point it was 207 deaths and 36,679 injuries. Serious injuries rose to 520 from 505. Source.

      The bodies are spread across boroughs and modes. The pattern doesn’t blink. It only pauses, then resumes.

      Crosswalk doesn’t mean safe

      In the Queens case, the woman was crossing at an intersection, in a marked crosswalk. The driver was turning right. The record lists “failure to yield.” Source.

      On the Upper East Side, a pedestrian died on York and 72nd. Midtown to the river, the story repeats. Source.

      The fixes are known

      Lower the default speed. Use the law we already have. Fit repeat speeders’ cars with devices that keep them under the limit. The steps are on our page here.

      A city that slows its streets spares families the knock at the door. The next move belongs to City Hall and the Council. Start with speed. Then stop the worst offenders. Details.

      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets and filtered for NYC, Jan 1, 2022–Sep 4, 2025. Totals of people killed and seriously injured come from the Persons table by selecting records where injury_severity equals “Killed” or “Serious Injury.” Year‑to‑date comparisons use Jan 1–Sep 4 for 2024 and 2025. Reproducible queries: total deaths since 2022 here, 2025 YTD deaths here, 2024 YTD deaths here. Injury totals come from the Crashes table’s injury fields for the same windows.
