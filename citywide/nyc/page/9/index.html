      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      New York’s Streets Keep Taking Lives

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The week that didn’t stop

      Queens, 2:30 a.m., Aug. 13. A driver hit a 52-year-old man at 155th Street and South Conduit Avenue, then fled. He died at Jamaica Hospital. Police said the driver “sped off without stopping” and no arrests have been made said police. “Police were looking early Wednesday for a driver they said struck and killed a man… before fleeing the scene” reported Gothamist.

      The day before in Astoria, an 84-year-old driver lost control. Three people died: the driver and two men at a food cart. The street was left with wreckage. “Three people are dead… in a horrific morning crash,” amNY reported.

      Two days earlier, the Bronx River Parkway. Two moped riders, 19 and 21, were ejected and killed. Four vehicles in the crash, two mopeds and two sedans, southbound. Two young lives gone city data.

      The toll behind the headlines

      Since 2022, New York logged 344,459 crashes, with 1,106 deaths and 194,465 injuries city data. In the last 12 months alone, 285 people died and 774 were seriously hurt. This year to date: 182 deaths and 491 serious injuries. The numbers do not blink.

      For people on foot, cars and SUVs do the worst harm. Pedestrian injuries by vehicle: sedans 11,663 and SUVs 11,039. Trucks 1,485. Buses 614. Bikes 1,543. Motorcycles and mopeds 1,126. The pattern is clear, and it is heavy city data.

      Officials speak. Streets answer back.

      In Queens, a prosecutor described one driver who went the wrong way on a highway and smashed into cars: he “terrorized other drivers” said DA Katz. A judge gave him eight years. The damage remains.

      City Hall touts projects. “The redesign aims to improve the pedestrian experience,” NY1 reported about 14th Street, a $3 million design with public and BID funds NY1. Plans help. Pavement still kills.

      What would stop the bleeding

      Lower the default speed limit citywide. Slower streets save people walking at night, to work, to school. Require speed limiters for repeat offenders. Take the worst risks out of the throttle.

      Do one thing now

      Tell City Hall and Albany to slow the city and rein in repeat speeders. Start here: take action. Do it today. Tomorrow is already booked.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in New York City?
      Cars and Trucks: 28,310 total pedestrian injuries and deaths from sedans and SUVs; plus 2,394 from trucks and buses. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 1,126. Bikes: 1,543. Source: NYC Open Data, 2022–2025.
      ▸ What does it take to change traffic laws here?
      Real change needs both city and state. Local steps like street redesigns need a council member’s backing, City Council passage, and the mayor’s signature. State tools like automated enforcement require bills to pass the Assembly and Senate and be signed by the Governor. Local officials can push hard, but they can’t act alone.
