      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      Queens nights, dead ends

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      A body on South Conduit

      • A 52-year-old man crossed 155th Street at South Conduit Avenue near JFK just after 2:30 a.m. A driver hit him and kept going. He was taken to Jamaica Hospital and died. Police said, “The operator of the vehicle fled the scene.” ABC7. Gothamist. NY Daily News. The crash location and death are also listed in city data.

      • NYPD said detectives were looking for footage. No arrests were announced. Gothamist.

      • As of that week, 68 pedestrians had been killed this year citywide. NY Daily News.

      Astoria corner, three dead

      • On August 12, at 42nd Street and 19th Avenue in Queens, two pedestrians, ages 70 and an infant, were recorded as killed. A driver, 84, also died. The police log lists multiple vehicles; one SUV was turning left. Crash ID 4834594 in NYC Open Data.

      • The dataset lists the pedestrians as “Other Actions in Roadway,” not at an intersection. It notes “Crush Injuries.” The driver is recorded “Unconscious” and killed. NYC Open Data.

      Bronx River Parkway, two young riders

      • Just after midnight on August 11, two moped drivers, ages 21 and 19, were killed on the Bronx River Parkway. Both were ejected. Two sedans are listed as involved. Both mopeds are marked “Demolished.” Crash ID 4834345 in NYC Open Data.

      • The record shows both riders as unlicensed. The sedans were traveling south. NYC Open Data.

      Wrong way on the Clearview

      • In Queens, a man drove north in the southbound lanes of the Clearview Expressway in 2023. He struck five vehicles. Two motorists were badly hurt. A jury convicted him. A judge gave him eight years. He told police, “I entered the Clearview Expressway in the wrong direction because I wanted to hurt people and I felt ‘liberated’ by what I had done.” amNY.

      • Queens DA Melinda Katz said, “Joseph Lee terrorized other drivers.” “Two motorists were badly hurt and still have not fully recovered.” amNY.

      Relentless math

      • In the last 12 months: 85,404 crashes. 52,640 injured. 280 killed. NYC Open Data.

      • Pedestrians are struck most often by cars and SUVs. Those vehicles have killed at least 299 pedestrians in the period tracked. NYC Open Data rollup.

      Streets built for speed, people pay

      • On 14th Street, the city says it will redesign the corridor “to improve the pedestrian experience,” with $3 million for design work from the city and two BIDs. NY1.

      • Policy moves continue elsewhere. City data shows 17 traffic deaths in Queens South this year through August 10. Gothamist.

      What breaks the pattern

      • Albany renewed 24-hour school-zone speed cameras through 2030, according to our prior coverage. The cameras cut speeding where installed. Now the city must slow the whole grid and stop the worst repeat offenders. See our plan and the bills it names here: Take Action.
