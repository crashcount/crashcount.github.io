Another Week, Another Four Dead: NYC’s Streets Keep Killing

By The Count of Crash

February 10, 2025

The week of January 27

The Toll Keeps Rising

Four more lives lost. Another 750 injured. That’s this week’s count. Since the start of the year, 18 people are dead. Nearly 4,000 injured. Some will walk again. Others won’t.

A bicyclist died in the early morning hours of January 29. A man, 59. Crushed. On January 31, a pedestrian died trying to cross with the signal. He was 29. Two car occupants lost their lives as well—one at 3 a.m., another during the morning rush. The details blur. The outcome is the same.

A City That Still Won’t Change

We should be getting better at this. We were promised a safer city. Vision Zero. Speed cameras. Street redesigns. But the numbers don’t lie. Serious injuries are up 23% from last year. The deaths keep coming. The crashes, too—more than 1,400 last week alone.

Speed is still the biggest killer. Albany finally gave the city the power to lower speeds with Sammy’s Law. The city hasn’t done it. Why? How many more bodies does it take?

Blood on the Asphalt

Last week, a man waited for a tow truck in East New York. Someone shot him in the legs after a crash. “Cops are asking for the public’s help,” reported the Daily News. Violence breeds violence on these streets.

Meanwhile, the crashes keep coming. A bicyclist, 61, suffered severe bleeding from a head injury. An 88-year-old pedestrian was left in shock, his head bleeding after being struck while crossing. Another pedestrian, 40, suffered crush injuries to her hip while using a marked crosswalk. These aren’t accidents. They are the inevitable result of bad streets and worse policies.

What’s Next? More Deaths—Unless We Act

We know what works. Lower the speed limits. Redesign the streets. Enforce the rules. Albany gave us the power. The mayor needs to use it. Lower the citywide speed limit to 20 mph. No excuses, no delays. Demand action now.
