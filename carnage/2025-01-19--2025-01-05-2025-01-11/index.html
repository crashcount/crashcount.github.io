Week of Jan 05

She Was Crossing the Street

She was small. Maybe six, maybe seven. She stepped into the crosswalk at Avenue L and East 88th Street. The light was red. She had the right of way. A driver turned anyway. The SUV struck her. She died there in the street.

She was one of two people killed in New York City this week. One of hundreds lost this year. One of too many. NYC Open Data.

The City Knew This Would Happen

The driver failed to yield. That is what the report says. It is the same failure that kills pedestrians again and again. The city knows this. It has known for years.

Mayor Adams promised safer intersections. He said, “We’re doubling our pace of intersection safety improvements.” But the deaths keep coming.

Speed cameras work. Lower speed limits save lives. But the city moves too slow. “Our leaders are failing New Yorkers. 2024 is on pace to be the deadliest year.”

How Many More?

A child is dead. Another will die soon. The city will say the right words. Officials will promise action. And the streets will stay the same.

This is not inevitable. This is not an accident. This is a failure. And it will happen again unless we stop it.

Call your council member. Demand safer streets. Because if we don’t, another child will die. And another. And another.

Take action now.
