Two More Dead. How Many More Before We Act?

By The Count of Crash

January 20, 2025

The week of January 6

Blood on the Asphalt

The new year brought death. A man, 68, crossing in a marked crosswalk. Struck. Head trauma. Dead. A baby, not even old enough to walk, killed at an intersection. Crushed. Two lives gone in the first weeks of 2025.

Traffic violence was supposed to be ending. Vision Zero was meant to bring deaths to nothing. But here we are. Again.

The Toll So Far

Since January began, 2,340 crashes. 1,295 injuries. Twenty of them life-altering. Two dead. Compared to last year, deaths are down. But statistics don’t bring back the lost. And every injury, every crash, leaves scars.

Last week alone: 1,405 crashes. 788 people hurt. Fifteen seriously. Two more dead.

The numbers are better. But better isn’t good enough.

A City That Could, But Won’t

Congestion pricing is in place. The first of its kind in the country. The plan aims to cut cars in Manhattan, ease gridlock, and push people onto transit. “The nation’s first Congestion Relief Zone opened at 12:00:01 a.m. on Sunday, January 5, 2025, and is fully operational,” reported NY1. It should help. But it won’t be enough.

Albany has already given New York City the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. The mayor and council could act today. They haven’t. They must.

The data is clear: Lower speeds save lives. Pedestrians hit at 30 mph die five times more often than those hit at 20 mph. A citywide 20 mph limit isn’t radical. It’s necessary.

Act Before the Next Name is Added

Two more are dead. They won’t be the last unless something changes. Demand action. Demand safer streets. Tell your representatives that what we need now is to take action. Slow the speed. Stop the carnage. Before another name joins the list.
