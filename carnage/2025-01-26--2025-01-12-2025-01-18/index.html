Week of Jan 12

She Crossed With the Light. It Didn’t Matter.

She was 70. She stepped into the crosswalk at 7th Avenue and 44th Street in Brooklyn. The light was in her favor. A sedan turned anyway. It struck her head-on. She never got up.

A witness called 911. The driver stayed. The report says she suffered a head injury. It does not say if she had family waiting for her. It does not say where she was going. It only says she died.

She was one of three people killed on New York City streets this week. Two were over 65. They had lived long lives. The city did not let them live longer.

A Pattern the City Ignores

The numbers tell the story. Of the 207 crashes involving people aged 65 to 74 this week, two ended in death. A 71-year-old woman in the Bronx suffered severe head wounds while crossing. A 67-year-old in Queens was left bleeding in the street.

Older pedestrians are the most at risk. Their bodies break easier. Their reflexes are slower. But the streets do not slow down for them.

The City Knew This Would Happen

New York has promised safer streets. Vision Zero was supposed to end deaths like these. Speed cameras have helped, but not enough. “The program has reduced speeding, decreased the number of injuries, and made our streets safer,” said DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez. But the deaths keep coming.

Mayor Adams pledged to “double the pace of intersection safety improvements.” But the city moves too slow. The elderly do not have time to wait.

Advocates have warned about this for years. “We need to have the political courage…to create a city that is walkable, prioritizes pedestrians, and ends these senseless murders,” said Council Member Shahana Hanif.

How Many More?

A 70-year-old woman died in Brooklyn. A 73-year-old man died on the BQE. A 71-year-old woman in the Bronx may never fully recover.

This is not an accident. This is a failure. And it will happen again unless we stop it.

Call your council member. Demand safer streets. Demand lower speed limits. Demand raised crosswalks. Demand pedestrian-first design.

Because if we don’t, another elder will die. And another. And another.

Take action now.
