Six Dead This Week. Thirty This Year. How Many More?

By The Count of Crash

February 24, 2025

The week of February 10

Another Week, Another Tragedy

Six more dead. Thirty this year. The goal was zero.

An 83-year-old woman, crossing with the signal, was hit and killed. A 41-year-old driver died in a wreck at 4 a.m. Three elderly passengers—two of them 78 years old—died in one crash. A cyclist suffered severe lacerations. A baby, not yet one, was left bleeding on the pavement.

The streets remain relentless.

The Numbers Tell the Story

There were 1,495 crashes last week. That’s lower than the average, but still too many. 834 people were injured. Ten suffered serious injuries—amputations, paralysis, crush wounds. Six didn’t make it at all.

Since the year began, 9,883 crashes have maimed or killed 5,296 people. Serious injuries are up. Deaths are down slightly, but 30 lives lost is not a victory. It is failure.

What We Could Do, But Don’t

Traffic violence is preventable. London cut fatalities by 30% with lower speed limits. Queens Boulevard was once the ‘Boulevard of Death’ until protected bike lanes and pedestrian islands changed the numbers.

New York City can lower the speed limit to 20 mph right now. Albany gave the city the power with Sammy’s Law. But action hasn’t come.

The Department of Transportation made small changes last week, adjusting traffic lights on Third Avenue to create a ‘green wave’ for cyclists. It’s a start—but not enough.

What You Need to Do

Take action. Demand the city drop the speed limit now. Every week we wait, someone else dies. Maybe six more next week. Maybe more. The numbers do not lie. And the streets do not forgive.
