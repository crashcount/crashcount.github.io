Week of Apr 20

The Street Takes and Does Not Return

Christopher Gayton walked with a cane. He was sixty-two. He stepped off the curb on Linden Boulevard, near his home in Queens. A black car came fast. It hit him. The driver did not stop. Another car, a Ford Edge, rolled over him. The driver stayed. Christopher did not get up. He died the next day in the hospital. His family wrote, “Today is a sad day. We lost a brother, father, son, uncle, and cousin. He has been snuffed from us by a hit and runner driver.” He was just crossing the street to get home. “We will miss you forever. Your laugh, calm personality.”

A witness saw it happen. “It felt so surreal witnessing a hit and run crash in front of my eyes,” said Japneet Singh. “I saw an elderly man with a cane get ran over twice. It was so horrific.” Police are still searching for the first driver who fled the scene.

The Toll of Ordinary Streets

Christopher was not alone. In one week, 1,448 crashes. 846 hurt. 6 dead. The city keeps moving. The street keeps taking. A Mack truck on Liberty Avenue struck a woman. Her shoulder torn open. Blood on the pavement. The driver walked away. A Ford SUV crashed head-on. The driver died, harness still buckled. Another man survived. The road remembers.

A young woman, twenty-three, crossed Woodhaven Boulevard. A motorcycle ran the light. Metal struck flesh. She died there, whole body broken, as the city slept. “The only reason why she was out that night is because she was chasing her dreams as usual,” said her friend. Her mother waited for a text that never came. “Usually she texts me when she’s on her way back home. And I did text her but she didn’t text me back. So I figured that she was busy. And then that’s when I found about the accident.” Her mother called her “a beacon of light.”

The City Absorbs Another Wound

Every week, the names change. The pain does not. The city absorbs another wound. The numbers do not stop. The street does not care. The dead do not come home.

You can take action. The city will not change itself. The next name could be yours.
