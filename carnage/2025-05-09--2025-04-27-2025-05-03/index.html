Week of Apr 27

The Crash on Broome Street

On May 1, George Smaragdis rode west on Broome Street. He was a musician, known as Starcadian. He made music that moved people. That morning, a van door swung open. George hit it and was thrown into the street. A red box truck rolled over him. He died on the pavement. His last words to fans were, “Hi I’m alive and working very hard on the best album I’ve ever made, I can’t wait to play it for you.” He never got the chance. “George was one of the most influential guys to touch the scene. So many people were inspired to pursue synthwave careers because of his music,” said Mark Ryan, a fan.

The Scene and the Silence

The van driver said, “It happened so fast. I opened the door. I didn’t even see the guy. I only saw the accident.” Paul, a worker nearby, watched the aftermath. “I saw a body on the ground, and then the fire people were trying to revive him. It didn’t look good,” he said. “They ripped his shirt off, and it was terrible.”

George was 44. He lived in Brooklyn. He rode a bike because he cared about the world. “He was socially and politically conscious. He was a very smart guy,” said Julian Green, another fan. The street did not care.

The Toll This Week

One death. 621 injured. Nine seriously hurt. The city keeps moving. The numbers drop, but the pain does not. “On May 1, a cyclist was struck and killed after he rode into a van’s open door, propelling him into the intersection of Broome and Centre Streets in Soho, where he was subsequently crushed under a moving commercial truck,” the news said. The city calls it progress. The street keeps the blood.

What You Can Do

Join those who fight for the living. Take action. One name lost is too many. The city will not remember unless you make it.
