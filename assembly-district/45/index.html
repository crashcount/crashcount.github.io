      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 45?

      Who Dies for Misha Novakhov’s Votes?

      Written July 9, 2025

      AD 45: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 9, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      In Assembly District 45, the street does not forgive. Since 2022, 15 people have died and 1,973 have been injured in crashes. Eighteen were left with injuries so grave they may never walk the same. See the NYC Open Data.

      The dead are not numbers. They are children, elders, neighbors. On March 29, a mother and her two daughters were crossing Ocean Parkway with the signal. A sedan and a taxi struck them. Three did not get up. The street was quiet after. A child’s shoe left behind.

      Just last month, a 90-year-old man was hit by a masked moped rider. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said an eyewitness. The rider fled. The old man clung to life.

      The Toll of Inaction

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. Of the deaths and injuries to pedestrians, nine came from cars and SUVs, one from a truck or bus, and none from bikes. The numbers do not lie. The danger comes on four wheels, heavy and fast.

      The violence is not random. It is allowed. In the last year alone, five people died, and 657 were hurt. Three of the dead were children. The youngest was four.

      Leadership: Who Stands Up, Who Stands Aside

      Assembly Member Misha Novakhov has stood in the way of proven safety tools. He voted against renewing the city’s speed camera program—a measure that saves lives in school zones. He called for higher speed limits on Ocean Parkway, where families have been killed crossing with the light. He dismissed the need for speed limiters on cars owned by repeat offenders, saying, “any driver can get much more than six” tickets in a year.

      The street is not safer for it.

      What Now

      Call Assembly Member Novakhov. Demand he support speed cameras, lower speed limits, and real penalties for repeat dangerous drivers. Do not wait for another child’s name to be written in chalk.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws and oversees state policy, including traffic safety measures.
      ▸ Where does AD 45 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 48 and state senate district SD 22.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 45?
      It includes the Gravesend (South), Brighton Beach, Gravesend (East)-Homecrest, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 44, District 47, and District 48, and State Senate Districts SD 22 and SD 23.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 45?
      Cars and Trucks: 9 deaths, 310 minor injuries, 89 moderate injuries, 6 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 8 minor injuries, 1 moderate injury, 0 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 3 minor injuries, 2 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. See NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are not random. Most deaths and injuries come from cars and trucks, and proven tools like speed cameras and lower speed limits can prevent them.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can support speed cameras, lower speed limits, and back laws that hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable.

      Novakhov Supports Safety Boosting Stop Super Speeders Bill
      ▸

      After a crash killed a mother and two daughters in Gravesend, advocates and Council Member Shahana Hanif rallied for the Stop Super Speeders bill. The law would force repeat reckless drivers to use speed-limiting tech. Survivors demand action. Lawmakers promise change.

      On April 1, 2025, Council Member Shahana Hanif joined a rally at Brooklyn Borough Hall demanding passage of the Stop Super Speeders bill. The bill, sponsored in Albany by State Sen. Andrew Gounardes and Assemblymember Emily Gallagher, would require drivers with repeated violations to install intelligent speed assistance (ISA) devices. These devices cap speed at 5 mph over the limit for those with 11 or more license points in 24 months or six camera tickets in a year. The rally followed a fatal Gravesend crash that killed a mother and her two daughters. Hanif and other lawmakers called current enforcement—ticketing, suspensions, fines, jail—ineffective. Assembly Member Jo Anne Simon said, 'The speed limiter technology is available to us. Let’s use it. It will save lives.' The bill is modeled on EU and Virginia laws. Some opposition remains, but supporters say the measure is urgent and practical.
