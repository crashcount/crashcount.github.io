      Fourteen Dead, Still No Change: Who Pays for Brooklyn’s Broken Streets?

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 18, 2025 – Fixed duplicate headline in body, deduped and cleaned citations.)

      AD 45: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      In Assembly District 45, the street is a gauntlet. Fourteen dead. Sixteen with injuries so grave they will not forget. In the last twelve months, three children died. Two more children were cut down, their bodies broken but alive. A mother and her daughters, crossing with the light, never made it home. The numbers are a drumbeat: 997 crashes, 667 injured, 9 left with wounds called “serious” by the city, but the word is too small.

      A five-year-old girl, an eight-year-old girl, a four-year-old boy, and their mother—all crossing with the signal—were killed at Ocean Parkway and Quentin Road. The city’s data is blunt: “Crush Injuries. Apparent Death.” NYC Open Data.

      An 87-year-old man was pinned under a bus at East 12th and Avenue Z. He was standing near the corner. The bus turned left. The man was taken away in critical condition. Police said officers who responded to the scene discovered an 87-year-old man pinned under the city bus… Gothamist.

      Leadership: Action and Evasion

      Assembly Member Misha Novakhov has stood on both sides of the line. He co-sponsored a bill to require complete street design for state-funded projects, a step toward safer roads for all A 324. He also backed a bill to boost street safety for all users A 1077. But Novakhov voted against a bill to bring speed cameras to school zones A 7652, a measure proven to slow drivers where children walk.

      He co-sponsored a bill to require e-scooter licenses and insurance A 116. This bill puts the burden on those with the least power, not on the drivers or the roads that kill. No bill from Novakhov has forced the city to lower speed limits or redesign deadly intersections.

      The Cost of Delay

      A chain-reaction crash at Avenue U and Coney Island Avenue left two NYPD officers with broken bones and a passenger in critical condition. One police officer suffered a broken pelvis and arm, as well as head trauma, while another suffered a broken hip NY Daily News. The driver was charged with DWI. The street remains unchanged.

      What Now?

      The dead do not write laws. The living must. Call Assembly Member Novakhov. Demand he fight for lower speed limits, more speed cameras, and street redesigns that protect the smallest and the oldest. Do not wait for another child’s name in the data.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws and oversees state policy, including traffic safety.
      ▸ Where does AD 45 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn and city council district District 48.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 45?
      It includes the Gravesend (South) and Gravesend (East)-Homecrest neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 44, District 47, and District 48, and State Senate Districts SD 22 and SD 23.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 45?
      Cars and Trucks: 9 deaths, 88 moderate injuries, 6 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 1 death, 7 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 1 moderate injury, 0 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 2 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or can they be prevented?
      Crashes are not fate. Lower speeds, safer street design, and enforcement prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass laws for lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, and expand speed camera programs—measures proven to save lives.
