      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 45?

      Assembly District 45: Kids Die, Drivers Speed, Novakhov Blocks Safety

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 45: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Death Toll Grows

      In Assembly District 45, the street is a gauntlet. Since 2022, 15 people have died and 1,973 have been injured in crashes. Children, elders, mothers. The numbers do not flinch. In the last year alone, five more lives were lost. Three were children under 18. The disaster does not pause for grief.

      Just days ago, a 90-year-old man was killed crossing Avenue U. A blue moped hit him and fled. A witness described the scene: “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open.” The driver did not stop. The family waits for answers. The street remains the same.

      Patterns of Harm

      Cars and SUVs are the main killers. Of the deaths and injuries to pedestrians, nine came from cars and SUVs, one from a truck, and four from taxis. Motorcycles and mopeds left their mark too, but the carnage is driven by the largest vehicles. The old and the young are hit hardest. In the last year, three children died. Nineteen people over 75 were injured.

      Leadership: Votes and Silence

      Assembly Member Misha Novakhov has stood in the way of proven safety tools. He voted against renewing speed cameras in school zones, a measure that saves lives. He called for higher speed limits on Ocean Parkway, even as families mourned the dead. At the funeral for a mother and her two children, killed by a repeat speeder, Novakhov dismissed the need for speed limiters, saying, “any driver can get much more than six” tickets. He called speed cameras a punishment for “regular drivers.”

      He has sponsored bills that shift the burden to cyclists and e-scooter riders, demanding licenses and insurance for the most vulnerable, while opposing stronger rules for drivers.

      The Call

      The blood on the street is not fate. It is policy. It is votes. It is delay. Call your Assembly Member. Demand speed cameras, lower speed limits, and real protection for people outside cars. Do not wait for the next siren.

      Novakhov Supports Safety Boosting Stop Super Speeders Bill
      ▸

      After a crash killed a mother and two daughters in Gravesend, advocates and Council Member Shahana Hanif rallied for the Stop Super Speeders bill. The law would force repeat reckless drivers to use speed-limiting tech. Survivors demand action. Lawmakers promise change.

      On April 1, 2025, Council Member Shahana Hanif joined a rally at Brooklyn Borough Hall demanding passage of the Stop Super Speeders bill. The bill, sponsored in Albany by State Sen. Andrew Gounardes and Assemblymember Emily Gallagher, would require drivers with repeated violations to install intelligent speed assistance (ISA) devices. These devices cap speed at 5 mph over the limit for those with 11 or more license points in 24 months or six camera tickets in a year. The rally followed a fatal Gravesend crash that killed a mother and her two daughters. Hanif and other lawmakers called current enforcement—ticketing, suspensions, fines, jail—ineffective. Assembly Member Jo Anne Simon said, 'The speed limiter technology is available to us. Let’s use it. It will save lives.' The bill is modeled on EU and Virginia laws. Some opposition remains, but supporters say the measure is urgent and practical.
