      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 79?

      No More Blood for Convenience: Demand Safer Streets Now

      Written August 11, 2025 (Revised August 12, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      AD 79: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 11, 2025

      The Toll in AD 79: Lives Cut Short, Families Shattered

      In the last twelve months, five people died and over 700 were injured on the streets of Assembly District 79. The dead include a 43-year-old father, dragged half a block by a speeding Mercedes on Webster Avenue this May. His mother described the horror: “That car deliberately went straight speeding, didn’t stop, hit my son all the way up into the air and came down, dragged him half a block, they killed my son.” The driver fled. The family is left with a hole that will never close.

      Just weeks later, a car slammed into a sidewalk shed in Melrose, injuring six. The shed was meant to shield pedestrians from falling bricks, not from cars that leave the road. The numbers do not let up: in three years, 15 killed, 2,237 injured, 39 seriously hurt—and the wounded are often the young, the old, the ones who never saw it coming.

      Who Pays the Price? Vulnerable Lives, Repeat Offenders

      Pedestrians and cyclists take the brunt. In this district, SUVs and sedans are the main weapons—4 deaths and 5 serious injuries by cars, more by trucks and buses, mopeds, and bikes. The pattern is clear. The pain is not spread evenly. Children, elders, and those on foot or bike are most at risk.

      What Has Assembly Member Jackson Done?

      Assembly Member Chantel Jackson has voted to expand school speed cameras and extend school speed zones, moves that help protect children near schools. She has also co-sponsored a bill to boost street safety for all users. But Jackson has backed measures that make it harder to build bike lanes and weaken bus lane rules, putting vulnerable road users at greater risk.

      The silence is loudest where it matters most: no push for a 20 mph citywide speed limit, no action against repeat speeders, no fight for street redesigns that put people before cars.

      The Call: Demand More, Demand It Now

      Every day of delay is another day of blood on the street. Call Assembly Member Jackson. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit, real protection for people walking and biking, and an end to policies that slow safety for the sake of driver convenience.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like AD 79 in Albany.
      ▸ Where does AD 79 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 17 and state senate district SD 29.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 79?
      It includes the Melrose, Morrisania, Claremont Village-Claremont (East), Crotona Park East, Crotona Park, Tremont, and Bronx CB3 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 15, District 16, and District 17, and State Senate Districts SD 29 and SD 32.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 79?
      Most deaths and serious injuries to pedestrians came from SUVs and Sedans (4 deaths, 5 serious injuries), with more from Trucks and Buses (no deaths, 4 serious injuries), Motorcycles and Mopeds (no deaths, 1 serious injury), and a smaller number from Bikes (no deaths, 0 serious injuries). NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The patterns are clear and repeatable. With safer street designs, lower speed limits, and enforcement against reckless drivers, most deaths and injuries can be prevented.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can push for a 20 mph citywide speed limit, support street redesigns that protect people, expand speed cameras, and fight for laws that hold reckless drivers accountable.
