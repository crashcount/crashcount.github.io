      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 35?

      Speed Kills. Silence Kills More.

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      AD 35: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Dead Keep Coming

      Northern Boulevard. 4:12 a.m. A BMW moves north on 107th Street. A Mercedes runs the red. The BMW is hit, thrown into a parked car. The roof is torn off. Justin Diaz, 23, is dead. He was driving to work at LaGuardia. The man who killed him was an off-duty firefighter, drunk, high, and driving 83 in a 25. He had 25 prior school zone speeding tickets. The DA called it plain: “Drunk, drugged and reckless driving are dire threats to everyone on our shared roadways.” Diaz’s brother said, “Pena will walk the streets for a $50,000 bail… Justin will never walk the streets again.”

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      Seven people have died on these streets since 2022. Nearly 2,000 have been hurt. Eighteen were left with injuries so grave they may never walk right again. Children, elders, cyclists, workers. Most killed by cars and trucks. Some by motorcycles, a few by bikes. The dead are not numbers. They are sons, daughters, neighbors. They are gone.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Assembly Member Larinda Hooks voted to extend and expand school speed cameras. She voted yes to keep children safer near schools. She also voted to weaken bus lane rules, making it easier for cars to block the way for buses, bikes, and people on foot. She has not led on lowering the speed limit or stopping repeat speeders. The silence is loud.

      What Next? The Cost of Waiting

      Every day without action is another day someone is seriously hurt or killed. Call Assembly Member Hooks. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real penalties for repeat speeders. Demand streets where a man can drive to work and come home alive. The dead cannot speak. You must.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, votes on budgets, and represents local districts like AD 35. Open States
      ▸ Where does AD 35 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 21 and state senate district SD 11. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 35?
      It includes the East Elmhurst, North Corona, Corona, Laguardia Airport, Flushing Meadows-Corona Park, Queens CB80, Queens CB81, and Queens CB3 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 21, District 22, and District 24, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 13, and SD 15. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 35?
      Most injuries and deaths came from Cars and Trucks (237 incidents, 2 deaths), followed by Motorcycles and Mopeds (10 incidents, no deaths), and Bikes (4 incidents, no deaths). NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data shows patterns—speeding, red-light running, repeat offenders. These deaths are preventable with better laws and enforcement.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, and pass laws to keep repeat dangerous drivers off the road. They can fight for safer streets, not just safer drivers.

      Bill S 8344, titled 'Extends provisions and makes technical corrections to school speed zones in NYC; repealer,' moved through the Senate and Assembly in June 2025. Sponsored by Senator Andrew Gounardes, the bill passed Senate votes on June 12 and June 13, and cleared the Assembly on June 17. The measure extends and corrects school speed zone laws in New York City, repealing outdated provisions. Gounardes led the push, with strong support from most Senate Democrats and a split Assembly. The bill's technical fixes aim to keep protections in place for children and other vulnerable road users near schools. No safety analyst note was provided.
