      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 58?

      East Flatbush: deaths at dusk, promises at noon

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 58: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      East Flatbush keeps a ledger. The entries are names and hours.

      Church Avenue: a walk signal, then silence

      On May 14, 2025, a driver in a 2025 Mercedes made a left onto Rutland Road. She hit 68‑year‑old Maurette Lafleur in the crosswalk, where Lafleur had the walk. Lafleur died at Kings County Hospital. The driver stayed. No immediate charges. NY Daily News and Streetsblog reported the crash. “The lady spin around and sped through,” a witness said. “I heard the van run over her and her bones crunching.” Streetsblog

      Six months earlier, at Church and Kings Highway, a 30‑year‑old man crossing against the signal was struck by a sedan going straight. He died. The state crash file lists “Unsafe Speed.” NYC Open Data

      On July 17, 2025, an SUV driver hit a 36‑year‑old man at E. 55th St. and Church. Police later charged the driver with vehicular manslaughter. The Daily News said he had been drinking. NY Daily News The city’s dataset records the death. NYC Open Data

      Linden Boulevard: wide lanes, heavy grief

      A 44‑year‑old man on an e‑scooter died after contact with an MTA bus on Linden Boulevard near Church Avenue on Oct. 20, 2024. He was ejected. The file lists him as unlicensed; the bus was going straight. NYC Open Data

      Linden is a hotspot in this district, with the most injuries and multiple serious cases since 2022. District data puts it at the top. Church Avenue is next. District data

      The bodies add up. Since 2022, this district counts at least seven pedestrians killed. “Other motorized” riders and vehicle occupants are dead, too. Nights take more. Deaths spike at 6 p.m., 7 p.m., and 9 p.m. District data

      What drives the harm

      The state crash files show what keeps breaking people here. “Other” factors lead the list, but the named patterns are clear: inattention, unsafe speed, disregarding signals. Failure to yield injures many. District data

      Pedestrians bear the worst of it: seven dead, nearly 500 injured since 2022. SUVs and sedans are the main killers of people on foot in these records. Trucks and vans do their share. District data

      This year is worse. Through Aug. 26, crashes are up 18% over last year to 877. Deaths are up from 1 to 5 year‑to‑date. Injuries up 44%. Period stats

      The corner fixes are known

      Start at the killers. Harden left turns on Rutland at E. 95th and along Church. Add leading walk time. Daylight the mouths of Linden Boulevard and Church Avenue. Build raised crossings at the bus stops and schools. Target the late‑day and night hours when deaths cluster.

      Speed cameras in school zones run 24/7 through 2030 under a June vote in Albany. Keep them working. Lawmakers advanced a bill to extend and clean up the rules. Open States

      Citywide steps that save lives

      Lower speeds mean fewer funerals. New York now has the authority to set lower limits and expand 20 mph zones. The city can act. It has not finished the job. Our call is simple: a default 20 for residential streets, and real consequences for the worst repeat speeders. See how to press City Hall and Albany here: Take Action.

      Voices that won’t fade

      “Next thing I know, he was into the fence. Absolutely crazy,” a witness said after a fleeing driver in Canarsie put a Jeep on the L tracks. Police took him in. The Brooklyn Paper

      “As of Thursday, 60 pedestrians had been killed by vehicles across the city this year,” the Daily News noted in July. NY Daily News

      The numbers do not grieve. People do.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where are the worst crash locations in AD 58?
      Linden Boulevard and Church Avenue lead the district for injuries and severity, according to NYC Open Data since 2022.
      ▸ When do deadly crashes cluster here?
      Deaths peak in the evening: around 6 p.m., 7 p.m., and 9 p.m., per the district’s hourly distribution.
      ▸ What factors show up most in severe crashes?
      Unsafe speed, inattention/distraction, and disregarding signals recur in state crash files for this area.
      ▸ What policy moved recently on speed cameras?
      Albany advanced S 8344 to extend and fix NYC school speed zone rules; the program operates 24/7 through 2030.
