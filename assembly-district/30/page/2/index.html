      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 30?

      Blood on Broadway: End the Killing, Reclaim Our Streets

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      AD 30: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      Fifteen dead. Twenty-three left with wounds that will not heal. In the last three and a half years, Assembly District 30 has seen 498 people injured and six seriously hurt in just the past twelve months. No one under 18 died, but thirty-two children were hurt. The old were not spared. A 75-year-old man, struck at Broadway and Roosevelt, never made it home. A 78-year-old woman, hit by a moped at Grand Avenue, did not get up again. A cyclist, crushed on Maurice Avenue, left his bike behind. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie. NYC Open Data

      Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and cars killed three. Trucks and buses, one. Motorcycles and mopeds, one. Bikes, one. The dead were walking, riding, crossing. They were not protected by steel or speed. They were not the ones who made the rules.

      What Has Been Done

      Assembly Member Steven Raga has sponsored and co-sponsored bills to force repeat speeders to install speed-limiting tech, to require complete street design, and to hold vehicle owners liable for harm. He has pushed for automated cameras to ticket double-parkers and clear blocked crosswalks. He voted yes on school speed cameras. These are steps. But the street does not wait. The next crash is already set in motion.

      What Comes Next

      Every day without action is another day of blood on the asphalt. Call your Assembly Member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras that never sleep. Demand streets built for people, not just cars. The dead cannot speak. The living must.

      Take action now.
