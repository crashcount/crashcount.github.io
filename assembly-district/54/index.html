      Another Body, Another Excuse—End the Bloodshed on Brooklyn’s Streets

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 17, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      AD 54: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      Blood on the Streets

      A man crosses Fulton Street in Cypress Hills. A burgundy Ford Explorer comes fast. The man does not make it to the other side. The driver does not stop. Police say, “the man was crossing the street in Cypress Hills when he was hit by a burgundy Ford Explorer” CBS New York. He dies at Brooklyn Methodist Hospital. The car keeps going. The street forgets, but his family will not.

      In the last twelve months, one person died and fifteen suffered serious injuries in Assembly District 54. Over 580 were hurt. Children, elders, cyclists, pedestrians. The numbers do not bleed, but people do.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did most of the damage. Trucks, vans, and motorcycles followed. In three years, five people died. Thirty-three were left with wounds that do not heal. The dead do not get a second chance. The living limp on.

      Leadership: Words, Votes, and Silence

      Assembly Member Erik Dilan co-sponsored bills to make streets safer for all users A 1077, A 1280. These bills demand complete streets—sidewalks, bike lanes, crossings. But when it came time to vote on a bill for more school speed cameras, Dilan said no. The bill passed anyway. The children in Schenectady will get cameras. The children here still wait.

      A man is killed crossing the street. The driver flees. “A burgundy Ford Explorer with Pennsylvania license plates struck and killed the 55-year-old man… The vehicle did not remain on the scene” ABC7.

      What Now?

      The crisis is not fate. It is policy.

      Call Erik Dilan. Call your council member. Demand more speed cameras, lower speed limits, and streets built for people, not machines. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.
