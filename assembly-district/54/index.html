      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 54?

      Five Dead, Dozens Broken—Dilan’s District Bleeds While Drivers Speed

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned up citations for deduplication and order.)

      AD 54: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Toll on Our Streets

      In Assembly District 54, the violence does not stop. Since 2022, five people are dead. Thirty-four more are left with serious injuries. The numbers do not tell you how it feels to see a man struck in the crosswalk, or a cyclist thrown from his bike. But the numbers do not lie. In the last year alone, there were 1,120 crashes, 631 injuries, and 15 serious injuries. The dead do not speak. The wounded limp on.

      Just last month, a grocery delivery worker pulled over on Lincoln Avenue. A driver clipped his open door. Words were exchanged. The driver opened his hatchback, pulled out a pipe, and beat the worker in the head and body. “A 36-year-old man delivering groceries was hospitalized after he was viciously beaten with a pipe during a road rage-fueled attack on a Brooklyn street, police said” (NY Daily News). The attacker fled. The worker went to Jamaica Hospital.

      A month before, a man tried to cross Fulton Street in Cypress Hills. A burgundy Ford Explorer hit him. The driver did not stop. “Investigators say a burgundy Ford Explorer with Pennsylvania license plates struck and killed the 55-year-old man… The vehicle did not remain on the scene” (ABC7).

      Who Pays the Price?

      The old and the young are not spared. In the last twelve months, one person aged 65–74 died. Forty-seven children under 18 were injured. Most of the wounds come from cars and SUVs. Trucks, vans, motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes all play their part, but the steel and speed of cars do the most harm.

      What Has Dilan Done?

      Assembly Member Erik Dilan has voted to extend and expand school speed zones and speed cameras (Open States). He co-sponsored bills to require safer street designs for all users. But he also voted to weaken bus lane rules, putting pedestrians and cyclists at greater risk. He voted no on expanding speed camera hours in 2022. The record is mixed. The blood on the street is not.

      What Next?

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Dilan. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people on foot and on bikes. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      The street is not safe until the most vulnerable are safe.

      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, oversees state agencies, and represents local districts like AD 54. Open States

      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 37 and state senate district SD 18. Open States

      It includes the Bushwick (East), The Evergreens Cemetery, Cypress Hills, East New York (North), East New York-City Line, Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (South), Brooklyn CB4, and Brooklyn CB5 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 37 and District 42, and State Senate Districts SD 18 and SD 19. Open States
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 54?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm: 1 death, 235 minor injuries, 63 moderate injuries, and 6 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 1 serious injury and 14 other injuries. Bikes caused 4 minor injuries and 1 moderate injury. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and better enforcement. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can pass and enforce laws for lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, and demand safer street designs that protect people walking and biking. Open States
