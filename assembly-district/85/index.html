      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 85?

      No More Blood in the Crosswalks—Fix the Streets Now

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      AD 85: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Toll in Plain Sight

      In Assembly District 85, the numbers are not just statistics—they are bodies in the street. Since 2022, 16 people have died and 2,119 have been injured in traffic crashes here. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians are among the dead. In the past year alone, two more lives ended—one a child, one an older adult. Twelve others suffered serious injuries.

      Just this February, a teenager and a 14-year-old girl were thrown from a motorbike at East 172nd and Metcalf. The boy died. The girl survived. The driver stayed at the scene. Police said, “Both teens were thrown from the bike and rushed to Jacobi Hospital. The older victim succumbed to his injuries while the girl was in stable condition.” No arrests. No answers. The street is quiet again, but the loss remains.

      Who Pays the Price?

      The dead are not just numbers. A 56-year-old man, crossing East 163rd at Westchester, was struck and killed by an SUV. A 23-year-old moped rider died on Intervale Avenue. A 32-year-old woman died walking on the Bruckner. Each crash leaves a hole. Each crash is a warning. Most victims are on foot, on bikes, or riding mopeds.

      What Has Been Done—And What Hasn’t

      Assembly Member Emérita Torres has voted to extend and expand school speed zones and speed cameras, moves that protect children and slow some drivers. Torres voted yes to extend school speed zones, improving child pedestrian safety. But Torres also voted to weaken bus rules, a move that puts more pedestrians and cyclists at risk. The work is not finished. Speed kills. Delay kills.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Every crash is preventable. Every death is a failure of policy, not fate. Call Assembly Member Torres. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people outside of cars. Do not wait for another child’s name to be written in blood on the street.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, oversees state agencies, and represents local districts like AD 85.
      ▸ Where does AD 85 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 18 and state senate district SD 11.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 85?
      It includes the Longwood, North & South Brother Islands, Crotona Park East, Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River, Soundview-Clason Point, Soundview Park, Rikers Island, Bronx CB2, and Bronx CB9 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 17, District 18, and District 22, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 29, SD 32, and SD 34.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 85?
      Most injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 85 were caused by Cars and Trucks (5 deaths, 332 total incidents), followed by SUVs (2 deaths, 115 incidents), and Unspecified vehicles (2 deaths, 84 incidents). Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes caused fewer incidents and no pedestrian deaths.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data shows these crashes are preventable, not random. Policy and street design can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand automated enforcement, redesign dangerous streets, and pass laws that protect people outside of cars.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
