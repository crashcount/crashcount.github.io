      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Crown Heights hit-and-run. Another name lost on a fast street.

      AD 43: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 20, 2025

      A driver hit and killed a woman in a Crown Heights hit-and-run on Sep 19, 2025. Police were still looking for the driver. CBS New York.

      She is one of 11 people killed on the streets of Assembly District 43 since 2022, with 3,120 injured. Most of the people killed were walking or biking. NYC Open Data.

      This month

      • Sep 19: A driver fled after killing a woman in Crown Heights. CBS New York.
      • Sep 14: During a police pursuit, a teenage driver struck a person walking in Brooklyn. amNY.
      • Aug 31: A driver hit an NYPD officer from behind at Schenectady and Church in East Flatbush and fled. ABC7.

      Where the pain concentrates

      Eastern Parkway leads the board here: 3 people killed and 194 injured since 2022. Utica Avenue shows 2 deaths and 180 injured. Flatbush, Nostrand, and Albany also carry heavy tolls. NYC Open Data.

      Crashes swell in the late afternoon. Injuries peak at about 4 PM with 250 hurt, and deaths stack up in the evening hours, including three at 6 PM and three at 8 PM. NYC Open Data.

      What keeps showing up

      Police records in this district list distraction and failure to yield among the repeat causes of harmed people. Inattention is tied to 43 injuries. Failure to yield is tied to 18 injuries. NYC Open Data.

      People walking bear the worst of it: 6 killed and 609 injured. People on bikes: 1 killed and 345 injured. Drivers and passengers fill ERs too: 1,993 injured. NYC Open Data.

      Streets still fast. Votes still thin.

      Assembly Member Brian Cunningham represents this district. He backed Sammy’s Law in 2023. Streetsblog NYC. In June, he missed a key vote on a bill fixing school speed-zone rules. Open States.

      Cunningham has pushed to ticket illegal parking with street sweeper cameras. “Sometimes we can’t account for whether or not the trucks actually got down the block, or whether or not cars are obstructing them,” he said. Streetsblog NYC.

      Fix what we can see

      • Slow turns and protect crossings on the killers: Eastern Parkway, Utica, Flatbush, Nostrand, Albany. Use daylighting and hardened turns. Target failure-to-yield hot spots. NYC Open Data.
      • Aim enforcement at the evening window when deaths spike, and the 4–6 PM hours when injuries surge. NYC Open Data.

      The citywide tools that would save lives here

      Two moves would cut the bloodletting on these blocks:

      • Lower speeds on local streets. The city has the power and has begun to use it; the job isn’t done. /take_action/.
      • Stop the worst repeat offenders with intelligent speed assistance for “super speeders.” The policy is ready; it needs passage and use. /take_action/.

      A woman died on a Crown Heights street this week. The fixes are known. The clock is loud. /take_action/.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We analyzed NYC’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets — Crashes (h9gi-nx95), Persons (f55k-p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k-52h4) — for incidents from 2022-01-01 to 2025-09-20 within Assembly District 43. We used spatial filtering to include only crashes whose location falls in AD 43. Mode counts (e.g., people walking or biking) and injury severity come from the Persons table. Intersection tallies and hourly patterns come from the Crashes table. Data were accessed on 2025-09-20. You can explore the base datasets here.
      ▸ Which streets in AD 43 are the most dangerous?
      Eastern Parkway has 3 deaths and 194 injuries since 2022. Utica Avenue has 2 deaths and 180 injuries. Flatbush, Nostrand, and Albany also show high injury totals. Source: NYC Open Data (Crashes).
      ▸ When do serious crashes tend to happen here?
      Injuries peak around the late afternoon, with 250 injuries at about 4 PM. Deaths pile up in the evening, including three at 6 PM and three at 8 PM. Source: NYC Open Data (Crashes).
      ▸ Who represents this area, and what have they done on street safety?
      Assembly Member Brian Cunningham represents AD 43. He backed Sammy’s Law in 2023. He was marked ‘excused’ on a June 2025 vote to extend and fix school speed-zone rules. Source: Streetsblog NYC (June 7, 2023) and Open States (S 8344). State Senator Zellnor Myrie and Council Member Rita C.
