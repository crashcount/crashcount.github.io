      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 52?

      Twelve Dead in AD52—How Many Bodies Before Albany Acts?

      Written August 12, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      AD 52: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 12, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Assembly District 52, the numbers do not lie. Twelve dead. Thirty-eight left with serious injuries. Over 2,500 hurt since 2022. The bodies are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. They are the woman crushed by a dump truck turning on Butler Street. The cyclist, 24, thrown from his bike and killed on Sackett and 4th. The 72-year-old woman, struck by a taxi at Flatbush and 5th, never made it home. These are not accidents. They are the cost of inaction.

      The Usual Suspects

      Cars and trucks did the most harm. They killed two, seriously injured eleven, and left over 300 battered. Motorcycles and mopeds hurt nineteen. Bikes, too, left their mark—one dead, one seriously injured, thirty-seven hurt. The street does not care who you are. It only cares how fast and how heavy.

      Leadership: Small Steps, Big Gaps

      Assembly Member Jo Anne Simon has moved. She voted to extend school speed zones, a step to protect children near schools (extended school speed zones). She co-sponsored bills to force repeat speeders to install limiters (speed limiter bills). She called for daylighting—removing parked cars from corners—calling it a “no-brainer” because “people feel a lot safer crossing those intersections.” But the pace is slow. The deaths keep coming. The city waits for the next name, the next number.

      The Call: No More Waiting

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day without action is another day someone does not come home. Call your Assembly Member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed limiters for repeat offenders. Demand daylight at every corner. Do not wait for another body in the road.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, oversees the budget, and represents districts like AD 52 in Albany. Open States
      ▸ Where does AD 52 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 33 and state senate district SD 20. Open States
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 52?
      It includes the Brooklyn Heights, Downtown Brooklyn-Dumbo-Boerum Hill, Brooklyn Navy Yard, Carroll Gardens-Cobble Hill-Gowanus-Red Hook, Park Slope, Brooklyn CB2, and Brooklyn CB6 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 35, and District 39, and State Senate Districts SD 20, SD 25, and SD 26. Open States
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 52?
      Cars and trucks killed 2, seriously injured 11, and hurt over 300. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 19 injuries. Bikes were involved in 1 death, 1 serious injury, and 37 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not fate. They are the result of policy decisions, street design, and enforcement. They can be prevented. Streetsblog NYC
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, pass and enforce speed limiter laws for repeat offenders, and redesign streets to protect people walking and biking. Open States
