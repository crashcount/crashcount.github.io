      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 68?

      Bodies in the Crosswalk, Blood on Their Hands

      Written June 22, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 68: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 22, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      A man steps into the crosswalk. A car does not stop. In the last twelve months, five people died on the streets of Assembly District 68. Ten more were left with injuries so grave they may never walk the same. In that same year, 682 people were hurt in crashes. The numbers do not fade. They pile up.

      A cyclist is thrown from his e-bike in Central Park, his head striking the curb. He does not get up. The pedestrian he tried to avoid walks away with a cut on his hand. The city records another death. Nico-Garcia was on an e-bike and was not wearing a helmet at the time he was thrown. The law does not require one. The law does not bring him back.

      On Randall’s Island, a mother rides home from work. She is beaten for her e-bike. Her daughter stands in the dark and demands more protection, more lights, and calls it “ridiculous that the park… had no lights and no cameras.”

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs, sedans, and trucks do most of the damage. In three years, cars and trucks killed four people and injured hundreds more. Motorcycles and mopeds took another life. Bikes killed one. The rest is blood and paperwork.

      The Record of the Lawmaker

      Assembly Member Eddie Gibbs has signed his name to bills that promise safer streets. He co-sponsored measures for “complete streets” design and school speed cameras. He praised new subway funding, but the trains do not run on the street. The dead do not ride the subway.

      But the bills wait. The deaths do not. The streets remain the same.

      What Comes Next

      The crisis is not fate. It is policy. Call Eddie Gibbs. Call the council. Demand the city use its new power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. Demand protected crossings, working lights, and cameras that never go dark. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.
