      Blood on Myrtle, Silence in Albany: Demand Action Now

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed citation formatting, deduped and sorted citations.)

      AD 53: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      Five dead. Eleven left with injuries that will never fully heal. In the last year alone, 600 people in Assembly District 53 have been hurt in crashes (NYC Open Data). Two were children. One was just crossing with the light. The numbers are not numbers. They are bodies on the street, families waiting in hospital halls, a phone that will never ring again.

      On Myrtle Avenue, a 22-year-old died in the back seat of a sedan. On Wyckoff and DeKalb, a 59-year-old man was crushed by a van while crossing with the signal. On Evergreen Avenue, a 26-year-old cyclist was killed, his ride ending in the dark. These are not accidents. They are the cost of streets built for speed, not for life.

      Broken Promises, Broken Bodies

      Assembly Member Maritza Davila has taken some steps. She co-sponsored bills to force speed limiters on repeat offenders and to require safer street design for all users. She voted yes to bring speed cameras to school zones. She signed on to a letter demanding the state fix the deadly BQE.

      But the blood keeps running. The intersection at Scott and Flushing saw 39 crashes in four years before the city finally moved to close it. “Unfortunately, somebody has to die before something gets done,” said Joann Perahia after her neighbor was killed there.

      The Human Cost

      The pain does not fade. Ruari Fay-Handebeaux was thrown fifteen feet by a hit-and-run driver in Bushwick. “Broken ankle, broken humorous, bunch of fractures in my skull,” he told reporters. His father was killed by a driver too. The intersection remains unchanged. The driver is still out there.

      Hayden Wallace, 29, died in a hit-and-run. His aunt remembered, “He lived life to the fullest. He was only 29 years old and lit up every room he entered.” (NY Daily News)

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Davila has backed some good bills. But the carnage continues. Advocates must demand more.

      Call Assembly Member Davila. Demand she fight for lower speed limits, more protected crossings, and full funding for street redesigns. Demand she push for real accountability for drivers who kill and maim. Do not wait for another body in the road.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, oversees the state budget, and represents local districts like AD 53.
      ▸ Where does AD 53 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn and city council district District 34.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 53?
      It includes the East Williamsburg and Bushwick (West) neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 34 and District 37, and State Senate District SD 18.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 53?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm to pedestrians in AD 53, with 2 deaths and 53 moderate injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 1 serious injury and 5 moderate injuries. Bikes caused 5 moderate injuries (NYC Open Data).
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not random accidents—they are the result of policy, street design, and enforcement. Safer streets and lower speeds can prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and support laws for lower speed limits, fund protected crossings and bike lanes, and demand accountability for dangerous drivers.

      Sedan Slams Parked SUV, Passenger Crushed
      ▸

      A sedan struck a parked SUV on Bushwick Place. Metal met stillness. A 27-year-old man, riding outside, took the blow. His leg crushed. He stayed conscious. Driver inattention and inexperience set the scene. The street bore the mark.

      A sedan traveling east on Bushwick Place crashed into a parked SUV. According to the police report, a 27-year-old man riding outside the sedan suffered crush injuries to his leg but remained conscious. Several other passengers, including young adults and a child, were involved but did not report serious injuries. The report lists 'Driver Inattention/Distraction' and 'Driver Inexperience' as contributing factors. The sedan’s front end struck the SUV’s rear. No safety equipment was used by those riding outside. The crash underscores the danger when drivers lose focus and lack experience behind the wheel.
