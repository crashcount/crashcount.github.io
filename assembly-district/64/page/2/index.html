      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 64?

      Six Dead in District 64—How Many More Before Tannousis Acts?

      Written June 23, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 64: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 23, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Assembly District 64, the road does not forgive. Six people killed. Four left with injuries so grave they may never walk the same. In the last year alone, 567 hurt, 893 crashes. The old, the young, the ones just trying to cross the street. A man, 80, struck dead on Hylan Boulevard, a block from home. The driver stayed. The man did not. Police said, “She was heading north on Hylan Blvd. when she struck the victim” NY Daily News.

      A 58-year-old man, Jose Luis Gomez-Guallazaca, killed by an e-scooter on Targee Street. He suffered “severe trauma throughout his body” New York Post. The scooter rider survived. The city moved on.

      Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. In the past three years: 6 deaths, 256 injuries, 5 serious injuries from SUVs and cars alone. Trucks and buses: 1 dead, 29 hurt. Bikes: 0 dead, 11 hurt. The numbers do not lie. The street is not safe for those outside a car.

      What Has Mike Tannousis Done?

      Assembly Member Mike Tannousis has not led. He voted no on a bill to bring speed cameras to school zones in Schenectady—cameras proven to slow drivers and save children A 7652. He sponsored a bill to repeal congestion pricing, a move that would put more cars and more danger on city streets A 6680. Not one bill to protect the people walking or biking in his district.

      What Comes Next

      No one is coming to save you. Not unless you make them. Call Mike Tannousis. Demand action. Demand speed cameras, lower speed limits, and real street redesigns. Stand with the families who have lost everything. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Take action now.
