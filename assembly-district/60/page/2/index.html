      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 60?

      Names on Asphalt—How Many More Will We Lose Before They Act?

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised July 13, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 60: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll: Lives Lost, Bodies Broken

      A woman steps into the crosswalk at Blake and Pennsylvania. An SUV turns left. She is struck. She dies that day. Her name was Janet Henriquez. The driver stayed. The charges came months later: failure to yield, failure to exercise due care. The street stayed the same. The NY Daily News reported the arrest, but the city moved on.

      A few blocks away, a man in a BMW loses control on the Belt Parkway. The car leaves the road, slams a tree. He is dead before the ambulance arrives. The parkway keeps its speed. The SUV skidded off the road and slammed into a tree along the parkway.

      In the last 12 months, 3 people died and 9 suffered serious injuries on these streets. Children, elders, workers—no one is spared. Over 900 hurt. The numbers do not bleed, but the people do.

      The Record: Leadership and the Long Wait

      Assembly Member Nikki Lucas missed the vote on a bill to expand school speed cameras in Schenectady. She was excused. No recent bills, no public push for lower speeds or safer crossings here. The silence is heavy. The streets stay fast.

      The city talks of redesigns and studies. The Conduit, a deadly corridor, will finally see a plan. “Brooklyn and Queens Residents deserve a Conduit Boulevard that improves daily life, not a roadway that puts pedestrians and drivers at risk,” said DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez. But plans do not slow cars. Studies do not stop funerals.

      The Next Step: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      Speed kills. Delay kills. The law now allows New York City to lower speed limits. The city can act. Your Assembly Member can push for more cameras, more enforcement, more street redesigns. Or they can do nothing. The choice is theirs. The cost is ours.

      Call Nikki Lucas. Call the Mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras that work all day, every day. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. Act now. Take action.
