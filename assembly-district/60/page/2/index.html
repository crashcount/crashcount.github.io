      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Pennsylvania and Hegeman: speed, a curb, and a man bleeding on a Sunday

      AD 60: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 19, 2025

      Just before 10 AM on Sep 14, a driver in a sedan hit a 71-year-old man at Pennsylvania Avenue and Hegeman Avenue in East New York. The station said it was “just before 10 a.m.” and left “debris all over the street.” ABC7 Police records list unsafe speed and an unlicensed driver at the scene. NYC Open Data

      This Month

      • Sep 14: a driver hit a person walking at Pennsylvania and Hegeman; police recorded unsafe speed. NYC Open Data
      • Aug 24: a person on a bike was severely injured on Bethel Loop. NYC Open Data

      The toll on these blocks

      Since Jan 1, 2022, Assembly District 60 has recorded 15 people killed and 4,441 injured in 7,090 crashes. NYC Open Data

      People walking bear the brunt: 8 killed and 671 injured. People on bikes: 1 killed and 179 injured. Inside vehicles: 6 killed and 3,476 injured. NYC Open Data

      Evening into night is when bodies break. At 4 PM there were 307 injuries and 10 serious injuries; at 5 PM, 292 injuries; at 8 PM, two deaths were logged. NYC Open Data

      What keeps killing here

      Police most often cite inattention and distraction, failure to yield, unsafe speed, and drivers blowing past signals. In this district, those factors are recorded alongside dozens of injuries: inattention (56), failure to yield (22), unsafe speed (8), traffic control disregarded (6). NYC Open Data

      Some corners show the pattern in full view. Linden Boulevard and Pennsylvania Avenue. The Belt Parkway. New Lots Avenue. These are the repeat sites in the data. NYC Open Data

      Fixes that fit these streets

      Make drivers turn slower and see the people in front of them. Hardened left turns and daylighting at wide, multi-lane corners like Linden and Pennsylvania. Give people walking a head start with leading pedestrian intervals on New Lots and along Pennsylvania. Target speed: enforce where the injuries spike late in the day, and at repeat hotspots named above. NYC Open Data

      Albany moved once. City Hall and Albany must move again.

      Assembly Member Nikki Lucas voted yes to extend school speed zones this June. Open States That keeps cameras running, but the bodies keep coming on Linden and Pennsylvania.

      Two next steps are on the table. Lower the default city speed limit and fit repeat speeders’ cars with speed limiters. The city has the authority to lower speeds, and Albany has bills to curb habitual speeders. The Assembly Member, State Senator Roxanne Persaud, and Council Member Chris Banks know these streets. They can act. Open States

      A man was hit on a Sunday morning at Pennsylvania and Hegeman. The fixes are known. Act now: take action.

      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We analyzed NYC’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets for Crashes, Persons, and Vehicles, filtered to Jan 1, 2022–Sep 19, 2025 and geographically clipped to Assembly District 60 using CrashCount’s geofilter. We pulled records on Sep 18, 2025. The base datasets are listed here with related tables for Persons and Vehicles. For the Sep 14 crash, see CrashID 4842244; for the Aug 24 bike crash, see CrashID 4837485.
