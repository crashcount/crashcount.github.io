      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 50?

      Twelve Dead, Thousands Injured—Brooklyn Streets Are Killing Fields

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 50: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Assembly District 50, the numbers do not lie. Twelve people killed. Twenty-six left with serious injuries. Over two thousand hurt. These are not just numbers—they are lives ended or broken on the streets of Greenpoint, Williamsburg, and South Williamsburg since 2022. The dead include a ten-year-old girl, crushed by an SUV while crossing with the signal at Franklin Avenue and Wallabout Street. The driver failed to yield. She never made it home. NYC Open Data

      The violence does not stop. In March, a man was killed by an e-bike rider who ran a stop. In June, the city moved to strip protection from the Bedford Avenue bike lane after a series of crashes. The city called it a safety fix. The result: cyclists forced back into the path of cars and trucks.

      Whose Safety Counts?

      The fight over Bedford Avenue exposed the fault lines. One resident pleaded, “I beg the court, please for my safety and the safety of my children and all the 2,000 children living close to the dangerous new bike lane, please allow the mayor to shift the bike lane’s location on the roadway.” NY Daily News

      But others warned, removing the protected lane would bring “irreparable harm” to the neighborhood. The city’s own plan now puts cyclists back in the open, with nothing but paint between them and speeding steel. NY Daily News

      Leadership: Action and Delay

      Assembly Member Emily Gallagher has not been silent. She backed the McGuinness Boulevard redesign, calling out opposition as “about fear, bad faith and control.” She sponsored a bill to force repeat speeders to install speed-limiting devices. She stood with families after the Gravesend crash, demanding Albany pass the Stop Super Speeders Act. But the bill sits in committee. The carnage continues.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Every day of delay means another body on the street. Call Assembly Member Gallagher. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for bike lanes and real consequences for repeat offenders. Do not wait for another child’s name to be added to the list.

      Gallagher Supports Safety Boosting Stop Super Speeders Bill

      After a crash killed a mother and two daughters in Gravesend, advocates and Council Member Shahana Hanif rallied for the Stop Super Speeders bill. The law would force repeat reckless drivers to use speed-limiting tech. Survivors demand action. Lawmakers promise change.

      On April 1, 2025, Council Member Shahana Hanif joined a rally at Brooklyn Borough Hall demanding passage of the Stop Super Speeders bill. The bill, sponsored in Albany by State Sen. Andrew Gounardes and Assemblymember Emily Gallagher, would require drivers with repeated violations to install intelligent speed assistance (ISA) devices. These devices cap speed at 5 mph over the limit for those with 11 or more license points in 24 months or six camera tickets in a year. The rally followed a fatal Gravesend crash that killed a mother and her two daughters. Hanif and other lawmakers called current enforcement—ticketing, suspensions, fines, jail—ineffective. Assembly Member Jo Anne Simon said, 'The speed limiter technology is available to us. Let’s use it. It will save lives.' The bill is modeled on EU and Virginia laws. Some opposition remains, but supporters say the measure is urgent and practical.

The measure now awaits DOT action.
