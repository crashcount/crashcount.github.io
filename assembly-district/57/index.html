      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 57?

      Crushed in the Crosswalk: Stop the Killing, Fix the Streets

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised July 5, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 57: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      Three dead. Eight left with injuries that will not heal. In the last year alone, Assembly District 57 saw 1,088 crashes. Pedestrians and cyclists paid the price—crushed bodies, broken lives. A 55-year-old woman, struck by an SUV on Washington Avenue, died with crush injuries to her whole body. A 39-year-old man, killed by a truck and SUV on Atlantic Avenue, never made it home. These are not numbers. They are neighbors.

      The Blame Game and the Cost of Delay

      SUVs and trucks led the body count. Of the pedestrian injuries, SUVs caused 39, trucks 5, and sedans 33. The city talks of safety, but the street tells another story. “Before this bike lane from 2018 to 2022 this is the area that had the second largest numbers of pedestrian fatalities and crashes in the city,” said DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez about Bedford Avenue (Streetsblog NYC).

      The city removed a protected bike lane after a child was hit—not by a car, but by a cyclist dodging double-parked cars. The mayor called it a response to “community concerns” (amNY). The cars stayed. The danger stayed. The protection is gone.

      Leadership: Action and Inaction

      Assembly Member Phara Souffrant Forrest has taken steps. She co-sponsored bills to force speed limiters on repeat dangerous drivers and to require streets designed for all users. She voted yes on school speed cameras. She stood with other Brooklyn leaders to demand a complete bike lane network and condemned crackdowns that target cyclists instead of reckless drivers (Streetsblog NYC).

      But the carnage continues. Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home.

      What Now? No More Waiting

      Call your council member. Call your assembly member. Demand 20 mph speed limits. Demand protected bike lanes that stay. Demand enforcement against the drivers who kill and maim.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Judge Halts Removal Of Brooklyn Bike Lane
      ▸

      A judge stopped the city from tearing out a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue. The lane stays for now. A toddler was struck by an e-bike. City Hall acted without consulting locals. The fight over street safety continues.

      The New York Post reported on June 18, 2025, that Brooklyn Supreme Court Judge Carolyn Walker-Diallo blocked Mayor Adams' plan to remove a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue. The ruling followed a lawsuit by Transportation Alternatives, who argued that removing the lane would endanger road users. The judge found City Hall 'acted arbitrarily, capriciously, and illegally' by failing to consult local officials or the community. The city cited 'several dangerous incidents—many of which involved children getting seriously hurt' as the reason for the proposed removal, after a 3-year-old was struck by an e-bike in May. The protected lane will remain until a court hearing in August. The case highlights the tension between rapid policy changes and the need for community input on street safety.
