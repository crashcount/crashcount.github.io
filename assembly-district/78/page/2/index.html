      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 78?

      Nine Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Still No 20 MPH. Demand Action Now.

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 78: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      In Assembly District 78, the street is a wound that never closes. Nine people have died since 2022. Twenty people suffered injuries so severe they changed lives. In the last year alone, 484 neighbors were hurt, five of them gravely. The dead include a 65-year-old man crushed by a van while crossing with the light, and a 15-year-old girl ejected from a moped, her life ended on University Avenue. The numbers are not just numbers. They are fathers, daughters, elders, children.

      The Human Cost

      A man lay dying on the Major Deegan Expressway. The driver who hit him ran. Police said, they were looking for a man who ran away after crashing his car. No arrest. No answers. Only another family left with silence.

      On Southern Boulevard, two drivers turned the street into a shooting gallery. A deli worker watched the chaos and said, “It was like a movie.” But there was no hero. Only gunfire, a crash, and a body in the street.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Assembly Member George Alvarez has signed on to bills that could help. He co-sponsored a law to force speed limiters on repeat dangerous drivers—those who rack up tickets or points (A 2299). He backed a “complete streets” bill to make roads safer for everyone (A 1077). He voted yes on speed cameras near schools (A 7652).

      But the blood on the street says it is not enough. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not. Cameras catch speeders, but only if Albany keeps them turned on. Each delay is another risk, another name for the list.

      What You Can Do

      Call George Alvarez. Call the Mayor. Call the DOT. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand streets built for people, not just cars. The dead cannot speak. The living must.

      Take action now.
