      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 78?

      Blood in the Bronx: How Many Deaths Before Alvarez Acts?

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 78: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt: Recent Crashes in AD 78

      A man runs from a wreck on the Deegan. A 39-year-old driver dies alone in the night. Police say, “the hit-and-run driver responsible escaped on foot”. No arrest. No answers. Just another family left waiting for a call that will never come.

      A few blocks away, a van turns right at Kingsbridge and University. A 65-year-old man is crossing with the signal. He does not make it to the other side. The city records another death. The street stays the same.

      In the last twelve months, 471 people were hurt in 735 crashes here. Three suffered injuries so grave they may never walk right again. One did not survive.

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      Children, elders, workers—no one is spared. In three and a half years, nine people have died and over 1,500 have been injured on these streets. Cars and SUVs did most of the killing. Vans, trucks, and mopeds took their share. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care if you are young or old.

      Leadership: Promises and Pressure

      Assembly Member George Alvarez has signed his name to bills that could save lives. He co-sponsored A 2299, a measure to force repeat speeders to install devices that keep them from breaking the limit. He backed A 1077, a bill for streets built for people, not just cars. He voted yes on school speed cameras.

      But the blood keeps coming. The bills sit. The streets do not change fast enough. “Police said Monday they were looking for a man who ran away after crashing his car into a disabled vehicle on the Major Deegan Expressway and killing its driver over the weekend” (Gothamist).

      What Next: No More Waiting

      Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home. Call Assembly Member Alvarez. Tell him to fight for speed limiters, for complete streets, for real enforcement against reckless drivers—not just more tickets for cyclists and walkers. Demand action. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.
