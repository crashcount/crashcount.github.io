      Astoria Bleeds—Who’s Still Standing By?

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised August 6, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      AD 36: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt: Recent Crashes in AD 36

      The streets of Astoria and Long Island City do not forgive. Since 2022, nine people have died in traffic crashes across Assembly District 36. Over 2,000 have been injured. In the last twelve months alone, 609 people were hurt, three were seriously injured, and two did not make it home. The youngest lost was just 36, a cyclist killed at 34th Avenue and 37th Street. The oldest, 59, died riding a moped on Northern Boulevard. The numbers do not tell you about the silence that follows, or the families left behind.

      Just last month, a cyclist was killed in a police chase. Weeks before, a pick-up truck struck an infant on Steinway Street. The pattern repeats: cars, trucks, and SUVs do the most harm. The city counts the bodies. The crisis crawls on.

      Leadership: Action and Absence

      Assembly Member Zohran Mamdani has backed bills to curb repeat speeders, co-sponsoring legislation to force speed-limiting tech into the cars of the worst offenders (A2299). He has called for more protected bike lanes and for moving traffic enforcement out of NYPD hands (called for more protected bike lanes). He stood with advocates against the removal of proven safety measures, like the Bedford Avenue bike lane (stood with advocates).

      But when it mattered, Mamdani missed a key vote to extend school speed zone protections (missed a key vote). The bill passed, but absence leaves a mark. The work is not done.

      The Human Cost

      A mother’s words after her son died subway surfing cut through the noise: “We were blindsided. We never planned for a funeral for a child. They’re kids. They think they are having fun, they think it doesn’t affect other people but then it does.” (her son died subway surfing)

      The city’s transit chief put it plain: “This was as avoidable as it is tragic.” (transit chief put it plain)

      What Comes Next

      Every crash is preventable. Lower the speed limit. Build the lanes. Enforce the law against the reckless, not the vulnerable. Call Assembly Member Mamdani. Call your council member. Demand action.

      Do not wait for another child’s funeral.
