      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 36?

      Astoria Bleeds While City Hall Sleeps

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised July 1, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 36: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Toll in Astoria and Long Island City

      Two people are dead. Five more are seriously hurt. In the last twelve months, 570 people in Assembly District 36 have been injured in 1,004 crashes. These are not just numbers. They are bodies on the street, families waiting in hospital halls, children learning to limp.

      A cyclist named Amanda Servedio was killed at 37th Street and 34th Avenue. She was riding home from a cycling event when a man fleeing police in a pickup truck ran her down. She flew thirty feet. She did not scream. “She went airborne. She flew like 30 feet. It was a lot of force,” said a witness. The police stopped their chase to help her. She died at Elmhurst Hospital. Her father said, “It was probably not the place to be doing a high-speed chase, in the residential neighborhood, and we were very upset that that’s what actually happened” said Frank Servedio.

      A seven-year-old girl was hit outside her school on 35th Avenue. An unlicensed driver reversed into a box truck, spun onto the sidewalk, and broke the girl’s femur. Two others were hurt. The driver was charged with reckless endangerment and driving without a license reported the New York Post.

      Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and cars did most of the damage. In three years, cars and SUVs caused 306 pedestrian injuries, including one serious injury. Trucks and buses hurt 27. Motorcycles and mopeds injured 23. Bikes, eight. The street is a gauntlet. The sidewalk is not safe. The crosswalk is not safe. The bike lane is not safe.

      Leadership: Words, Votes, and Silence

      Assembly Member Zohran Mamdani has spoken for safer streets. He has called for protected bike lanes, bus lanes, and moving traffic enforcement out of the NYPD see Streetsblog NYC. He backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst repeat offenders to slow down. He opposed the removal of the Bedford Avenue protected bike lane, calling it reckless see Streetsblog NYC. But the deaths keep coming. The city moves slow. The blood dries fast.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Call your leaders. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand the Stop Super Speeders Act. Demand protected bike lanes and sidewalks that do not double as parking lots. Every day you wait, another family waits for a call that will break them. Take action now.

      Mamdani Backs Safety Boosting Free MTA Buses Plan
      ▸

      State lawmakers Mamdani and Gianaris unveiled a four-year plan to fund free MTA buses. The proposal boosts bus service, freezes fares, and expands camera enforcement. They call it urgent. The plan aims to make transit faster, cheaper, and safer for all riders.

      On December 14, 2022, Assembly Member Zohran Mamdani and State Senator Michael Gianaris introduced the Formula Three Act, a legislative proposal to overhaul MTA funding and fare policy. The plan, part of the larger Fix The MTA Package, would phase in free local and Select Bus Service, increase bus frequency by 20%, freeze fares, and expand permanent bus lane camera enforcement. Funding would rise from $200 million in 2023 to $638 million in 2026, with $488 million yearly for more buses and $300 million annually for frequent subways. Mamdani called the proposal 'urgent and necessary,' stressing that 'seniors, working class New Yorkers, people who use the bus the most are able to do so without fear of being priced out.' Gianaris said, 'incrementalism...is not working.' The lawmakers urge bold action to ensure public transit is accessible, affordable, and excellent.
