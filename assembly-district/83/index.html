      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 83?

      Heastie’s Silence, Bronx Blood: Who Will Stop the Next Death?

      Written June 18, 2025 (Revised June 20, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 83: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Toll in the Bronx: Lives Lost, Streets Unforgiving

      In Assembly District 83, the numbers do not lie. Nine people killed. Twenty-three left with serious injuries. In just the last twelve months, four deaths and seven more lives changed forever. The dead are not numbers. They are elders crossing White Plains Road, a 76-year-old woman struck by an SUV. They are a 60-year-old man crushed on Givan Avenue, a 64-year-old killed at an intersection, a 44-year-old thrown from his moped. The street does not care about age or reason. The street only takes.

      The Vehicles That Kill and Maim

      SUVs, sedans, trucks, mopeds. In AD 83, SUVs and cars are the main weapons: five deaths, seventy moderate injuries, five serious injuries. Trucks and buses add four more serious or moderate injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds, two more. Even bikes and buses are not blameless. The pattern is clear. The bigger the vehicle, the greater the harm.

      What Has Carl Heastie Done?

      Assembly Speaker Carl Heastie holds the gavel. He has the power to move bills, or to bury them. In 2023, he let Sammy’s Law—a bill to let New York City set its own speed limits—die without a vote. Amy Cohen, whose son was killed by a driver, called it “Albany backroom politics at its worst”. Heastie told her, “I am only one vote”. The bill had broad support. It never reached the floor.

      Heastie did back the expansion of red light cameras, pushing them from 150 to 600 intersections. Assemblymember Dinowitz said, “People shouldn’t run red lights… when they do that they endanger other people’s lives, and people have died,” and Heastie controlled the vote. The cameras passed. But the speed limit bill—the one that could have saved the most lives—stalled.

      What Next? The Fight Is Not Over

      The dead cannot call for change. The living must. Call Carl Heastie. Demand he move every bill that puts people before cars. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand more cameras, more enforcement, more street redesigns. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Take action now.
