      Three Dead Kids, No Brake: Who Will Stop the Killing on Ocean Parkway?

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      AD 48: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Silence

      A mother and her two daughters stepped into the crosswalk on Ocean Parkway. An Audi came fast—68 miles an hour, prosecutors say. It ran the red. It hit another car. Then it hit the family. The mother and her girls, ages 8 and 5, died. The boy, 4, is still in a coma. The driver, Miriam Yarimi, had a suspended license and over 20 speed camera tickets. She never braked. Brooklyn’s District Attorney called it “one of the worst collisions I’ve ever seen on a New York City street”.

      In the last twelve months, 4 people died and 430 were injured in traffic crashes in AD 48. Five were left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. The dead are mostly older. The injured are often young. Children, parents, elders—none are spared.

      The Record of Leadership

      Assembly Member Simcha Eichenstein has not led on street safety. He voted no on a bill to bring speed cameras to school zones in Schenectady—cameras proven to catch drivers who speed where children cross. He opposed it. He did sponsor a bill to regulate e-bikes and mopeds, but that bill targets the most vulnerable, not the drivers of SUVs and trucks who cause most deaths. The cars keep coming. The law keeps missing them.

      Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and trucks killed or hurt more pedestrians here than any other vehicle. In three years, SUVs caused 7 deaths and 55 injuries. Trucks killed at least one. Bikes and mopeds together caused far fewer injuries, and no deaths. The numbers are clear. The danger is heavy, fast, and unchecked.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy.

      Call Assembly Member Eichenstein. Call your council member. Demand lower speed limits, more speed cameras, and laws that stop reckless drivers before they kill. Do not wait for another child’s name in the news. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws and oversees state policy, including traffic safety. Source
      ▸ Where does AD 48 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn and city council district District 44. Source
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 48?
      It includes the Borough Park and Mapleton-Midwood (West) neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 40 and District 44, and State Senate District SD 22. Source
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 48?
      Cars and Trucks: SUVs and trucks caused the most deaths and injuries to pedestrians. Motorcycles and Mopeds: Far fewer injuries, no deaths. Bikes: Caused a handful of injuries, no deaths. Source
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed camera programs, and pass laws that keep reckless drivers off the road.
