      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Bruckner at Leggett: a mother in the crosswalk, a driver who kept going

      AD 84: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 3, 2025

      Just after midnight on Aug 26, 2025, at Bruckner Boulevard and Leggett Avenue, a driver hit 32-year-old Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez as she crossed in the crosswalk, then fled, police said (ABC7; NY Daily News).

      She was one of 25 people killed on the roads of Assembly District 84 since Jan 1, 2022; 3,400 more were hurt in that same span, according to our analysis of NYC Open Data.

      YTD, deaths in this district stand at 7, up from 3 at this point last year (CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data). The crisis does not pause.

      Where the street takes people

      The danger pools on the Bruckner. “Bruckner Blvd” shows the highest concentration of harm in the district; East 149 Street follows close behind (CrashCount small-area analysis from NYC Open Data).

      At E 149 St and Brook Ave, a bus making a left turn killed a 57‑year‑old cyclist late on Feb 25, 2025 (NYC Open Data crash 4795059). At E 164 St and the Grand Concourse, a driver going at unsafe speed ran a signal and killed a 75‑year‑old woman in the crosswalk on Apr 12, 2024; the car was unlicensed in New York, per the police report (NYC Open Data crash 4716652).

      Night hides nothing. Deaths stack up around midnight and into the small hours, again and again (district hourly distribution from NYC Open Data).

      The machines that do the harm

      Trucks and buses are part of the toll. In pedestrian strikes here since 2022, heavy vehicles account for at least 2 deaths and 8 serious injuries (CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data). The Bruckner funnels them. So do neighborhood truck routes.

      When drivers turn, people die. Left-turning vehicles killed a cyclist at E 149 St. A left-turning sedan and a missed signal killed a woman at the Grand Concourse (NYC Open Data crash 4795059; crash 4716652).

      On July 3, at Courtlandt and E 149, a Ford Mustang jumped the curb and hit six people before the driver ran. “People were yelling… crying,” a witness said (New York Post).

      What the record says leaders did — and didn’t

      Your Assembly Member in AD 84 is Amanda Septimo. She co‑sponsored A 2299, which would require speed‑limiting tech after 11 DMV points in 24 months or 6 camera tickets in a year (Open States: A 2299). She also co‑sponsored A 1077 to apply complete‑street design principles statewide (Open States: A 1077).

      On school‑zone safety, the Legislature moved a fix‑and‑extend bill, S 8344. Septimo was marked “excused” on the June 17 vote (Open States: S 8344). Days earlier she voted “yes” on another school‑speed‑camera measure, S 5677 (CrashCount timeline; source Open States). The record is mixed; the deaths are not.

      Fix the turns. Guard the crossings. Slow the corridor.

      At Bruckner and Leggett, and along E 149 St, the fixes are plain: daylight corners, give leading pedestrian intervals, harden left turns, and calm speeds with concrete. Add truck‑safe designs and targeted enforcement where the data shows repeat harm (CrashCount small-area analysis from NYC Open Data).

      Citywide, two steps would save lives here: drop the default speed limit and force repeat speeders to obey it. The first is within reach; the second sits in Albany as A 2299. Both need public pressure. Start here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What area does this cover?
      Assembly District 84 in the Bronx, including Mott Haven–Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, Concourse–Concourse Village, and Yankee Stadium–Macombs Dam Park.
      ▸ What patterns did you find?
      Since Jan 1, 2022, this district has seen 25 traffic deaths and 3,400 injuries. Harm clusters on Bruckner Boulevard and East 149 Street, with deaths often occurring late at night. Heavy vehicles are involved in a share of the most severe pedestrian crashes. Sources: CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Who represents this area, and what have they done?
      Assembly Member Amanda Septimo co‑sponsored A 2299 (speed limiters for repeat violators) and A 1077 (complete streets). She was marked “excused” on S 8344’s June 17, 2025 vote; she also voted yes on S 5677, a school speed‑camera measure. Sources: Open States entries in our timeline.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets — Crashes (h9gi-nx95), Persons (f55k-p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k-52h4) — filtered to crashes within Assembly District 84 from 2022‑01‑01 to 2025‑09‑03. We geocoded crash points to the district and tallied deaths, injuries, serious injuries, vehicle types, locations, and hourly patterns. Extracted Sep 3, 2025. See the datasets here.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
