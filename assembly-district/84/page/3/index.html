      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 84?

      Outrunning Red Lights, Outrunning Justice: Bronx Blood on Their Hands

      Written July 1, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      AD 84: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 1, 2025

      The Toll: Blood on the Sidewalk

      In Assembly District 84, the street does not forgive. Five people have died here since January. Four hundred eight have been hurt. Six were left with injuries so grave they may never walk right again. These are not numbers. They are fathers, daughters, neighbors.

      Just days ago, a driver ran a red light at Hunts Point Avenue and Bruckner Boulevard. He hit three men on the sidewalk. One is fighting for his life. Police said the driver “slammed on the gas and sped off east on Hunts Point Ave., blowing through a red light” before the crash NY Daily News. He ran. The men he hit could not.

      A week before, a 15-year-old boy rode his scooter in Highbridge. A motorcyclist “blew through a red light, slammed into him and sped away,” police said NY Daily News. The boy is in the hospital. The driver is gone.

      Patterns: The Same Old Story

      Red lights mean nothing if no one enforces them. Unlicensed drivers, hit-and-runs, speeders—these are not rare. They are the rule. In the last year, SUVs and cars caused the most harm: five deaths, 278 minor injuries, 109 moderate, 11 serious. Trucks and buses killed two and left seven with serious wounds. Motorcycles and mopeds, one dead, 17 more hurt. Even bikes are not blameless—one serious injury, eight others hurt. The street is a war zone, and the most vulnerable pay the price.

      Leadership: What Has Been Done

      Assembly Member Amanda Septimo has signed on to bills that matter. She co-sponsored a law to force repeat speeders to install speed limiters A2299. She backed “complete streets” design for all users A1077. She voted yes on school speed cameras A7652. These are steps. But the blood keeps flowing. Every day without action is another day someone does not come home.

      What Now: No More Waiting

      Call Assembly Member Septimo. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against repeat offenders. Join Families for Safe Streets and Transportation Alternatives. Stand with the families who have lost everything. The street will not change itself. Only you can force their hand.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents the people of New York in state government.
      ▸ Where does AD 84 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and state senate district SD 29.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 84?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, Concourse-Concourse Village, Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Bronx CB1, Bronx CB2, and Bronx CB4 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, and District 17, and State Senate District SD 29.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 84?
      Cars and Trucks: 5 deaths, 278 minor injuries, 109 moderate injuries, 11 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 1 death, 17 minor injuries, 6 moderate injuries, no serious injuries. Bikes: No deaths, 8 minor injuries, 7 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Patterns of red-light running, speeding, and repeat dangerous driving show that better laws and enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, support speed cameras, require speed limiters for repeat offenders, and redesign streets to protect people walking and cycling.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.

      Septimo Frames Transit Funding as Economic and Racial Justice
      ▸

      Lawmakers and advocates rallied in Manhattan. They demanded more money for the MTA. They want six-minute bus and subway service. They warned against service cuts and fare hikes. They called for gas tax revenue to fund transit. Riders need safe, frequent service.
