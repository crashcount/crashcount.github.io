      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 84?

      Blood on the Boulevard: Bronx Streets Are Killing Fields—Who Will Stop the Slaughter?

      Written June 30, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      AD 84: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 30, 2025

      The Toll This Year: Flesh and Blood on Bronx Asphalt

      In Assembly District 84, the numbers do not lie. Five people killed and over 400 injured on these streets since January. In the past twelve months, six dead, fourteen left with life-altering wounds. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors. A 33-year-old man now fights for his life after a car ran a red light and plowed onto the sidewalk at Bruckner and Hunts Point. Two others were thrown down beside him. Police said the driver “allegedly ran a red light” and then “got out of his vehicle and ran away, but he was later arrested” (CBS New York).

      A week before, a 15-year-old boy on a scooter was hit by a motorcyclist who “blew through a red light, slammed into him and sped away”. The boy is in critical condition. The driver did not stop. The street did not forgive (NY Daily News).

      Patterns of Violence: No End in Sight

      The violence is not new. It is relentless. In the last year, SUVs and cars did the most harm—five deaths, 398 injuries. Trucks and buses killed two, hurt thirty. Motorcycles and mopeds took one life, left seventeen more battered. Bikes injured eight. The old, the young, the ones just crossing the street—none are spared.

      Leadership: What Has Been Done, What Has Not

      Assembly Member Amanda Septimo has signed on to bills that could help. She co-sponsored a law to force repeat speeders to install speed limiters on their cars (A 2299). She backed “complete streets” design, which means safer roads for everyone (A 1077). She voted yes on more speed cameras near schools (A 7652). These are steps. But the blood on the street says it is not enough. The city can lower speed limits now. The state can pass the Stop Super Speeders Act. The time for waiting is over.

      Call to Action: Make Them Hear You

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call Assembly Member Septimo. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action on repeat speeders. Join Families for Safe Streets. Join Transportation Alternatives. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents the people of New York State.
      ▸ Where does AD 84 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and state senate district SD 29.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 84?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, Concourse-Concourse Village, Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Bronx CB1, Bronx CB2, and Bronx CB4 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, and District 17, and State Senate District SD 29.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 84?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 5 deaths and 398 injuries. Trucks and buses caused 2 deaths and 30 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 1 death and 17 injuries. Bikes caused 0 deaths and 8 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and holding repeat dangerous drivers accountable can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, support bills that target repeat dangerous drivers, fund safer street designs, and ensure enforcement focuses on drivers, not pedestrians or cyclists.
