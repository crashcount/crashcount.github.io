      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 84?

      Bruckner and Leggett: a crosswalk, a hit‑and‑run, a family shattered

      Written September 1, 2025 (Revised September 1, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 84: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 1, 2025

      Just after midnight at Bruckner Boulevard and Leggett Avenue, a driver hit 32‑year‑old Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez in the crosswalk and fled. She died at Lincoln Hospital. Police say she was crossing with the light when the northbound vehicle struck her; coverage named her days later. The Daily News reported her death.

      She is one of 24 people killed on these streets since Jan 1, 2022, in Assembly District 84. The city’s crash ledger shows 5,537 recorded crashes here over that span. NYC Open Data.

      Bruckner shows up again and again. So does East 149th.

      —

      Where the blood pools

      Bruckner Boulevard is a repeat scene. The district’s top danger points include “BRUCKNER BLVD” and “BRUCKNER BOULEVARD,” along with EAST 149 STREET. Together they account for hundreds of injuries and multiple deaths since 2022. NYC Open Data.

      The hours matter. Fatal crashes stack up late. At 1 AM alone, the district logged three deaths in this period. Night brings fewer people, more speed, and the same crosswalks. NYC Open Data.

      Speed and failure to yield keep showing up on the tapes. In April 2024, a 75‑year‑old woman crossing with the signal at Grand Concourse and East 164th was killed by an unlicensed driver moving at unsafe speed who blew a control. CrashID 4716652. On East 149th, a taxi going straight hit and killed a 61‑year‑old crossing at night. CrashID 4735638.

      —

      Sidewalks are not safe

      On July 3, a Ford Mustang failed a left turn at East 149th and Courtlandt, jumped the curb, and hit six people. The driver ran. One witness said, “People were yelling, were in pain… yelling, crying.” New York Post reporting; Daily News confirmed injuries.

      Trucks and buses add weight to the harm here. District logs show pedestrians struck by SUVs and sedans most often, but heavy vehicles appear in a deadly share of cases. NYC Open Data.

      The pattern is plain: left turns, night speeds, wide arterials, and crosswalks that don’t forgive error.

      —

      Fix the corners, slow the turns

      Start where the bodies fall:

      • Daylight the corners and harden left turns on Bruckner Boulevard and East 149th Street. Cut the turning radius; force drivers to slow before they sweep the crosswalk. NYC Open Data.
      • Add leading pedestrian intervals at Bruckner & Leggett and Courtlandt & E 149th to give people head‑start time in the crosswalk. NYC Open Data.
      • Tighten truck routes and step up targeted enforcement during the deadliest late‑night hours. NYC Open Data.

      These are standard tools. They meet the facts on the ground.

      —

      Albany’s record, and what is still on the table

      Your Assembly Member for this district is Amanda Septimo. She co‑sponsors A 2299, which would require intelligent speed assistance for repeat offenders, and A 1077 to embed complete streets in road work. Open States. Open States.

      But the record also shows missed chances. Septimo missed votes tied to school‑zone safety measures, including S 8344 and S 7678. Open States.

      One more fact on deterrence: New York renewed 24‑hour school speed cameras through 2030 in June. That buys time. It is not a shield for a wide boulevard at 1 AM. Open States.

      —

      The next step is known

      Slow every street and stop the worst repeat speeders. Pass and implement the speed‑limiter bill here. Build the cheap, physical fixes at Bruckner, East 149th, and the other corners that keep bleeding. Then keep going.

      A mother died in a crosswalk at Bruckner and Leggett. We do not need another vigil to know what to do. Start here.

      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?

We filtered records to the period 2022‑01‑01 through 2025‑09‑01 and to crashes whose mapped coordinates fall within New York State Assembly District 84. Figures include all modes and injury severities unless otherwise stated. Data was accessed Sept 1, 2025. You can explore the base datasets here.
