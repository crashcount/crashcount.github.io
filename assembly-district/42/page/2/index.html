      Flatbush Bleeds While Leaders Stall

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 42: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in Flatbush and Beyond

      A mother and her son walk down Bedford Avenue. A school bus jumps the curb. The boy’s arm breaks. His mother clutches her hip in pain. They survive, but the street does not forgive or forget. The bus jumped the curb and struck the pedestrians before smashing into a fence. The crash is called an accident. The pain is not.

      In the last twelve months, 567 people were injured on the streets of Assembly District 42. Seven were seriously hurt. Two people died last year. The dead do not speak. The numbers do. Children, elders, workers. No one is spared. NYC Open Data

      The System Fails at the Corners

      Most of the blood is spilled at intersections. Nearly half of city traffic deaths happen there. The city knows this. The DOT knows this. They promise change. “Using barriers to clear space at the intersection will help ensure pedestrians, cyclists and turning vehicles can see each other and enhance safety,” said DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez. But the corners stay crowded. The cars keep coming.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Assembly Member Rodneyse Bichotte Hermelyn has signed on to bills for safer streets. She co-sponsored measures for complete streets and bike lane enforcement. She voted yes on school speed cameras. She backed Sammy’s Law, giving the city power to lower speed limits. But when the fight came to Bedford Avenue, she stood with the party machine against a protected bike lane. The lane would have kept cyclists and children out of the path of cars. The fight is not over. The street is not safe.

      What Comes Next

      The crisis is not fate. It is policy. The street can be changed. The law can be changed. The will must be found. Call Assembly Member Bichotte Hermelyn. Call the mayor. Call the DOT. Demand barriers at every deadly corner. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand that every child, every elder, every cyclist gets home alive.

      Do not wait for another mother to scream in the street.

      Take action now.

      Bichotte Hermelyn Backs Safety Boosting Apolline’s Garden Plaza
      ▸

      DOT will turn a Brooklyn street into Apolline's Garden, a car-free plaza. The move comes after a reckless driver killed a baby and injured her mother. Officials and neighbors pushed for this change. Cars lose space. Pedestrians gain safety.

      On February 22, 2022, DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez confirmed the conversion of Gates Avenue’s stub end into 'Apolline's Garden,' a pedestrian plaza. The announcement followed a virtual Vanderbilt Avenue Block Association meeting. The plaza honors Apolline Mong-Guillemin, a 3-month-old killed by a wrong-way driver on September 11, 2021. Rodriguez stated, 'Traffic violence is a solvable crisis and I'm committed to carrying out Mayor's Adams's safety vision [by] expanding pedestrian plazas and open streets.' Assembly Member Rodneyse Bichotte called for unity, saying, 'There is no doubt that the creation of Apolline's Garden is the appropriate answer to honor the short life of this 3-month-old baby.' The plan aligns with the NYC 25X25 initiative to reclaim street space for pedestrians and cyclists. Community leaders and officials support the plaza as a step to prevent future tragedy.
