      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 59?

      Blood on the Asphalt: Hold Power to Account Before Another Child Dies

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 59: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Toll: Death and Injury on Local Streets

      A boy, 14, crushed at an intersection. An old man, 66, struck crossing Rockaway Parkway. A motorcyclist, 30, dead after colliding with a fire truck at Flatbush and Avenue U. In the last twelve months, five people died and thirteen suffered serious injuries in Assembly District 59. Nine hundred twenty-one were hurt. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie.

      The dead are not numbers. They are children, elders, neighbors. A girl’s body in the crosswalk. A man’s blood on the curb. The city moves on. The street stays hungry.

      Who Pays, Who Acts

      The killers are cars and trucks. SUVs took nine lives. Trucks and buses, one. Motorcycles and mopeds, none this year, but the scars run deep. Bikes, one moderate injury. The rest—flesh and bone against steel and speed.

      After a crash at Flatbush and Avenue U, the FDNY said only, “incident is under investigation.” No comfort. No change. After a cyclist’s throat was cut by a string on the Marine Parkway Bridge, a local leader called it “absolutely dangerous and unacceptable. We can’t do this no more because someone is going to get killed.”

      Leadership: Action or Excuse

      Assembly Member Jaime Williams co-sponsored bills for safer streets—A 1077 and A 1280—meant to protect all who use the road. But when it came time to vote for a bill to put speed cameras near schools, Williams voted no. The bill passed anyway. The cameras will catch speeding drivers where children walk. Williams called congestion pricing unfair, standing with those who say their commutes matter more than your life.

      The Next Step: Demand More

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every death is a choice made by those in power. Call Jaime Williams. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras in every school zone. Demand streets built for people, not just cars. Do not wait for another child’s body in the road.

      Take action now.
