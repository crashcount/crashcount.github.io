      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 25?

      Twelve Dead, Hundreds Hurt: Assembly 25’s Streets Run Red

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, clarified FAQ citation, deduped and sorted citations.)

      AD 25: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Blood on the Asphalt

      In Assembly District 25, the numbers do not lie. Twelve dead. Thirty-seven left with injuries so grave they may never walk the same. In just the last twelve months, three people died and seventeen suffered serious wounds. The youngest lost was 18. The oldest, 74. No one is spared.

      The carnage does not slow. On July 11, a city bus in Flushing jumped the curb. Eight people were hurt. The driver, just 25, told investigators he had a green light. Video showed he had fallen asleep. “I was all the way in the back and all of a sudden the bus hit the curb, I guess, jumped the curb, I went this way and that way and banged into the side of the bus,” said a passenger. The pole at the stop still stands. Two people almost did not.

      The Usual Weapons: Cars, SUVs, and Speed

      Cars and SUVs do most of the killing. In this district, sedans and SUVs together have taken at least two lives and left over 300 people with injuries. Trucks, buses, mopeds, and bikes add to the toll. Speed is the silent accomplice. In December, a 72-year-old woman died when a sedan tore through Peck Avenue at unsafe speed. Another crash on the expressway crushed a 56-year-old man. The numbers pile up. The bodies do too.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      Assembly Member Nily Rozic has voted for and co-sponsored bills to extend school speed zones, require safer street designs, and clamp down on repeat speeders with speed-limiting tech. She voted yes to extend school speed cameras, a move that boosts pedestrian safety. She co-sponsors the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst repeat offenders to slow down. But some votes have missed the mark. Rozic missed a committee vote that allowed an unsafe bus regulation exemption to advance. She voted to exempt some employees from bus lane rules. Each delay, each loophole, leaves someone else at risk.

      What You Must Do

      This is not fate. It is policy. Call Assembly Member Rozic. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand passage of the Stop Super Speeders Act. Join Families for Safe Streets and Transportation Alternatives. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, oversees the budget, and represents districts like AD 25.
      ▸ Where does AD 25 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 20 and state senate district SD 16.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 25?
      It includes the East Flushing, Queensboro Hill, Kissena Park, Pomonok-Electchester-Hillcrest, Fresh Meadows-Utopia, Cunningham Park, Auburndale, Bayside, and Queens CB8 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 19, District 20, District 23, and District 24, and State Senate District SD 16.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 25?
      Cars and Trucks: Sedans and SUVs caused the most harm, with at least two deaths and over 300 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: Involved in a handful of crashes, with several injuries. Bikes: Involved in a few incidents, with minor injuries. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes can be prevented with safer street design, lower speed limits, and better enforcement. They are not random—they are the result of choices and policies.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, support speed cameras, and close loopholes that let repeat offenders keep driving.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.

      A 2299
      Rozic co-sponsors bill to boost street safety with speed limiters.
      ▸

      Assembly bill A 2299 targets reckless drivers. Eleven points or six camera tickets in a year triggers forced speed control tech. Lawmakers move to curb repeat speeders. Streets demand fewer deadly risks.

      Assembly bill A 2299, now in sponsorship, sits with the New York State Assembly. Introduced January 16, 2025, the bill 'requires the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices if a driver accumulates eleven or more points on their driving record during a 24 month period, or receives 6 speed camera or red light camera tickets during a twelve month period.' Primary sponsor Emily Gallagher leads a bloc of co-sponsors, including Rebecca Seawright, Andrew Hevesi, and others. The measure aims to clamp down on repeat speeders with mandatory speed-limiting tech. No safety analyst note was provided.
