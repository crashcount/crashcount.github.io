      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 27?

      AD27: Two men die at a food truck. Buses jump curbs. The clock keeps running.

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 27: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Speed is the thread. Metal is the method. People are the cost.

      Two men at a food truck, then silence

      A speeding car hit two pedestrians ordering from a food truck in Queens. Both men died. The driver died too. Witnesses said the car “slammed into the men.” That was Aug. 13, 2025. The crash sits like a stain on the calendar. CBS New York.

      Sidewalk isn’t safe either

      On July 11, an MTA Q20 bus mounted the curb at 57th Road and Main Street, smashed a light pole and a ticket machine, and injured seven to eight people. One rider said, “That would be scary.” Another: “Accidents do happen.” CBS New York and CBS New York.

      Highways cut through. So do deaths.

      The district’s worst pain clusters on the expressways: four deaths tied to the Long Island Expressway, two on the Whitestone Expressway, and more on the Van Wyck and Grand Central. One case on the LIE listed “Unsafe Speed” and “Driver Inattention/Distraction” for a 73‑year‑old driver who died before dawn on April 14, 2024. NYC Open Data crash 4716969.

      Nights are bloody

      From midnight to 2 a.m., this small map lights up: 3 deaths at midnight, 2 at 1 a.m., 2 at 2 a.m. Injuries stack highest around 8 p.m. to 10 p.m. and again near 8 a.m. The late hours keep taking people and giving back sirens. NYC Open Data.

      Who gets hit, and by what

      In this district period, pedestrians saw 5 deaths and 296 injuries; cyclists, 1 death and 84 injuries. Car occupants died most, but people outside the car have no shell. Sedans and SUVs are the main striking vehicles in pedestrian cases. Trucks and buses add weight. NYC Open Data.

      A year later, handcuffs

      Police arrested a 63‑year‑old man a year after a Whitestone Expressway hit‑and‑run that killed 43‑year‑old David Berney. The driver had no license, left his Mercedes, and ran. A grand jury indicted him for leaving the scene and driving without a license. “Relief,” said Berney’s sister after twelve months. NY Daily News.

      Hot spots with names, not mysteries

      • Long Island Expressway: four deaths, 163 injuries.
      • Whitestone Expressway: two deaths, 250 injuries.
      • Van Wyck: two deaths, 427 injuries.
      • Grand Central Parkway: one death, 333 injuries.
      • Roosevelt Avenue: 13 injuries, two serious.
        All in the district’s footprint. All in the data. NYC Open Data.

      Why here, why now

      The ledger blames “other” most often, but the named failures still show: failure to yield, distraction, unsafe speed. Nights magnify it. Repeat places do too. NYC Open Data.

      Fix the corners. Slow the steel.

      Concrete answers exist:

      • Daylight crosswalks along Roosevelt and Main. Harden the turns. Give walkers a head start.
      • Target the late‑night hours at the LIE, Whitestone, Van Wyck, and Grand Central. Keep the cameras hot where the injuries spike.
      • Protect bike space on busy corridors where the bodies keep piling up. Corrals, not chaos.

      Citywide moves that would bite here

      • Lower the default to 20 mph. Albany passed the tool. NYC can use it. Do it where people live and cross. Take action.
      • Shut down the repeat speeders with intelligent speed assistance for drivers who rack up violations. The state bill would force the worst to slow down. Take action.

      The list of names is longer than this page. The streets are the same tomorrow unless someone moves today.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where are the worst crash locations in AD27?
      The Long Island Expressway, Whitestone Expressway, Van Wyck Expressway, Grand Central Parkway, and Roosevelt Avenue show the heaviest toll in district data.
      ▸ When are crashes most deadly here?
      Late night. From midnight to 2 a.m., deaths spike, with additional injury peaks around 8 p.m.–10 p.m. and near 8 a.m., per NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What is causing the harm?
      The dataset flags broad “other,” plus failure to yield, distraction, and unsafe speed. Sedans and SUVs are the main striking vehicles in pedestrian cases.
