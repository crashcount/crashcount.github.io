      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Van Wyck death, and the long toll in Assembly District 27

      AD 27: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 21, 2025

      A man driving a motorcycle died on the Van Wyck Expressway on Sep 8, 2025. He was 40. He was ejected. He did not make it. NYC Open Data

      This Week

      • Sep 8: A 40-year-old man driving a motorcycle was killed on the Van Wyck Expressway. NYC Open Data
      • Aug 23: A driver in a Ford sedan hit a 38-year-old man in the road and killed him; police recorded driver inattention. NYC Open Data

      The number that won’t move

      He is one of 22 people killed on Assembly District 27 streets since Jan 1, 2022, with 3 deaths so far this year and 5 at this point last year. NYC Open Data

      Since 2022, people killed here include 10 people walking, 1 person on a bike, and 11 vehicle occupants. Those are lives, not categories. NYC Open Data

      Where the blood pools

      The expressways cut through and they take. The Whitestone Expressway, the Van Wyck, and the Long Island Expressway account for the heaviest hurt, with recorded deaths at each. NYC Open Data

      Night does damage. So do the hours when families head home. The midnight hour shows 4 deaths, 1 AM has 2, 2 AM has 2. At 4 PM there are 3, and at 10 PM there are 2. The pattern is routine; the grief is not. NYC Open Data

      Causes we can name

      Police recorded “Unsafe Speed” in two deadly crashes: a 68-year-old woman crossing near Main Street and Reeves Avenue on Feb 15, 2024, and a 43-year-old man in the roadway on the Whitestone Expressway on Feb 10, 2024. Both died. NYC Open Data – Feb 15, 2024 · NYC Open Data – Feb 10, 2024

      In the past month, police recorded driver inattention in the Aug 23 killing of a 38-year-old man. He died in the street. NYC Open Data – Aug 23, 2025

      The map points to fixes

      Intersections and ramps near the Whitestone Expressway and Van Wyck need hard edges. Daylighting corners, hardened centerlines, and longer walk starts can keep turning drivers in check. Targeted speed enforcement at repeat hotspots after dark can catch the patterns the data shows. These are standard tools. They prevent crashes when used.

      Who will move first?

      Assembly District 27 is represented by Assembly Member Sam Berger, Council Member Sandra Ung, and State Senator Toby Stavisky. Their records here matter. CrashCount Civics

      On Jun 17, 2025, a bill to extend and correct NYC school speed-zone laws advanced in Albany; Assembly Member Berger was marked “excused” on that vote. Open States: S 8344

      In 2024, as Queens debated safe mobility, Berger’s name appeared with officials opposing a new e-scooter pilot, despite city data showing high ridership and no reported serious injuries at the time. Streetsblog NYC

      Two citywide steps would matter on these corridors and side streets alike: lowering more speeds under the city’s new authority and reining in repeat speeders with mandatory limiters. The path is laid out. See the details and how to press your officials here.

      Act: One call. One email. It is not enough. It is what we have. Take action.

      ▸ Where is this?
      Assembly District 27 in Queens includes parts of College Point, Whitestone–Beechhurst, Kew Gardens Hills, Pomonok–Electchester–Hillcrest, Flushing Meadows–Corona Park, and more. It sits in Council District 20 and overlaps State Senate District 11.
      ▸ How many people have been killed here since 2022?
      According to NYC Open Data compiled for AD 27, 22 people have been killed on district streets since Jan 1, 2022. That includes 10 people walking, 1 person on a bike, and 11 vehicle occupants.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi‑nx95, Persons f55k‑p6yu, Vehicles bm4k‑52h4). We filtered records between 2022‑01‑01 and 2025‑09‑21 to crashes geofenced to Assembly District 27 and tallied fatalities, injuries, times, and street locations. Data was accessed on Sep 21, 2025. You can view the source datasets here.

      Assembly Member Sam Berger, Council Member Sandra Ung, and State Senator Toby Stavisky represent this area in Albany and City Hall, respectively.
