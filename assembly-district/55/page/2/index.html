      Hit-and-Run, Hit-and-Repeat: End the Bloodshed on Our Streets

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, ensured proper citation formatting, and deduped citations.)

      AD 55: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in AD 55: Lives Lost, Families Shattered

      A man steps out for a cookout. He never makes it. Larry Maxwell, 72, is struck and killed on Sutter Avenue by a driver fleeing another crash. The driver keeps going. Maxwell’s son, Larnce Vargas, stands at the scene, police and EMS between him and his father. “There are so many cameras. So why is he still at large, if there’s so many cameras? Y’all can find any and everybody else, but y’all can’t find him. That don’t make sense.” asked Maxwell’s son.

      In the last twelve months, three people have died in crashes in AD 55. Seven more suffered serious injuries. There have been 723 injuries and 1,254 crashes. The numbers do not slow. The pain does not fade.

      The Pattern: Speed, Recklessness, and No Escape

      Imani Vance, 26, rides in a car with an unlicensed driver. He runs a stop sign. He hits a school bus. He flees, leaving her to die in the front seat. Brooklyn DA Eric Gonzalez calls it what it is: “The tragic loss of Imani Vance was allegedly caused by the defendant’s reckless decision to ignore a stop sign and drive at excessive speeds. His actions showed a shocking disregard for human life and safety, made worse by his attempt to flee the scene instead of helping the victim.” said DA Gonzalez.

      The streets do not forgive. In AD 55, the most vulnerable—pedestrians, children, cyclists—pay the price. Cars and SUVs cause most injuries. Trucks, bikes, mopeds, and buses add to the toll. The violence is relentless.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Miles to Go

      Assembly Member Latrice Walker has co-sponsored bills to require safer street design for all users (A 1077, A 1280). She voted yes to expand speed cameras near schools (A 7652). These are steps. But the deaths keep coming. The bills sit. The cameras do not catch every fleeing driver. The work is not done.

      What Comes Next: Demand Action Now

      Every crash is preventable. Every death is a failure. Call Assembly Member Walker. Call your council member. Demand lower speed limits, more cameras, and streets built for people, not cars. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, including those that can make streets safer for everyone.
      ▸ Where does AD 55 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn and city council district District 41.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 55?
      It includes the Lincoln Terrace Park, Ocean Hill, Brownsville, and Brooklyn CB16 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 37, District 41, and District 42, and State Senate Districts SD 19, SD 20, and SD 25.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 55?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most pedestrian injuries (115 incidents), motorcycles and mopeds caused 4, and bikes caused 4. Trucks and buses caused 12 injuries combined.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and enforcement save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and support laws for lower speed limits, more speed cameras, and safer street design. They can push for enforcement that protects people walking and biking.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
