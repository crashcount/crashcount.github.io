      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 55?

      Nobody’s Safe Until Streets Are Safe

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 55: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      Blood on the Streets: Recent Deaths and Injuries

      The streets of Assembly District 55 do not forgive. In the past twelve months, four people died and 704 were injured in crashes here. Five suffered injuries so grave they may never walk the same. The dead do not speak. The living carry scars.

      Just last week, a driver struck a 36-year-old man at Broadway and East New York Avenue. The driver kept going. The man died at Brookdale Hospital. Police are still looking for the killer. As one witness asked, “There are so many cameras. So why is he still at large?”

      In May, Larry Maxwell, 72, was killed crossing Sutter Avenue. He was on his way to a family cookout. The driver who hit him had just fled another crash. Maxwell’s son said, “I was just on the phone with him. We just finished watching the Knicks game… He got struck right in front of the building.”

      Patterns of Harm: Who Pays the Price

      In AD 55, the most vulnerable pay first. Children and elders are not spared. In the last year, 68 people under 18 and 20 people over 75 were injured. Cars and SUVs cause most of the pain—408 pedestrian injuries and one death—but trucks, bikes, and mopeds also leave bodies broken. The numbers do not lie. The pattern does not change.

      Leadership: What Has Been Done, What Must Be Done

      Assembly Member Latrice Walker has co-sponsored bills to require safer street design for all users, supported additional legislation, and voted to expand school speed cameras. These are steps, not solutions. The dead do not wait for committee hearings. The injured do not heal with promises.

      Call to Action: Make Them Hear You

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Assembly Member Walker. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit, real street redesign, and enforcement that targets the drivers who do the most harm. Do not let another family plan a funeral because leaders moved too slow.

      Act now. The next name could be yours.
