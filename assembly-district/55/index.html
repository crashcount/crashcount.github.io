      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 55?

      Blood on Broadway—How Many Deaths Before Albany Wakes Up?

      Written July 11, 2025

      AD 55: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A man tried to cross Broadway at East New York Avenue. A car hit him. The driver kept going. He died at Brookdale Hospital. It happened on July 3rd. The police are still looking for the driver. There are no answers, only another family left behind. As the paper reported, “A hit-and-run driver struck and killed a 36-year-old man crossing a Brooklyn street.” The car did not stop.

      Two months earlier, Larry Maxwell, 72, was walking to a family cookout. A driver fleeing another crash hit him on Sutter Avenue. Maxwell died at the hospital. His son stood on the sidewalk and asked, “There are so many cameras. So why is he still at large?” The city had no answer.

      The Numbers Behind the Names

      In the past year, five people have died in Assembly District 55. Nine more suffered serious injuries. There were 1,363 crashes. Pedestrians and cyclists bleed most.

      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: two deaths, five serious injuries. Trucks and buses left two more with life-changing wounds. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes added to the toll. The numbers are not just numbers. They are people who will not come home.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      Assembly Member Latrice Walker has backed bills for safer streets. She co-sponsored measures for complete street design and school speed cameras. She voted yes to expand speed cameras near schools. But the deaths keep coming. The streets are not safe. The cameras did not save Larry Maxwell. The laws did not stop the car on Broadway.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Every day of delay is another risk. Call Assembly Member Walker. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people walking and biking. Do not wait for another name on the list. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents local districts like AD 55.
      ▸ Where does AD 55 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 41 and state senate district SD 19.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 55?
      It includes the Lincoln Terrace Park, Ocean Hill, Brownsville, and Brooklyn CB16 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 37, District 41, and District 42, and State Senate Districts SD 19, SD 20, and SD 25.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 55?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 5 serious injuries. Trucks and buses: 0 deaths, 2 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 0 deaths, 2 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 2 serious injuries. These figures are based on the most recent crash data for AD 55.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and better enforcement. They are not just accidents—they are the result of choices and policies.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, expand automated enforcement, and pass laws that protect people walking and biking.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
