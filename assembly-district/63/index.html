      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 63?

      Who Will Stop the Killing on Staten Island Streets?

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 63: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Toll: Death and Injury on Staten Island Streets

      A man crossing Targee Street is struck by an e-scooter. He dies in the hospital. The driver is thrown from the scooter and survives. No arrests. No answers. Police could not confirm the speed or direction.

      A worker stands by a loading dock at the Amazon warehouse on Gulf Avenue. A box truck backs up. The man is crushed. He had two daughters. He will not go home again. “He was 34 years old. He had two daughters. He was killed on the job.”

      In the last twelve months, 2 people died and 12 suffered serious injuries in Assembly District 63. There were 679 injuries in 1,197 crashes. Children, elders, workers—no one is spared.

      Who Pays the Price?

      Cars and trucks killed and maimed the most. In three years: 15 deaths, 21 serious injuries, 2,109 hurt. Pedestrians are struck by sedans, SUVs, trucks, buses. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse?

      Assembly Member Sam Pirozzolo had a choice. On June 7, 2024, he voted no on a bill to install speed cameras near schools. The bill aimed to catch drivers who speed where children walk. He opposed it. He voted no.

      When Staten Island’s transit was threatened, Pirozzolo signed a letter to protect the borough’s railcars. He spoke up for trains. He did not speak up for the dead on the street. He signed the letter.

      What Now? Demand More

      This is not fate. This is policy.

      Call Sam Pirozzolo. Call your council member. Demand speed cameras at every school. Demand lower speed limits. Demand streets where a man can cross without dying, where a worker can come home to his daughters.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. Act now.

      Take action today.
