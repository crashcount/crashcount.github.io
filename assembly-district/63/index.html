      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 63?

      Blood on Bay Street, Silence in Albany: Demand Action Now

      Written July 18, 2025 (Revised August 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, deduped and sorted citations, fixed vehicle stats citation in FAQ.)

      AD 63: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 18, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      Four people killed. Twelve left with life-changing wounds. In the last year alone, Assembly District 63 has seen 1,292 crashes, 762 injuries, and 12 serious injuries. The dead do not speak. Their names fade. The pain remains for those left behind.

      Just last month, a man died on Bay Street. A motorcycle hit the door of a Toyota making a K-turn. The rider, Jeremy Claudio, was thrown from his bike. He died at the hospital. Police are still investigating. There were no arrests. The street is quiet again, but the blood is not forgotten. A motorcyclist was killed after colliding with a driver awkwardly turning their car on Staten Island during the Fourth of July holiday weekend.

      In May, an e-scooter struck and killed a 58-year-old man crossing Targee Street. The driver was thrown from the scooter. The pedestrian died at Richmond University Medical Center. No arrests. No answers. Upon impact, the driver was violently ejected off the scooter while Gomez-Guallazaca sustained severe trauma.

      The Numbers Are Not Just Numbers

      Seventeen dead since 2022. Twenty-two seriously injured. Over two thousand hurt. Most were walking, biking, or just trying to get home. Cars and trucks did most of the killing—3 deaths and 247 injuries from sedans and SUVs, 6 deaths and 28 injuries from trucks and buses. Bikes and mopeds are not blameless, but the scale is not the same.

      Leadership: Votes Against Safety

      Assembly Member Sam Pirozzolo has voted against bills to protect children in school speed zones. He voted no on extending and improving speed camera laws. He voted yes to weaken bus rules, making streets more dangerous for those outside cars. He co-sponsored a bill to repeal congestion pricing, a move that would put more cars on the road and more lives at risk.

      He has not led on safety. He has not stood with the families who mourn. The record is public. The silence is louder than any speech.

      What Next: Demand Action

      The deaths will not stop until the laws change. Call Assembly Member Pirozzolo. Demand he support speed cameras, safer street designs, and real accountability for reckless drivers. Join with others. Refuse to let another name be added to the list.

      Contact your leaders. Demand action. Do not wait for another body in the road.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, votes on the state budget, and represents districts like AD 63.
      ▸ Where does AD 63 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Staten Island, city council district District 50 and state senate district SD 23.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 63?
      It includes the Tompkinsville-Stapleton-Clifton-Fox Hills, Rosebank-Shore Acres-Park Hill, West New Brighton-Silver Lake-Grymes Hill, Westerleigh-Castleton Corners, Mariner’S Harbor-Arlington-Graniteville, Todt Hill-Emerson Hill-Lighthouse Hill-Manor Heights, New Springville-Willowbrook-Bulls Head-Travis, Freshkills Park (North), Staten Island CB1, and Staten Island CB2 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 49, District 50, and District 51, and State Senate Districts SD 23 and SD 24.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 63?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 247 injuries (sedans/SUVs); 6 deaths, 28 injuries (trucks/buses). Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 0 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 5 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The numbers show a pattern. Crashes happen again and again in the same ways, on the same streets, and can be prevented with better laws and safer design.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can vote for speed cameras, safer street designs, and laws that hold reckless drivers accountable. They can refuse to weaken safety rules or delay proven solutions.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.

      Pirozzolo Criticizes Safety Boosting Speed Camera Program
      ▸

      Eleven city lawmakers voted no on speed cameras. Their votes keep streets exposed. Pedestrians and cyclists lose a shield. Reckless drivers win. The city’s most basic defense—rejected. The toll will be measured in blood, not words.

      On June 13 and June 17, 2025, the New York State Senate and Assembly voted on reauthorizing New York City's school zone speed camera program. The Senate passed the measure 38-21; three city senators—Stephen Chan, Andrew Lanza, Jessica Scarcella-Spanton—voted no. The Assembly passed it 110-31, with nine city lawmakers—Alec Brook-Krasny, Lester Chang, Simcha Eichenstein, Michael Novakhov, Stacey Pheffer Amato, Sam Pirozzolo, Michael Reilly, David Weprin, Kalman Yeger—opposing. The Streetsblog NYC article, 'Ye Shall Know Their Names! Meet the Dirty Dozen City Pols Who Voted Against Speed Camera Program,' quotes lawmakers dismissing speed cameras as revenue grabs or burdens. The safety analyst notes: 'The event text does not describe a specific policy or legislative action, so no direct safety impact on pedestrians and cyclists can be assessed.' Still, the votes signal disregard for proven tools that protect those outside cars.

      Senate passes S 8344. School speed zone rules in New York City get extended. Lawmakers make technical fixes. The bill keeps pressure on drivers near schools. Streets stay a little safer for kids.

      Bill S 8344, titled 'Extends provisions and makes technical corrections to school speed zones in NYC; repealer,' moved through the Senate and Assembly in June 2025. Sponsored by Senator Andrew Gounardes, the bill passed Senate votes on June 12 and June 13, and cleared the Assembly on June 17. The measure extends and corrects school speed zone laws in New York City, repealing outdated provisions. Gounardes led the push, with strong support from most Senate Democrats and a split Assembly.       City Crushes Illegal Mopeds In Staten Island
      ▸

      Bulldozers flattened over 200 illegal mopeds. Smoke rose. Officials watched. The city sent a message: no more unregistered, reckless machines on the streets. The crackdown aims to clear danger from crosswalks and sidewalks. The threat to walkers lingers.

      amNY reported on June 12, 2025, that New York City officials destroyed more than 200 illegal mopeds in Staten Island. Mayor Eric Adams and Police Commissioner Jessica Tisch oversaw the event, where a Sanitation Department bulldozer crushed the vehicles. Adams said, 'New Yorkers have strong feelings about illegal mopeds and scooters, especially when they are driving the wrong way down streets or sidewalks or in the dark without lights.' The city links these vehicles to traffic violations and crimes, including robberies and reckless riding. Since Adams took office, police have removed about 62,000 illegal vehicles, aiming to reduce hazards for pedestrians. NYPD data shows a 57% drop in moped-related crime in 2025. The crackdown highlights the city's push to address systemic dangers posed by unregulated vehicles.
