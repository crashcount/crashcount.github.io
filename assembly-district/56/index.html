      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Bed-Stuy’s Long Walk Through Traffic Violence

      AD 56: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 19, 2025

      A man went down on Atlantic Avenue at New York Avenue in the afternoon. Police logged severe injuries from a turning driver and distraction at the scene. Source.

      “The defendant floored the gas, instantly striking Tiffany, causing her body to become lodged beneath the vehicle.” New York Post.

      This Week

      • Gates and Ralph: police recorded unsafe speed before a motorcycle crash into a turning sedan; the rider was ejected and badly hurt. Source
      • Patchen and Macon: an SUV driver listed as unlicensed hit a 21-year-old on a bike; the cyclist suffered head injuries. CrashID 4832107

      The pattern is set in the street

      Since Jan 1, 2022, 15 people have been killed and 3,145 injured on Assembly District 56 streets. Police logged 5,638 crashes here in that time. NYC Open Data.

      Pedestrians died at intersections like Flushing Avenue at Broadway, where officers cited failure to yield by the driver, and at Marcy Avenue at Quincy Street, where an 82-year-old man crossing with the signal was killed by a left‑turning SUV, according to police reports. Open Data: 4671690 Open Data: 4749067.

      Deaths recur at hard hours: two around 4 AM, two around 6 PM, and two around 11 PM, with more scattered through the day. Analysis.

      Hot spots keep lighting up: Broadway, Atlantic Avenue, and Ralph Avenue sit near the top for injuries and deaths. Local stats.

      What police wrote down

      • Unsafe speed. Distraction. Disregarded signals. Failure to yield. These appear again and again in the police fields for this district. Recent records include unsafe speed before the Gates and Ralph crash and driver inattention in the Atlantic and New York case. Open Data.
      • At Patchen and Macon, officers marked the SUV driver as unlicensed after the driver hit a 21‑year‑old on a bike. CrashID 4832107.

      Bedford-Stuyvesant asked for protection. It stalled.

      DOT proposed a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue. Officials said protected lanes cut pedestrian injuries by 21% where they exist. Assembly Member Stefani Zinerman questioned the safety benefits at the time. Streetsblog.

      The district’s most wounded are people on foot and on bikes. Police data show 621 pedestrian injuries and 389 cyclist injuries here since 2022. NYC Open Data.

      What leaders did — and didn’t

      Assembly Member Stefani Zinerman voted yes in June to extend and fix New York City’s school speed‑zone laws. Open States: S 8344.

      Citywide, the tools to slow drivers exist. The city now has authority to lower speed limits and is rolling out more 20 MPH zones and automated enforcement, according to documented initiatives. Our action brief explains the steps and the law. /take_action/

      One more lever is on the table: the Stop Super Speeders Act would require drivers with repeated violations to use intelligent speed assistance to prevent further speeding. The bill needs sponsors and votes. /take_action/

      What would fix these corners

      • Daylight every intersection on Broadway, Atlantic, and Ralph. Harden left turns and add leading pedestrian intervals where walkers were killed while crossing with the signal. Local stats.
      • Build out protected bike lanes on Bedford and through the east‑west grid to cut turning conflicts. Streetsblog.
      • Target speed and failure‑to‑yield enforcement at the identified hot hours and hot spots. Open Data.

      The man on Atlantic is still a record in a database. Tiffany Cifuni’s family is still waiting in a courthouse hallway. The street keeps moving. New York Post

      Take one step that moves numbers: help press the city to lower speeds and the state to rein in repeat speeders. Act here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where is this happening?
      Assembly District 56 covers Bedford-Stuyvesant (West and East) and parts of Brooklyn Community Board 3. Key corridors in the crash data include Broadway, Atlantic Avenue, and Ralph Avenue.
      ▸ What do the numbers show for Assembly District 56 since 2022?
      Police reports record 5,638 crashes, 15 deaths, and 3,145 injuries in this district from Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 19, 2025. Pedestrians suffered 621 injuries; people on bikes suffered 389. Source: NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Which intersections stand out as trouble spots?
      Ralph Avenue, Broadway, and Atlantic Avenue rank among the top for injuries and deaths in the district. These are based on counts of injuries, serious injuries, and fatalities in NYC Open Data.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95, Persons f55k-p6yu, Vehicles bm4k-52h4). Filters: dates 2022-01-01 to 2025-09-19; geography limited to Assembly District 56; all modes. We summed deaths, injuries, and crash counts and reviewed police-listed contributing factors and locations. Data were accessed Sep 19, 2025. You can start from the Crashes dataset here.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
