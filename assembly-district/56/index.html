      No More Widows: End the Killing on Brooklyn Streets Now

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations for deduplication and order.)

      AD 56: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A woman steps out of her car at Van Buren and Marcus Garvey. An SUV guns the engine. She is struck, dragged, and left dying in the street. Her name was Tiffany Cifuni. She was pregnant. Her husband said, “I lost my whole family tonight and I don’t think I’ll ever be the same” (NY Daily News).

      In the last twelve months, two people have died and 611 have been injured in crashes in Assembly District 56. Nine were seriously hurt. The dead include a 32-year-old woman and a 4-year-old boy. The numbers do not stop. They only grow.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and the Young

      SUVs killed five pedestrians here in recent years. Cars, trucks, and bikes left hundreds more injured. Children are not spared. A four-year-old boy died in a crash on Atlantic Avenue. The street does not care if you are young or old. It only takes.

      What Has Been Done — And What Has Not

      Assembly Member Stefani Zinerman has co-sponsored bills to make streets safer for all users (A 1077, A 1280). She voted yes on a bill to bring speed cameras to school zones (A 7652). These are steps. But the blood on the street says it is not enough.

      When the city proposed a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue, Zinerman questioned the safety benefits. The DOT replied: “a 21% drop in pedestrian injuries where protected lanes exist” (DOT replied). The lane is still not built.

      The Call: Demand More, Demand It Now

      Every day of delay means another family shattered. Call Assembly Member Zinerman. Call your council member. Demand protected bike lanes, lower speed limits, and cameras that never go dark. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws and oversees state policy, including street safety. Open States
      ▸ Where does AD 56 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn and city council district District 36. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 56?
      It includes the Bedford-Stuyvesant (East) and Brooklyn CB3 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 36 and District 41, and State Senate Districts SD 20 and SD 25. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 56?
      Cars and Trucks: 5 deaths, 101 moderate injuries, 4 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 2 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 8 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and enforcement. They are not random or inevitable.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and fund protected bike lanes, lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, and demand street redesigns that protect people, not just cars.

The article states, "Authorities say a female driver slammed into the victim as she stood in the roadway after a traffic incident."       SUV and Sedan Crash on Tompkins Avenue Injures Passengers
      ▸

      Two cars slammed together on Tompkins Avenue in Brooklyn. Metal twisted. Glass broke. Three men hurt, one bleeding from the head. Police say drivers were distracted and speeding. The street bore the mark of reckless force.

      On Tompkins Avenue near Jefferson Avenue in Brooklyn, a station wagon/SUV and a sedan collided. According to the police report, three men were injured. One passenger suffered severe head bleeding, while two others reported whiplash and injuries to the entire body. The crash involved a left turn and a straight-ahead movement. Police cited 'Driver Inattention/Distraction' and 'Unsafe Speed' as contributing factors. One driver was unlicensed. No mention of helmet or signal use appears in the report. The impact struck the left front bumpers of both vehicles, leaving both cars damaged. The crash highlights the danger when drivers lose focus and exceed safe speeds.
