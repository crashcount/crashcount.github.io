      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 70?

      Silence Kills: Demand Action Before Another Child Dies

      Written July 2, 2025 (Revised August 9, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 70: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 2, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A three-year-old was killed crossing with the signal at Lenox and West 135th. Her mother was hurt beside her. The SUV kept turning left. That was July 2024. Four months later, a 66-year-old man died on 5th Avenue. He was not at the intersection. The car kept going. In January, a 59-year-old man on an e-bike was crushed by a bus at Lenox and 138th. The street was quiet. He did not get up.

      In the last twelve months, three people died on these streets. Thirteen more were left with serious injuries. Most were walking or riding. Most never saw it coming. The numbers pile up: 936 crashes, 479 hurt, three dead. The names fade. The pain does not.

      The Policy Response: Slow, Then Slower

      The city talks of Vision Zero. The state passed Sammy’s Law, letting New York City lower speed limits. The city started to drop some zones to 20 mph. But the default is still 25. The clock ticks. The streets do not wait.

      Assembly Member Jordan Wright represents these blocks. There is no record here of a public stand, a bill, or a press quote from Wright on traffic violence. The silence is heavy. The dead do not vote. The living remember.

      The Cost of Inaction

      The city rolled out a certified e-bike trade-in after a fire killed a young journalist in Harlem. “Fires from unregulated e-bike batteries have already caused too much harm, including tragic loss of life,” said Councilmember Keith Powers. The city moved. The state moved. But on the streets, the cars keep coming. The left turns keep killing. The numbers keep rising.

      This is not fate. This is policy.

      Call to Action

      Call Assembly Member Jordan Wright. Demand a public stand. Demand a citywide 20 mph limit. Demand action for every child, every elder, every neighbor who walks these streets.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.
