      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 82?

      Blood on Benedetto’s Watch: Who Pays for Deadly Streets?

      Written June 22, 2025 (Revised July 6, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      AD 82: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 22, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      In Assembly District 82, the numbers are blunt. Four people killed. 572 injured. Six left with wounds so deep they are called serious. These are not numbers. They are lives. They are families who will never be the same.

      On a Saturday before dawn, a crash on the Hutchinson River Parkway tore through the quiet. A scooter, a Honda Pilot, a Honda Accord. Seven hurt. An 18-year-old woman thrown from the scooter, left in the road, fighting for her life. An 18-year-old was in critical condition after being thrown from a moped in the hit-and-run, according to NYPD. The driver fled. The road kept its secrets.

      A year before, a 13-year-old boy and a 21-year-old man died together on Newbold Avenue. A 64-year-old cyclist was killed on Williamsbridge Road. A 66-year-old pedestrian was crushed on the Parkway. The dead do not speak. Their absence does.

      Benedetto’s Record: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Assembly Member Michael Benedetto has voted for school speed cameras that protect children. He has co-sponsored a bill for “complete streets”—roads built for people, not just cars. But he has also backed a bill that would force e-scooter riders to get licenses and insurance, shifting the burden onto the most vulnerable and doing nothing to slow the cars that kill them A 116.

      Benedetto stood up for a campaign donor, pushing to let their buses idle and pollute the air, using disabled children as a shield. “I believe DJ Ambulette should be … given a variance to let them have their buses idle to properly maintain their temperature on their buses.” The city and advocates said no. The children kept breathing the air.

      What Comes Next: The Fight Is Not Over

      The disaster is not fate. It is policy. Speed kills. Cameras and lower limits save lives. But laws that target cyclists and scooter riders, not drivers, leave the real danger untouched.

      Call your Assembly Member. Call your Council Member. Demand safer speeds, more cameras, and streets built for people, not just cars. Do not let another name become a number. Take action now.
