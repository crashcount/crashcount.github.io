      Bronx Bleeds While Benedetto Stalls: Four Dead, Hundreds Broken, No End in Sight

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      AD 82: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in AD 82

      The Bronx does not forget. In the last twelve months, four people died on the streets of Assembly District 82. Five hundred seventy-six were injured. Six were left with wounds so deep they will not heal. The dead include a 13-year-old boy thrown from a motorcycle, a 66-year-old man crushed on the Hutchinson River Parkway, a 64-year-old cyclist struck down on Williamsbridge Road, and a Navy veteran shot after a minor crash. The numbers do not flinch: 843 crashes in a year. Every week, another family waits in a hospital hallway.

      Faces Behind the Numbers

      A mother holds a card from her son, now gone. “My last Mother’s Day I just spent with him, I’ll never have another one back. So at least I have this, I could look at this each time and read the words he left behind for me,” said Suzette Thomas. Her son, Keino Campbell, was shot after a fender bender. His father said, “If you have insurance, you use insurance. Stop jumping out of car and shooting people, guys. Use your insurance” he told reporters.

      A 32-year-old man lost his leg trying to stop a car thief in Pelham Bay. The street was slick with blood. The Bronx saw car thefts rise even as the rest of the city fell. “This has absolutely happened before,” a neighbor said.

      Benedetto: Action and Inaction

      Assembly Member Michael Benedetto has stood on both sides of the line. He co-sponsored a bill for safer streets, demanding sidewalks and crossings for all users A 1077. He voted yes to speed cameras near schools A 7652. But he also backed a bill to require licenses and insurance for e-scooter riders A 116—a move that burdens the most vulnerable and lets drivers off the hook. He testified for a campaign donor to keep idling buses running, polluting the air children breathe testified for a campaign donor.

      The streets are not safe. The work is not done.

      What Now?

      Call Benedetto. Call the Mayor. Call the DOT. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people walking, biking, and living in AD 82. Do not wait for another name to be carved into stone. Take action now.
