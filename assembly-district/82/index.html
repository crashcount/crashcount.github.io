      Bronx Streets Bleed—When Will Benedetto Make Them Safe?

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, deduped and cleaned citations.)

      AD 82: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      Broken Bodies, Broken Streets

      In Assembly District 82, the road does not forgive. In the last twelve months, four people died and 574 were injured in crashes. Six suffered injuries so severe they may never walk the same. A 13-year-old ejected from a motorcycle. A 66-year-old man crushed crossing the Hutchinson River Parkway. A cyclist, age 64, struck down by a truck on Pierce Avenue. The numbers are not just numbers. They are fathers, daughters, neighbors.

      On Wilkinson Avenue, a man tried to stop a car thief. He was dragged, left with a shattered leg and head trauma. He may lose his limb. The street was slick with blood. “This has absolutely happened before,” a neighbor told the Daily News. “There have been multiple times last year, the year before that, of attempted car robberies. Also, I think there was a carjacking as well.”

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Assembly Member Michael Benedetto has voted for school speed cameras, a proven tool to slow drivers near children. He co-sponsored a bill for “complete streets”—roads built for people, not just cars. But Benedetto also backed a bill that would force e-scooter riders to get licenses and insurance, shifting the burden onto the most vulnerable and doing nothing to slow the cars and trucks that kill. He stood up for a campaign donor seeking exemption from anti-idling laws, arguing for more pollution on streets where children breathe. “I believe DJ Ambulette should be … given a variance to let them have their buses idle to properly maintain their temperature on their buses,” Benedetto said in testimony.

      The Road Ahead: Action, Not Excuses

      Speed and inaction kill. In AD 82, SUVs and trucks caused most deaths. Pedestrians and cyclists pay the price. The Bronx saw a rise in car thefts even as the rest of the city improved. The district attorney and police point fingers. The streets stay dangerous.

      Call your leaders. Demand lower speed limits. Demand street redesigns that protect people, not just cars. Join forces with groups like Families for Safe Streets and Transportation Alternatives. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents local districts across New York.
      ▸ Where does AD 82 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx and city council district District 13.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 82?
      It includes the Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Co-Op City, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Hutchinson Metro Center, Pelham Bay Park, Bronx CB10, and Bronx CB28 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 12 and District 13, and State Senate Districts SD 34 and SD 36.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 82?
      SUVs and trucks caused the most deaths to pedestrians in AD 82, with 3 deaths and 6 serious or moderate injuries. Sedans also caused deaths and injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 1 moderate injury. Bikes caused no deaths or serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and enforcement save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, expand speed cameras, and pass laws that protect people walking and cycling.

      Bronx Car Theft Leaves Man Critically Hurt
      ▸

      A man clung to his car as a thief sped off. The car crashed into parked vehicles. He was crushed and struck again by a fleeing SUV. Blood pooled on the Bronx street. He was left with critical head and leg injuries.

      ABC7 reported on April 24, 2025, that a 32-year-old man suffered critical injuries while trying to stop a car thief on Wilkinson Avenue in the Bronx. Surveillance video captured the suspect entering the double-parked Infiniti as the owner ran to intervene. The man was dragged for a block and a half before the car crashed into parked vehicles. ABC7 notes, 'Witnesses described a bloody scene and severe leg injury.' After falling, the victim was also struck by the suspect’s SUV. Police said the suspect abandoned the stolen car and fled on foot. The incident highlights the dangers of vehicle theft and high-speed flight on city streets. No arrests have been made.
