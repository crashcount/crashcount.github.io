      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Bowery and Canal: two lives gone, a corridor still deadly

      AD 65: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 21, 2025

      Kevin Cruickshank, 55, and May Kwok, 63, died at Bowery and Canal on Jul 19, 2025. A stolen car came off the Manhattan Bridge at more than 100 mph, prosecutors said (NY Daily News; NY1).

      Since 2022, 16 people have been killed in this district; ten were walking and two were on bikes (NYC Open Data: Crashes, Persons, Vehicles). In the same span, people walking here suffered 2,471 injuries and 45 serious injuries; 15 of those walking deaths are recorded in the pedestrian subset, with heavy vehicles involved in six (NYC Open Data: Persons).

      Canal keeps taking

      Seven have died on the Canal Street corridor since 2011, city officials and reporters noted after the July crash (Gothamist). The city promised new barriers and a redesign. “We are taking immediate steps to fortify this intersection while developing a long-term safety redesign for the entire Canal Street corridor,” the transportation commissioner said (Gothamist).

      The fixes came too late for Kevin and May. NY1 reported both were killed at the scene (NY1).

      Where the street fails

      Water Street saw four deaths in this period. Allen Street, Delancey Street, East Houston Street, and the FDR Drive section nearby each carry a heavy injury load (NYC Open Data: Crashes). Failures repeat: police records cite driver inattention on Canal, failure to yield at Allen, and alcohol on East Houston (NYC Open Data: CrashID 4707766, CrashID 4660648, CrashID 4524167).

      Deaths cluster in daylight and evening here. Four at about 10 AM. Four around 8 PM. Serious injuries spike at night, including about 10 PM (NYC Open Data: Crashes/Persons).

      Heavy vehicles hit hard. Among people walking injured or killed here since 2022, trucks and buses were present in six deaths (NYC Open Data: Persons).

      Promises and power

      After the Bowery crash, the city moved on Canal. Advocates warned most of the corridor remains dangerous. “Canal Street is only as safe as its most dangerous block,” said one local leader (Gothamist).

      Albany extended the school speed-zone camera program this June. Assembly Member Grace Lee voted yes on S 8344, which keeps protections in place near schools (Open States).

      The city has tools to slow cars now. It can lower speeds and harden turns. It can daylight corners and protect crossings at Canal, Allen, Delancey, and Water. The district’s record shows where to start (NYC Open Data: Crashes).

      End the repeat harm

      A small group of drivers does the greatest damage. The fix is simple: slow them. Our action guide lays out two steps the city and state can take right now — a lower default speed and requiring speed limiters for repeat offenders. The details and call scripts are here.

      Commissioner Rodriguez promised action on Canal. Families need action on every block that shows the same pattern. The names change. The corners do not.

      Take one step today. Ask your leaders to use the power they already have to slow the street here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What area does this cover?
      Assembly District 65 in Manhattan, including Financial District–Battery Park City, SoHo–Little Italy–Hudson Square, Chinatown–Two Bridges, and the Lower East Side.
      ▸ How many people have been killed here since 2022?
      Sixteen people have been killed in traffic crashes across Assembly District 65 since Jan 1, 2022, including ten people walking and two people on bikes (NYC Open Data: Crashes/Persons).
      ▸ Where are the worst spots?
      Water Street accounts for four deaths in this period. Allen Street, Delancey Street, East Houston Street, and the FDR Drive segment in the district show high injury counts (NYC Open Data: Crashes).
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets — Crashes (h9gi-nx95), Persons (f55k-p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k-52h4) — filtered to Assembly District 65 (by the NTAs MN0101, MN0201, MN0301, MN0302) and dates Jan 1, 2022–Sep 21, 2025. We grouped by victim type, location, hour, and vehicle class to produce the counts cited. Data were accessed Sep 21, 2025. You can view the base datasets here.
      ▸ Who represents this area and what have they done?
      Assembly Member Grace Lee voted yes to extend NYC school speed-zone camera provisions (S 8344) in June 2025 (Open States). The area is also represented by Council Member Christopher Marte and State Senator Brian Kavanagh.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
