      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 71?

      Three Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Assembly District 71 Streets Are Killing Fields

      Written August 5, 2025 (Revised August 13, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      AD 71: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 5, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Silence

      Three people have died on the streets of Assembly District 71 in the past year. Four hundred ninety-two more were injured. Six suffered wounds so deep they count as serious. The numbers do not rest. They do not heal. They only grow.

      Just days ago, a cyclist was struck in a hit-and-run at West 181st and Cabrini. The driver made a U-turn, hit the woman, and ran. The officers checked her, then left. Neighbors watched. One said, “No one stops at these stop signs. We see people go through these red lights all the time.” The street is a gauntlet. The city lets it stand.

      Last summer, a 17-year-old drove the wrong way on the Henry Hudson Parkway. He killed a man one day from his wedding. The crash left families with nothing but ashes and questions. “Kirk was taken from me just as we were starting our new life together. He was the love of my life,” said his fiancée.

      Who Pays the Price?

      SUVs and cars did most of the harm. In three years, they killed two, left 239 hurt, and six with injuries that will not fade. Trucks and buses hit twenty. Motorcycles and mopeds, nine. Bikes, fifteen. The dead do not care what hit them. But the living must.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      Assembly Member Al Taylor has voted for school speed zones and speed cameras. He co-sponsored bills for safer street designs and speed limiters on repeat offenders. He backed Sammy’s Law, which lets the city lower speed limits. But he also voted to weaken bus rules, risking more harm to those on foot and bike.

      The work is not finished. The streets still bleed. The clock runs out for someone every week.

      Call to Action: Make Them Hear You

      Call Al Taylor. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people who walk and bike. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Assembly and how does it work?
      The New York State Assembly is the lower house of the state legislature. It passes laws and oversees state policy, including traffic safety.
      ▸ Where does AD 71 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 10 and state senate district SD 30.
      ▸ Which areas are in AD 71?
      It includes the Hamilton Heights-Sugar Hill, Harlem (North), Washington Heights (South), Washington Heights (North), Inwood Hill Park, Manhattan CB9, and Manhattan CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 7, District 9, and District 10, and State Senate Districts SD 30 and SD 31.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in AD 71?
      SUVs and Cars: 2 deaths, 239 minor/moderate injuries, 6 serious injuries (total 241 incidents). Trucks and Buses: 0 deaths, 20 minor/moderate injuries (total 20 incidents). Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 9 minor/moderate injuries (total 9 incidents). Bikes: 0 deaths, 15 minor/moderate injuries (total 15 incidents).
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speed limits, and enforcement focused on dangerous driving—not on blaming those walking or biking.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to prevent traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, expand speed cameras, and support laws that hold reckless drivers accountable.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
