      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in AD 71?

      Nine Dead, No Excuses: Demand Streets That Don’t Kill

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised July 20, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      AD 71: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In Assembly District 71, the numbers do not lie. Nine people killed. Sixteen left with serious injuries. In just the last twelve months, three more dead, five more lives shattered. Cyclists, pedestrians, children. The street does not care how old you are. A man on a bike, thrown headfirst. A 76-year-old crossing Broadway, struck down. A crash on the parkway—two dead, one left broken. The city moves on. The blood stays in the cracks.

      The Silence and the Votes

      Al Taylor has not stood in the way of safety. He co-sponsored bills to make streets safer for everyone, not just drivers. He backed A 1077 and A 1280, both demanding streets built for people. He voted yes on school speed cameras, a small shield for children walking to class. But the work is not done. The dead do not care about bills that sit in committee. The injured do not heal because a law is “in sponsorship.”

      The Cost of Delay

      Every day of delay is another risk. The city has the power to lower speed limits. It has the tools to redesign streets. But the pace is slow. The pain is fast. “They could’ve helped get him out, get him assistance,” said Carmen Colon after a man burned in his car while officers drove away. “No help was offered, and then how long he burned for. It was a lot,” said Shakira Guzman. The system failed. The street stayed the same.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Call Al Taylor. Call the council. Demand speed limits that save lives. Demand streets that protect the people who walk and ride. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.
