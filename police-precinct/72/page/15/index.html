      Thirteen Dead, Still Waiting: Who Will Stop the Killing on These Streets?

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 6, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations.)

      Precinct 72: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies on the Asphalt

      In Precinct 72, the numbers do not lie. Thirteen dead. Twenty left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. Over three years, the streets have claimed the old and the young, the walker and the cyclist. A 70-year-old woman, crossing with the signal, struck down by a turning sedan at 44th Street and 7th Avenue. A 49-year-old man, crushed on his bike by a left-turning pickup at 53rd and 4th. An 82-year-old, killed by a motorcycle on 60th and 5th. Each crash is a life ended or broken, a family left with silence and loss. See the data.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and trucks are the main weapons. In the last three years, they killed at least five people and left dozens more with broken bodies. Motorcycles and mopeds took two more lives. Bikes, too, have caused pain, but the scale is not the same. The violence is relentless, and it is not random. It follows the same routes, the same blind corners, the same wide avenues where speed is easy and stopping is not.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      The city has tools. Sammy’s Law gives New York the power to lower speed limits. Speed cameras are proven to cut speeding and injuries. But in Precinct 72, the carnage continues. The police can enforce speed limits, ticket drivers who fail to yield, and target crash hotspots. They can act, but too often, they wait. Every delay is another name on a slab.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Residents and advocates must demand more. Call the precinct. Call your council member. Demand speed enforcement, safer crossings, and real accountability. Do not wait for another body in the street. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 72 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn. It overlaps District 38, District 39, and District 43 in the City Council, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 49, AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 20, and SD 26.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 72?
      It includes the Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Sunset Park (West), Sunset Park (Central), Green-Wood Cemetery, and Brooklyn CB7 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, and District 43, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 20, and SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 72?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 49 moderate injuries, 5 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 2 deaths, 3 moderate injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 5 moderate injuries.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 72 can enforce speed limits, issue tickets for failure to yield, and target known crash hotspots. They can respond to dangerous conditions and crack down on reckless driving. The tools are there. They just need to act.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. Patterns repeat at the same corners and crossings. Enforcement, safer street design, and lower speeds save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can push for lower speed limits, fund safer crossings, and demand police enforcement. They can pass laws that protect people, not just cars.
