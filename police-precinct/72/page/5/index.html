      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 72?

      Third Avenue Doesn’t Stop for the Dead

      Written July 13, 2025 (Revised July 13, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, ensured proper citation formatting, and cleaned citations.)

      Precinct 72: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 13, 2025

      Blood on Third Avenue

      Friday morning, July 11th. Two men stepped into the crosswalk at 3rd Avenue and 52nd Street. A BMW ran the red. The car did not slow. Kex Un Chen, 80, and Faqiu Lin, 59, died where they fell. The driver kept going. Police found him later. He told them, “I had a six pack of Modelos and two drinks. I was driving the car by myself last night. It’s my fault. After I hit them, I came home.” admitted the driver.

      The street was quiet except for the line at the food pantry. “I pass through here almost every morning… The line is really long. So, they get there early so they can get their stuff early,” said a neighbor.

      This is not rare. In the last twelve months, 703 people were hurt and 2 killed in crashes in Precinct 72. Six were seriously injured. In three and a half years, 13 people have died and 2,253 have been injured here. Most were walking or biking. Most never saw it coming.

      The Same Streets, The Same Story

      The crash at 3rd and 52nd was not the first. In five years, a three-block stretch around that corner saw 59 crashes and 80 injuries. Neighbors say drivers speed here. reported CBS New York.

      The dead are old, young, working, waiting for food. They are not statistics. They are names, now gone.

      What Has Been Done, What Must Be Done

      The city has new laws. Speed cameras run 24/7. The council can set speed limits lower. But the blood keeps coming. Police can enforce speed, failure to yield, reckless driving. They can target the corners where people die. They have the tools. They need to use them.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call Precinct 72. Demand action.

      Every day of delay is another family broken. The clock is ticking. The street does not care who you are. It only takes.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 72 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 72?
      It includes the Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Sunset Park (West), Sunset Park (Central), Green-Wood Cemetery, and Brooklyn CB7 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, and District 43, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 20, and SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 72?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 151 minor injuries, 53 moderate injuries, 5 serious injuries (212 total). Motorcycles and Mopeds: 2 deaths, 16 minor injuries, 3 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries (21 total). Bikes: 0 deaths, 25 minor injuries, 7 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries (32 total).
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 72 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target known crash hotspots and respond to dangerous conditions. Police have the tools—they just need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are not fate. Most deaths and injuries here come from speeding, failure to yield, and reckless driving—actions that can be stopped with enforcement and safer street design.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, and push for stronger enforcement. They can listen to residents and act before the next tragedy.
