      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 72?

      Third Avenue Kills—City Hall Lets It

      Written July 12, 2025

      Precinct 72: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      The Death Toll on Third Avenue

      Two men are dead. On July 11, a BMW tore down Third Avenue in Sunset Park. It did not stop. It hit Kex Un Chen, 80, and Faqiu Lin, 59, as they crossed the street. The car sent their bodies flying. The driver ran. Police found him later and charged him with manslaughter and leaving the scene. The men were walking to a food pantry. They never made it. “We’re very heartbroken. He was a good person,” said Lin’s sister, Qin Xiao Chen.

      This was not a fluke. Since the city paused a safety redesign for Third Avenue, there have been 96 crashes and 80 people hurt on that stretch. “It was only a matter of time before somebody else died on this corridor and here we are,” said Julio Peña.

      The Numbers Behind the Names

      In the last 12 months, 2 people have died and 707 have been injured in traffic crashes in Precinct 72. Six were seriously hurt. Since 2022, the toll is 13 dead, 2,253 injured, 21 seriously hurt. Most victims are people on foot or bike. Cars and trucks did the most harm, killing 3 and injuring 209. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1, injured 14. Bikes injured 32. The bodies pile up. The story does not change.

      Leadership: Action and Delay

      The city has tools. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can set lower speed limits. The NYPD can crack down on speeding, failure to yield, and reckless driving. But on Third Avenue, the city paused a safety plan after business pushback. The deaths followed. Community Board 7 supported the redesign. City Hall stalled. The police can target crash hotspots. They can write tickets. They can save lives. They need to act.

      Call to Action: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      Every death is preventable. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the city finish the Third Avenue redesign. Demand real enforcement against speeding and reckless driving. Tell Precinct 72 to protect the people who walk and bike. Do not wait for the next body in the crosswalk.

      ▸ Where does Precinct 72 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.

      It includes the Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Sunset Park (West), Sunset Park (Central), Green-Wood Cemetery, and Brooklyn CB7 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, and District 43, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 20, and SD 26.

      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 72 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and respond to dangerous conditions. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield. They can target known crash hotspots. The tools exist. They need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are preventable. Every death on Third Avenue and elsewhere could have been stopped with better street design, lower speeds, and real enforcement. As one advocate said, “Every death is preventable.”
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      Local politicians can push for street redesigns, lower speed limits, and real enforcement. They can support laws like Sammy’s Law and make sure safety projects are not delayed. They can hold city agencies and the police accountable for action.
