      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 67?

      Precinct 67: Death in the Crosswalk, Speed on the Boulevard

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 67: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another driver. Same ending.

      • On January 25, a sedan going straight on Church Avenue hit a 30‑year‑old man in the intersection at Kings Highway. He died at the scene. The NYPD crash report lists Unsafe Speed for the driver. The man was crossing against the signal. Only one person ended up dead. NYC Open Data (CrashID 4788144).
      • On May 14 at Rutland Rd and E 95 St, a 68‑year‑old woman crossing with the signal was struck and killed by a left‑turning sedan. The driver’s factor: Driver Inattention/Distraction. NYC Open Data (CrashID 4812813).
      • On July 17 at Church Ave and E 55 St, a 36‑year‑old pedestrian was killed. The vehicle was a 2011 Ford SUV. The report logs the pedestrian as crossing against the signal. He died. The SUV kept going straight. NYC Open Data (CrashID 4828979).
      • On October 20, 2024, at Linden Blvd and Church Ave, an unlicensed e‑scooter rider collided with an MTA bus and died. The rider was ejected. The bus was going straight. The listed factor was Driver Inattention/Distraction. NYC Open Data (CrashID 4764879).

      In the last 12 months, Precinct 67 logged 1,720 crashes, 1,233 injuries, and 6 deaths. Year‑to‑date, crashes are up 19%, injuries up 27%, and deaths up from 1 to 5. NYC Open Data.

      Church Avenue and Linden Boulevard keep coming up. Church Avenue has seen 2 deaths and 165 injuries since 2022. Linden Boulevard adds another death and 129 injuries. NYC Open Data.

      “Criminal charges for him were still pending.” Police said that after a separate Brooklyn moped crash that killed a rider earlier this month. The pattern is familiar. Gothamist.

      Night brings no mercy. Injuries spike from the evening into late night. The worst is 5–9 p.m. and again 8–11 p.m., when the precinct recorded some of its highest injury counts and multiple deaths. NYC Open Data.

      Speed. Turn. Impact.

      • Since 2022, the precinct’s crashes show pedestrians bearing the brunt: 7 pedestrian deaths, 625 pedestrian injuries. People on bikes: 1 death, 234 injuries. NYC Open Data.
      • The worst listed factors are a catch‑all “other” with 11 deaths and 1,094 injuries, plus vulnerable road user error with 2 deaths and 65 injuries. But the file also names inattention/distraction, failure to yield, and unsafe speed in deadly cases. NYC Open Data.

      Three corners. One fix.

      • Church Avenue. Linden Boulevard. Tilden Ave. These are repeat sites with deaths and serious injuries.
      • Daylighting crosswalks removes the blind corners. Hardened left turns slow drivers who cut across people walking. Leading pedestrian intervals give a head start. Targeted night enforcement at these exact blocks hits the hours when bodies fall. These remedies are standard DOT tools. The crash files show where to use them. NYC Open Data.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      • Pedestrian injuries in this precinct are driven mostly by sedans and SUVs: sedans account for 247 pedestrian victims, SUVs 189. Trucks and vans kill too. NYC Open Data.
      • The police have tightened pursuit rules citywide after too many chases ended in crashes. As the Attorney General said, “the evidence is clear: police vehicle pursuits and high‑speed car chases can be dangerous and even fatal, and it is time for a change.” Times Union | AP.

      Slow the city, save a life.

      • A citywide 20 mph default and speed cameras work together. The city now has the power to lower speeds and Albany renewed 24/7 school‑zone cameras. Use both. Our Take Action page has the steps and contacts.
      • The worst repeat speeders do outsized harm. The Stop Super Speeders Act would force chronic offenders to use intelligent speed assistance. Fewer high‑speed impacts means fewer funerals. See details and contacts on Take Action.

      One corner. One second. A walk sign. A turn. Then the sirens.
