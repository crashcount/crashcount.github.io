      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 67?

      Nine Dead, Thousands Hurt—Precinct 67 Bleeds While Leaders Blink

      Written July 11, 2025

      Precinct 67: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      The Slow March of Loss

      In Precinct 67, the numbers do not lie. Nine people killed. Forty seriously injured. Over 3,300 hurt since 2022. Each number is a body on the street, a family left waiting for someone who will not come home. The dead are not just numbers. They are elders, children, neighbors.

      Just this May, a woman, 68, was crossing with the signal at Rutland Road and E 95th Street. A sedan turned left. She died there, chest crushed. The cause: driver inattention. No storm, no darkness—just a driver who did not see, or did not care. NYC Open Data

      Earlier this year, a 30-year-old man was killed at Church Avenue and Kings Highway. The car was speeding. He crossed against the light. Only one person died. The car kept going. The street stayed open.

      The Usual Suspects

      Cars and SUVs do most of the killing. Four deaths, hundreds of injuries. Trucks and buses take more. Motorcycles and mopeds leave bodies and broken bones. Bikes, too, but the toll is smaller—no deaths, a handful of injuries.

      The stories repeat. A truck on Linden Boulevard. A sedan on Avenue D. A moped, a mask, a man left bleeding in the crosswalk. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said an eyewitness. The driver left. The city moved on.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city has new laws. Speed cameras. Lower limits. But in Precinct 67, the blood keeps flowing. The police have the tools. They can ticket speeders, crack down on reckless turns, target the corners where people die. They know the hotspots. They know the hours. They just need to act.

      Some leaders call for more. “If we would require any vehicles, any motorized vehicles, moving over a speed of 25 mph to be registered, to be insured, it would be much easier to find,” said Assemblyman Michael Novakhov. But words are not enough. The dead do not care about speeches.

      What Comes Next

      Every crash is preventable. Every death is a failure. The police can enforce the law. The city can redesign the streets. The council can lower the speed limit. The time for waiting is over.

      Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand action. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 67 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 41, assembly district AD 41 and state senate district SD 19.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 67?
      It includes the East Flatbush-Erasmus, East Flatbush-Farragut, East Flatbush-Rugby, East Flatbush-Remsen Village, Holy Cross Cemetery, and Brooklyn CB17 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 40, District 41, District 42, and District 45, Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 42, AD 43, and AD 58, and State Senate Districts SD 19, SD 20, and SD 21.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 67?
      Cars and Trucks: 7 deaths, 548 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 8 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 5 injuries. Cars and SUVs are the main killers. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 67 police can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding, failure to yield, and other violations that kill. They have the tools. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes just 'accidents' or are they preventable?
      Every crash is preventable. Every death is a failure of policy, design, or enforcement. Streets can be made safe. The tools exist.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, and demand police enforce the law. They can push for laws that hold dangerous drivers accountable. They can act now, not after another death.
