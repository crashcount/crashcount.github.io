      Precinct 67: Seven pedestrians dead, the clock still runs

      Precinct 67: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Church Avenue runs long. So does Linden Boulevard. In Precinct 67, people die trying to cross them.

      Since 2022, 11 people have been killed on these streets, including seven pedestrians. Another 3,700 were hurt. These are NYPD crash records, not rumors. See the city’s data for yourself in Crashes, Persons, and Vehicles.

      Church and Linden: bodies in the crosswalk

      Two corridors carry the worst burden. Church Avenue has seen two deaths and 165 injuries. Linden Boulevard has one death and 129 injuries. They are among the top danger spots in the precinct’s own rollup of top intersections and corridors, led by Church Avenue and Linden Boulevard.

      On January 25, 2025, at Church Ave and Kings Hwy, a driver going straight hit a 30‑year‑old man in the crosswalk. The record lists “Unsafe Speed.” The man died. The state table shows him as “Crossing Against Signal.” Only one of them is gone. This is crash 4788144.

      On May 14, 2025, at Rutland Rd and E 95 St, a driver turning left struck a 68‑year‑old woman who was “Crossing With Signal.” The file reads “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” She died at the scene. This is crash 4812813.

      Nights bleed more

      Harm rises after dark. Injuries crest from the evening into night. The death count climbs too. The city’s hourly ledger shows fatalities peaking at 7 p.m., 9 p.m., and 10 p.m., with steady injury surges from the rush into late night, including heavy totals at 5–8 p.m. and after 8 p.m. See the hourly distribution.

      Across modes, people on foot carry the weight: seven pedestrian deaths, 628 injured. Cyclists are hit too: one killed, 235 injured. The vehicles that do most harm to walkers are sedans and SUVs, accounting for the vast majority of pedestrian injuries and deaths in this precinct’s rollup. Source: NYC Open Data.

      What the records say about cause

      The files point again and again to the driver. “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” “Unsafe Speed.” “Failure to Yield.” These are among the leading flags in the precinct’s contributing‑factor rollup, which lists inattention, failure to yield, speed, and other driver behaviors tied to the worst outcomes. The data are in Crashes and Persons.

      A 2011 Ford SUV hit and killed a 36‑year‑old man at Church Ave and E 55 St on July 17, 2025. The state record shows the pedestrian “Crossing Against Signal.” It also lists “Alcohol Involvement.” He died in the intersection. This is crash 4828979.

      Small fixes on the corner; big fixes for the city

      Start with the corners that maim. Hardened left turns and daylighting at Church Ave and Kings Hwy. Tighten turns and add leading walk time at Rutland Rd and E 95 St. Focus late‑day enforcement where the injuries spike. The precinct’s own map flags Church Avenue and Linden Boulevard. Work there now.

      But the pattern is bigger than one corner. Lower the citywide default to 20 mph and save lives. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. New York City has the power to set safer speeds. Use it. The case is laid out in our Take Action brief, with sources and contacts.

      The worst repeat speeders do outsized harm. Mandate intelligent speed assistance for drivers who rack up camera violations or points, as detailed in our campaign. It makes the car obey the limit. The driver can’t wish it away.

      Voices on the record

      “Criminal charges for him were still pending,” police told Gothamist after a Brooklyn crash that killed a moped rider.

      “A driver struck and killed a 47-year-old pedestrian… then left the scene,” police said, quoted by the Daily News.

      “No criminality is suspected in either case,” police told the Daily News in a separate report about two subway track deaths.

      The number that won’t go away

      Year to date, deaths in this precinct are up. Crashes too. Compared to last year at this point: crashes up about 19%, injuries up about 28%, serious injuries up about 67%, deaths from 1 to 5. These figures come from the precinct’s year‑to‑date comparison in NYC Open Data.

      A woman with the walk signal. A man at the curb at dusk. The light changes. The car does not.

      Take one step that saves a life. Ask the city to set 20 mph and rein in repeat speeders. Start here: Take Action.

      Nov 2 - A 24-year-old male motorcyclist crashed into two parked SUVs on East 31 Street. He was ejected and suffered a serious head injury. The rider was unconscious and not wearing a helmet. Unsafe speed was cited as a factor in the crash.

      According to the police report, a 24-year-old male motorcyclist traveling north on East 31 Street collided with two parked SUVs. The motorcycle struck the left rear quarter panel of one SUV and the right rear quarter panel of another. The rider was ejected from the motorcycle and sustained a severe head injury, rendering him unconscious. The report lists unsafe speed as a contributing factor. The motorcyclist was not wearing any safety equipment at the time of the crash. No other drivers were involved or injured. The crash highlights the dangers posed by excessive speed near parked vehicles.
