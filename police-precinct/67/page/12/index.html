      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 67?

      No One Should Die Crossing the Street—Hold Precinct 67 Accountable Now

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 67: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Precinct 67, the street is a gauntlet. Since 2022, nine people have died in crashes. Forty people have suffered serious injuries. The numbers do not bleed, but the people do. Most of the dead were walking. Some were old. Some were young. None of them came home.

      Just weeks ago, a woman, age 68, was killed crossing Rutland Road. The driver was turning left. She had the signal. The driver did not see her. The report lists only: “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” NYC Open Data

      A year before, a 74-year-old woman was killed on Avenue D. She was crossing with the light. The car turned left and took her life. The record is silent about the driver. The silence is loud.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and the Rest

      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 4 deaths, 389 minor injuries, 135 moderate, 7 serious. Trucks and buses killed 2. Motorcycles and mopeds, 0 deaths, but 6 injuries. Bikes, 0 deaths, 5 injuries. The street is not equal. The bigger the vehicle, the greater the damage.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse?

      The police in Precinct 67 have the tools. They can ticket speeders. They can crack down on failure to yield. They can target the corners where people die. But the record shows the dying goes on.

      Local leaders have the power to demand more. They can push for lower speed limits, for daylighting intersections, for more enforcement where it matters. But every day of delay is another day of risk. The numbers do not lie. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said a witness after a recent crash.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Every day you wait is another day someone does not come home.
