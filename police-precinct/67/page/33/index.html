      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 67?

      Precinct 67: nights of sirens, days of grief

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 67: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      The sirens don’t stop on Church and Linden. Since 2022, this precinct logged 5,565 crashes, injuring 3,708 and killing 11. Pedestrians took 628 injuries and seven deaths. SUVs and sedans hit them most. Trucks and buses added more hurt. The toll sits in the city’s own ledgers.

      In the last 12 months alone, there were 1,734 crashes here, with 1,244 injured and six killed. This year to date, crashes are up 19% over last year’s pace, with five people dead already. The trend line isn’t a line. It’s a heartbeat.

      Church, Linden, Utica: the ground gives way

      Church Avenue shows the most pain: 165 injuries and two deaths. Linden Boulevard follows with 129 injuries and one death. Utica Avenue adds 82 injuries. These are not one‑offs. They are repeat scenes.

      On January 25, a sedan going straight struck a 30‑year‑old man in the crosswalk at Church Avenue and Kings Highway. He died there. The state marked “Unsafe Speed.” The data marked “Killed.” City records carry his case.

      On May 14 at Rutland Road and E 95th Street, a left‑turning driver hit a 68‑year‑old woman crossing with the signal. She died too. The file reads “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” The dataset doesn’t say more. It doesn’t need to.

      Nights hit hardest

      The hurt spikes after dark. From 7 p.m. to midnight, injuries pile up: 203 at 7 p.m., 218 at 8, 190 at 9, 207 at 10, 145 at 11. Deaths strike at 7 p.m. (three), 9 p.m., 11 p.m., and through the late hours. That’s when families wait for a door that doesn’t open.

      The city’s breakdown lists “other” factors most often. Then distraction. Then failure to yield. Speed shows up in the bloodiest lines. One block, one blink, and the light goes out.

      The bodies in the ledger

      A 44‑year‑old on an e‑scooter was struck at Linden and Church on October 20, 2024, and later died. A bus and a small device met in the same lane. The rider was ejected. The record says “Unconscious,” then “Killed.” The city’s crash file holds the time stamp.

      On July 5 this year, a 34‑year‑old on a moped collided with an SUV on Clarendon Road at E 35th Street just after midnight. He was ejected and died. The form notes improper passing. The street kept moving. The data entry did not.

      On July 17 at Church and E 55th, a 36‑year‑old man on foot died after a crash with an SUV at the intersection. The sheet lists “Apparent Death.” That is all a mother gets to read. NYC Open Data adds a line; a family loses its center.

      What the numbers say to the street

      • Heavy vehicles do damage beyond their count: trucks and buses are linked to 41 pedestrian injury cases with two deaths. Cars and SUVs drive the bulk of the harm, tied to 578 pedestrian cases and five deaths. The rollup tells it straight.
      • Pedestrians are hit most by sedans and SUVs. Four pedestrian deaths tied to sedans. One to an SUV. Same source.
      • The worst hours are evening into night. The city clocked the bodies and the minutes. Collision data marks the surge.

      Fix the corners that kill

      Name the corners. Fix them. Start with Church Avenue and Linden Boulevard. Add daylighting and hardened turns. Give people a head start at the light. Slow the lanes at night where the injuries rise. Hit the repeat spots until the repeats stop. Target drivers who hurt, at the hours that hurt.

      Citywide tools that are on the table

      Albany renewed the city’s 24‑hour school‑zone speed cameras through 2030. That is one guardrail that held. Our streets still run fast. The city now has the power under Sammy’s Law to drop residential limits to 20 mph. Use it. Repeat speeders kill out of proportion. The Stop Super Speeders Act would force the worst drivers to use speed limiters after enough tickets or points. These are the levers. Pull them. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where are the most dangerous spots in Precinct 67?
      Church Avenue and Linden Boulevard lead the injury counts, with two deaths on Church and one on Linden since 2022, per NYC Open Data.
      ▸ When are crashes most likely here?
      Evenings into late night. Injuries peak from 7 p.m. through 11 p.m., with multiple deaths in those hours, according to the city’s hourly crash records.
      ▸ Who is getting hurt?
      Pedestrians, cyclists, and riders of small devices are hit hard. Pedestrians suffered 628 injuries and seven deaths since 2022 in Precinct 67.
      ▸ What can fix this locally?
      Daylighting, hardened turns, and leading pedestrian intervals at repeat hotspots like Church and Linden. Targeted night enforcement where injuries surge.
