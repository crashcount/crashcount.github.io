      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 68?

      Bay Ridge Bleeds: City Inaction Kills, Again

      Written June 23, 2025 (Revised June 23, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations.)

      Precinct 68: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 23, 2025

      The Toll: Lives Lost, Families Broken

      In Precinct 68, the numbers do not lie. Fourteen people killed. Over 1,700 injured. No one is spared—children, elders, workers on their way home. In the last year alone, three more deaths, 616 injuries, and not a single serious injury classified as survivable. The disaster moves slow, but it does not stop.

      Joel Mota, 22, died on a Bay Ridge street. His brother remembered him as “a hardworking man. He had a lot of love for his family. He was always passionate about the things that he did” said Francisco Mota. The driver who killed him was drunk and unlicensed. The city let her drive. The city let him die.

      The Pattern: Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. Four deaths and 243 injuries to pedestrians and cyclists came from cars and SUVs. Trucks and buses add more. Bikes and mopeds are not blameless, but the weight of harm falls from behind a steering wheel. The old, the young, the ones on foot or two wheels—they pay the price.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city talks about Vision Zero. The mayor says, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” Bills pass in Albany. Sammy’s Law lets the city lower speed limits. But in Precinct 68, the deaths keep coming. Enforcement is a choice. The police have the tools. They can crack down on speeding, reckless driving, failure to yield. They can target crash hotspots. They just need to act.

      What Comes Next: Demand Action

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by leaders who look away. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call the precinct. Demand lower speed limits. Demand enforcement. Demand streets where a child can cross without fear.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. Act now.

      Take action today.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 68 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn. It overlaps Brooklyn CB10, Council Districts District 38, District 43, District 47, and District 50, Assembly Districts AD 46, AD 49, and AD 64, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 23, and SD 26.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 68?
      It includes the Bay Ridge, Dyker Heights, Fort Hamilton, Dyker Beach Park, and Brooklyn CB10 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 43, District 47, and District 50, Assembly Districts AD 46, AD 49, and AD 64, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 23, and SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 68?
      Cars and SUVs: 4 deaths, 243 injuries. Trucks and Buses: 0 deaths, 20 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 9 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 17 injuries. Most deaths and injuries come from cars, SUVs, trucks, and buses.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 68 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target known crash hotspots and respond to dangerous conditions. The police have the tools—they just need to act.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. They are the result of choices—by drivers and by leaders who set policy and enforce the law. Every crash is a failure to protect.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, and demand enforcement. They can push for more speed cameras and hold agencies accountable. They can listen to residents and act before another life is lost.
