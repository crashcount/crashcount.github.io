      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Precinct 115: Streets That Keep Killing

      Precinct 115: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      The week, the corner, the names

      Thirteen people have been killed in Precinct 115 since 2022 (NYC Open Data). The trail runs through the same streets.

      On Feb. 1, 2024, at 37th Avenue and 90th Street, a driver going straight at unsafe speed and who disregarded a signal struck and killed a 60‑year‑old woman in the intersection (NYC Open Data). On March 13, 2024, at 31st Avenue and 100th Street, a pickup truck turned left and killed an 8‑year‑old boy crossing with the signal (NYC Open Data). On June 10, 2024, near 37th Avenue and 90th Street again, a motorcycle with a reported accelerator defect hit and killed a 44‑year‑old woman not at an intersection (NYC Open Data).

      The map of harm

      The hot spots are 37th Avenue (3 deaths, 55 injuries) and Northern Boulevard (1 death, 152 injuries). Grand Central Parkway added another death and 282 injuries. These are not mysteries; they are addresses (NYC Open Data).

      The hour the lights go out

      Deaths cluster after dark. Midnight through dawn takes lives here, and evening rush does too. Overnight and evening enforcement is not optional (NYC Open Data).

      What hits us

      For people on foot, sedans caused the most total harm (183), while SUVs killed more (2). Trucks killed one; another death came from an unspecified vehicle. Bikes, mopeds, and motorcycles injured dozens more. Cars and trucks do most of the killing (NYC Open Data).

      Queens voices

      “Joseph Lee terrorized other drivers,” said Queens DA Melinda Katz after a wrong‑way highway attack that left two motorists badly hurt. “Two motorists were badly hurt and still have not fully recovered” (amNY). He told police, “You want to fight?” and admitted he drove the wrong way to hurt people (amNY). When a driver near JFK hit a man and fled, police said, “The operator of the vehicle fled the scene after hitting the man” (ABC7). After a driver mowed down two men at a food cart in Astoria, one witness said, “I have never seen anything like this” (amNY).

      What Precinct 115 can do now

      Target the corridors that bleed: 37th Avenue and Northern Boulevard. Work nights. Ticket failure‑to‑yield, unsafe turns, and speed. Daylight corners. Install LPIs and hardened turns. Push truck turn restrictions where lefts kill. These are standard tools. Use them.

      The fixes above one precinct

      This pattern will not break without citywide action. Lower the default speed limit. Force repeat speeders to use limiters.

      Act now. Tell City Hall and Albany to slow the cars and save lives. Start here: take action.
