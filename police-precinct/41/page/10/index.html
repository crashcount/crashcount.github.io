      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 41?

      Crosswalk, Bruckner, midnight

      Written August 31, 2025 (Revised August 31, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 41: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 31, 2025

      Just after midnight at Bruckner and Leggett, a driver going north hit 32‑year‑old Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez in the crosswalk. “Police believe a driver was traveling northbound on Bruckner Boulevard… when Lopez de Rodriguez was attempting to cross the street in the crosswalk,” ABC7 reported. She was taken to Lincoln Hospital and pronounced dead.

      She is one of 11 people killed on streets in the 41st Precinct since 2022, with 1,629 injured, according to city crash records here. A crash report filed for that corner lists a pedestrian killed at about 12 AM on Aug 26, 2025 (NYC Open Data, CrashID 4837606).

      —

      Bruckner and Westchester bleed

      The danger is concentrated. Since 2022, Bruckner Boulevard logged 255 injury‑causing crashes; Westchester Avenue saw two deaths and 52 injuries. Those are among the worst corridors in this precinct, drawn from city data here.

      Pedestrians and cyclists carry the harm. In this span, pedestrians suffered 244 injuries and five deaths; cyclists 104 injuries and one death, per the precinct rollup here.

      —

      Nights hit hardest

      The wrecks spike after dark. The 9 PM hour alone recorded 144 injuries and five serious injuries in these years, with deaths also clustered late night and early morning, according to the hourly distribution in city data here.

      Signal violations and failures to yield keep showing up in the reports, with dozens of injuries tied to those factors in this precinct window (NYC Open Data). Trucks and buses figure in the harm too, including a pedestrian death among 30 truck/bus‑involved pedestrian cases in this period (NYC Open Data).

      —

      The line does not bend

      The toll is not easing. In the last 12 months, this precinct saw 845 crashes, 537 injuries, and three deaths. Year‑to‑date, crashes are up 13.9% over last year, with injuries and serious injuries also higher (NYC Open Data). The names change. The corners don’t.

      —

      Who moves first on these blocks?

      This is Council District 17 and Assembly District 84, represented by Rafael Salamanca Jr., Amanda Septimo, and Jose Serrano. The record here shows danger on Bruckner, Westchester, Southern, and Hunts Point Ave. The precinct can target these corridors. The city can harden turns, add daylighting and LPIs, and slow the right‑turns that crush people at corners. Trucks need tighter routing and enforcement where they mix with crosswalks.

      Citywide fixes are on the table now. A lower default speed and a crackdown on repeat speeders are laid out on our action page. The Stop Super Speeders Act would force the worst offenders to obey the limit. Read the steps and make the calls here.

      —

      One woman in a crosswalk at Bruckner and Leggett. Eleven dead in four years on these streets. The same lanes. The same hours. It does not stop by itself.

      Bruckner Boulevard and Westchester Avenue stand out in this period. Bruckner logged 255 injury crashes; Westchester saw two deaths and 52 injuries, per NYC Open Data.

      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets: Crashes (h9gi-nx95) and Person (f55k-p6yu). We filtered for Police Precinct 41 and the date range 2022-01-01 through 2025-08-31. We counted deaths and injuries overall, then reviewed injury distributions by hour, street rollups, modes, and vehicle involvement for the same filters. Data were accessed Aug 31, 2025.
