      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 103?

      No More Names: End the Killing on Our Streets

      Written July 11, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Fixed duplicate headline in body, improved citation clarity, and deduped citations.)

      Precinct 103: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Precinct 103, the road does not forgive. Nine people are dead since 2022. Seventeen more are marked by serious injury. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care about age or reason. They only climb.

      A man, 56, crossing Hillside Avenue, struck and killed by an SUV. No crosswalk. No signal. Only the street and the body left behind. NYC crash data

      A 19-year-old on a motorcycle, dead at 90th Avenue and 188th Street. A 63-year-old driver, killed on 143rd Street. A 62-year-old thrown from a moped, dead on Jamaica Avenue. The stories repeat. The names change. The outcome does not.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs do most of the harm. They killed at least two pedestrians and left hundreds more injured. Trucks, buses, and motorcycles add to the toll. Bikes and mopeds, too, but the steel machines do the worst damage. NYC crash data

      A survivor of a bus crash recalled, “We didn’t see the crane coming… then the crane just hits us.” Four people hurt. The bus driver did nothing wrong. The street did not care.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      The city has new tools. Lower speed limits. More speed cameras. More daylight at corners. But the blood keeps running. Crashes are up 12% this year. Injuries are up 30%. Deaths have tripled.

      Police in Precinct 103 can do more. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket reckless drivers. They can stand at the corners where people die and say, Not here, not today. But only if they see the problem.

      The Call

      Every death is preventable. Every injury is a failure. The numbers are not fate. They are a choice. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 103 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 24, assembly district AD 29 and state senate district SD 10.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 103?
      It includes the Jamaica, South Jamaica, Hollis, and Queens CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 23, District 24, District 27, and District 28, Assembly Districts AD 29, AD 32, and AD 33, and State Senate Districts SD 10, SD 11, and SD 14.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 103?
      Cars and SUVs: 491 pedestrian injuries and deaths. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 18. Bikes: 9. Most harm comes from cars and SUVs. NYC crash data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 103 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and respond to dangerous conditions. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure-to-yield, and target known crash hotspots. The tools are there. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Every death is preventable. Every injury is a failure. The numbers are not fate. They are a choice.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, expand automated enforcement, and demand police focus on the most dangerous drivers. They can listen to residents and act before the next death.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
