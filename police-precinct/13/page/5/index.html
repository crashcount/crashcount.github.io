      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 13?

      Five Dead, Still Waiting: City Lets Drivers Kill

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 13: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Toll in Plain Sight

      Five dead. Twenty-two left with life-changing wounds. That’s the count in Precinct 13 since 2022. The numbers are cold, but the pain is not. A 77-year-old woman, crossing with the signal, was killed by a left-turning SUV. The driver failed to yield. She never made it to the other side, according to NYC Open Data.

      Pedestrians and cyclists take the brunt. Cars, SUVs, and trucks caused most deaths and injuries. Bikes and mopeds hurt too, but the steel does the killing. The street does not forgive. The city counts the bodies. The families count the empty chairs.

      Recent Crashes: No End in Sight

      The carnage does not pause. In the last year alone, 327 people were hurt in 581 crashes. Eleven suffered serious injuries. No one was spared by age: children, the old, the young. The pattern repeats. A cyclist killed on East 21st. A pedestrian crushed on Broadway. A woman dead at Lexington and 29th. The details are always the same. Metal meets flesh. Flesh loses.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      The city has tools. Speed cameras. Lower speed limits. New laws. But the pace is slow. The police can crack down on speeders, reckless drivers, and failure to yield. They can target the corners where blood stains the curb. They can write tickets. They can show up. But too often, they don’t.

      The message from officials is clear. “Our message to drivers was clear: follow the rules or face the law,” said NYPD Commissioner Jessica Tisch. But the rules are only as strong as the will to enforce them. The city says, “One traffic fatality is one too many,” said Deputy Mayor Meera Joshi. Yet the deaths keep coming.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. Demand more. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Tell Precinct 13 to enforce the law. Tell them to protect the crosswalk, not just the car. Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home.

      Don’t wait for another name on the list. Act now.
