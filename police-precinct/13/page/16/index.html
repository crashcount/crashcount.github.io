      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 13?

      Blood on Fifth Avenue: Inaction Is Killing New Yorkers

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, deduped and cleaned citations.)

      Precinct 13: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Slow Grind of Loss

      In Precinct 13, the numbers do not lie. Six people killed. Twenty-three left with serious injuries. Over a thousand hurt since 2022. The dead do not speak, but the wounds remain. A 49-year-old man crushed by a bus at 3rd Avenue and East 28th. A 23-year-old cyclist, body broken, left bleeding on Park Avenue South. A woman, 48, struck and killed by an SUV while walking on Broadway. These are not accidents. They are the price of inaction.

      The Latest Crashes: No End in Sight

      The carnage continues. Just last month, a convertible struck a child at East 14th. A taxi turned left and threw a woman to the pavement on 1st Avenue. Four pedestrians were hit at once on Fifth Avenue. The stories pile up. Each one is a life changed or ended. Each one is a warning ignored.

      Leadership: Promises and Pressure

      The city has tools: speed cameras, lower limits, new laws. But progress is slow. “We want a real solution to the bike and pedestrian safety issue,” said CB 5 Vice Chair Samir Lavingia. Yet the Fifth Avenue redesign cut out protected bike and bus lanes, leaving the most vulnerable exposed. Another board member put it plain: "[Cyclists] are going to be there anyway, whether you accommodate them or not." link

      The police in Precinct 13 have the power to enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target crash hotspots. They can act. They must act. Every delay is another body on the street.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Residents and advocates must demand more. Call your council member. Demand the precinct crack down on speeding and failure to yield. Insist on protected lanes, not empty promises. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 13 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 3, assembly district AD 66 and state senate district SD 28.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 13?
      It includes the Midtown South-Flatiron-Union Square, Stuyvesant Town-Peter Cooper Village, Gramercy, Murray Hill-Kips Bay, and Manhattan CB6 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 2, District 3, and District 4, Assembly Districts AD 66, AD 74, and AD 75, and State Senate Districts SD 28 and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 13?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 7 serious injuries, 226 total crashes. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 14 total crashes. Bikes: 0 deaths, 3 serious injuries, 42 total crashes. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 13 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and respond to dangerous conditions. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield, and target known crash hotspots. The precinct has the tools. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These crashes are preventable. They are the result of policy choices, street design, and enforcement. Every injury and death is a warning.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund protected bike lanes, and demand enforcement against reckless drivers. They can listen to residents and act before another life is lost.
