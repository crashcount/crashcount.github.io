      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 114?

      How Many Dead Kids Will It Take?

      Written July 8, 2025

      Precinct 114: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 8, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Precinct 114, the numbers do not lie. Thirteen people are dead. Twenty more are left with injuries so severe they are called serious. Since 2022, more than 2,600 have been hurt. Every week, the count grows. The pain does not fade.

      Just days ago, a BMW tore across the Belt Parkway. It hit the divider, vaulted into oncoming traffic, and caught fire. Two young lives ended. Three more in the car survived. The drivers they struck survived too, but not unchanged. Police said, “No one in the doomed BMW was wearing a seat belt.” The NYPD’s Collision Investigation Squad closed the road and began to count the cost. “Speed may have been an issue,” police told reporters. The rest is silence.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and the Young

      Cars and SUVs have done the most harm. They killed two, left 282 with minor injuries, and 111 with moderate wounds, plus 4 serious injuries. Trucks and buses injured 30 more, including 2 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds left 27 hurt. Even bikes are not blameless—15 injuries, one serious. The young are not spared. Two children have died. Over 190 have been injured. The oldest victim was 75. The youngest was not old enough to vote.

      Leadership: Promises and Pressure

      The city has tools. The precinct has power. They can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target the places where blood stains the street. They can act. Sometimes they do. But often, they wait.

      The city has started to lower speed limits. Cameras catch speeders day and night. But the carnage continues. Every day of delay is another day a family waits for a call that never should come.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Demand action before another name is added to the list.

      The dead cannot speak. You can.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 114 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 22, assembly district AD 34 and state senate district SD 11.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 114?
      It includes the North & South Brother Islands, Upper East Side-Lenox Hill-Roosevelt Island, Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Rikers Island, Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, and Astoria Park neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 22 and District 26, Assembly Districts AD 34, AD 36, and AD 85, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 12, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 114?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 282 minor injuries, 111 moderate injuries, 4 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 30 injuries, 2 serious. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 27 injuries, 1 serious. Bikes: 15 injuries, 1 serious. The toll is highest from cars and SUVs.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 114 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield. They can respond to dangerous conditions before another person is hurt. The NYPD’s Collision Investigation Squad responds after the fact, but enforcement can prevent the next crash.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes can be prevented. Speeding, reckless driving, and unsafe streets are not acts of fate. Lowering speed limits, enforcing the law, and redesigning dangerous roads can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and push for stronger enforcement. They can listen to residents and act before another person is killed. Every delay costs lives.
