      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 114?

      Blood on 114’s Streets—Who Will Stop the Killing?

      Written July 7, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Precinct 114: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 7, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      In Precinct 114, the numbers do not lie. Thirteen dead. Twenty seriously hurt. Over 2,600 injured. These are not just numbers. They are people. A cyclist crushed at night. A teen bled out in a seat. A pedestrian struck on a quiet street. The disaster does not come all at once. It comes day after day, year after year.

      Just last month, a BMW vaulted a divider on the Belt Parkway and slammed into oncoming cars. Six people were hurt, three left fighting for their lives. Police said the driver then struck a gray 2016 CR-V, as well as a red 2022 Hyundai. The road closed. The city moved on.

      A year ago, a 15-year-old on a moped was killed at 19th Avenue and 43rd Street. A cyclist died at 34th Avenue and 37th Street. The list grows. SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. Trucks, bikes, and mopeds take their share. The dead do not get a second chance.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city has new tools. Speed cameras. Lower speed limits. More daylight at crossings. But the work is not done. The police have the power to act. They can crack down on speeding, reckless driving, and failure to yield. They can target the corners where people keep dying. They can write the tickets that save lives.

      But too often, the focus drifts. The NYPD investigates after the fact. The Collision Investigation Squad was probing the wreck. The lanes reopen. The cycle repeats.

      What Comes Next

      Precinct 114 can do more. The tools are there. The need is plain. Every crash is a warning. Every delay is a risk. The dead cannot speak. The living must demand action.

      Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. The blood is fresh. The clock is running.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 114 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 22, assembly district AD 34 and state senate district SD 11.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 114?
      It includes the North & South Brother Islands, Upper East Side-Lenox Hill-Roosevelt Island, Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Rikers Island, Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, and Queens CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 22 and District 26, Assembly Districts AD 34, AD 36, and AD 85, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 12, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 114?
      Cars and Trucks: 2 deaths, 4 serious injuries, 393 minor/moderate injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 1 serious injury, 27 minor/moderate injuries. Bikes: 1 serious injury, 14 minor/moderate injuries. Most deaths and injuries come from cars, SUVs, and trucks. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 114 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield, and respond quickly to dangerous conditions. The NYPD’s Collision Investigation Squad responds after crashes, but real safety comes from prevention.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes are preventable. Speeding, reckless driving, and poor street design cause death and injury. Every crash is a warning that something can be fixed.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can push for lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and demand police enforcement at dangerous locations. They can listen to residents and act before the next tragedy.
