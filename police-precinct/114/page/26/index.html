      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 114?

      Blood on 34th Avenue: Hold Them Accountable for Every Broken Life

      Written June 28, 2025

      Precinct 114: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In Precinct 114, the numbers do not flinch. Thirteen people killed. Twenty seriously injured. Over 2,600 hurt. These are not abstractions. They are lives ended or broken on the streets of Astoria, Ditmars, Ravenswood, and beyond. In the last twelve months alone, a cyclist was struck and killed at 37th Street and 34th Avenue. She was coming home from a ride. She had the right of way. The driver was fleeing police. She “flew like 30 feet. It was a lot of force,” said a witness.

      A man in his fifties was left unresponsive in the street after a black SUV hit him and kept going. The driver did not stop. The man was taken to Jamaica Hospital, fighting for his life. The SUV disappeared into the night. Police say the victim was struck by a black SUV traveling eastbound on 101st Avenue that kept going.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and the Young

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. Two deaths from cars. One from a bike. One from a truck. Children are not spared. In the last year, one child died, and 58 more were injured. The violence does not discriminate by age. It only spares the lucky.

      Leadership: Words, Delays, and What Comes Next

      The police have the tools. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket drivers who fail to yield. They can target the corners where blood pools most often. But action is not the same as possibility. “The defendant allegedly led police on a 10-minute chase through the crowded streets of Long Island City and Astoria to avoid responsibility for his actions,” said Queens District Attorney Melinda Katz.

      Local leaders have the power to demand more. They can push for lower speed limits, more cameras, and real consequences for repeat offenders. They can call out silence and delay. They can reward courage and punish cowardice. But every day they wait, another family waits for a call that will not come from the hospital.

      Call to Action: Make Them Feel the Heat

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Demand that every death is treated as a failure, not a footnote. Do not let them look away. Take action now.
