      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 114?

      Astoria Bleeds While Leaders Stall

      Written June 29, 2025 (Revised June 29, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 114: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 29, 2025

      Blood on the Streets

      In Precinct 114, the numbers do not lie. Thirteen dead. Twenty seriously injured. Over 2,600 hurt. This is not a war zone. It is Astoria, Ravenswood, Ditmars, and the streets where children walk to school and workers ride home in the dark. The violence comes in waves—SUVs, sedans, trucks, bikes, mopeds. No warning. No mercy.

      Amanda Servedio, 36, was riding home from a cycling event. A Dodge Ram, chased by police, ran her down at 37th Street and 34th Avenue. She flew thirty feet. She did not scream. “She went airborne. She flew like 30 feet. It was a lot of force,” said a witness. Her father, Frank, said, “We were certainly upset that they didn’t stop the chase… It was probably not the place to be doing a high-speed chase, in the residential neighborhood, and we were very upset that that’s what actually happened” he told the Daily News.

      A 15-year-old moped rider crushed by a bus. A 17-year-old killed in a sedan crash. A pedestrian struck and left critical by a black SUV that did not stop. Every week, another name. Another family.

      The Numbers Behind the Names

      In the last twelve months:

      • 3 killed, 5 seriously hurt, 778 injured in 1,376 crashes.
      • Children are not spared: 1 killed, 1 seriously hurt, 58 injured under 18.
      • Cars and SUVs are the main killers—2 deaths, 4 serious injuries, 397 pedestrian injuries. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes add to the toll.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse?

      Local leaders have the power. The police can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target crash hotspots. They can act. But the blood keeps flowing. The silence is loud. The policies are slow. The pain is fast.

      Call to Action

      This is not fate. This is failure. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Demand that Precinct 114 use every tool to protect the living before another name is added to the list.

      Do not wait for another body in the road.
