      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 114?

      Blood in the Streets, Power in Their Hands—Hold Them Accountable Now

      Written July 6, 2025 (Revised July 6, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Precinct 114: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 6, 2025

      The Blood on the Asphalt

      One dead. Hundreds wounded. In the last twelve months, Precinct 114 saw 669 crashes. One person died. Three were left with injuries so grave they may never heal. Three hundred fifty-nine more were hurt. The numbers are not just numbers. They are bodies in the street, families in waiting rooms, shoes left behind on the curb.

      Just days ago, a BMW tore across the Belt Parkway. It hit the divider, vaulted into oncoming traffic, and slammed into two cars. Six people were hurt. Two are fighting for their lives. Police said, “speed may have been an issue”. The NYPD closed the road and sent in the Collision Investigation Squad. The scene was fire and twisted steel. The cause was simple: a driver lost control. The cost was paid by everyone in his path. “A BMW driver and a passenger were fighting for their lives Saturday after their out-of-control luxury car vaulted into oncoming traffic on the Belt Parkway in Queens, slamming into two unsuspecting motorists in a fiery crash, the NYPD said.”

      The Slow Grind of Policy

      Leadership has tools. They have laws. They have the power to act. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can set lower speed limits. Cameras catch speeders day and night. But the carnage continues. The police can enforce the law. They can crack down on speeding and failure to yield. They can target the corners where people keep getting hit. They just need to use the power they already have.

      Who Pays the Price?

      SUVs and sedans do most of the harm. In three years, cars and SUVs killed two people here. Motorcycles and mopeds hurt dozens more. Trucks, buses, and bikes all play their part. But the pattern is clear: the bigger the vehicle, the harder the blow.

      The Next Move Is Yours

      Every day of delay is another day of blood on the street. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand speed enforcement. Demand safer streets. Do not wait for another crash.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 114 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 22, assembly district AD 34 and state senate district SD 11.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 114?
      It includes the North & South Brother Islands, Upper East Side-Lenox Hill-Roosevelt Island, Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Rikers Island, Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, and Queens CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 22 and District 26, Assembly Districts AD 34, AD 36, and AD 85, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 12, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 114?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 282 minor injuries, 111 moderate injuries, 4 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 18 minor injuries, 9 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 10 minor injuries, 4 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Data source
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 114 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and respond to dangerous conditions. They can issue speeding and failure-to-yield tickets, and target known crash hotspots. Police have the tools. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Speeding, failure to yield, and reckless driving are choices. Enforcement and safer street design can stop the next tragedy.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and demand police enforcement. They can listen to residents and act before the next crash.
