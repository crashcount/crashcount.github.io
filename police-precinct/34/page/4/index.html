      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 34?

      No More Excuses: Lives Burn While Leaders Look Away

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 34: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Bodies Left Behind

      Francisco Guzman Parra burned alive in a car on Dyckman Street. The police came. They left. The fire kept burning. His family watched the video later. “No help was offered, and then how long he burned for. It was a lot,” said his sister.

      In the last twelve months, one person died and eleven suffered serious injuries in Precinct 34. Two hundred seventy-four people were hurt. The numbers do not slow. They do not care about age. Twenty children were injured. No one is spared. NYC Open Data.

      The Police and the Power to Act

      Precinct 34 has the tools. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket reckless drivers. They can target the corners where blood pools and glass scatters. But too often, the law is silent. After Guzman Parra’s death, the officers were suspended. The department said it would investigate. “In that one moment where they effectively weighed their careers to be more important than Francisco’s life, they played the role of judge, jury and executioner,” said the family’s attorney.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse

      Local leaders talk of Vision Zero. They talk of safer streets. But the deaths keep coming. The city has the power to lower speed limits. The precinct can crack down on speeding and failure to yield. The time for talk is over. Every day of delay is another day of risk for the people who walk, bike, or wait for a bus.

      What You Can Do

      Call your Council Member. Call the Mayor. Call the precinct. Demand action. Demand enforcement. Demand that every crash is treated as a crime scene, not a routine. The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a choice. It is time to choose life. Take action now.
