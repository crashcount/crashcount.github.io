      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 49?

      Two young riders killed on Bronx River Parkway. Precinct 49 keeps counting.

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 49: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Two men on mopeds died before dawn. Police say a 21‑year‑old in a Mercedes tried to pass, clipped a Volkswagen, then hit the riders on the Bronx River Parkway near E. 223rd St. He was charged with vehicular manslaughter and DWI. Their names were Enrique Martinez, 21, and Manuel Amarante Penalo, 19. Gothamist reported the charges and names. amNY detailed the pass and the arrests.

      “Police have arrested and charged a man with vehicular manslaughter and driving while intoxicated,” Gothamist wrote.

      “He faces a list of charges including vehicular manslaughter, driving while intoxicated and driving while ability impaired,” amNY reported.

      A sister spoke outside court. “Two people were killed. He was drunk,” she said. “Think about how he took two lives.” NY Daily News recorded her words.

      The parkway keeps taking

      Since 2022, at least three people have died on the Bronx River Parkway inside Precinct 49. City data shows a driver killed there on Jan. 21, 2023 CrashID 4599380. Another died in a high‑speed SUV wreck on Apr. 19, 2024 CrashID 4718704. And now two riders in August 2025, reported by Gothamist and NY Daily News. The parkway ranks among the precinct’s top trouble spots, with 111 injuries and three deaths tallied in the period. Open data lists BRONX RIVER PARKWAY as a hotspot.

      Nights cut deepest. Injuries spike between 8 p.m. and 1 a.m., with the worst hour at 9 p.m. and another peak at 7–8 a.m. Over the period, the 9 p.m. hour saw 96 injuries and two deaths; the 11 p.m. hour saw one more death. Precinct 49 hourly data shows these peaks.

      Where streets hurt most

      Bronxdale Avenue is another wound. It shows 16 injuries and seven serious injuries in this window. City data flags BRONXDALE AVE as a top intersection. A 91‑year‑old man working in the roadway at Rhinelander and Radcliff was killed by a westbound sedan in 2022 CrashID 4580960.

      Pedestrians and cyclists pay the price. Four pedestrians and one bicyclist have been killed in the precinct since 2022, with 298 injured on foot and 90 on bikes. SUVs and cars account for most pedestrian harm, with 119 pedestrian victims tied to SUVs in this period. Open data rollups show these counts.

      Patterns in the rubble

      Contributing factors are plain in the ledger. “Other” causes lead with six deaths and 484 injuries. Disregarding traffic control shows one death. Vulnerable road user error shows one death and 78 injuries. Unsafe speed appears in the files too. The precinct analysis lists these factors.

      On the parkway, speed and passing maneuvers show up in multiple deaths. A 2007 Jeep was “demolished” in the 2024 northbound crash; the driver was ejected and died. The passenger was found unconscious. Unsafe speed was the first listed factor. CrashID 4718704 details the scene. In 2025, police and prosecutors say the Mercedes tried to pass and hit two riders. Gothamist reported the sequence.

      Fix what we can touch now

      Start where blood is fresh: the Bronx River Parkway and Bronxdale Avenue. Daylight every corner near the parkway exits and on‑ramps. Add hardened left turns and longer leading intervals for people on foot at Bronxdale and along Rhinelander. Target late‑night speeding with focused operations at the 8 p.m.–1 a.m. peak. Open data shows the locations and hours.

      Then widen the lens. The city has the tools to slow the fleet. Albany renewed 24‑hour school‑zone speed cameras through 2030, and the city can lower limits. A citywide 20 mph default and cameras on all day and night cut risk. Our published guide explains how to press City Hall, DOT, and your Council office. See our action page for steps and contacts.

      ▸ How are pedestrians and cyclists faring?
