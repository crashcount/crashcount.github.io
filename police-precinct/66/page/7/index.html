      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      Blood on Bay Parkway: The Deadly Toll of City Neglect

      Written July 3, 2025

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 3, 2025

      The Bodies Pile Up

      In Precinct 66, the numbers do not lie. Twenty people killed. Sixteen left with serious injuries. Over 2,100 hurt since 2022. The dead include children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians. The living carry scars. The streets do not forgive.

      Just last November, a van changed lanes on Bay Parkway. The driver died. His passenger was left with broken bones and pain that will not fade. The crash was not an act of God. It was a man behind the wheel, a van, a choice, and a street that let it happen. NYC Open Data

      The Usual Weapons: SUVs, Trucks, and Speed

      SUVs and cars did the most harm—seven killed, over 500 injured. Trucks and buses killed two, injured dozens. Motorcycles and mopeds left more bodies and broken bones. Bikes, too, are not spared. The pattern is clear: the bigger the vehicle, the heavier the toll.

      The pain is not abstract. A witness at one crash described, “I just saw a lot of blood gushing out of his ears, his mouth.” There is no comfort in numbers. Only the cold fact that the street is dangerous, and the danger is allowed to remain. “Yeah, this street is dangerous,” said a neighbor after a boy was killed by an SUV.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse?

      The police in Precinct 66 have the power to act. They can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target the corners where people keep dying. They can treat traffic violence as the crime it is. But the silence is loud. The numbers keep rising. The bodies keep coming. Leadership is measured in lives saved, not words spoken.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by someone, and a system that lets it happen. If you want change, demand it. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Stand with the families who have lost. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 66 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 66?
      It includes the Sunset Park (East)-Borough Park (West), Borough Park, Kensington, Mapleton-Midwood (West), and Brooklyn CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, District 43, and District 44, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 45, AD 48, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 21, and SD 22.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 66?
      SUVs and Cars: 7 deaths, 372 minor injuries, 140 moderate injuries, 5 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 2 deaths, 30 minor injuries, 13 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Motorcycles and Mopeds: No deaths, 14 minor injuries, 6 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Bikes: No deaths, 17 minor injuries, 8 moderate injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 66 police can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield, and respond quickly to dangerous conditions. Every ticket, every patrol, every crackdown on a deadly corner can save a life.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These are not accidents. They are preventable. Speeding, reckless driving, and dangerous streets can be changed. Every crash is a failure of policy and enforcement.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      Local politicians can push for lower speed limits, demand street redesigns, and hold police accountable for enforcement. They can listen to families and survivors, and make safety the priority.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
