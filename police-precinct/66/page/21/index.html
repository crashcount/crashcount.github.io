      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      Children Die. Leaders Hide. Streets Stay Deadly.

      Written June 28, 2025

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      Five dead. Six seriously hurt. In the last year alone, Precinct 66 saw nearly a thousand crashes. Two children did not make it home. A 17-year-old boy died on a moped just days ago. His passenger, 14, was thrown and left unconscious. The street was Avenue N. The hour was just before 10 a.m. The city kept moving. The boy did not. Jhoan later died at the hospital.

      Last month, a woman was killed on Ditmas Avenue. The cause: driver inattention. The numbers do not stop. In the past twelve months: 673 injured, 5 dead, 6 left with wounds that will not heal.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage. In three years: 7 killed, 5 seriously hurt, 372 left with minor injuries. Trucks and buses killed 2, left 1 with life-changing injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds, bikes—smaller numbers, but the bodies are just as broken. The dead do not care what hit them.

      Leadership: Words, Not Action

      The police have the tools. They can ticket speeders. They can crack down on drivers who fail to yield. They can target the corners where blood stains the curb. But the silence is thick. No new bills. No bold plans. No public reckoning. The dead are left to speak for themselves.

      A brother mourns. “He was a hardworking man. He had a lot of love for his family.”

      The Call That Must Be Made

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by those in power. The precinct can act. The council can act. The state can act. But they will not move unless you make them. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safe streets. Do not wait for another child to die.

      The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a warning.
