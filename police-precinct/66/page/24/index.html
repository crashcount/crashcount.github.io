      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      Children Die. Leaders Hide. Streets Stay Deadly.

      Written June 28, 2025

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      Five dead. Six seriously hurt. In the last year alone, Precinct 66 saw nearly a thousand crashes. Two children did not make it home. A 17-year-old boy died on a moped just days ago. His passenger, 14, was thrown and left unconscious. The street was Avenue N. The hour was just before 10 a.m. The city kept moving. The boy did not. Jhoan later died at the hospital.

      Last month, a woman was killed on Ditmas Avenue. The cause: driver inattention. The numbers do not stop. In the past twelve months: 673 injured, 5 dead, 6 left with wounds that will not heal.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage. In three years: 7 killed, 5 seriously hurt, 372 left with minor injuries. Trucks and buses killed 2, left 1 with life-changing injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds, bikes—smaller numbers, but the bodies are just as broken. The dead do not care what hit them.

      Leadership: Words, Not Action

      The police have the tools. They can ticket speeders. They can crack down on drivers who fail to yield. They can target the corners where blood stains the curb. But the silence is thick. No new bills. No bold plans. No public reckoning. The dead are left to speak for themselves.

      A brother mourns. “He was a hardworking man. He had a lot of love for his family.”

      The Call That Must Be Made

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by those in power. The precinct can act. The council can act. The state can act. But they will not move unless you make them. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safe streets. Do not wait for another child to die.

      The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a warning.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 66 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 66?
      It includes the Sunset Park (East)-Borough Park (West), Borough Park, Kensington, Mapleton-Midwood (West), and Brooklyn CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, District 43, and District 44, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 45, AD 48, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 21, and SD 22.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 66?
      Cars and SUVs: 7 deaths, 5 serious injuries, 372 minor injuries. Trucks and buses: 2 deaths, 1 serious injury, 30 minor injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 0 deaths, 1 serious injury, 14 minor injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 17 minor injuries. The largest toll comes from cars, SUVs, and trucks. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 66 can enforce speed limits, issue tickets for failure to yield, and target crash hotspots. Police can crack down on reckless driving and respond to dangerous conditions. Every ticket, every patrol, every crackdown is a chance to save a life.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These are not accidents. They are preventable. Better enforcement, safer street design, and real accountability can stop the next death.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      Local politicians can pass laws for safer streets, fund enforcement, and demand action from city agencies. They can push for lower speed limits, more cameras, and real consequences for reckless drivers.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
