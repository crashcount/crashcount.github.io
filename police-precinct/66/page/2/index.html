      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      No More Excuses: How Many Neighbors Must Die on Avenue U?

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A 90-year-old man tried to cross Avenue U. A blue moped hit him and kept going. His head struck the pavement. He died at the hospital. A witness described the scene: “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open. It was a blue scooter. The driver had a mask. He hit the guy and he left.”

      He is not alone. In the last twelve months, five people have died in Precinct 66. Six were seriously injured. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians are all at risk. Two children died. One woman was crushed by a turning SUV. A teenager on a bike was killed by a truck. The numbers do not stop. In three and a half years, this precinct has seen 20 deaths and 16 serious injuries, according to NYC Open Data.

      The System Fails the Vulnerable

      The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors. They are the old man on the corner, the child on a bike, the woman in the crosswalk. “We want to make sure what happened doesn’t happen again,” said a neighbor after the latest hit-and-run.

      Most deaths come from cars, SUVs, and trucks. But mopeds and bikes are not harmless. In this precinct, SUVs and cars caused seven deaths and five serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed two and seriously injured one. Mopeds and motorcycles left one dead and one seriously hurt. Bikes injured 25 people. No one is safe when the street is lawless.

      Leadership: Action or Excuses?

      Local leaders have the power to change this. They can lower speed limits. They can demand enforcement at crash hotspots. They can push for real penalties for hit-and-run drivers. They can fight for protected crossings and daylighted corners. But too often, the response is slow.

      The police in Precinct 66 have the tools. They can ticket speeders. They can crack down on reckless turns and failure to yield. They can target the corners where people keep dying. They just need to act.

      Call to Action

      This is not fate. It is failure. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand speed enforcement. Demand safe crossings. Demand action before another neighbor dies. The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a warning.
