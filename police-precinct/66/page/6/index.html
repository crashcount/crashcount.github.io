      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      Another Child Dead. Another Excuse. Hold Precinct 66 Accountable.

      Written June 30, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations list.)

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 30, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Precinct 66, the streets do not forgive. Since 2022, 20 people have died in crashes here. Sixteen more suffered serious injuries. Children, elders, cyclists, and walkers—none are spared. In the past year alone, two children died. One was just a boy. His life ended under the wheels of a car. The numbers are not just numbers. They are families torn open.

      Last week, a 17-year-old boy died on a moped. He was thrown from the seat, his passenger left fighting for life. “Jhoan later died at the hospital,” said police. No arrests. No comfort. Only the sound of sirens and the scrape of glass on asphalt.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage. They killed seven people and injured hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed two. Motorcycles and mopeds left one dead, many hurt. Bikes caused injuries, but not a single death. The pattern is clear. The bigger the machine, the deeper the wound.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse?

      The city passed new laws. Speed cameras now watch day and night. The law allows lower speed limits. But in Precinct 66, the carnage continues. The police have the power to act. They can ticket speeders, stop reckless drivers, and protect the crosswalks. But the blood keeps flowing. “He was a boy who loved life,” said Baila Krainer. The city mourns, then moves on.

      What Now?

      Precinct 66 can do more. The police can target crash hotspots. They can enforce speed limits and failure-to-yield laws. They can make it clear: these are not accidents. They are preventable deaths. But only if someone cares enough to stop them.

      Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand action. Every day of delay is another day a child might not come home.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 66 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 66?
      It includes the Sunset Park (East)-Borough Park (West), Borough Park, Kensington, Mapleton-Midwood (West), and Brooklyn CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, District 43, and District 44, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 45, AD 48, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 21, and SD 22.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 66?
      SUVs and Cars: 7 deaths, 372 minor injuries, 138 moderate injuries, 5 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 2 deaths, 30 minor injuries, 13 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 14 minor injuries, 6 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Bikes: 0 deaths, 17 minor injuries, 8 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 66 police can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield. They have the tools to act. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These deaths and injuries are preventable. They happen because of choices—speeding, reckless driving, failure to yield. With enforcement and safer street design, lives can be saved.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can push for lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and hold police accountable for enforcement. They can listen to residents and act before another life is lost.
