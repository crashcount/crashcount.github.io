      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      Kids Die, SUVs Roll On—Who Will Stop the Killing in Precinct 66?

      Written July 1, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations list.)

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 1, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Precinct 66, the streets do not forgive. Since 2022, 20 people have died in crashes here. Sixteen more have been seriously injured. These are not just numbers—they are bodies on the pavement, families left with silence where a voice should be. In the last year alone, two children died. One was just a boy, crossing the street. He did not make it home.

      A witness tried to help. “I saw the kid being dragged from underneath the car by a woman,” said one bystander. Blood on the street. Towels pressed to a small head. It was not enough.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. In this precinct, they are responsible for at least seven deaths and hundreds of injuries. Trucks and buses have killed two. Motorcycles and mopeds, none. Bikes, none. The pattern is clear: the bigger the vehicle, the greater the harm. The street does not care who had the light—only who is left standing.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city has new laws. Speed cameras run all day. The speed limit can be lowered. But in Precinct 66, the danger remains. Police have the tools. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket drivers who fail to yield. They can target the corners where people keep dying. They just need to act. “The driver was really crushed at what happened,” said a witness. But regret does not bring a child back.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. It is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit on every street. Demand enforcement at the corners where people die. Demand action, not words. The next victim is only a day away.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 66 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 66?
      It includes the Sunset Park (East)-Borough Park (West), Borough Park, Kensington, Mapleton-Midwood (West), and Brooklyn CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, District 43, and District 44, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 45, AD 48, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 21, and SD 22.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 66?
      SUVs and Cars: 7 deaths, 372 minor injuries, 139 moderate injuries, 5 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 2 deaths, 30 minor injuries, 13 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 14 minor injuries, 6 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Bikes: 0 deaths, 17 minor injuries, 8 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 66 police can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target known crash hotspots and respond to dangerous street conditions. Every ticket, every patrol, every intervention can save a life.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These deaths and injuries are preventable. They happen because of policy choices, street design, and enforcement gaps. They are not random. They can be stopped.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      Local politicians can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and demand police enforcement at dangerous intersections. They can listen to residents and act before another life is lost.
