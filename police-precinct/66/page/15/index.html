      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      No More Blood on East 14th: Hold Precinct 66 Accountable

      Written July 11, 2025

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Precinct 66, the streets do not forgive. Since 2022, 21 people have died in crashes here. Six in the last year alone. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. They are the man who never made it across Avenue U, the teen thrown from a moped, the woman crushed by an SUV. The ground remembers every one.

      Just days ago, a 90-year-old man was killed crossing East 14th Street. A blue moped hit him and kept going. A witness said, “He hit the guy and he left.” The man died at the hospital. His family waits for answers. The street stays the same.

      The Pattern: Vulnerable Lives, Unforgiving Roads

      In the last 12 months, children and elders have paid the highest price. Two kids and three people over 55 killed. Pedestrians take the brunt. SUVs and sedans do most of the damage—8 deaths, 5 serious injuries—but mopeds, trucks, and bikes leave their own scars. The numbers do not lie: over 2,200 injured since 2022, with 16 left with life-changing wounds.

      The stories repeat. A masked rider. A driver not looking. A body on the pavement. “We want to make sure what happened doesn’t happen again,” said a neighbor. But it does. Again and again.

      Leadership: Promises, Delays, and What Must Change

      The city has new laws. Speed limits can drop. Cameras can catch speeders. But in Precinct 66, the pace is slow. No bold local action. No surge in enforcement. The police have the tools: they can ticket speeders, crack down on reckless drivers, target the corners where people die. They just need to use them.

      Every day of delay is another family broken.

      Call to Action: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand real enforcement. Tell them the deaths must stop. Tell them to use every tool—speeding tickets, failure-to-yield crackdowns, hotspot patrols. Do not wait for another obituary.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 66 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 44 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 66?
      It includes the Sunset Park (East)-Borough Park (West), Borough Park, Kensington, Mapleton-Midwood (West), and Brooklyn CB12 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, District 43, and District 44, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 45, AD 48, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 21, and SD 22.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 66?
      Cars and Trucks: 8 deaths, 5 serious injuries. Mopeds: 0 deaths, 1 serious injury. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries. (Source: NYC Open Data, 2022–2025)
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 66 police can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue speeding and failure-to-yield tickets, and respond to dangerous conditions. The tools exist. They must be used.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. Speeding, failure to yield, and inattention are choices. Enforcement and safer street design save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, expand automated enforcement, and demand police focus on dangerous driving. They can act now, or answer for every life lost.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
