      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 66?

      Stop Counting Bodies. Start Saving Lives.

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      Precinct 66: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      Five dead. Six seriously hurt. In the last year, Precinct 66 saw 996 crashes. Two children died. Eighty-five more were injured. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie. They only count the bodies.

      A 17-year-old boy rode a moped north on East 8th Street. A 71-year-old man turned left. Metal hit metal. The boy and his passenger were thrown. The boy died at Maimonides. The passenger, 14, left with broken bones. There were no arrests. The police said only, “The NYPD highway district collision squad is investigating.”

      Days before, Joel Mota, 22, was killed riding his moped. The driver who struck him was drunk and unlicensed. Joel’s brother said, “He never stopped working.” The driver was arrested, then released. The street stayed the same.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs, trucks, and sedans do most of the damage. In three years, cars and trucks killed seven, left 514 more hurt. Trucks and buses killed two. Motorcycles and mopeds left 21 injured. Bikes, 25. The dead do not care about the make or model.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      Local leaders have the tools. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket reckless drivers. They can crack down on failure to yield. They can target crash hotspots. They can do it today. But the numbers do not move. The bodies keep coming.

      The police in Precinct 66 can act. They can slow the cars. They can protect the children. They can make the streets less cruel. But only if they see the crisis for what it is.

      Call to Action: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand speed enforcement. Demand failure-to-yield tickets. Demand action at every deadly crossing. Do not wait for another child to die.

      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 66 police can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and crack down on failure to yield. They can target crash hotspots and respond to dangerous conditions. They have the tools. They just need to act.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are not fate. They happen on streets that can be made safer. Enforcement, better design, and real action can prevent them.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      Local politicians can push for lower speed limits, demand more enforcement, and fund safer street designs. They can hold agencies accountable and make sure every crash is answered with action.
