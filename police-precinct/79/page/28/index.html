      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Bed‑Stuy’s daily toll, counted at the corner

      Precinct 79: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 22, 2025

      Just after 9 AM on Sep 9, at Fulton Street and Tompkins Avenue, a 26-year-old on a bike collided with a driver in a sedan and was hurt, police records show (NYC Open Data).

      This Week

      • Sep 8: At Throop and Lafayette, police recorded a driver in an SUV backing unsafely into a person walking, who was injured (NYC Open Data).
      • Sep 7: At Albany and Decatur, a driver in a sedan hit a person on a bike; he was knocked unconscious, police noted (NYC Open Data).
      • Sep 2: At Myrtle and Bedford, a 25-year-old moped rider was killed in a three-vehicle crash; police listed “traffic control disregarded” for one driver (NYC Open Data, CrashID 4839231).

      The count in Precinct 79

      Since Jan 1, 2022, crashes here have killed 13 people and injured 2,210, including 23 with serious injuries (NYC Open Data). Pedestrians account for eight of those deaths; one was a person on a bike (precinct records). The toll comes from 4,064 crashes in this period.

      In the past 12 months alone, there were 984 crashes, with 581 injured and three killed in this precinct (NYC Open Data). Year to date, crashes are up compared with last year, with injuries rising as well (711 this year vs. 702 last year; 419 injuries vs. 353) (NYC Open Data).

      Corners that don’t forgive

      Bedford Avenue is a repeat site, with two deaths and 158 injuries in this span (precinct records). One of those deaths was at Myrtle and Bedford on Sep 2, when the 25-year-old rider never left the intersection (NYC Open Data, CrashID 4839231). Nostrand Avenue has 146 injuries on the books (precinct records).

      Police have recorded specific driver actions in these crashes: failure to yield at Flushing and Broadway killed a 68-year-old woman crossing with the signal on Oct 18, 2023 (CrashID 4671690); backing unsafely at Lafayette and Throop injured a 60-year-old man on Sep 8, 2025 (CrashID 4841192); and a driver disregarding a traffic signal was noted in the Sep 2, 2025 fatal at Myrtle and Bedford (CrashID 4839231).

      Nights and mornings, sirens and tape

      Over these years, deaths recur at harsh hours. Records show fatalities logged around 2–4 AM and again late at night, with more in the early afternoon too (precinct records). The pattern is steady. The tape comes out. The names go quiet.

      What leaders can do now

      This precinct sits in Council District 36 under Chi A. Ossé, Assembly District 56 under Stefani Zinerman, and State Senate District 25 under Jabari Brisport. City and state have tools on the table.

      • Lower speeds on neighborhood streets. City law now allows more 20 MPH zones; advocates urge a citywide 20 MPH default because speed decides who lives and who doesn’t (CrashCount: Take Action).
      • Stop repeat speeders. The Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045C/A2299C) would require drivers with 11 DMV points in 18 months or 16 camera tickets in a year to use intelligent speed assistance that keeps them near the limit (CrashCount: Take Action). Will Ossé, Zinerman, and Brisport back it? What gives if they don’t?

      The corner at Fulton and Tompkins will fill again tomorrow. Slower streets and checked speeders would change what happens there. If you want that change, start here.

      This report covers Police Precinct 79 in Brooklyn, including parts of Bedford‑Stuyvesant (West) and Brooklyn CB3.

      From Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 22, 2025, Precinct 79 recorded 4,064 crashes, with 13 people killed and 2,210 injured, including 23 serious injuries, per NYC Open Data.

      Bedford Avenue and Nostrand Avenue stand out in precinct records. A recent death occurred at Myrtle Avenue and Bedford Avenue on Sep 2, 2025 (CrashID 4839231).

      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes, Persons, Vehicles). We filtered records to Police Precinct 79 and the period 2022‑01‑01 through 2025‑09‑22, and tallied injuries, serious injuries, deaths, and total crashes. Data were accessed Sep 22, 2025. You can start with the Crashes dataset here.

      Mar 4 - A 23-year-old man crossing Marcy Avenue with the signal was struck by a vehicle. The impact fractured and dislocated his lower leg and foot. The driver was inattentive and distracted. The pedestrian suffered serious injuries at the intersection.

      According to the police report, a 23-year-old male pedestrian was crossing Marcy Avenue at an intersection with the signal when a vehicle struck him. The pedestrian sustained fractures and dislocations to his knee, lower leg, and foot. The report identifies driver inattention and distraction as the contributing factor to the crash. No other vehicle details or driver information were provided. The pedestrian was seriously injured but was not ejected from the scene. The report does not list any pedestrian errors or safety equipment as factors. The crash highlights the dangers posed by distracted driving to vulnerable road users.
