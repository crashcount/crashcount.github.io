      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 1?

      Precinct 1: West Street to Canal, the hits keep coming

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 1: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      West Street. Broadway. Two names. Most injuries.

      • West Street tops the list with 83 people hurt since 2022. Data
      • Broadway follows with 43 injuries. Data

      Bikes take heavy hits here. Since 2022, 199 cyclists were injured, with five seriously hurt. Pedestrians were struck 257 times, seven with serious injuries. One person died. Data

      Unsafe speed shows up in the ledger with serious harm. Distraction, failure to yield, and blown signals do work too. Night brings pain. Midnight and 8 a.m. each saw 40 injuries; 6 p.m. hit 60. Late night and the rush both spill blood. Data

      Canal and Bowery: 109 mph and two dead

      On July 19, a driver allegedly hit 109 mph off the Manhattan Bridge and slammed into Bowery and Canal. Two people died: Kevin Cruickshank, 55, and May Kwok, 63. The city promised barriers, lower limits, and narrower lanes. “We are taking immediate steps to fortify this intersection,” said the transportation commissioner. Advocates warned the rest of Canal stays dangerous. “The vast majority of the corridor will remain deadly,” said one. Seven people have died on Canal since 2011. NY1 | Gothamist

      Hot blocks, cold comfort

      The pain clusters. West Street. Broadway. Avenue of the Americas. West Houston. Church Street. Each logged serious injuries. Data

      Doorings cut riders down. Taxis tangle with bikes on Broadway and Mercer. One cyclist, 31, suffered severe leg cuts at Mercer and West Houston. Data

      A parked car can be a weapon. A sedan door on Broadway. A taxi at Broadway. Records stack up. Data

      The clock never stops

      Crashes rise at noon and again into the night. Noon saw 80 injuries. Six in the evening saw 60. After dark, the count stays high through midnight. Data

      The causes read like a checklist. “Inattention/Distraction.” “Disregarded traffic control.” “Failure to yield.” “Unsafe speed.” Vulnerable road users take most of the damage. Data

      City promises, slow fixes

      After the Chinatown killings, officials promised near‑term work and a full redesign of Canal. “We are taking immediate steps,” the commissioner said. But as one advocate put it, “Canal Street is only as safe as its most dangerous block.”

      Fourteen Street is slated for a redesign next year with a focus on people on foot. The money is split between the city and two BIDs. NY1

      What would make Precinct 1 safer now?

      • Daylight the corners and harden turns on West Street and Broadway. Keep sightlines clear. Protect the crosswalk first. Data
      • Build protected bike lanes and loading zones on Broadway and Church to stop doorings and swerve‑outs. Data
      • Target late‑night speeding with 24/7 enforcement at West Street and Canal. The midnight–6 a.m. hours keep hurting people. Data

      Citywide levers we need pulled

      Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can lower speeds. It hasn’t done it citywide. A 20 mph default would save lives. Join the push. /take_action/

      A small share of drivers causes outsized harm. Bills in Albany would force repeat speeders to use speed‑limiters. The case is strong: drivers with heavy camera records are far more likely to kill or maim. Streetsblog

      The toll

      In the last 12 months, this precinct recorded 808 crashes. 331 injuries. Four serious. No deaths. This year’s crashes are up 10% over last year to date. Serious injuries are down. The bodies still fall. Data

      Two people died at Canal this summer. Their names are in the record. The street is still the same width today.

      Act. Push leaders. Slow the cars. Protect the people. Take action.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where are people getting hurt most in Precinct 1?
      West Street has 83 injuries, the most. Broadway has 43. Avenue of the Americas, West Houston Street, and Church Street also show high injury counts. NYC Open Data
      ▸ When are crashes peaking?
      Injuries spike at noon (80) and again in the evening (60 at 6 p.m.). Late night stays high through midnight (40 at 12 a.m.). NYC Open Data
      ▸ What’s driving the harm?
      The records cite inattention/distraction, disregarded traffic control, failure to yield, and unsafe speed as recurring factors. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What did the city promise after the Canal Street deaths?
      Barriers, lower speed limits, and narrowed lanes at Bowery and Canal, plus a longer‑term redesign. “We are taking immediate steps,” the DOT commissioner said. Gothamist | NY1
