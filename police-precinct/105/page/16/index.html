      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Precinct 105: Four weeks, four people on foot or bike hit

      Precinct 105: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 18, 2025

      A man on a bike met an SUV at Braddock Avenue and Ransom Street on Sep 9. Police records say he was going straight when the driver turned right and hit him (NYC Open Data).

      This Week

      • On Sep 7, a driver going straight injured two men walking near 237 Street; a parked Jeep was also involved (NYC Open Data).
      • On Sep 6, at 74 Avenue and 260 Street, a driver turning right hit a 19-year-old on a bike who was going straight (NYC Open Data).
      • On Sep 2, at 223 Street and 114 Avenue, a driver going straight injured a 7-year-old girl crossing at the intersection (NYC Open Data).

      The toll does not let up

      Since Jan 1, 2022, Precinct 105 has recorded 4,998 crashes, causing 3,039 injuries and 15 deaths (NYC Open Data). Six of the dead were people walking. None were people on bikes. Eight were vehicle occupants. One was another motorized user (same source).

      This year, crashes are up 17.1% versus last year to 1,014. Injuries are up 23.7% to 688. Deaths stand at 4, the same as last year by this point (NYC Open Data).

      Where it keeps happening

      Police list the Cross Island Parkway as the worst corridor here: 5 deaths and 618 injuries. Jamaica Avenue shows 1 death and 78 injuries (same source).

      Injuries stack up in the late day and night. About 205 injuries hit around 5 PM, and another 205 around 9 PM (same source).

      What police record drivers doing

      Injury cases here include police entries for inattention/distraction and failure to yield. Those two alone account for 23 and 22 injuries, respectively (NYC Open Data).

      Fix the turns. Slow the corridors.

      Daylight every corner on Jamaica Avenue and along the Cross Island service roads. Add hardened right turns and leading pedestrian intervals at signalized intersections near schools and bus stops. Target evening enforcement for failure to yield where injuries peak. These are basic steps, drawn from what the crash log shows here.

      Citywide, two moves would cut speed and harm. Lower the default speed limit. And require speed limiters for the worst repeat offenders. As the State Senate has noted, “Speed cameras have cut speeding by over 60% in locations where installed” (NYS Senate). The tools exist; the will is the question.

      Who’s responsible to act

      Your local officials here are Council Member Linda Lee, Assembly Member Ed Braunstein, and State Senator Toby Stavisky. Our record shows no sponsorship status here for the speed‑limiter bill named in our guide (S4045C/A2299C). Will they back it? See what the bill does and how to press them here.

      The man on the bike at Braddock and Ransom is counted now. So are the men walking on 237 Street. So is the child at 114 Avenue. Count them, then make the call. Start here.

      NYPD Precinct 105 in Queens. It includes Glen Oaks–Floral Park–New Hyde Park, Bellerose, Queens Village, and Cambria Heights, and overlaps Queens CB13.
      ▸ How many people have been killed in Precinct 105 since 2022?
      Fifteen people. Six were people walking, eight were vehicle occupants, and one was another motorized user, based on NYC Open Data from Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 18, 2025.
      ▸ When do injuries spike here?
      Late day and night. Around 5 PM and 9 PM each show about 205 injuries in the dataset window.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95; Persons f55k-p6yu; Vehicles bm4k-52h4). We filtered for crashes within NYPD Precinct 105 between 2022-01-01 and 2025-09-18, and tallied injuries, deaths, times of day, contributing factors, and locations. You can view the datasets here and reproduce the counts by applying the same date and precinct filters.
