      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 104?

      Blood on the Boulevard: No More Excuses, No More Deaths

      Written July 12, 2025

      Precinct 104: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      The Toll in Plain Sight

      Five dead. Eleven left with serious injuries. In the past year alone, 729 people have been hurt in 1,233 crashes across Precinct 104. The numbers do not bleed, but the streets do. A cyclist crushed by a turning truck on Juniper Boulevard North. A motorcyclist thrown and killed on the Jackie Robinson Parkway. A man on a bike, dead on Maurice Avenue, his body broken by a box truck. These are not numbers. These are lives ended, families gutted.

      Recent Crashes: No End in Sight

      The carnage does not pause. On April 19, a 54-year-old cyclist was killed by a right-turning box truck. The driver disregarded traffic control, leaving the man with crush injuries, dead at the scene. On June 26, a 39-year-old motorcyclist was ejected and killed on the parkway. The record shows the same pattern: trucks, SUVs, motorcycles, bikes. The street is a gauntlet. SUVs and cars alone have killed at least one and injured 326 pedestrians here since 2022.

      What the People See

      “I was all the way in the back and all of a sudden the bus hit the curb, I guess, jumped the curb, I went this way and that way and banged into the side of the bus,” said Ken Baur, after a Q20 bus mounted the sidewalk and injured eight. Another rider, Samantha Hart, said, “I have a baby with me. That would be scary. I’ll be more cautious of my surroundings.”

      Leadership: Promises and Pressure

      The city has new powers. Sammy’s Law lets New York set its own speed limits. The DOT has started to lower speeds near schools and in slow zones. But the default speed on most streets remains deadly. The police in Precinct 104 have the tools: they can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target crash hotspots. They just need to use them. Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand action. Lower the speed limit. Enforce the law. Protect the people who walk, bike, and wait for the bus. The blood on the street is not an act of God. It is a choice. Make them choose life.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 104 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 32, assembly district AD 28 and state senate district SD 12.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 104?
      It includes the Maspeth, Ridgewood, Glendale, Middle Village, Mount Olivet & All Faiths Cemeteries, Middle Village Cemetery, St. John Cemetery, Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (North), and Queens CB5 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 30, District 32, and District 34, Assembly Districts AD 28, AD 30, AD 37, AD 38, and AD 39, and State Senate Districts SD 12, SD 15, and SD 18.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 104?
      Cars and Trucks: 1 death, 328 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 1 death, 13 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 9 injuries. (2022–2025, NYC Open Data)
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 104 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target known crash hotspots and respond to dangerous conditions. The tools exist. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better enforcement, and safer street design can save lives. Every crash is a policy choice.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and demand data-driven enforcement. They can push for policies that protect people, not just cars.
