      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 44?

      Red Lights, Dead Kids—How Many More Will You Let Die?

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 23, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 44: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Toll on the Streets

      A boy on a scooter, 15 years old, struck down at Jerome and E. 167th. The motorcyclist ran the red, hit him, and fled. The boy was left in the road, fighting for his life. Police said, “A 15-year-old boy riding his scooter was critically hurt when a Bronx motorcyclist blew through a red light, slammed into him and sped away” (NY Daily News).

      In the last twelve months, Precinct 44 has seen 528 injuries, 1 serious injury, and 1 death. Children are not spared—58 injured, none killed, but luck is not a strategy. The numbers do not stop. They do not rest.

      The Pattern: Speed, Impact, Silence

      A 75-year-old woman, crossing with the signal at Grand Concourse and E. 164th, was killed by a sedan. The driver was unlicensed. The cause: “Unsafe Speed” and “Traffic Control Disregarded” (NYC Open Data).

      Kelvin Mitchell, father and neighbor, was killed crossing Webster Ave. The driver did not stop. His sister described him: “Great father, great person—everybody in the community loved him… Everybody is missing Kelvin. It’s not gonna be the same over here without him.”

      Leadership: Words, Laws, and the Waiting

      The city has tools. Speed cameras. Lower speed limits. Enforcement. But the pace is slow. The police can crack down on speeding, reckless driving, and failure to yield. They can target the corners where blood stains the crosswalk. They can act, or they can wait for the next call.

      What Now: No More Waiting

      Every day without action is another day of risk. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Do not wait for another child to bleed in the road.

      Take action now.
