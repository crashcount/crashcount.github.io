      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 76?

      BQE, Atlantic exit: a cop, a truck, and a fire

      Written August 31, 2025 (Revised August 31, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      Precinct 76: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 31, 2025

      Just before 9 AM on Aug 27, 2025, a motorcyclist hit a box truck near the Atlantic Ave exit on the BQE. “The driver… did not stay at the scene,” ABC7 reported. The bike burned. He died at the hospital. Police later arrested a truck driver and charged him in the hit-and-run, ABC7 wrote.

      He was one of 5 people killed on streets in the 76th Precinct since 2022-01-01, with 12 seriously hurt and 840 injured in 2,011 crashes, according to NYC Open Data.

      The pattern doesn’t blink

      In the last 12 months, this precinct had 678 crashes, 2 deaths, and 312 injuries (NYC Open Data). Year to date through Aug 31, crashes rose from 323 to 455 and injuries from 145 to 222 compared with last year — a 40.9% and 53.1% jump, respectively (NYC Open Data).

      Injuries stack up after dark. From midnight to 2 AM alone, there were 60 injuries in this period (NYC Open Data).

      Where it breaks, again and again

      The worst pain sits on the BQE and spills at Atlantic Ave — a top hotspot by injuries and deaths in this precinct (NYC Open Data). Court St and Columbia St also rack up harm (NYC Open Data).

      Heavy vehicles hit hard here. Trucks are tied to pedestrian deaths in this data slice, while SUVs and sedans cause most of the pedestrian injuries (NYC Open Data). In one fatal case at Butler and Bond on Jun 26, 2024, a dump truck turned right and killed an 83-year-old woman crossing outside a crosswalk, the city’s records show (Open Data crash record).

      Named driver errors run through the files: failure to yield and distraction appear again and again among injury crashes in this precinct (NYC Open Data). Speed also kills here. An 18-year-old passenger died at Atlantic and Court just after 3 AM on Aug 18, 2023; police flagged unsafe speed in that crash record (Open Data crash record).

      The precinct’s job — and City Hall’s

      Precinct 76 knows the corners. Enforce failure-to-yield at Atlantic and Court. Watch truck turns on Columbia and Bond. Focus on overnight hours when injuries surge. The data points to the targets (NYC Open Data).

      But this is larger than one command. Citywide speed policy still decides who lives. We have tools on the table: New York can lower the default speed limit on local streets, and Albany can force repeat speeders to slow their cars. Both steps are laid out with contacts and scripts in our Take Action page.

      The repeat-offender bill would require intelligent speed assistance for drivers who rack up violations, aimed at the small group who do outsized harm. Survivors and advocates have already done the hard work to get it written; the call now is to pass it. Details and call info are at Take Action.

      Your local officials here are Council Member Alexa Avilés, Assembly Member Marcela Mitaynes, and State Senator Andrew Gounardes. The record provided to us does not list their sponsorship positions on the repeat-speeder bill. Ask them, directly, to back it and to push a lower city speed limit. The steps and numbers are at Take Action.

      Back at the Atlantic exit

      “The driver of the box truck never stopped,” the Daily News wrote of the Aug 27 crash on the BQE by Atlantic Ave (Daily News). The flames went up. The road reopened. The count kept climbing.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What area does this cover?
      This report covers NYPD Precinct 76 in Brooklyn, which includes parts of Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Gowanus, and Red Hook.
      ▸ How many people were killed in Precinct 76 during the period?
      Five people were killed between Jan 1, 2022 and Aug 31, 2025, with 12 seriously injured and 840 injured in 2,011 crashes, per NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Where are the worst trouble spots?
      BROOKLYN QUEENS EXPRESSWAY near the Atlantic Ave exit is the top hotspot by injuries and deaths. Court St and Columbia St also see high crash counts, according to NYC Open Data.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets: Crashes (h9gi-nx95), Persons (f55k-p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k-52h4). We filtered records to Precinct 76 and the period 2022-01-01 to 2025-08-31, and counted crashes, deaths, serious injuries, and injuries. Data was accessed Aug 31, 2025. You can start from the Crashes dataset here and apply the same filters.
      ▸ What can the precinct do now?
      Target failure-to-yield and distraction at Atlantic Ave and Court St, enforce safer truck turns on Columbia and Bond, and focus overnight when injuries spike. These priorities are drawn from the crash records for Precinct 76.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
