      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 45?

      Eight Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Precinct 45 Bleeds While Leaders Stall

      Written July 13, 2025

      Precinct 45: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 13, 2025

      The Slow March of Harm

      In Precinct 45, the numbers do not lie. Eight dead. Twenty-nine left with serious injuries. Nearly 1,800 people hurt since 2022. The violence comes in waves—cars, SUVs, trucks, motorcycles. The streets do not forgive mistakes. The bodies are young and old. Children, elders, workers, neighbors. No one is spared.

      Just days ago, a 71-year-old woman died in Co-op City. A 79-year-old man behind the wheel slammed into two cars, then a pole. Six others were hurt. “I saw one lady was out on the ground. They was giving her medical attention, checking her body. She was laid out,” said Samuel Cherry. The street was full of sirens and broken glass. The pain lingers.

      Last week, a truck overturned and poured liquid asphalt across a Bronx road. No one died, but the street became a hazard. “Liquid asphalt poured onto a road in the Bronx after a truck overturned Wednesday,” reported CBS New York. The cleanup took hours. The risk was real.

      Who Pays the Price?

      Cars and SUVs do the most harm. Since 2022, they have killed two, seriously hurt seven, and left nearly 200 people injured. Trucks and buses have killed one and hurt many more. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes add to the toll. The victims are not just numbers. They are people who crossed the street, waited for a bus, or rode a bike to work.

      Leadership: Promises and Power

      The city has new tools. Speed limits can drop. Cameras can catch the reckless. But change is slow. The police in Precinct 45 hold the power to enforce the law. They can ticket speeders, crack down on failure to yield, and target the corners where blood has been spilled. They know where the danger lies. They just need to act.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by those in power to act—or not. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand enforcement. Demand safer streets. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 45 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 12, assembly district AD 82 and state senate district SD 34.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 45?
      It includes the Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Co-Op City, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Pelham Bay Park, Bronx CB10, and Bronx CB28 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 12 and District 13, Assembly District AD 82, and State Senate Districts SD 34 and SD 36.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 45?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 7 serious injuries, 197 total injuries. Trucks and Buses: 1 death, 1 serious injury, 19 total injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 8 total injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 3 total injuries. Cars and SUVs are the main cause of harm.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 45 police can enforce speed limits, issue tickets for reckless driving and failure to yield, and target known crash hotspots. They can respond to dangerous conditions and use data to focus on the worst locations. The precinct has the tools. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. The same streets see the same harm, year after year. Speeding, failure to yield, and poor design can be fixed. Every crash is a chance to act.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      Local politicians can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and demand enforcement against reckless drivers. They can push for more cameras, more daylighting, and more protection for people walking and biking.
