      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 45?

      No More Bodies in the Road: Hold 45th Precinct Accountable

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Precinct 45: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      In Precinct 45, the numbers do not lie. Eight people killed. Twenty-nine left with serious injuries. Since 2022, nearly 1,800 neighbors have been hurt in crashes. Each number is a body on the asphalt, a family changed forever.

      Just days ago, a 71-year-old woman died after a 79-year-old driver crashed into two cars and a light pole in Co-op City. “I saw one lady was out on the ground. They was giving her medical attention, checking her body. She was laid out,” said a witness. Seven others were injured. There were no arrests. The cause is still under investigation. Police said only that all drivers remained at the scene.

      This is not an isolated event. In the last twelve months alone, three people have died and over 600 have been injured in this precinct. Children, elders, cyclists, pedestrians—no one is spared.

      Who Bears the Risk

      Cars and SUVs do the most harm. Of the deaths and injuries to pedestrians, SUVs and sedans are the main weapons. Trucks and buses kill too. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes cause a fraction of the pain, but the carnage comes on four wheels. The streets are not safe for the old or the young. “It was a terrible sound – it was a terrible incident that happened,” said another neighbor.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      The city has tools. Speed cameras now run 24/7. The law allows lower speed limits. But in Precinct 45, the blood keeps flowing. Police can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target crash hotspots. They can make a difference. But they must act. Local leaders must demand more. Every delay is another body in the road.

      Call to Action

      Demand more from your leaders and your precinct. Call Council Member Kevin C. Riley, Assembly Member Michael Benedetto, and State Senator Nathalia Fernández. Tell them the deaths must stop. Tell the 45th Precinct to enforce the law, target speeders, and protect the most vulnerable. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 45 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 12, assembly district AD 82 and state senate district SD 34.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 45?
      It includes the Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Co-Op City, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Pelham Bay Park, Bronx CB10, and Bronx CB28 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 12 and District 13, Assembly District AD 82, and State Senate Districts SD 34 and SD 36.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 45?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 7 serious injuries, 197 total pedestrian injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 8 total injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 3 total injuries. The vast majority of deaths and serious injuries come from cars, SUVs, trucks, and buses.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 45 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target known crash hotspots and respond to dangerous street conditions. Police have the tools—they just need to use them.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable, or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and strong enforcement save lives. Every crash is a chance to act before the next one.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can push for lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, demand better enforcement, and pass laws that protect people walking and biking. They can hold city agencies and the precinct accountable for results.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
