      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 111?

      Precinct 111: Highways, Hard Turns, and the Bodies We Count

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 111: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another driver. Same ending.

      • A 74-year-old man died at Northern Blvd and 217th Street. An SUV going straight hit him in the intersection at night. The record lists him as a pedestrian. He did not survive. The SUV driver was unlicensed. This is in the city’s crash file, case 4819696.

      • A 55-year-old woman on an e‑bike was killed at Hollis Court Blvd and 50th Avenue. She was going straight. An SUV turned left. The city logged it as fatal, case 4832001.

      • A 31-year-old man died on the Cross Island Parkway, listed as a pedestrian. Multiple vehicles in the northbound lanes. Fatal, case 4763849.

      • A 56-year-old man died on the Clearview Expressway. The database lists him as a pedestrian. A chain crash. Fatal, case 4781815.

      Highways carve through this precinct. So do deaths.

      Wrong way, five hits

      On Jan. 18, 2023, a man drove north in the southbound lanes of the Clearview Expressway, striking five cars and badly hurting two motorists. In court, he said he entered “in the wrong direction because I wanted to hurt people.” The Queens DA said he “terrorized other drivers.” A judge gave him eight years. amNY reported the plea and the sentence. The quotes are theirs: “terrorized other drivers” and “in the wrong direction because I wanted to hurt people”.

      The death count

      • Since 2022, this precinct recorded 11 deaths and 2,281 injuries across 4,139 crashes. That is the city database for 2022‑01‑01 to 2025‑08‑24.
      • People outside the car carry the weight: pedestrians, 5 dead and 211 hurt; cyclists, 1 dead and 66 hurt. Occupants tally 5 dead and 1,984 hurt. Source: precinct rollup.
      • SUVs and cars dominate pedestrian harm here: 97 pedestrian casualties from SUVs (including 3 deaths) and 86 from sedans (1 death) in the period. Source: causes of pedestrian injuries.

      When and where it hits

      • The map lights up the highways: Long Island Expressway, Cross Island Parkway, Clearview Expressway. They top the list for injuries and deaths in this precinct’s own data. The worst sites are labeled by the city as top locations.
      • The hours stack up: injuries spike at 8 a.m., 3 p.m., 5–8 p.m., and 10 p.m. Fatal crashes touch both night and morning. Source: hourly distribution.

      Three corners. One fix.

      • Northern Blvd and 217th Street: a pedestrian killed by an eastbound SUV going straight. Case 4819696. Harden the turn. Daylight the corners. Give pedestrians a head start.
      • Hollis Court Blvd at 50th Avenue: left‑turning SUV against a cyclist going straight. Case 4832001. Install hardened left‑turn treatments. Protect the bike movement through the junction.
      • Cross Island Parkway: repeated deaths in the corridor. Limit access points. Add barrier and refuge where on‑ and off‑ramps meet local streets. Target high‑injury hours with automated enforcement.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      • In this precinct, the data flags “other” as the lead contributing factor for deaths and injuries. Vulnerable road user error trails far behind. Speed and turns still break bodies, even when the form says “unspecified.” Source: contributing factors.
      • The worst harm clusters on the highways and big arterials. The rollup shows SUVs and cars as the chief source of pedestrian injury and death.

      Citywide levers, local lives

      • Albany renewed 24‑hour school‑zone speed cameras through 2030, locking in a tool that cuts speeding where installed. That fight is won. The next one is speed everywhere and the worst repeat offenders. See our Take Action.
      • The law now lets the city set lower speeds. A citywide 20 mph default would slow crashes like the ones that kill on Northern and Hollis Court. The call is simple. Make it the rule. See Take Action.
      • Repeat speeders do outsized harm across the city. The proposed Stop Super Speeders Act would force the worst to use speed limiters. It targets cars that rack up camera tickets and points. See Take Action.

      What we can say, and what we can count

      • This is Precinct 111. Since 2022: 11 dead. Names aren’t listed. The records show ages, body parts, impacts. “Apparent Death.” “Center Front End.”
      • One line in the file: “Ejected.” Another: “Left Front Bumper.” They read like instructions. They aren’t. They are endings.

      Action

      • Slow the default speed. Stop the super speeders. Demand it now. Start here: Take Action.
