      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 48?

      Blood on the Asphalt: Bronx Streets Demand Action Now

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised July 18, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Precinct 48: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Toll in Plain Sight

      In Precinct 48, the numbers do not lie. Ten people have died on these streets since 2022. Another 1,765 have been injured. Thirteen of those injuries were serious—lives changed in a heartbeat. The dead include pedestrians, cyclists, passengers, and riders. The living carry scars you cannot see.

      Just days ago, a 79-year-old driver crashed into two cars and a light pole. His 71-year-old passenger, Stella Nyarko-Dei, never made it home. Seven others were hurt. Police said, “The cause of the crash was not immediately known.” No arrests. No answers. Only loss.

      On July 3rd, a driver in a Ford Mustang failed a left turn at East 149th and Courtlandt. He mounted the sidewalk and struck six people. He ran. The victims went to the hospital. Police are still looking for him. “Cops are looking for a man who rammed into six people after botching a turn at an intersection in the Bronx, authorities said.”

      Who Pays the Price?

      Most of the harm comes from cars and SUVs. Sedans and SUVs alone account for over 280 pedestrian injuries and 2 deaths. Trucks and buses add more. Motorcycles and mopeds leave bodies broken. Bikes cause a handful of injuries, but the carnage comes on four wheels.

      Children are not spared. In the last year, 54 people under 18 were hurt in crashes here. The old are not safe either. Nine people over 75 were injured. The street does not care how old you are.

      Leadership: Action or Delay?

      The police have the tools. They can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target crash hotspots. They can make a difference. But the numbers show the work is not done. Local leaders have the power to demand more enforcement, safer street design, and lower speed limits. Every day of delay is another day of risk.

      The Call

      This is not fate. It is policy. Demand action. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Tell them to enforce the law, slow the cars, and protect the people who walk these streets. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 48 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 15, assembly district AD 78 and state senate district SD 32.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 48?
      It includes the West Farms, Tremont, Belmont, and Bronx CB6 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council District District 15, Assembly Districts AD 78, AD 79, AD 86, and AD 87, and State Senate Districts SD 32 and SD 33.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 48?
      Cars and Trucks: Sedans and SUVs caused 2 pedestrian deaths and 284 pedestrian injuries. Trucks and buses caused 19 pedestrian injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: Caused 19 pedestrian injuries. Bikes: Caused 6 pedestrian injuries.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 48 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield. They can respond to dangerous conditions before someone gets hurt. The tools exist. They just need to use them.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The numbers show patterns—left turns, speeding, sidewalk incursions. These are preventable with enforcement, safer street design, and lower speeds.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can demand more enforcement, push for safer street design, and lower speed limits. They can hold the precinct accountable for protecting people, not just cars.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
