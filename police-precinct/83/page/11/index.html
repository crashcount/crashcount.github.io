      Stop the Bleeding: End Bushwick’s Deadly Hit-and-Run Cycle

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Precinct 83: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In Precinct 83, the numbers do not soften. Eight people killed. Seventeen left with serious injuries. In the last year alone, five dead, nine with wounds that will not heal. Four hundred forty-nine injured.

      A man stands in a Bushwick crosswalk. A car hits him, throws him ten feet. The driver flees. Ruari Fay-Handebeaux lists the damage: “Broken ankle, broken humorous, bunch of fractures in my skull” he told ABC7. His father was killed by a driver too. The street does not change. The next person waits.

      Hayden Wallace, 29, dies in a hit-and-run. His aunt remembers: “He lived life to the fullest. He was only 29 years old and lit up every room he entered. He was loved by so many” she told NY Daily News. The man who killed him had done it before. He was out on bail. He drove again. He killed again.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and Silence

      Most injuries come from cars and trucks. Vans, sedans, SUVs, box trucks. They kill. They maim. Motorcycles and mopeds add to the toll. Bikes too, but the numbers are smaller. The streets are not safe for the old, the young, or anyone in between.

      The police have the tools. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket drivers who fail to yield. They can target the corners where blood stains the curb. But the bodies keep coming. The silence is heavy.

      Leadership: Words and Waiting

      Local leaders have the power. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can lower speed limits. Cameras can catch speeders. But every day of waiting is another risk. The law is there. The action is not. Precinct 83 can do more.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by someone with power. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand lower speed limits. Demand enforcement. Demand streets where people live, not die.

      Do not wait for the next name. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 83 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn. It overlaps Council Districts District 34 and District 37, Assembly Districts AD 53 and AD 54, and State Senate District SD 18.
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 83?
      It includes the Bushwick (West), Bushwick (East), The Evergreens Cemetery, and Brooklyn CB4 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 34 and District 37, Assembly Districts AD 53 and AD 54, and State Senate District SD 18.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 83?
      Cars and Trucks: 2 deaths, 67 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 8 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Bikes: 0 deaths, 3 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. See NYC Open Data for details.
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 83 police can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target crash hotspots and respond to dangerous street conditions. The tools exist. Enforcement saves lives.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better enforcement, and safer street design can stop deaths and injuries. Every crash is a policy failure, not fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed camera programs, fund safer street designs, and demand police enforcement. They can act now, or answer for the next death.
