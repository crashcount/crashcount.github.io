      Brooklyn Streets, Broken Lives: Demand Action Before the Next Body Falls

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Precinct 84: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      A woman, age 76, crushed in an SUV on Furman Street. A 74-year-old man, thrown from his e-bike, dead under the wheels of a bus at Tillary and Jay. A 45-year-old passenger, chest crushed, dead on Flatbush. Four deaths in Precinct 84 since 2022. One every few months.

      In the last year alone, 334 people were hurt, four left with injuries so severe they may never walk right again. Two did not survive. Children are not spared. Ten under 18 were injured, two seriously. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie. NYC Open Data

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs strike most often. They broke bones and ended lives—49 moderate injuries, 7 serious, just in the last three years. Trucks and buses hit hard too. Bikes and motorcycles add to the toll, but the weight of steel does most of the damage.

      On Court Street, the city plans a protected bike lane. The reason is plain: “Court Street’s not great, all the way Downtown Brooklyn is just double parked cars. So it’s you feel like you’re taking your life in your hands a little bit. My son’s school is actually on Court, so I can’t avoid it fully,” said Ryan Greet. The city’s own numbers show 165 crashes, one death, 15 cyclists and 23 pedestrians hurt on that mile of street in two years. Most pedestrians were hit while crossing with the light.

      The Leaders and the Law

      Local leaders talk of action. After a mother and her two daughters were killed by a driver with a suspended license and a record of violations, State Senator Andrew Gounardes said, “It’s no longer simply enough to shake our heads in despair when these preventable tragedies occur—it’s time for us to act.”

      The city has the power to lower speed limits. It has the power to install speed cameras. It has the power to enforce the law. The police in Precinct 84 can ticket speeders, crack down on reckless drivers, and target the corners where people die. They just need to act.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call the precinct. Tell them you want slower streets, more enforcement, and real protection for people who walk and bike. Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Precinct 84 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn. It overlaps Council Districts District 33 and District 35, Assembly District AD 52, and State Senate District SD 26. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in Precinct 84?
      It includes the Brooklyn Heights and Downtown Brooklyn-Dumbo-Boerum Hill neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33 and District 35, Assembly District AD 52, and State Senate District SD 26. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Precinct 84?
      Cars and SUVs: 56 incidents (49 moderate injuries, 7 serious). Motorcycles and Mopeds: 2 incidents (2 moderate injuries). Bikes: 10 incidents (9 moderate injuries, 1 serious). NYC Open Data
      ▸ What can police do to protect vulnerable road users here?
      Precinct 84 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and target known crash hotspots. They can issue tickets for speeding and failure to yield. They can respond to dangerous conditions and focus on places where people are hurt or killed. As State Senator Andrew Gounardes said, “It’s no longer simply enough to shake our heads in despair when these preventable tragedies occur—it’s time for us to act.”
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. Most deaths and injuries happen because of speeding, reckless driving, or failure to yield. These are choices, not fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, expand protected bike lanes, fund street redesigns, and push for stronger enforcement. They can support laws that keep dangerous drivers off the road and make sure the police act.
