      No More Excuses: Blood on the Streets of Precinct 109

      Written July 9, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 109: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 9, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie: Death and Injury in Precinct 109

      In the past twelve months, two people died and 987 were injured in crashes across Precinct 109. Thirty-eight suffered serious injuries. The dead are not numbers. They are elders, children, neighbors. In three and a half years, 22 people have been killed and over 3,000 injured on these streets. NYC Open Data

      The violence is relentless. In March, a 78-year-old woman was struck and killed by an SUV on Parsons Boulevard. She was not at an intersection. She did not stand a chance. NYC Open Data In June, a 63-year-old woman riding her bike was killed by a left-turning SUV on Bowne Street. The crash report lists only two words: “Driver Inattention.” NYC Open Data

      The Human Cost: Voices from the Wreckage

      After a crane slammed into a city bus in Queens Village, passenger Emily McKinnon said, “I was holding on for my life back there.” She ran to help others, found two of them crying, shaken. “Even now I’m scared to go on the bus again, but I have to take the bus to get to work.”

      On the Belt Parkway, a BMW lost control, vaulted the divider, and hit two cars. The NYPD said, “their out-of-control luxury car vaulted into oncoming traffic… slamming into two unsuspecting motorists in a fiery crash.” Two died. Others survived, but not unchanged. BMW Crash Kills Two on Belt Parkway

      What Leaders Have Done—and What They Haven’t

      City and state leaders have taken steps. The city now has the power to lower speed limits. Speed cameras have been renewed. But the pace is slow. Precinct 109 still sees death and injury, week after week.

      The police have the tools. They can enforce speed limits, ticket reckless drivers, and target crash hotspots. They can make it clear: no more excuses, no more waiting.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand action. Every day without change is another day someone does not come home. The toll is not fate. It is a choice. Make them choose life.
