      Blood on the Boulevard: East New York’s Streets Are Killing Us

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      Precinct 75: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Three people killed. Seventeen left with serious injuries. In the past year alone, 1,405 people were hurt in 2,246 crashes across Precinct 75. The dead do not speak. The wounded carry scars. Most were walking, riding, or just trying to cross the street. The youngest victim was a child. The oldest, someone’s grandparent. The violence does not choose.

      The Streets Are Not Safe

      A 55-year-old man tried to cross Fulton Street in Cypress Hills. A burgundy Ford Explorer struck him. The driver did not stop. The man died at the hospital. The car kept going. Police are still looking for the driver. The vehicle did not remain on the scene.

      At Blake and Pennsylvania, a woman was crossing with the signal. A Nissan Pathfinder turned left and hit her. She died later that day. The driver was charged months later with failure to yield and failure to exercise due care. She was arrested Wednesday following a police investigation, and was charged with failure to yield to a pedestrian, and failure to exercise due care, cops said.

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. In three years, cars and trucks caused six deaths and nine serious injuries to pedestrians. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes left more bodies broken.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city promises action. The state passed Sammy’s Law, giving New York City the power to lower speed limits. But the streets of East New York, Cypress Hills, and Spring Creek still run fast and wide. The police have the tools. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket reckless drivers. They can target the corners where blood stains the curb. They just need to act.

      DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez says, Brooklyn and Queens Residents deserve a Conduit Boulevard that improves daily life, not a roadway that puts pedestrians and drivers at risk and physically divides entire neighborhoods. The Conduit will be redesigned. But for too many, it comes too late.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call the 75th Precinct. Demand lower speed limits. Demand enforcement. Demand safe crossings. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Take action now.

      Precinct 75 can enforce speed limits, crack down on reckless driving, and issue failure-to-yield tickets. They can target crash hotspots and respond to dangerous conditions. The police have the tools. They just need to act.
