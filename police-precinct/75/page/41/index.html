      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 75?

      Precinct 75: Death on Pennsylvania, metal on the Belt

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 75: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another corridor. Same ending.

      • Since 2022, Precinct 75 has seen 15 people killed and 5,374 injured in 8,926 crashes. Pedestrians account for 8 deaths and 793 injuries; cyclists, 1 death and 246 injuries. SUVs and cars drive most of the harm to people on foot, with 713 pedestrian strikes tallied by vehicle class. These figures come straight from city crash data.
      • The bodies fall at known places: the Belt Parkway and Pennsylvania Avenue top the list for injuries and deaths.

      “Criminal charges for him were still pending.”

      • Police said that after a car hit a moped in Brooklyn, killing the rider, the driver was in custody and charges were pending, reported Gothamist. The story was one of three crashes citywide that morning, including a Belt Parkway rollover.
      • In another Brooklyn case, a 47‑year‑old man was struck and dragged more than 50 feet. The driver fled. “Police said they were seeking information about the car or its driver,” reported Gothamist. The Daily News put it plainer: “A driver struck and killed a 47‑year‑old pedestrian… then left the scene,” said police.

      Belt Parkway at 4 a.m.

      • February 16, 2025. A 41‑year‑old in a BMW SUV was ejected and killed on the Belt Parkway. The data lists unsafe speed and a slippery roadway.
      • The late hours bite hard here. Deaths spike after dark: 18:00 (3 deaths), 20:00 (1), 21:00 (2), 23:00 (1). Dawn and early morning also hurt: 04:00 through 06:00 saw steady fatalities. The hourly toll comes from the city dataset.

      Crossing and never getting home

      • July 3, 2025. A 36‑year‑old man was hit and killed at Broadway and E New York Ave. The record says “Crossing, No Signal, or Crosswalk.” The vehicle was a sedan going straight. Source: city crash record.
      • July 13, 2025. A 21‑year‑old pedestrian was killed near 395 Gateway Dr. Driver inattention and unsafe speed are listed. Source: city crash record.

      Three corners. One fix.

      • The worst repeat sites are Belt Parkway and Pennsylvania Avenue. Linden Boulevard also racks up hundreds of injuries. Source: top intersections.
      • The data names the killers: “other” factors dominate the filings, then vulnerable road user error. But among coded causes we can see, failure to yield, aggressive driving, inattention, and unsafe speed recur. Source: contributing factors.
      • Local steps are not mysteries: harden turns on Pennsylvania. Add daylighting and LPIs along Linden. Slow the ramps and weave zones feeding the Belt. Target speeding and failure‑to‑yield at the hours when blood runs here.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      • Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can set safer speeds. We need a 20 mph default on local streets. Read how and push leaders to act in our Take Action guide.
      • The worst drivers do the worst harm. The Stop Super Speeders Act would require speed limiters for the repeat offenders who stack tickets. It’s in our call to action.

      This is East New York, Cypress Hills, Starrett City. It is also the Belt, and Pennsylvania, and a crosswalk a man never cleared. The map is public. The hours are named. The fixes are known.
