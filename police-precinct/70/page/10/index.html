      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 70?

      Precinct 70: Flatbush streets, nine dead on foot, two on bikes

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 70: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Flatbush does not forgive

      • Since 2022, Precinct 70 has counted nine pedestrians and two cyclists killed, with 610 people on foot and 294 people on bikes injured. SUVs and cars did most of the harm. Trucks and buses killed three pedestrians. The hours from 2 p.m. to 6 p.m. stack the bodies and the broken bones. City data shows it.

      • One cyclist, 47, died on Avenue O in March 2024. Police records say he was ejected and suffered head trauma. A Kia SUV was going the other way. He died at the scene. The crash record is here.

      • A woman, 58, crossing with the signal at Tilden and Bedford in March 2024. A dump truck turned right. “Failure to Yield Right-of-Way,” the report says. She did not make it home. The record is here.

      Where the street bites hardest

      • Flatbush Avenue leads the toll: 164 injured and one dead. Ocean Avenue is close behind with 126 hurt. Coney Island Avenue repeats, block after block. These are not one-offs. They are corridors. See the top locations.

      • The day’s heat cuts deepest. Injuries and deaths peak from school let-out through the commute. At 2 p.m., 174 injuries and three deaths. At 5 p.m., 191 injuries and two deaths. At 6 p.m., 147 injuries and three deaths. The clock tells the story. The hourly breakdown is public.

      • Trucks and buses are a small share of crashes here but a large share of death. In this precinct, they account for three pedestrian deaths. The bodies are soft. The vehicles are not. The mode rollup shows it.

      Names we do not forget

      • A 73-year-old woman, crossing with the signal at Avenue L and East 12th in December 2024. A Jeep made a right. Center front end. She died there. The record is here.

      • A 64-year-old man in the roadway at Flatbush and Caton on New Year’s Eve 2024. An Audi sedan struck him. Police marked him dead. The record is here.

      • In August 2023 on Ocean Avenue, a Toyota SUV was parked. A man, 58, listed as a driver/occupant, is recorded dead in the collision. The file is spare. It is enough. The record is here.

      What the data screams

      • “Failure to Yield Right-of-Way.” “Inattention/Distraction.” “Other.” These are the boxes checked. Fifteen deaths fall under “other,” three under vulnerable user error. The labels feel thin next to the loss. The contributing factors are listed here.

      • SUVs and cars injured 220 pedestrians and killed six. Trucks and buses killed three pedestrians. This is not a mystery. It is mass plus speed. The vehicle tallies are here.

      Fix the corners. Slow the turns

      • These streets have patterns. Right turns kill at signals. Tighten the turns. Add hardened corners. Give walkers a head start with leading intervals. Daylight the crosswalks so drivers can see the person, not the hood. Target Flatbush Avenue, Ocean Avenue, and Coney Island Avenue.

      • Afternoon and rush hours bleed. Aim enforcement and signal timing then. Keep trucks out of turning conflicts at the peaks. This is not theory. It is where the harm clusters. City crash data backs the timing.

      Citywide moves that save lives

      • Lower the default speed. A person hit at lower speed lives more often. Albany gave the city the tool. The city must use it. Read our call to action and ask your council member to move now. Learn how to push it.

      • Stop repeat speeders with intelligent speed assistance for the worst offenders. The state bill is written. The risk it targets is real. Back the push here.

      Two blocks. Two minutes. A life

      • The records are dry. The sidewalk is not. The toll in Precinct 70 is written in blood and numbers. The hours keep coming. The fixes are known. The delay is a choice.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where are the worst danger zones in Precinct 70?
      Flatbush Avenue led with 164 injuries and one death, followed by Ocean Avenue with 126 injuries. Coney Island Avenue appears repeatedly. These come from NYC Open Data’s top locations.
      ▸ When do crashes peak here?
      From 2 p.m. through the evening commute. At 2 p.m. there were 174 injuries and three deaths; at 5 p.m., 191 injuries and two deaths; at 6 p.m., 147 injuries and three deaths.
      ▸ What vehicles hurt pedestrians most in this precinct?
      SUVs and cars caused most pedestrian injuries and six pedestrian deaths. Trucks and buses killed three pedestrians, despite fewer crashes.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ What can the city change now?
      Tighten and harden turns, add daylighting and leading pedestrian intervals on Flatbush, Ocean, and Coney Island Aves, and aim enforcement and signal timing at the afternoon peak. Citywide, lower the default speed and require speed limiters for repeat offenders.
