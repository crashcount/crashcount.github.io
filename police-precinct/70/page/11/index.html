      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 70?

      Bodies in the Road, Blood on Their Hands

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Precinct 70: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      In Precinct 70, the numbers do not lie. Twelve people killed. Twenty-seven left with serious injuries. Two thousand three hundred forty-five hurt. Three thousand three hundred sixty-one crashes since 2022. Each number is a body, a family, a life split open on the street.

      The dead are old and young. A 73-year-old woman crushed by a turning SUV at Avenue L and East 12th. A 47-year-old cyclist thrown from his bike by a Kia SUV on Avenue O. A 58-year-old woman struck in the crosswalk by a dump truck that failed to yield. The list runs on. The pain does not end.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the harm. Five dead, eleven seriously hurt, four hundred eighty-eight more injured by these machines. Trucks and buses killed three, left three with grave wounds. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes add to the count. The pattern is clear. The weight and speed of steel decide who walks home and who does not.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      The police in Precinct 70 have the tools. They can ticket speeders, stop reckless turns, target the corners where blood pools most often. But the numbers show the work is not done. The city has started to lower speed limits and redesign streets, but the pace is slow. Every day of delay means another family waits for a knock at the door.

      The Words That Remain

      A sister-in-law spoke after a crash: “It’s devastating. It’s affecting everyone in our family, especially (Ruiz’s) mom. Maddy was her only daughter.”

      A witness to another death said, “I have no idea why he was doing donuts in the parking lot.”

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the precinct. Demand speed enforcement, failure-to-yield crackdowns, and real protection for people who walk and bike. Do not wait for another name to join the list.
