      Four Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Who’s Stopping the Killing on Staten Island Streets?

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 6, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations.)

      Precinct 122: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      Four people are dead in Precinct 122 in the past year. Two were over 75. One was 64. One was 57. They were walking. They did not make it home. In the last twelve months, 536 people were injured in crashes here. Five were left with serious injuries.

      The numbers do not bleed, but the people do. On Hylan Boulevard, an 80-year-old man was struck crossing the street. He died a block from his home. The driver stayed. No charges. Police said they were “still investigating how fast the woman was driving and what the traffic signals at the intersection were displaying at the time” (Gothamist).

      A 64-year-old woman was killed by an SUV turning left. A 57-year-old woman was killed by a pickup truck, also turning left. A 61-year-old man was killed by an SUV. The pattern is plain. The dead are old. The vehicles are heavy. The drivers turn and do not see.

      Leadership: Action or Excuse?

      The city has tools. Speed cameras. Lower speed limits. Enforcement. But tools unused are nothing. Speed cameras cut speeding by 63% where installed. Injuries drop 14%. But the law authorizing these cameras is again facing expiration. Albany must reauthorize it—or cameras go dark, and streets go dangerous. “Speed cameras save lives. I’m urging you to support immediate reauthorization of the NYC speed camera program. Protect our communities. Prevent more needless deaths” (Take Action).

      Local police can do more. They can enforce speed limits. They can ticket drivers who fail to yield. They can target the corners where people die. They just need to know it matters.

      The Work Left Undone

      The deaths keep coming. The injuries do not stop. The city passed Sammy’s Law. It can lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not done so. The police can crack down on reckless driving. They have not done enough.

      Every day of delay is another family broken.

      Call to Action

      Call your Council Member. Call the Mayor. Call the 122nd Precinct. Demand lower speed limits. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand enforcement at the corners where people die.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Act now. Take Action.
