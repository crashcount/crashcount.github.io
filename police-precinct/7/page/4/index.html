      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Precinct 7?

      No More Excuses: End the Killing on Our Streets

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised July 1, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      Precinct 7: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In Precinct 7, the numbers do not lie. Nine people killed. Fourteen left with serious injuries. Seven hundred sixty-seven injured since 2022. The dead are not numbers. They are the old man crossing Delancey. The child struck on Water Street. The cyclist thrown on Pitt. The pain does not fade. It spreads.

      Cars and trucks do most of the killing. Four deaths by SUV or car. Four more by truck or bus. Motorcycles, mopeds, bikes—they hurt, but the big machines do the worst. See the rollup.

      The Precinct’s Power—and Its Silence

      The police in Precinct 7 have the tools. They can ticket speeders. They can stop drivers who fail to yield. They can stand at the corners where the blood pools and say, Not here, not again. But the silence is heavy. The tickets are few. The crashes keep coming.

      Leadership: Action or Delay?

      Local leaders have the power to act. The city has new laws. Speed cameras. Lower speed limits. But change comes slow. Each delay means another family broken. Each excuse is a wound that does not heal.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This is not fate. These are not accidents. They are choices—by drivers, by leaders, by those who look away. If you want the killing to stop, do not wait. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand enforcement. Demand redesign. Demand that Precinct 7 use its power to protect the living.

      Do not let another name become a number.

      NYPD Officer Veers Into Dirt Biker’s Path
      ▸

      A police car crossed the line. Metal struck flesh. Samuel Williams, riding his dirt bike, was thrown and killed. Body cam footage shows the officer’s move. Another NYPD car tried the same. The city sent Williams’ family a bill.

      NY Daily News (2024-12-23) reports that NYPD body camera footage shows an officer veering into the path of Samuel Williams, a 36-year-old dirt bike rider, during a pursuit on the University Heights Bridge. Williams was struck, thrown, and died from his injuries. The article states, “An officer pursuing dirt bikers suddenly crossed the double yellow line into Williams' path, causing a collision.” Another NYPD vehicle attempted a similar maneuver. The case raises questions about police pursuit tactics and accountability.
