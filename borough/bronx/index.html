      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Bronx Bleeds While City Stalls: Lower the Speed, Save a Life

      Written June 26, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 26, 2025

      The Toll in Flesh and Blood

      In the Bronx, the numbers do not lie. In the last 12 months, 34 people died and 6,295 were injured on Bronx streets. Ninety-three suffered injuries so grave they may never walk the same. Children, elders, workers—none are spared. The dead do not get second chances. The wounded carry scars.

      Last week, a crash on the Hutchinson River Parkway left seven hurt, one woman fighting for her life. The police said, “The Honda Pilot then collided with the Razor Scooter, causing the 24-year-old man operating the scooter and an 18-year-old woman to be ejected from the scooter.” ABC7. She was left in the road. The driver fled. The city moved on.

      A month before, a 75-year-old man was killed crossing Wood Avenue. The driver turned left, struck him, and stayed at the scene. The neighbor described the accident as “so terrible. I’ve never seen anything like this in my life.”

      Who Bears the Weight?

      SUVs and cars do the most harm. In three years, they killed 38 pedestrians and left 70 with serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed eight. Motorcycles and mopeds, one. Bikes, none. The pattern is clear. The bigger the vehicle, the greater the damage.

      Leadership: Words, Delays, and the Clock Ticking

      Local leaders talk of Vision Zero. They tout speed cameras and lower limits. But the carnage continues. Policies stall. Promises fade. The Bronx bleeds. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not. The cameras are on, but the dead keep coming. Every day of delay is another family broken.

      What Now?

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph limit. Demand streets that protect the living, not just the drivers. Do not wait for another name on the list. The time for patience is over. The Bronx cannot afford more silence.
