      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Bronx Blood on the Asphalt: Lower the Speed, Save a Life

      Written July 9, 2025

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 9, 2025

      The Toll in Flesh and Bone

      Six people lay on the sidewalk at East 149th and Courtlandt. A Mustang turned, hit a woman in the crosswalk, then plowed into five more under the scaffolding. The driver and passenger ran. The car stayed behind, its front end smashed, plates gone. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. All six went to the hospital. They survived. This time.

      In the last year, 14 people died on Bronx streets. Over 3,300 were hurt. Forty-five suffered injuries so grave they may never walk the same. The dead include children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians. The city calls them accidents. The numbers say otherwise.

      Patterns in the Blood

      Cars and SUVs do most of the harm. Since 2022, they killed 38 people and injured thousands more. Trucks and buses killed eight. Motorcycles and mopeds, one. Bikes, none. The pattern is clear. The danger is heavy, fast, and steel-bound.

      The Mustang at 149th Street is not alone. A 76-year-old woman was killed by an SUV on East 216th. A man died crossing Webster Avenue. A moped passenger, just 22, lost his life at Bruckner and Southern. The stories change. The ending does not.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      The city lowered some speed limits and kept speed cameras running. New 20 mph zones are coming near schools and in slow zones. Cameras will keep watching, day and night. But the default speed limit is still too high. The city has the power to lower it to 20 mph everywhere. It has not. The law is there. The will is not.

      Police crack down on ghost cars and e-bikes. But the worst harm comes from repeat speeders and reckless drivers. “People were yelling, were in pain,” said a witness. The pain is not theirs alone.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      No one is safe until the streets are safe for the most vulnerable. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit everywhere. Demand action against repeat offenders. Do not wait for another body on the sidewalk.

      Take action now.

      ▸ Where does Bronx sit politically?
      It belongs to city council district District 11, assembly district AD 78 and state senate district SD 29.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx?
      Most deaths and injuries to pedestrians in Bronx were caused by Cars and SUVs (38 deaths, 3,278 injuries), followed by Trucks and Buses (8 deaths, 262 injuries), Motorcycles and Mopeds (1 death, 158 injuries). Bikes caused no deaths and 83 injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      The numbers show a pattern, not random chance. Speed, heavy vehicles, and repeat offenders drive the harm. These are preventable deaths, not fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower the default speed limit to 20 mph, expand automated enforcement, and pass laws targeting repeat dangerous drivers. They can act now, or wait for the next tragedy.
