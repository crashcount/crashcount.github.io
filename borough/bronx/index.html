      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Hit-and-Run at Bruckner and Leggett, Then Silence

      Written August 31, 2025 (Revised August 31, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 31, 2025

      Just after midnight at Bruckner Blvd and Leggett Ave on Aug 26, 2025, a driver hit 32-year-old Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez in the crosswalk and fled, police said (ABC7; NY Post).

      “She was found on Bruckner Boulevard and Leggett Avenue around 12:10 a.m.,” the station reported (ABC7).

      She is one of 142 people killed on Bronx streets since Jan 1, 2022, according to city crash data (NYC Open Data).

      The numbers don’t blink

      This year through Aug 31, the Bronx logged 7,910 crashes, 5,511 injuries, and 21 deaths. At the same point last year: 6,581 crashes, 4,243 injuries, 22 deaths (NYC Open Data). Crashes are up 20.2%. Injuries are up 29.9%. The dead remain the dead.

      Across the full window since 2022, the borough counts 24,421 injuries and 142 deaths on its streets (NYC Open Data).

      The pattern has names and corners

      Aug 26 wasn’t the first body on Bruckner this year. On May 24, a 76-year-old woman was struck on White Plains Rd at E 216 St and died (NYC Open Data). On May 10, a driver traveling at unsafe speed killed a 43-year-old pedestrian at E 168 St and Webster Ave (NYC Open Data). On Apr 17, a box truck driver starting from a parked position crushed a 61-year-old man on Oak Point Ave near Coster St (NYC Open Data). On Apr 2, three SUVs converged on E 149 St at Morris Ave; a 52-year-old pedestrian died there too (NYC Open Data).

      Corners. Sirens. Next of kin.

      Power sits with people who can act today

      Lower speeds save lives. New York City can set them. The city has the power to lower residential limits under Sammy’s Law, and Albany is weighing a bill to force the worst repeat speeders to slow down. The Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045C/A2299C) would require habitual violators to use speed limiters. Details and call scripts are here.

      This crash happened in the Bronx. Your representatives for this geography include Council Member Eric Dinowitz, Assembly Member George Alvarez, and State Senator Jose Serrano. They can back these fixes. The bills are on the table. The deaths are on our streets.

      What now

      One woman in a crosswalk at Bruckner and Leggett. A city that can slow the cars. A state that can rein in the worst drivers. Do it. Take action.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What happened at Bruckner and Leggett?
      Police and multiple outlets reported that Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez, 32, was struck in the crosswalk at Bruckner Blvd and Leggett Ave just after midnight on Aug 26, 2025. The driver fled. She was taken to Lincoln Hospital and pronounced dead (ABC7; NY Post; NY Daily News).
      ▸ How bad is traffic violence in the Bronx during this period?
      From Jan 1, 2022 to Aug 31, 2025, Bronx crashes caused 24,421 injuries and 142 deaths. In 2025 through Aug 31, the borough recorded 7,910 crashes, 5,511 injuries, and 21 deaths, compared to 6,581 crashes, 4,243 injuries, and 22 deaths over the same period in 2024 (NYC Open Data).
      ▸ Who are the local officials for this geography?
      Council Member Eric Dinowitz, Assembly Member George Alvarez, and State Senator Jose Serrano represent this geography in our lookup data. Their offices are listed on our site: District 11, AD 78, SD 29.
      ▸ What can actually change this?
      Two steps are ready now: 1) lower NYC’s default residential speed limit under Sammy’s Law; 2) pass the Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045C/A2299C) to require speed limiters for habitual speeders. See details and how to contact officials here.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95, Persons f55k-p6yu, Vehicles bm4k-52h4). We filtered for crashes in the Bronx between 2022-01-01 and 2025-08-31 and tallied injuries and deaths across all modes. Data was accessed Aug 31, 2025. You can start with the crash dataset here and filter by borough and date range to reproduce these counts.
