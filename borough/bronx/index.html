      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      No More Bodies in the Bronx: Demand Safe Streets Now

      Written June 23, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed citation formatting, deduped citations)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 23, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In the Bronx, the numbers do not bleed, but the people do. In the last twelve months, 35 people died and 94 suffered serious injuries in crashes. The youngest was a child. The oldest, a grandfather. Over 6,200 were hurt. Each number is a name, a family, a life split open by steel and speed.

      Last month, an 18-year-old woman was thrown from a moped on the Hutchinson River Parkway. She hit the pavement and did not get up. The driver left her behind. Police said the moped driver sped away from the 3:45 a.m. crash, leaving the young woman to die on the road. Six others landed in the hospital. The road was quiet again by sunrise.

      A week earlier, a man crossing Wood Avenue was struck and killed by a turning BMW. He tried to wave the driver off. The driver kept coming. A neighbor said the accident is so terrible. I’ve never seen anything like this in my life.

      The Weight of Inaction

      SUVs, sedans, trucks, and buses do most of the damage. In the Bronx, cars and SUVs killed 38 pedestrians and cyclists in three years. Trucks and buses killed 8. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1. Bikes killed none. The streets are not safe for the young or the old. No one is spared.

      Local leaders talk of Vision Zero. They pass laws. They hold press conferences. But the blood dries fast. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. They have not used it. Speed cameras work, but Albany lets them expire. The silence is louder than the sirens.

      What Comes Next

      Every death is preventable. The city can lower speed limits. The state can keep cameras rolling. Leaders can build streets that forgive mistakes. But they wait. And people die.

      Call your council member. Demand action. Do not wait for another body in the road. Take action now.
