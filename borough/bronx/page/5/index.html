      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Bronx Streets: Blood on Their Hands

      Written June 22, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, deduped and sorted citations, fixed link formatting.)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 22, 2025

      The Toll in Flesh and Bone

      In the Bronx, the numbers do not blink. In the last twelve months, 35 people died and 94 were left with serious injuries on the borough’s streets. Over 6,200 were hurt. Each number is a body. Each body is a story cut short or changed forever.

      A woman, 18, thrown from a scooter on the Hutchinson River Parkway. She lay in the road, clinging to life. The driver sped away. Police found her, but not him. “The moped’s driver fled with the bike before cops arrived.”

      A 75-year-old man, walking home from mosque, struck in a crosswalk. The driver turned left, did not yield. The man tried to wave him down. The car did not stop. “The accident is so terrible. I’ve never seen anything like this in my life.”

      These are not accidents. They are the result of choices, speed, and design.

      Who Pays, Who Acts

      The dead are old and young. Two children died this year. Nine people over 55. The most common killers: cars and SUVs. In three years, SUVs and sedans killed 38 pedestrians here. Trucks and buses killed eight. Motorcycles and mopeds, one. Bikes, none. See the breakdown below.

      Local leaders have tools. Sammy’s Law lets the city lower speed limits to 20 mph. Speed cameras cut speeding by more than half. But the city waits. Albany drags its feet on renewing camera laws. The council delays. Each day lost is another day someone does not come home.

      The Path Forward

      Every delay is a death sentence.

      Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand more speed cameras. Demand street redesigns that put people first. Join Families for Safe Streets or Transportation Alternatives. Stand with the families who have lost. Refuse to let the Bronx bleed in silence.

      Act now. The next name could be yours.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx?
      Cars and Trucks: SUVs and sedans killed 38 pedestrians and caused over 3,200 injuries. Trucks and buses killed 8, with 261 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 1 death, 155 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 83 injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents?
      No. The data shows patterns—speed, failure to yield, and dangerous street design. These deaths and injuries are preventable.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, expand speed camera programs, and redesign streets to protect people, not cars. They can act now, or answer for the next death.
      ▸ How many people have died or been seriously injured in Bronx traffic crashes recently?
      In the last year, 35 people died and 94 suffered serious injuries on Bronx streets.
