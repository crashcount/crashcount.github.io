      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Two young riders dead on the Bronx River Parkway

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Two men are dead. A car tried to pass. Metal met flesh on the Bronx River Parkway.

      Early August 11, police say a 2019 Mercedes tried to pass a Volkswagen heading south near East 223rd Street. The Mercedes hit the VW, then struck two mopeds. Both riders were thrown. Both died. Officials named them as Manuel Amarantepenalo, 19, and Enrique Martinez, 21, both from the Bronx, according to Gothamist and amNY.

      Prosecutors charged the driver, Mauricio Neyra Yuyes, 21, with vehicular manslaughter and DWI. Police said he had a strong odor of alcohol and refused a chemical test, per Gothamist. NYPD said all drivers stayed at the scene, reported the Daily News and amNY. The highway shut down near Exit 9 at Gun Hill Road for hours, Gothamist said.

      “Two people were killed. He was drunk,” a victim’s sister said, asking why the driver walked free after arraignment, in the Daily News.

      “How could they let him go? How could they do that?” the victim’s mother asked, in the same Daily News.

      “My client is prepared to contest these charges,” the defense attorney said, per the Daily News.

      Southbound. A pass. Two lives.

      The city’s crash data records the same chain: two mopeds, two sedans, southbound, center-front damage, riders ejected, both killed at 12:27 a.m. on the Bronx River Parkway near E. 223rd St. The riders were 21 and 19. Both listed as moped drivers. Both “demolished” vehicles. Both “apparent death.” That is from the city dataset for CrashID 4834345, with matching persons and vehicles files.

      The Bronx keeps burying its own

      In this borough, people outside cars absorb the hit. In the last 12 months, the Bronx logged 11,894 crashes, 40 deaths, and 8,196 injuries, per city data (Open Data crash tables). Year to date, crashes are up versus last year. Injuries are up too, according to the same dataset.

      The mopeds were not alone. A 44‑year‑old woman, a pedestrian, died on W 174 St at Macombs Rd on August 6. The record lists “Driver Inattention/Distraction” and “Apparent Death” (CrashID 4833327). On May 24, a 76‑year‑old woman was killed on White Plains Rd at E 216 St, also coded as “Driver Inattention/Distraction” (CrashID 4815461).

      What must change now

      Speed and passing moves turn small mistakes into funerals. The city can act.

      • Lower the default limit. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. New York City can set a 20 mph residential speed tomorrow. That is the call in our own coverage: Take Action.
      • Stop the worst drivers. The proposed Stop Super Speeders Act would force repeat offenders to install speed limiters. Details and contacts are in Take Action.

      Slow the cars. Make it stick.
