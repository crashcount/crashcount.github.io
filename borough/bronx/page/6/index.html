      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Bronx Streets Bleed While Politicians Stall: Who Will Stop the Killing?

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Toll on Bronx Streets

      The Bronx does not rest. Neither does traffic violence. Since 2022, 130 people have died on Bronx roads. 318 more have been seriously injured. The numbers do not tell you about the pain. They do not show you the blood on the sidewalk or the shoes left behind.

      Just last week, a Ford Mustang jumped the curb at East 149th and Courtlandt. Six people were walking. The car hit them, then smashed into scaffolding. The driver ran. The street was left with sirens and broken glass. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. All six went to the hospital. None died. This time.

      A few days before, a truck overturned and spilled liquid asphalt across a Bronx road. No one died, but the street was left slick and dangerous. The city cleaned up. The risk remains, as CBS New York reported.

      Who Pays the Price?

      SUVs and cars kill the most. Since 2022, they have taken 38 lives and left 73 with serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed 8. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1. Bikes killed none. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. The old and the young. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh.

      What Has Been Done—and What Hasn’t

      The city has lowered speed limits in some zones. Speed cameras now run 24/7. But the blood keeps coming. Enforcement cracks down on cyclists, not just drivers. The city redesigns some streets, but not enough. The Bronx still bleeds. The leaders talk. The cars keep coming.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Tell them to lower the speed limit to 20 mph everywhere. Tell them to redesign every deadly street. Tell them to focus on the drivers who do the most harm. Do not wait for another siren. Do not wait for another name on the list. Demand action. Demand it now.
