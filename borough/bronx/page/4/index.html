      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—City Hall Stalls

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised June 28, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      The Toll of Ordinary Streets

      In the Bronx, the violence is relentless. In the last twelve months, 34 people died and 6,303 were injured on its roads. Ninety-six suffered serious injuries. The numbers do not flinch. They do not pause for grief.

      Just last week, before dawn on the Hutchinson River Parkway, a chain of cars and a scooter collided. Seven were hurt. One woman, eighteen, left fighting for her life. The driver who carried her sped away, leaving her on the asphalt. Police said the moped’s driver fled with the bike before cops arrived. She was rushed to Jacobi Hospital and remains critical. Six others from the cars went to the hospital. Police are investigating a multi-vehicle collision involving a scooter that left seven people injured and one critically hurt in the Bronx.

      A month before, a 76-year-old woman was killed crossing East 216th Street. A Toyota SUV hit her head-on. She died from her injuries. The cause: driver inattention.

      Who Pays the Price

      Pedestrians, cyclists, children. They pay first. In the Bronx, SUVs and sedans do most of the damage. Cars and SUVs killed 38, injured 3,153, and seriously hurt 71 pedestrians in three years. Trucks and buses killed 8, injured 235, and seriously hurt 18. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1, injured 152, and seriously hurt 4. Bikes injured 80 and seriously hurt 3. The street is not neutral. It takes sides.

      Leadership: Words and Waiting

      Local leaders have tools. They have the power to lower speed limits, redesign streets, and enforce the law. Some steps have come. Sammy’s Law lets the city set safer speeds. Speed cameras now run 24/7. But the pace is slow. The Bronx still bleeds.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every delay is another family broken. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand streets that protect the living, not just the drivers. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Take action now.
