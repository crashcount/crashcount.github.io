      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx?

      Two young riders die on the Bronx River Parkway. The driver walks home.

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Drivers keep killing in the Bronx. The names pile up.

      Southbound, near E. 223rd Street

      Just after midnight, a 2019 Mercedes tried to pass a Volkswagen on the Bronx River Parkway. Police say the Mercedes hit the VW, then struck two mopeds. Both riders were thrown and died at hospitals. Officials identified them as 19-year-old Manuel Amarante Penalo and 21-year-old Enrique Martinez, both from the Bronx, according to Gothamist and amNY. The highway shut near Exit 9 at Gun Hill Road for the investigation, per Gothamist.

      Police arrested 21-year-old Mauricio Neyra Yuyes. He was charged with vehicular manslaughter and DWI, according to Gothamist and the Daily News. A family member stood in court and said, “Two people were killed. He was drunk,” as reported by the Daily News. His lawyer said, “My client is prepared to contest these charges,” per the Daily News. Coverage noted he was released without bail while the Bronx DA’s investigation continued, according to the Daily News.

      On the city dataset, the fatal parkway crash lists two moped drivers killed and two sedans involved that night on the southbound Bronx River Parkway (NYC Open Data).

      Elsewhere in the borough, the killing goes on

      A 76-year-old woman was struck and killed on White Plains Road at E. 216th Street. The SUV’s center front end hit her. The police coded driver inattention. She died. The database lists her as a pedestrian “not at intersection.” That is all it says (NYC Open Data).

      On Macombs Road at W. 174th Street, a 44-year-old woman died. “Driver Inattention/Distraction” again. One entry. One body. No driver name. No make. No model. Just “Unspecified” and a timestamp (NYC Open Data).

      On the Bruckner at Pelham Parkway, a left-turning SUV met a motorcycle going straight. The rider, 31, wore a helmet. He was ejected and died. The sheet shows “Right Front Quarter Panel.” The numbers do not look back (NYC Open Data).

      The ledger of harm

      In the Bronx since 2022, crashes, injuries, and deaths keep stacking up. In the last 12 months, there were 11,894 crashes, with 8,196 injured and 40 killed across all ages, according to our borough rollup of the city data (NYC Open Data). SUVs and cars lead the toll for people on foot: 2,587 minor injuries, 948 moderate, 85 serious, and 41 deaths tied to those vehicle types in this period snapshot (NYC Open Data).

      This year to date, Bronx crashes are up about 21% over last year’s pace, with injuries up about 31%, per the same dataset comparison window (NYC Open Data). The counting never stops.

      What will stop the next one?

      Lower speed saves life. New York City now has the power under Sammy’s Law to set safer default limits. A citywide 20 mph floor on residential streets is the next step. The worst repeat speeders need electronic leashes—intelligent speed assistance for cars with long rap sheets. The case is plain in our own coverage and policy briefs. See our action guide and make the calls.

      A sedan smashed into a snow plow on Major Deegan. The 41-year-old driver was thrown partway out. He died from head trauma and bleeding. Metal twisted. Blood pooled. The night stayed silent.

      A sedan crashed into the rear of a snow plow on the Major Deegan Expressway at 4:04 a.m. According to the police report, the sedan's 41-year-old driver was partially ejected and died at the scene from head trauma and severe bleeding. The snow plow driver, age 43, suffered back injuries but survived. Both vehicles were traveling straight. The report lists no specific driver errors or contributing factors. The sedan struck the snow plow's back end, crushing metal and ending a life in the dark. No mention of helmet or signal use appears in the report.

      Ambulance Turns Left, Kills Pedestrian in Bronx
      ▸

      A Ford ambulance turned left on East Gun Hill Road. Its bumper struck a 56-year-old man in the dark. He died where he fell. The vehicle showed no damage. The street stayed silent. The man’s body lay broken from head to heel.

      A fatal crash occurred at East Gun Hill Road and Dekalb Avenue in the Bronx. A Ford ambulance, making a left turn, struck a 56-year-old man in the intersection. According to the police report, 'A Ford ambulance turned left. Its bumper struck a 56-year-old man in the dark. No damage to the vehicle. The man died where he fell, his body broken from head to heel.' The pedestrian was killed on impact. The data lists 'Unspecified' as the contributing factor for both the driver and the pedestrian. No driver errors such as failure to yield or distraction are specified in the report. No mention of helmet or signal use appears in the data. The ambulance occupants were not reported injured.
