      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn?

      No More Dead Kids: Streets Are Still Killing, City Still Stalling

      Written July 2, 2025

      Brooklyn: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 2, 2025

      Children, Gone in an Instant

      The street does not forgive. On June 28, an eight-year-old boy crossed Eastern Parkway with his sister. A black SUV hit him. He died there. His sister watched. “He was a boy who loved life,” said Baila Krainer. The driver stayed. The street stayed dangerous.

      Just days later, a van carrying adults with special needs slammed into a tree in Bath Beach. Fourteen people hurt. “It’s sad because they definitely need a lot of attention more than anyone else. I hope that they’re all OK at the end of the day,” said Matthew Fischioni.

      The Toll: Relentless and Rising

      In the last twelve months, Brooklyn saw 54 deaths and 155 serious injuries on its streets. Children are not spared: six killed, eighteen seriously hurt. The numbers do not stop. They do not care about age or reason. Cars, SUVs, trucks, bikes, mopeds—each leaves its mark. Most deaths come from cars and SUVs, but no mode is blameless.

      Leaders Move—But Not Fast Enough

      After years of blood on the asphalt, the city has started to act. Speed cameras now run 24/7. The law allows a 20 mph limit. But the city moves slow. Streets stay wide. Corners stay blind. The dead pile up. “Yeah, this street is dangerous,” said Milli Cent.

      Some council members call for more redesigns. Some celebrate small wins. But the pace is glacial. The carnage is not.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph limit. Demand streets that put people first. Do not wait for the next siren.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Brooklyn sit politically?
      It belongs to city council district District 38, assembly district AD 41 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn?
      It includes the Greenpoint, Williamsburg, South Williamsburg, East Williamsburg, Brooklyn Heights, Downtown Brooklyn-Dumbo-Boerum Hill, Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Brooklyn Navy Yard, Bedford-Stuyvesant (West), Brooklyn CB8, and District 38 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 37, District 38, District 39, District 42, District 44, District 45, District 46, District 47, and District 48, Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 45, AD 46, AD 51, AD 59, and AD 60, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 18, SD 19, SD 20, SD 21, SD 22, SD 23, SD 25, and SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn?
      Cars and SUVs: Cars and SUVs were involved in the most deaths and injuries to pedestrians, with 68 deaths and 6,737 injuries. Trucks and buses caused 22 deaths and 582 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 4 deaths and 229 injuries. Bikes caused 3 deaths and 277 injuries. Source: NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes can be prevented with safer street design, lower speed limits, and better enforcement. The same streets see the same tragedies again and again.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign dangerous streets, expand automated enforcement, and prioritize people over cars. They can act now, or answer for every life lost.
