      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn?

      No More Dead Kids: Streets or Safety—Pick One

      Written June 29, 2025 (Revised June 29, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Brooklyn: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 29, 2025

      The Week the Streets Took More

      A boy crossed Eastern Parkway with his sister. He did not make it to the other side. On June 28, an eight-year-old was struck and killed by a black SUV in Crown Heights. The driver stayed. Police said, “They were walking, he was crossing the street with his sister” (police said). No arrest. The street stayed open. The city moved on.

      Just days before, two young men on a moped collided with a car in Midwood. One, seventeen, died at the hospital. His passenger was left broken. The car was turning left. The moped “slammed into” it, then hit a parked Honda Odyssey. No charges. The city counted another body (city counted another body).

      The Numbers That Do Not Lie

      In the last year, Brooklyn saw 53 deaths and 157 serious injuries from crashes. Children, elders, cyclists, and walkers are among the dead. Five children killed. Over a thousand children hurt. The city records the numbers. The families remember the names.

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. In three years: 67 deaths by SUVs and cars. Trucks and buses killed 22. Motorcycles and mopeds, 1. Bikes, 3. The street is not fair. The bigger vehicle wins.

      Leaders Act—Or Do Not

      The city talks about safety. It lowers speed limits on some streets. It installs cameras. It promises more. But the deaths keep coming. The city removed a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue after crashes—leaving riders exposed. Lawsuits followed. Promises for secure bike storage stall. The mayor and council speak of “community feedback” and “safety concerns.” The streets stay the same.

      A neighbor said, “Yeah, this street is dangerous” (neighbor said).

      What Now

      This is not fate. Every crash is a choice. Every delay is a risk. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for those outside cars. Do not wait for another child’s name to be added to the list.

      Take action now.
