      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn?

      Brooklyn Bleeds While City Hall Sleeps

      Written July 8, 2025

      Brooklyn: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 8, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Brooklyn, the street is a wound that never closes. In the last twelve months, 56 people died in crashes. Over 12,000 were hurt. One hundred fifty-three suffered injuries so severe they will never be the same. The numbers do not rest. They only climb.

      Just days ago, a 36-year-old man was crossing near Broadway and East New York Avenue. A driver hit him and kept going. He died at Brookdale Hospital. Police are still looking for the driver. The driver was traveling westbound on East New York Avenue when they struck a 36-year-old man walking on the road and kept going, reported ABC7.

      A week before, an eight-year-old boy was killed at an intersection in Crown Heights. An SUV, a clear day, a child crossing. The street took him. No warning, no mercy.

      The Pattern Is the Policy

      Most victims are not in cars. They are walking, riding, crossing. In Brooklyn, SUVs and sedans are the main killers. SUVs alone killed 41 pedestrians in three years. Trucks, buses, motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes all add to the toll. The city counts the bodies. The city counts the broken.

      The drivers flee. The families wait. The driver of the Ford sped off, never stopping, cops said. He remained at large on Saturday, wrote the Daily News.

      What Leaders Have Done—And Not Done

      This year, the city lowered speed limits in some zones and expanded speed cameras. It is not enough. The law lets New York set a citywide 20 mph limit. The city has not done it. The cameras catch speeders, but the worst repeat offenders keep driving. The council and mayor have the power. They have not used it.

      The Next Step Is Ours

      Every death is preventable. The street does not kill. Policy does. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph limit. Demand action against repeat speeders. Do not wait for another child’s name to be added to the list.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn?
      In Brooklyn, SUVs and sedans caused the most harm to pedestrians: SUVs killed 41 and sedans killed 20 in the last three years. Trucks, buses, taxis, vans, motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes also contributed to deaths and injuries, with motorcycles and mopeds causing 4 deaths and 230 injuries, and bikes causing 3 deaths and 277 injuries.       ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data shows patterns: speeding, hit-and-runs, repeat offenders. These deaths are preventable with safer streets, lower speed limits, and real enforcement.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can set a citywide 20 mph speed limit, expand automated enforcement, redesign dangerous streets, and crack down on repeat speeders. They have the power. They must use it.
      ▸ How many people have been killed or seriously injured in Brooklyn traffic crashes recently?
      In the last twelve months, 56 people died and 153 suffered serious injuries in Brooklyn traffic crashes.
