      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn?

      Brooklyn Bleeds, City Sleeps: Demand Safe Streets Now

      Written August 11, 2025

      Brooklyn: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 11, 2025

      The Toll in Flesh and Bone

      In Brooklyn, the numbers do not sleep. In the last 12 months, 83 people died on the borough’s streets. Another 15,859 were injured. 211 suffered injuries so grave they will never be the same.

      The dead are not numbers. They are men, women, children. Just last week, a 47-year-old man was crossing Broadway at Suydam Street. A driver hit him, dragged him more than 50 feet, and left him in the road. Police are still looking for the driver. Police were looking for the driver of a car that struck and dragged a 47-year-old man on a Brooklyn street over the weekend, killing him.

      On Morgan Avenue, another man was killed where there is no crosswalk. It is the third death on that stretch in three years. Juan Ignacio Serra was very frustrated that nothing has been done in more than three years since Daniel Vidal was killed.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did the most harm. In Brooklyn since 2022, they killed 82 people and injured thousands more. Trucks and buses killed 25. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 2. Bikes killed 3. The rest are numbers, but every number is a body.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      Local leaders have made some moves. The city renewed 24-hour speed cameras. They have the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph but have not used it. On Morgan Avenue, calls for protected bike lanes and crosswalks have gone unanswered. The city has not acted.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. It is policy. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected crossings. Demand action, not words. The dead cannot call. The living must.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Brooklyn sit politically?
      It belongs to city council district District 38, assembly district AD 41 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn?
      It includes the Greenpoint, Williamsburg, South Williamsburg, East Williamsburg, Brooklyn Heights, Downtown Brooklyn-Dumbo-Boerum Hill, Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Brooklyn Navy Yard, Bedford-Stuyvesant (West), Brooklyn CB8, and District 38 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 37, District 38, District 39, District 42, District 44, District 45, District 46, District 47, and District 48, Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 45, AD 46, AD 51, AD 59, and AD 60, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 18, SD 19, SD 20, SD 21, SD 22, SD 23, SD 25, and SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn?
      Cars and SUVs: 82 deaths, 7,379 injuries. Trucks and buses: 25 deaths, 641 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 2 deaths, 255 injuries. Bikes: 3 deaths, 293 injuries. Cars and SUVs do the most harm.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are not random. They follow patterns—speed, street design, lack of protection for people walking and biking. Change the rules, change the roads, and you change the outcome.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, build protected crossings and bike lanes, and enforce laws that target reckless drivers. They can act now, not after another death.
