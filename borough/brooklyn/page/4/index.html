      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn?

      Brooklyn Bleeds. Politicians Stall. How Many More Must Die?

      Written July 3, 2025

      Brooklyn: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 3, 2025

      The Toll in Brooklyn: Lives Lost, Streets Unchanged

      In Brooklyn, the crisis does not come all at once. It comes day after day, body after body. Since January 2022, 199 people have died on Brooklyn streets. Another 485 suffered serious injuries. The numbers do not bleed, but the people do.

      Just days ago, an eight-year-old boy was killed by an SUV while crossing Eastern Parkway with his sister. A witness said, “I just saw a lot of blood gushing out of his ears, his mouth” (NY Daily News). The driver stayed. The boy did not. Neighbors called the street dangerous.

      Fourteen people were hurt when a van slammed into a tree in Bath Beach. Most were adults with special needs, headed to day care. “It’s sad because they definitely need a lot of attention more than anyone else. I hope that they’re all OK at the end of the day,” said a bystander.

      The Pattern: Who Pays the Price

      Children, the elderly, and people walking or riding are the first to fall. In the last year, six children under 18 died. SUVs and cars are the main killers—cars and trucks caused 68 pedestrian deaths in this period, with hundreds more injured. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes add to the toll, but the weight of steel does most of the work.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      Local leaders have made moves. Sammy’s Law passed. The city can now lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the default remains 25. Cameras catch speeders, but the worst repeat offenders keep driving. The city claims progress, but the bodies keep coming. The streets do not change fast enough.

      What Comes Next: No More Waiting

      Every day of delay is another risk, another family broken. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand the city use its new power. Demand action against repeat speeders. Do not wait for another child’s blood on the street.

      Take action now.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn?
      Cars and Trucks caused 68 pedestrian deaths and thousands of injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds caused 4 deaths and over 400 injuries. Bikes caused 3 deaths and nearly 300 injuries. The largest toll comes from cars, SUVs, and trucks. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These are not random accidents. Lower speeds, safer street design, and enforcement can prevent deaths. Every delay means more lives lost.
