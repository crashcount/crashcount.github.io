      Brooklyn Bleeds: Speed Kills, Leaders Stall

      Written June 19, 2025 (Revised June 20, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      Brooklyn: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 19, 2025

      The Toll in Brooklyn

      A woman steps out of her car in Bedford-Stuyvesant. She is pregnant. She is struck, dragged, and left to die. Her name is Tiffany Cifuni. The driver flees. Her family is left with silence and rage. “We have been reeling from the day [Tiffany] was killed. We will not rest until it’s over and we get justice for Tiffany,” said her husband.

      In the last twelve months, 52 people died on Brooklyn streets. 162 were seriously injured. Children, elders, cyclists, pedestrians. The numbers do not flinch. Four children killed. Eight people over 65. The dead do not get second chances.

      The Pattern: No End in Sight

      The violence does not pause. A chain-reaction crash in Sheepshead Bay puts two NYPD officers in the hospital. A drunk driver behind the wheel. “One police officer suffered a broken pelvis and arm, as well as head trauma, while another suffered a broken hip,” reported police. A passenger is ejected, left in critical condition. The city keeps counting.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      Brooklyn’s leaders talk of Vision Zero. They point to new laws. Sammy’s Law passed. The city can now lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the limit has not changed. The cameras that catch speeders are at risk of going dark unless Albany acts. The city redesigns intersections, but the pace is slow. Streets remain wide, fast, and deadly.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Join Transportation Alternatives or Families for Safe Streets. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Take action now.
