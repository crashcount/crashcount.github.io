      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens?

      Queens, August: three dead at a cart, a hit‑and‑run by JFK, a cyclist struck on Hollis Court

      Written August 27, 2025 (Revised August 27, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 27, 2025

      Two mornings. One night. The same borough.

      Food cart, two men, and a car that did not stop

      On Aug 12, 2025, an 84‑year‑old in a Toyota jumped the curb in Astoria. The car hit a food truck and two men. All three died. Police named the pedestrians: 41‑year‑old Joaquin Venancio‑Mendez and 70‑year‑old Santiago Baires. The driver died at the scene. The outlet reported the driver had suffered a stroke two weeks earlier and had been told not to drive. An eyewitness said, “I have never seen anything like this”.

      City data records the crash at 19 Ave and 42 St. Two pedestrians were struck “not at intersection.” Vehicles listed: a parked Volkswagen sedan, a Toyota sedan “going straight ahead,” a parked Chevy SUV, and a Volvo SUV “making left turn.” Three people were recorded as killed. Cause fields read “Unspecified.” NYC Open Data.

      South Conduit, dark hour, driver runs

      Before dawn on Aug 13, near JFK, a car hit a 52‑year‑old man at 155 St and South Conduit Ave. The driver left him in the road and fled. Police said, “The operator of the vehicle fled the scene after hitting the man”. He died at Jamaica Hospital. Another account put it simply: “The driver sped off without stopping. No arrests have been made”. Detectives from the Collision Investigation Squad were on the case, and Queens South deaths were already climbing this year, reporters noted (Gothamist).

      City records match the time and place. The pedestrian was listed “not at intersection.” The striking vehicle was “Unspecified,” front‑end damage, headed east. The victim’s injury status: “Apparent Death.” NYC Open Data.

      Hollis Court, a left turn, a cyclist goes down

      Just after midday on Jul 31, at Hollis Court Blvd and 50 Ave, a 55‑year‑old woman on an e‑bike was hit. She was listed as the cyclist, “Driver,” “Ejected,” injury “Apparent Death.” The other vehicle: a 2015 Nissan SUV, “Making Left Turn,” with right‑front damage. City crash file shows the fatality and the movements.

      The count does not stop

      In Queens, deaths keep stacking up. In the last 12 months, the borough logged 73 deaths, 14,527 injuries, across 23,134 crashes. This year to date, deaths are up about 61% over last year’s pace. These are not numbers. They are names we often never learn. Open Data period stats.

      What leaders say—and what the street says back

      Some crashes are not mistakes. In 2023, a Bayside man drove the wrong way on the Clearview and hit five cars. A jury convicted him. At sentencing, the Queens DA said, “Joseph Lee terrorized other drivers as he purposefully drove the wrong way on a busy Queens highway and crashed into multiple cars”. Two motorists were badly hurt and “still have not fully recovered,” the DA said. Lee told police, “I wanted to hurt people”.

      The street offers no excuses. It records the impact. It keeps the stain.

      Slow it down. Stop the repeat harm.

      Queens needs fewer fast cars and fewer second chances for the worst drivers. Two steps would help: lower the city’s default speed limit, and force chronic violators to use speed‑limiters. If you want that to happen, add your name, your call, your voice. Start here: Take action.

      Mar 26 - A parked SUV became a tomb on 42nd Avenue. Distraction carved metal, left a woman, 43, lifeless in the daylight hush. The lap belt held her, but the impact did not forgive. The street bore witness, silent and unchanged.

      According to the police report, a fatal crash occurred outside 104-28 42nd Avenue in Queens when a parked Subaru SUV was struck on its left side. The sole occupant, a 43-year-old woman, was inside the vehicle at the time and was held in place by her lap belt. The report lists 'Driver Inattention/Distraction' as the contributing factor leading to the collision. The impact folded metal and resulted in the apparent death of the woman, who did not leave the car. The police report does not cite any victim behavior as a contributing factor. The focus remains on the danger created by driver distraction, which turned a parked vehicle into a scene of tragedy.
