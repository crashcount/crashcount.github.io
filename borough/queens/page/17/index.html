      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Queens: Four deaths in two weeks, and a toll that does not stop

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 11, 2025

      A driver in an SUV turned right at 30 St and 39 Ave and hit a 38-year-old woman in a marked crosswalk on Aug 31. She died. Police recorded failure to yield by the driver (NYC Open Data).

      This was not an outlier. She was one of 228 people killed on Queens streets since Jan 1, 2022 (NYC Open Data). In the last 12 months alone, Queens saw 73 deaths and 14,384 injuries in 22,846 crashes (NYC Open Data). Year-to-date, deaths are up 55.9% from last year; injuries are up 28.1% (NYC Open Data).

      This Week

      • Aug 23: A driver in a sedan hit and killed a 38-year-old man outside an intersection; police recorded driver inattention (NYC Open Data).
      • Aug 12: At Union Tpke and 189 St, a driver going straight hit and killed a 61-year-old man outside an intersection (NYC Open Data).
      • Aug 12: At 42 St and 19 Ave, a multi-vehicle crash killed two pedestrians and a driver (NYC Open Data).

      Queens corridors, human cost

      Crashes are not rare blips. They are the day’s weather. Since 2022, Queens has logged 71,285 crashes, with 41,491 injuries and 228 deaths (NYC Open Data). Pedestrians are most often hurt by drivers of SUVs and sedans. Those drivers account for a combined 59 deaths and thousands of injuries in that span (NYC Open Data – Persons).

      The pattern is steady. The toll is not abstract. “Police vehicle pursuits and high-speed car chases can be dangerous and even fatal, and it is time for a change,” New York’s attorney general said this year, backing tighter pursuit rules (Times Union).

      Year-to-date: up, again

      From Jan 1 to Sep 11, deaths and injuries in Queens are higher than the same period last year. Deaths are up 55.9%; serious injuries up 26.0%; injuries up 28.1%; crashes up 25.2% (NYC Open Data). That is the trend line.

      Power to slow the cars

      The City can set safer speeds and rein in the worst repeat speeders. A citywide 20 MPH default and intelligent speed limiters for habitual speeders are on the table. The steps are laid out here.

      Queens’s electeds for this area are Council Member Vickie Paladino, Assembly Member Stacey Pheffer Amato, and State Senator James Sanders. The record above shows the harm. Their positions on these fixes are not listed here.

      What happens now

      A woman died in a crosswalk on 39 Ave. Others fell days before and after. Slower streets and speed limiters would keep people alive. Tell City Hall and Albany to move. Start here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We analyzed NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets — Crashes (h9gi-nx95), Persons (f55k-p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k-52h4) — filtered to the borough of Queens between 2022-01-01 and 2025-09-11. We counted deaths, injuries, serious injuries, and total crashes, and compared this year-to-date totals to the same period last year. Data were accessed on 2025-09-11. A reproducible filtered query for total deaths is provided here.
      ▸ What recent crashes does this report highlight?
      Four fatal crashes in Queens from Aug 12 to Aug 31, 2025: a woman killed in a 30 St/39 Ave crosswalk; a man killed outside an intersection on Aug 23; a man killed at Union Tpke/189 St; and a multi-vehicle crash at 42 St/19 Ave that killed two pedestrians and a driver (NYC Open Data).
      ▸ Which officials cover this area?
      City Council Member Vickie Paladino (District 19), Assembly Member Stacey Pheffer Amato (AD 23), and State Senator James Sanders (SD 10) represent the overlapping districts listed on this Queens page.
      ▸ What can actually change this trend?
      Lowering the default NYC speed limit and requiring intelligent speed assistance for repeat speeders are concrete steps. See details and contacts on our action page: /take_action/.
