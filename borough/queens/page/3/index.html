      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens?

      Queens, August: Three dead at a cart, a body by JFK, and the drumbeat goes on

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Two scenes. Same borough. Same result.

      Astoria corner, three lives gone

      Police said an 84-year-old driver slammed into a food truck in Astoria on Aug. 12. The car hit two men. It also killed the driver. The victims were 41-year-old Joaquin Venancio-Mendez and 70-year-old Santiago Baires. A witness said, “I have never seen anything like this.” The street was left with wreckage and loss.

      Springfield Gardens, a hit-and-run at 2:30 a.m.

      A 52-year-old man tried to cross 155th Street at South Conduit Avenue near JFK before dawn on Aug. 13. A driver hit him and fled. Police said, “The driver sped off without stopping. No arrests have been made.” He was taken to Jamaica Hospital and died there, according to Gothamist and ABC7. City data logs the death at 155 St and South Conduit Ave at 2:26 a.m.

      The pattern does not blink

      In Queens this year through Aug. 26, crashes injured 41,091 people citywide and killed 224, with 471 seriously hurt, according to NYC’s own numbers for the period since 2022; SUVs and sedans account for the bulk of pedestrian harm (dataset rollup). In the borough’s recent week alone: two men on foot killed in Astoria; a 52-year-old man killed near JFK. The city’s open data lists more bodies on expressways and local streets: a 24-year-old motorcyclist killed on the Long Island Expressway on July 24; a 55-year-old woman on an e-bike killed at Hollis Court Blvd and 50th Ave on July 31 (LIE case file, Hollis Court case file). This is how it looks when a city fails to slow cars.

      Officials speak after the sirens

      The numbers mount even when a suspect flees. Police were “searching for surveillance footage” after the JFK killing, reporters noted, and “the investigation remains ongoing.” In another Queens case from 2023, a driver went the wrong way on the Clearview and hit five cars. A jury convicted him. At sentencing this month, the district attorney said, “Joseph Lee terrorized other drivers.” The line lands because it is true.

      What changes the odds

      Speed is the lever. NYC now has the power to set safer speeds and to rein in repeat speeders with limiters. Both save lives. The fixes are known. The cost of waiting is on the street.

      Take one step today. Tell City Hall and the Council to lower the default speed and back speed limiters for the worst offenders. Start here: Take action.
