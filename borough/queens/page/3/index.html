      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens?

      Queens Bleeds While City Stalls—Lower the Speed, Save a Life

      Written June 19, 2025 (Revised June 20, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Toll in Flesh and Bone

      A man lies unresponsive on 101st Avenue. Struck by a black SUV, the driver keeps going. The man is in his fifties. He is fighting for his life. Police say the SUV did not stop. The street is quiet again, but the blood remains. Police say the victim was struck by a black SUV traveling eastbound on 101st Avenue that kept going.

      A cyclist is left unconscious on 115th Avenue. Another hit-and-run. The driver flees. The city counts the bodies, but the pain is not counted. They are now looking for evidence to help them track down the driver.

      In the last 12 months, 53 people have died on Queens streets. 137 were seriously injured. Over 11,000 were hurt. The numbers do not flinch. They do not grieve.

      Leadership: Words, Laws, and Waiting

      The city says it is acting. Speed cameras, new laws, promises. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can now lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the limit is not yet lowered. The cameras work, but the law that keeps them running is set to expire. Each day of delay is another day of risk.

      Some leaders speak. Some are silent. The dead do not care who speaks. They are gone.

      The Work Ahead: No More Waiting

      Speed kills. Delay kills. The city has the power to act. Lower the speed limit. Keep the cameras on. Build streets that forgive mistakes. Hold drivers to account. Do not wait for another family to join the list.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand action.

      Take action now.
