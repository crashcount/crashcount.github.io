      Queens Streets Run Red While Politicians Wait

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      A man with a cane tries to cross Linden Boulevard. A car hits him. The driver flees. Another car runs him over. He dies a day later. His family says, “Today is a sad day. We lost a brother, father, son, uncle, and cousin. He has been snuffed from us by a hit-and-run driver.”

      Two little girls, four and eight, walk in a crosswalk. An SUV runs a red light and hits them. The driver flees. The girls survive. Police say the driver “went around another vehicle and then blew a red light, striking the girls… as they were walking south in the crosswalk.”

      In the last year, 52 people died on Queens streets. 136 suffered serious injuries. Most were walking, biking, or just trying to get home. The bodies pile up. The city counts them. The families remember.

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      SUVs and cars kill the most. In Queens since 2022, cars and SUVs caused 52 deaths and 93 serious injuries to pedestrians. Trucks and buses killed 12 more. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 4. Even bikes killed once. NYC Open Data

      Children are not spared. Four children died in the last year. Nearly a thousand were hurt. The old are not spared. Six people over 75 died. The city calls it Vision Zero. The numbers call it something else.

      The Leaders and the Law

      Local leaders talk. Some act. Most wait. The city now has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. They have not done it. Speed cameras work, but Albany lets their authority hang by a thread. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program lapsed. The city asks Albany for more power. The dead wait for nothing.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand streets that don’t kill.

      Every day of delay is another body in the road. Take action now.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens?
      Since 2022, cars and SUVs caused 52 deaths and 93 serious injuries to pedestrians in Queens. Trucks and buses killed 12 more. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 4. Bikes killed once and seriously injured 3. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents?
      No. Most crashes are preventable. Drivers run red lights, speed, and fail to yield. The numbers show a pattern, not random chance.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, keep speed cameras running, redesign dangerous streets, and restore programs that target repeat dangerous drivers. Delay costs lives.
      ▸ How many people were killed or seriously hurt in Queens recently?
      In the last 12 months, 52 people died and 136 suffered serious injuries in Queens traffic crashes.
