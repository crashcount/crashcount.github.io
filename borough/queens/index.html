      Queens Bleeds While Leaders Stall: Demand Slower Speeds, Save Lives Now

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in Queens

      A man steps into the street. An SUV keeps going. He does not come home. In the last twelve months, 53 people died on Queens streets. Over 11,000 were hurt. One hundred thirty-seven suffered wounds so deep they will not heal. The dead are old and young. Four children did not live to see another summer. Six elders did not make it home. The numbers do not stop. They do not care.

      The Faces Behind the Numbers

      A cyclist lies unconscious on 115th Avenue. The driver does not stop. Police search for a name, a face, a reason. The victim is in critical condition. The driver is gone. Police are now looking for evidence to help them track down the driver.

      A moped rider, Antonio Smith-Ortiz, is hit head-on by a car going the wrong way. The car does not stop. The family is left with silence. The driver, who was going against traffic in the eastbound lane, then struck an unoccupied parked van before speeding off.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      Speed kills. The city has new power. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. Queens can lower speed limits to 20 mph. The city can act now. But the limit stands. Each day of delay is another risk, another family broken. Speed cameras work. They cut speeding, they save lives. But the law that keeps them running is set to expire. The clock ticks. The city has built more bike lanes, more crosswalks. Still, SUVs and trucks do the most harm. The most vulnerable—pedestrians, cyclists, children—pay the price.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Join those who fight for the living. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.
