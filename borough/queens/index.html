      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens?

      Queens Bleeds While City Sleeps—Lower the Limit Now

      Written August 5, 2025 (Revised August 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Queens: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 5, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      In Queens, the toll does not stop. Since January 2022, 218 people have died on the borough’s streets. Over 40,000 have been hurt. 449 suffered injuries so severe they will never be the same.

      Just last week, a 55-year-old woman riding her e-bike was killed by a turning SUV on Hollis Court Boulevard. The driver stayed. Police are still investigating. The news was blunt: “e-bike rider Zhao Feng Zhen, 55, died after the 62-year-old driver of a 2015 Nissan Rogue struck her while heading south on Hollis Court Blvd.”

      On the same day, a 23-year-old man was run down in the street. The driver told police he was trying to get away. The DA is still deciding on charges. “Police say a domestic dispute led to a man being run over by another person.”

      The Usual Suspects

      The killers are not mysteries. SUVs and cars are the main weapons: 57 pedestrians dead, 108 seriously hurt. Trucks and buses killed 14 more. Motorcycles and mopeds, 5. Bikes, 1. The numbers do not lie. The bodies do not get up.

      Promises and Delays

      Leaders talk. Some act. Albany renewed speed cameras through 2030. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. They have not done it. The carnage continues. The city has started to redesign streets, but not fast enough. Every week, another family is broken.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action against repeat speeders. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens sit politically?
      It belongs to city council district District 19, assembly district AD 23 and state senate district SD 10.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens?
      It includes the Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, Astoria Park, Long Island City-Hunters Point, Sunnyside, Queens CB10, and District 25 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 19, District 23, District 24, District 26, District 27, District 28, District 30, District 31, and District 32, Assembly Districts AD 23, AD 24, AD 25, AD 26, AD 27, AD 29, AD 31, AD 33, and AD 37, and State Senate Districts SD 10, SD 11, SD 12, SD 14, SD 15, and SD 16.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens?
      Cars and SUVs: 57 pedestrian deaths, 108 serious injuries. Trucks and buses: 14 deaths, 16 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 5 deaths, 5 serious injuries. Bikes: 1 death, 3 serious injuries. Cars and trucks are the main killers.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths are not random. They follow patterns—speed, turning vehicles, wide roads. Policies can prevent them. Lower speed limits, redesign streets, and hold repeat offenders accountable.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, expand street redesigns, and crack down on repeat dangerous drivers. They can act now or let the toll rise.
