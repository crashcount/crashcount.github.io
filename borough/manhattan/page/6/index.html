      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan?

      Manhattan’s Streets Bleed—Speed Kills, City Stalls

      Written June 29, 2025

      Manhattan: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 29, 2025

      The Toll on Manhattan’s Streets

      Every week, another life is lost or broken. In the past twelve months, Manhattan saw 37 deaths and 124 serious injuries from crashes. The numbers are steady. The pain is not.

      On June 18, Salvador Nico-Garcia, 43, was thrown from his e-bike in Central Park after colliding with a pedestrian. He struck his head on the curb and died. The pedestrian walked away with a scraped hand. The NYPD is still investigating. Nico-Garcia was then flung from his bike and struck his head on the curb, the NYPD said.

      Just days earlier, a 74-year-old unlicensed SUV driver was arrested for killing Willow Hall, 53, in East Harlem. Hall was crossing Harlem River Drive. The driver stayed at the scene. Charges came six months later. Donald Pinnock was charged Sunday with aggravated unlicensed operation of a motor vehicle.

      The dead do not get a second chance.

      Who Pays the Price?

      Pedestrians, cyclists, children. In the last year, two children died. Six seniors over 75 died. The city’s most vulnerable are the first to fall. Cars and SUVs killed 38 people. Trucks and buses killed 15. Bikes killed 2. The street does not forgive.

      Leadership: Action or Delay?

      The city has tools. It does not always use them. Speed cameras now run 24/7. The city can set a 20 mph limit. But change is slow. Laws stall. Promises fade. Six months to charge an unlicensed killer. Years to fix a deadly crossing.

      Some leaders speak. Most wait.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every delay means another family grieves. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action, not words.

      Do not wait for another name on the list.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Manhattan?
      In the last period, Cars and SUVs killed 38 people and caused over 3,400 injuries. Trucks and Buses killed 15 and injured 373. Motorcycles and Mopeds caused 1 death and 219 injuries. Bikes caused 2 deaths and 466 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These deaths and injuries are not random. They happen on streets that can be made safer. Lower speeds, better crossings, and stronger enforcement save lives. Delay costs lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can set a 20 mph speed limit, redesign dangerous streets, and enforce laws against reckless driving. They can act now or wait for more deaths.
