      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan?

      Manhattan Streets, City’s Shame: End the Bloodshed Now

      Written July 6, 2025 (Revised July 6, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Manhattan: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 6, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Manhattan, the bodies do not stop. Since 2022, 112 people have died in crashes. Nearly 19,000 have been injured. In the last twelve months alone, 31 people lost their lives. Children, elders, cyclists, walkers. The city’s most vulnerable, broken on the street.

      Just weeks ago, a man riding an electric unicycle in Central Park was left in critical condition after a hit-and-run by a cyclist. Police said, “She was arrested Wednesday morning and charged with leaving the scene of an accident that caused a serious injury” (NY Daily News).

      On the same day, two men were killed by trains in separate Manhattan subway stations. “First responders rushed” to both scenes, but neither man survived (NY Daily News).

      The Numbers Are Brutal

      The violence is relentless. In the last year, 120 people in Manhattan suffered serious injuries. Cars and SUVs killed 38. Trucks and buses killed 15. Bikes killed 2. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1. The rest are numbers, but each number is a name, a family, a life cut short.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Failed to Do

      The city has power. It does not always use it. Albany passed Sammy’s Law, giving New York City the right to set its own speed limits. The city has started to lower some streets to 20 mph. But the default is still 25. The carnage continues.

      Council Member Erik Bottcher said, “The number of lives lost to traffic violence is simply unacceptable… 127 deaths in this calendar year alone is 127 too many” (transalt.org).

      Automated enforcement has expanded. Red-light cameras now cover more intersections. Speed cameras run 24/7. But the city drags its feet on redesigning deadly streets. Promises are made. Deadlines slip. The bodies pile up.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit citywide. Demand streets built for people, not for speed. Every day of delay is another life lost.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Manhattan?
      Cars and SUVs killed 38 people. Trucks and buses killed 15. Bikes killed 2. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1. Most deaths and injuries come from cars, SUVs, trucks, and buses. In the last year, 120 people suffered serious injuries in Manhattan crashes.
