      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan?

      E 15th and 2nd: another rider down. The count keeps climbing.

      Written September 2, 2025 (Revised September 2, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Manhattan: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 2, 2025

      Just before August, at E 15th and 2nd, a 65-year-old on a bike was hit and killed. The SUV kept straight. The rider did not. NYC Open Data records the death on Jul 29, 2025, CrashID 4831391.

      They were one of 126 people killed on Manhattan streets since Jan 1, 2022. In that span, the borough logged 43,554 crashes and 21,567 injuries, with 414 marked as serious, according to the same dataset’s window through Sep 2, 2025 (NYC Open Data).

      A driver arrest on the Queensboro Bridge did not change the math. “A driver was arrested following a horrific Ed Koch-Queensboro Bridge crash … that left a pedestrian dead,” police said, as reported by amNY.

      The slope is getting steeper

      The damage this year in Manhattan is worse than last. By year‑to‑date counts through Sep 2, crashes rose from 7,077 to 8,988 (up 27.0%), injuries from 3,627 to 4,800 (up 32.3%), and serious injuries from 74 to 97 (up 31.1%). Deaths ticked from 27 to 28 (up 3.7%) (NYC Open Data). The line does not break; it climbs.

      Names we elect; streets we bury on

      Manhattan falls under many districts. One of them is Council District 3, represented by Erik D. Bottcher. The overlapping Assembly AD 65 is represented by Grace Lee. The overlapping State Senate SD 27 is represented by Brian Kavanagh. The deaths and injuries above happened on their watch, among others. The record is public.

      Tools on the table

      New York has levers. A lower default speed limit. Real consequences for repeat speeders. Our action guide shows how to press for both, including a citywide residential 20 MPH limit and requiring intelligent speed assistance for the worst offenders. The steps are laid out here.

      This began with a rider at E 15th and 2nd. It ends, if it ends, when the numbers stop climbing. Start with the speed. Start now. Act.

We counted total crashes, total injuries, serious injuries, and total deaths during that window, and compared year-to-date (this year vs. last year) as shown in our PeriodStats.
