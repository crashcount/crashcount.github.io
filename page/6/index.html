      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in New York City?

      South Conduit, about 2:30 AM

      Written August 29, 2025 (Revised August 29, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      New York City: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 29, 2025

      About 2:30 AM on Aug 13, 2025, a 52‑year‑old man crossed South Conduit Avenue at 155 Street by JFK. A driver hit him and kept going. Police said, “The operator of the vehicle fled the scene after hitting the man.” ABC7 | NYC Open Data.

      He was one of 1,109 people killed on New York City streets since Jan 1, 2022, through Aug 29, 2025, per city crash records NYC Open Data.

      The count doesn’t rest

      Year to date, 185 people have died in crashes, compared with 197 by this point last year. Serious injuries are up: 511 so far, from 488 at the same time last year NYC Open Data.

      On the same day as the JFK hit‑and‑run, two men ordering at a Queens food truck were struck and killed; the driver died too CBS New York.

      A 21‑year‑old motorcyclist died on the Verrazzano‑Narrows Bridge. Investigators listed unsafe speed in the city’s crash log NYC Open Data.

      Wrong‑way lanes, broken bodies

      In Queens, a driver steered into oncoming traffic on the Clearview Expressway and rammed cars. A jury convicted him. In his own words: “I entered the Clearview Expressway in the wrong direction because I wanted to hurt people and I felt ‘liberated’ by what I had done.” amNY.

      The record shows what lawmakers did — and didn’t

      In June, the Legislature moved S 8344 to extend and fix NYC school speed‑zone laws Open States. Assembly Member Zohran Mamdani was recorded as excused on the June 17 action Open States.

      There’s a bill to stop the worst repeat speeders with mandatory speed limiters — the Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045C/A2299C). Lawmakers can advance it now. Details and contacts are here.

      Do one thing today

      Lower speeds save lives. Tell City Hall to set 20 MPH on local streets and press Albany to rein in repeat speeders. Start here.
