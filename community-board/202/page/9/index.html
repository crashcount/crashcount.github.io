      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx CB2?

      Crosswalk, then silence: another life lost at Leggett and Bruckner

      Written August 31, 2025 (Revised August 31, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx CB2: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 31, 2025

      Just after midnight at Leggett and Bruckner, a driver hit 32-year-old Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez in the crosswalk. She died at Lincoln Hospital. Police said the driver kept going.

      She is one of 11 people killed on Bronx Community Board 2 streets since 2022, alongside at least 1,649 injuries. The dead include 5 pedestrians and 1 cyclist. Cars and SUVs account for 4 pedestrian deaths and 5 serious pedestrian injuries; trucks and buses for 1 death and 3 serious injuries. NYC Open Data.

      Nights on the boulevard

      The deaths pile up at odd hours. At 3 AM, there are 2 deaths on record here. At 6 AM, there are 2 more. NYC Open Data.

      The danger clusters on familiar corridors. Westchester Avenue shows 2 deaths and 52 injuries. Bruckner Boulevard shows 271 injuries. NYC Open Data.

      On paper, simple failures repeat: failure to yield shows up with 20 injuries; disregarding signals with 9; distraction with 27. NYC Open Data.

      The line keeps ticking up

      Through Aug 31 this year, crashes in this district rose 12.8% versus last year-to-date (574 from 509). Deaths doubled from 1 to 2. Serious injuries rose from 1 to 6. NYC Open Data.

      Lopez de Rodriguez’s death was just after midnight on Aug 26, 2025, at Leggett Avenue and Bruckner Boulevard. Press reports identified her and said she was in the crosswalk when she was struck. ABC7 | NY Daily News.

      Who moves, who stalls

      Bronx CB2’s council member is Rafael Salamanca Jr. In a 2023 oversight hearing, he pressed DOT on leadership gaps: “There is no borough commissioner in the borough of the Bronx… Please speed it up.” Streetsblog NYC.

      He also pushed the city to crack down on last‑mile harms: “It gets to a point where you have to use your power of voting to get the attention of the city.” Gothamist.

      At the state level, Senator Jose Serrano co‑sponsored and voted yes in committee on S 4045, a bill to require speed limiters for repeat dangerous drivers. Senator Luis Sepúlveda also voted yes. Assembly Member Amanda Septimo co‑sponsored the companion A 2299. Open States.

      Fix the corners where people die

      Start where the bodies fall. Daylight the legs of Westchester Avenue and Bruckner. Add hardened centerlines and protected turns. Give long leading pedestrian intervals at Leggett and along Westchester. Target overnight enforcement on Bruckner when deaths recur in the early hours. Re‑route and calm truck turns at problem corners.

      Then end the source of speed. Lower the city’s default speed and fit the worst drivers’ cars with governors. The bills and the power exist. Albany moved on repeat speeders; the city must use its tools now. See how to push them here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where did this happen?
      Bronx Community Board 2 covers Hunts Point and Longwood. The fatal Aug 26, 2025 crash was at Leggett Avenue and Bruckner Boulevard. Sources: ABC7; NYC Open Data crash record 4837606.
      ▸ How many people have been killed here since 2022?
      Eleven people have been killed on CB2 streets from 2022-01-01 to 2025-08-31. That includes five pedestrians and one cyclist. Source: NYC Open Data (Crash, Persons datasets).
      ▸ What patterns stand out locally?
      Overnight hours are deadly (2 deaths at 3 AM and 2 at 6 AM). Westchester Avenue and Bruckner Boulevard see heavy harm. Failure to yield, running signals, and distraction recur. Source: NYC Open Data (hourly and corridor tallies in our analysis).
      ▸ Who represents this area, and what have they done?
      Council Member Rafael Salamanca Jr. has pressed DOT on Bronx leadership and backed oversight of last‑mile logistics. State Senator Jose Serrano co‑sponsored and voted yes on S 4045; Assembly Member Amanda Septimo co‑sponsored A 2299, the companion. Sources: Streetsblog; Gothamist; Open States.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95, Persons f55k-p6yu, Vehicles bm4k-52h4), filtering for crashes within Bronx Community Board 2 between 2022-01-01 and 2025-08-31. We tallied deaths, injuries, time-of-day, modes, and locations using DOT-provided geocoding. Data was accessed Aug 31, 2025. You can explore the base datasets here.

      Int 0346-2024 Salamanca votes yes to decriminalize jaywalking, boosting pedestrian safety citywide.
      ▸

      Sep 26 - Council ends jaywalking penalties. Pedestrians now cross anywhere, any time. No summons. Law strips drivers of excuses. Streets shift. Power tilts to people on foot.

      Int 0346-2024, now enacted, rewrites New York’s pedestrian rules. The bill, heard by the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure, passed on September 26, 2024, and became law on October 26, 2024. Its title: “A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to pedestrian crossing guidelines and right of way.” Sponsored by Council Member Tiffany Cabán and co-sponsored by Narcisse, Won, Hanif, Hudson, and Restler, the law lets pedestrians cross streets anywhere, even against signals. No more tickets for crossing outside crosswalks. The Department of Transportation must educate all street users on new rights and responsibilities. The law removes a tool police used to target walkers. It shifts blame from people on foot to the system and those behind the wheel.
