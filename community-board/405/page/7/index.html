      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB5?

      Twenty-Two Dead, Thousands Hurt—Queens Streets Still Bleed

      Written July 12, 2025

      Queens CB5: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      The Toll Mounts, Week by Week

      In Queens CB5, the numbers do not lie. Since January 2022, twenty-two people have died in crashes. Thirty more suffered injuries so severe they changed lives. Over 2,600 have been hurt. These are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, workers, elders. The disaster is slow, but it does not stop.

      Just this spring, a 54-year-old cyclist was killed by a turning box truck on Juniper Boulevard North. The crash left the rider ejected, crushed, dead at the scene. The driver survived. The bike was demolished. No one walks away from that. The data says: one more life gone, one more family broken. NYC Open Data

      On Maurice Avenue, a 47-year-old man on a bike was killed by a truck. He had no chance. The truck kept going straight. The cyclist did not. The record shows: crush injuries, apparent death, ejected.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did the most harm. They caused 1 death and 333 injuries to pedestrians and cyclists. Trucks and buses killed 3, injuring 27 more. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1, injured 12. Bikes injured 9. The street is a gauntlet. The odds are not in your favor.

      Leadership: Action and Delay

      Council Member Robert Holden has backed bills for raised speed reducers at camera locations and for safer crossings. But he also voted no on ending jaywalking enforcement—a law that would have protected the most vulnerable from police crackdowns and blame. The council passed it anyway. The law now says: streets are for people, not just cars.

      Holden did support new protected bike lanes on Cypress Avenue, a rare move. His office said, “This particular lane benefits those biking to and from the Ridgewood Reservoir and addresses a broader quality of life issue along Cypress Avenue.”

      But the pace is slow. The bodies pile up. The city waits.

      The Voices We Cannot Ignore

      After a bus crash in Queens, a passenger said, “I was all the way in the back and all of a sudden the bus hit the curb, I guess, jumped the curb, I went this way and that way and banged into the side of the bus.”

      Another rider, holding her baby, said, “That would be scary. I’ll be more cautious of my surroundings.”

      Call to Action: Demand More, Now

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand raised crosswalks, protected bike lanes, and real accountability for repeat offenders. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB5 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 30, assembly district AD 28 and state senate district SD 12.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB5?
      It includes the Maspeth, Ridgewood, Glendale, Middle Village, Mount Olivet & All Faiths Cemeteries, Middle Village Cemetery, St. John Cemetery, and Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (North) neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 30, District 32, and District 34, Assembly Districts AD 28, AD 30, AD 37, AD 38, and AD 39, and State Senate Districts SD 12, SD 15, and SD 18.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB5?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 1 death and 333 injuries. Trucks and buses killed 3 and injured 27. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1 and injured 12. Bikes injured 9. The streets remain dangerous for anyone not inside a car.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These are not random accidents. They are the result of policy choices—speed limits, street design, and enforcement. Safer streets, lower speeds, and better infrastructure can prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, install raised crosswalks, build protected bike lanes, and hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable. They can vote for laws that protect people, not just cars.

      Int 1173-2025
      Holden co-sponsors helmet mandate bill, likely reducing overall cyclist safety.
      ▸

      Council bill Int 1173-2025 would force all city cyclists to wear helmets or face a $50 fine. The measure, now in committee, targets riders not already covered by helmet laws. The city’s car carnage continues. Lawmakers focus on headgear, not street danger.

      Int 1173-2025 sits with the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure after introduction on January 23, 2025. The bill, titled 'A Local Law to amend the administrative code of New York, in relation to requiring bicyclists to wear protective headgear,' would require every cyclist not already mandated by other laws to wear a helmet.
