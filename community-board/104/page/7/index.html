      No More Waiting, No More Blood: Manhattan Streets Demand Action

      Written July 18, 2025 (Revised July 18, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Manhattan CB4: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 18, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      In Manhattan CB4, the toll does not stop. Twelve people have died on these streets since 2022. Another 1,573 have been injured. Thirty-five were hurt so badly they may never walk the same again. The numbers are not just numbers. They are bodies in crosswalks, bikes twisted on the curb, families waiting for a phone that will never ring.

      Cars and SUVs lead the count. They killed two, left 315 more battered or broken. Trucks and buses took two more lives. Bikes and mopeds are not blameless—two serious injuries, fifty hurt by bikes, twenty by mopeds and motorcycles. But the weight of steel does most of the damage. See the data.

      This Week’s Wounds

      The violence is not history. It is now. Just days ago, a city worker was slashed by an e-bike rider after a near miss at Broadway and Cedar. The DOT called it an “abhorrent assault”. A witness said, “They weren’t even in the bike lane, they were parked on the corner, fixing the light or the sign or something.” The worker bled on the sidewalk. The cyclist fled. No one has been arrested.

      On July 15, a van loaded with 76 propane tanks and 75 gallons of gasoline crashed in Midtown. The food cart it was towing broke loose and slammed into a parked car with a woman and child inside. Both went to the hospital. The driver faces reckless endangerment charges. Hazmat teams emptied the van.

      Leadership: Progress and Gaps

      Local leaders have moved. Senator Brad Hoylman-Sigal voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, a bill to force repeat speeders to install speed limiters. He also co-sponsored bills for more camera enforcement and safer bike lanes. Assembly Member Tony Simone co-sponsored bills to expand speed cameras and hold reckless drivers accountable. But both have also backed bills that could weaken camera enforcement. The fight is not finished.

      What Next? Demand More

      Every crash is preventable. Every delay is a choice. Call your council member. Call your state reps. Tell them: No more waiting. No more loopholes. No more blood.

      Find your representatives and take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Manhattan CB4 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 3, assembly district AD 75 and state senate district SD 47.
      ▸ Which areas are in Manhattan CB4?
      It includes the Chelsea-Hudson Yards and Hell’S Kitchen neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 3 and District 6, Assembly Districts AD 67 and AD 75, and State Senate Districts SD 28 and SD 47.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Manhattan CB4?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 8 serious injuries, 307 other injuries. Trucks and buses: 2 deaths, 1 serious injury, 29 other injuries. Bikes: 2 serious injuries, 48 other injuries. Mopeds and motorcycles: 2 serious injuries, 18 other injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Every crash is preventable. The numbers show a pattern, not fate. Safer street design and enforcement can stop the next one.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws to slow cars, expand speed cameras, redesign streets for safety, and hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable. They can also oppose bills that weaken enforcement.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
