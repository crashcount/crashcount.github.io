      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan CB4?

      Manhattan’s Streets Are Killing Fields—Who Will Stop the Bloodshed?

      Written July 19, 2025

      Manhattan CB4: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 19, 2025

      The Blood on the Asphalt

      In Manhattan CB4, the violence does not stop. Since 2022, 12 people have died on these streets. 1,573 have been injured. Thirty-five of those wounds were serious enough to change a life forever (NYC Open Data).

      Just last week, a van crashed in Midtown. Firefighters found 76 propane tanks and 15 fuel canisters inside. The driver was charged with reckless endangerment. The street could have gone up in flames. The city called it a violation. The rest of us call it luck (CBS New York).

      The Most Vulnerable Pay the Price

      Pedestrians and cyclists do not walk away. Cars and trucks have killed four. Bikes have left two with serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds have done the same. The numbers are not just numbers. They are bodies in the crosswalk, bikes twisted under bumpers, families waiting for a call that never comes.

      A city worker was slashed by an e-bike rider after a near miss. The DOT called it an “abhorrent assault” and said, “Our thoughts are with our employee and their loved ones, and we hope they make a quick recovery” (amny). The worker was fixing a sign. The cyclist fled. The street was quiet again, but nothing was fixed.

      Leadership: Votes, Bills, and the Silence Between

      Local leaders have moved. Senator Brad Hoylman-Sigal voted yes on a bill to force repeat speeders to install speed limiters. Assembly Member Tony Simone co-sponsored a bill to expand speed camera enforcement. These are steps. But the blood keeps coming.

      The city has the power to lower speed limits. It has not done it everywhere. Cameras catch some, but not all. Enforcement comes down hardest on those with the least power. The rest slip through.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every death is a choice made by someone in power. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against the drivers who kill and maim. Do not wait for another body in the road. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Manhattan CB4 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 3, assembly district AD 75 and state senate district SD 47.
      ▸ Which areas are in Manhattan CB4?
      It includes the Chelsea-Hudson Yards and Hell’S Kitchen neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 3 and District 6, Assembly Districts AD 67 and AD 75, and State Senate Districts SD 28 and SD 47.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Manhattan CB4?
      Cars and Trucks: 4 deaths, 8 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 1 death, 2 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 2 serious injuries. The toll falls hardest on those outside a vehicle.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are not fate. They are the result of choices—speed limits, street design, enforcement. Every injury and death can be prevented with the right action.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand camera enforcement, and pass laws targeting repeat dangerous drivers. They can refuse to delay or water down safety measures.

      A 7979
      Simone co-sponsors bill to boost safety with speed limiters for repeat offenders.
      ▸

      Assembly bill A 7979 targets reckless drivers. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers a speed limiter. Lawmakers move to curb repeat danger. No more unchecked speeding. Streets demand it.

      Assembly bill A 7979, now in sponsorship, sits before the New York State Assembly. Introduced August 18, 2023, it 'requires the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices if a driver accumulates eleven or more points on their driving record during an eighteen month period, or receives 6 speed camera or red light camera tickets during a twelve month period.' Assemblymember Emily Gallagher leads as primary sponsor, joined by Rebecca Seawright, Andrew Hevesi, Tony Simone, Jo Anne Simon, and others. The bill aims to force chronic speeders to slow down. No safety analyst note is available, but the intent is clear: rein in repeat offenders, protect those outside the car.
