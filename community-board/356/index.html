      Brooklyn Bleeds: Speed Kills, Leaders Stall, Lives Shatter

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, standardized and fixed inline links, cleaned citations.)

      Brooklyn CB56: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Behind the Bloodshed

      Six dead. Four hundred twenty injured. Three with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. That is the toll of traffic violence in Brooklyn CB56 since 2022. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care about hope or promises. They only count the bodies and the broken.

      In the last year alone, one person died and 134 were injured in 167 crashes. Children were not spared. Thirteen under 18 were hurt. One person between 35 and 44 did not come home. NYC Open Data

      Who Pays the Price

      Pedestrians and cyclists remain exposed. In the last three years, cars and SUVs killed three people and left four with moderate injuries. Trucks and buses did not kill, but they did not save. Bikes caused no deaths here, but the threat is constant. The road does not forgive.

      The Belt Parkway is a scar across the district. Each month, new crashes. Each year, new names for the list. NYC Open Data

      What Has Been Done—and What Has Not

      Local leaders have tools. They have the power to lower speed limits, redesign streets, and expand enforcement. Yet the carnage continues. Sammy’s Law now lets the city set safer speeds. The city can act. The council can act. The mayor can act. But action delayed is blood on the pavement.

      Speed cameras save lives. But their future is always in doubt. Each year, Albany must renew the law. Each year, advocates beg for what should be automatic. NYC Open Data

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand permanent speed cameras. Demand streets that do not kill. Do not wait for another name on the list. NYC Open Data

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn CB56?
      Brooklyn CB56 covers the neighborhoods of Barren Island-Floyd Bennett Field, Jamaica Bay (West), and Shirley Chisholm State Park. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 42 and District 46, Assembly Districts AD 59 and AD 60, and State Senate Districts SD 19 and SD 22.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn CB56?
      Cars and SUVs: Cars and SUVs killed three people and caused four moderate injuries to pedestrians. Trucks and Buses: No deaths or serious injuries reported. Motorcycles and Mopeds: No deaths or injuries reported. Bikes: No deaths or serious injuries reported.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are not just accidents. They are the result of policy choices—speed limits, street design, and enforcement. Change the policy, change the outcome.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      Local politicians can lower speed limits, expand speed camera programs, redesign dangerous streets, and push for stronger enforcement. They can act now, or they can wait for more deaths.
