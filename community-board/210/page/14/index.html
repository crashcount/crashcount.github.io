      Bronx Streets Bleed—Leaders Stall, Neighbors Die

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, and cleaned citations.)

      Bronx CB10: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll: Lives Lost, Bodies Broken

      The streets of Bronx CB10 do not forgive. In the last twelve months, one person died and 463 were injured in crashes. Five suffered injuries so severe they may never walk the same. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, parents, children. They are the man who never made it home, the child whose bike lies twisted in the gutter.

      Cars and SUVs did most of the harm. In three years, six people died and 1,428 were injured. Trucks and SUVs killed three. Motorcycles and bikes did not kill, but the machines that fill our streets with steel and speed did. The violence is steady, relentless, and it does not care who you are.

      The Human Cost: Names, Not Numbers

      A Navy veteran, Keino Campbell, was shot and killed after a minor crash near Co-op City. His father urged drivers to use insurance, not violence. His mother said she was heartbroken, wishing her son had the chance to do great things.

      A 32-year-old man lost his leg trying to stop a carjacker in Pelham Bay. He was left in the street, bleeding, while the thief sped away. The Bronx saw a rise in car thefts even as the rest of the city saw fewer.

      Leadership: Words, Laws, and Silence

      Local leaders have tools. Sammy’s Law lets the city lower speed limits to 20 mph. Speed cameras cut speeding by more than half. But action is slow. Laws stall. Cameras face expiration. The city has the power to act, but every day of delay is another day of risk.

      What have your leaders done? Some have spoken. Some have voted. But the carnage continues. Reward those who fight for safer streets. Call out those who stall or stay silent.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Join groups like Transportation Alternatives and Families for Safe Streets. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Bronx CB10 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 13, assembly district AD 82 and state senate district SD 34.
      ▸ Which areas are in Bronx CB10?
      It includes the Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Co-Op City, Hart Island, and Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 12, District 13, and District 18, Assembly District AD 82, and State Senate Districts SD 34 and SD 36.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx CB10?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 50+ injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 1 moderate injury. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 injuries. Most deaths and serious injuries come from cars, SUVs, and trucks. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The violence on our streets is not random. It is the result of policy, street design, and enforcement choices. Crashes can be prevented.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed camera programs, redesign dangerous intersections, and push for laws that protect people walking and biking. They can act now, not later.
