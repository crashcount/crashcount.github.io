      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx CB10?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—Leaders Stall

      Written July 14, 2025

      Bronx CB10: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 14, 2025

      The Recent Toll: Lives Lost, Bodies Broken

      In Bronx CB10, the road does not forgive. Since January 2022, six people have died and 27 have been left with serious injuries. The number of the wounded is higher—1,510 injured in 2,621 crashes. Each number is a life changed. Each crash is a story that ends in blood or fear.

      Just days ago, a 71-year-old woman died in Co-op City. A 79-year-old driver hit two cars and a pole. Seven others were hurt. “I saw one lady was out on the ground. They was giving her medical attention, checking her body. She was laid out,” said Samuel Cherry. The street was quiet after. The cause is still under investigation. No arrests. No answers. Only loss.

      Who Bears the Brunt?

      Cars and SUVs do most of the harm. They killed three people and left 137 with minor injuries, 47 with moderate, and six with serious wounds. Trucks and buses killed one, injured 13 more. Motorcycles and mopeds hurt seven. Bikes, two. The dead do not care what hit them. The living remember.

      Local Leaders: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Some leaders act. State Senator Nathalia Fernández voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, forcing repeat speeders to install speed-limiting tech. Assembly Member Michael Benedetto voted to extend school speed zones, keeping cameras near schools. These are steps, not solutions.

      But Council Member Kristy Marmorato has fought to keep parking mandates and called congestion pricing a “cash grab.” She stands with drivers, not the dead. The streets stay wide for cars, narrow for children.

      The Call: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash that kills or maims could be stopped. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand the end of parking mandates that choke the streets with cars. Demand that repeat speeders lose their right to endanger your family. Do not wait for another name to be carved into stone.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Bronx CB10 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 13, assembly district AD 82 and state senate district SD 34.
      ▸ Which areas are in Bronx CB10?
      It includes the Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Co-Op City, Hart Island, and Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 12, District 13, and District 18, Assembly District AD 82, and State Senate Districts SD 34 and SD 36.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx CB10?
      Cars and SUVs: 3 deaths, 137 minor injuries, 47 moderate injuries, 6 serious injuries. Trucks and buses: 1 death, 13 minor injuries, 3 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury. Motorcycles and mopeds: 0 deaths, 6 minor injuries, 1 moderate injury. Bikes: 0 deaths, 2 minor injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These crashes are the result of choices—speed, street design, enforcement. They can be prevented with better policy and action.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, end parking mandates, support speed cameras, and pass laws that target repeat dangerous drivers. They can put people before cars.

The bill extends the hours that speed cameras operate in New York City school zones. Sponsored by Senator Gounardes (District 26) with co-sponsors Biaggi, Cleare, Gianaris, Hoylman, Jackson, Kavanagh, Krueger, Myrie, Persaud, Ramos, Rivera, and Sepulveda, the measure drew strong support.
