      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB12?

      Queens Bleeds: Reckless Streets, Broken Lives—Who Will Stop the Killing?

      Written July 9, 2025

      Queens CB12: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 9, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      Seven dead. Fourteen left with injuries that will never heal. In the last twelve months, Queens CB12 has seen 2,166 crashes. The numbers are blunt. Pedestrians, cyclists, children—no one is spared.

      A mother, 45, killed crossing 111th Avenue on Christmas night. A 23-year-old man, dead at Rockaway Boulevard. A 19-year-old, crushed on 188th Street. The list runs long. The pain runs deeper. NYC Open Data.

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. In three years, cars and SUVs have taken four lives and left hundreds wounded. Trucks, buses, motorcycles, mopeds—they all play their part. The street is a gauntlet.

      The Faces in the Wreckage

      On July 8, a crane slammed into a city bus in Queens Village. The windows shattered. Four people hurt. Emily McKinnon was in the back. She said, “We didn’t see the crane coming… then the crane just hits us.” She ran to help the others. “Two of them were crying already. They were shaken up.”

      On the Belt Parkway, a BMW hit a divider, flew into oncoming traffic, and burned. Two young people died. No one in the car wore a seatbelt. The brother of one victim said, “Jewel was a loving, energetic mother who dreamed of entering the medical field so she could help others the way she always helped others.”

      Leadership: Progress and Paralysis

      Council Member Nantasha Williams has voted for bills to daylight intersections, build greenways, and require traffic calming near seniors. She co-sponsored laws for better lighting and safer street design. But some bills—like the one for pedestrian lighting—sit stalled in committee. The city studies. The streets wait.

      State Senator Leroy Comrie voted for complete street design, but opposed the Idaho Stop bill, citing concerns for seniors. Assembly Member Alicia Hyndman has not led on major safety bills. The pace is slow. The dead cannot wait.

      What Now: Demand Action

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every delay is another family broken. Call your council member. Call your state senator. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real street redesigns.

      Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB12 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 27, assembly district AD 29 and state senate district SD 14.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB12?
      It includes the Jamaica, South Jamaica, Baisley Park, Springfield Gardens (North)-Rochdale Village, St. Albans, and Hollis neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 23, District 27, and District 28, Assembly Districts AD 29, AD 32, and AD 33, and State Senate Districts SD 10, SD 11, and SD 14.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB12?
      SUVs and Sedans: 4 deaths, 568 minor injuries, 175 moderate injuries, 12 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 0 deaths, 38 minor injuries, 10 moderate injuries, 2 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 6 minor injuries, 8 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 9 minor injuries, 0 moderate injuries, 1 serious injury.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The numbers show a pattern. Speed, street design, and enforcement shape these outcomes. Crashes can be prevented.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and fund laws for lower speed limits, daylighting, protected bike lanes, and safer crossings. They can push for enforcement that targets dangerous driving, not vulnerable road users.
