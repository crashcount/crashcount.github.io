      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB12?

      Queens Dies While City Waits

      Written July 7, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Queens CB12: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 7, 2025

      The Death Count Rises

      In Queens CB12, the numbers do not lie. Twenty people killed. Thirty-five left with serious injuries. Over 8,000 crashes since 2022, according to NYC Open Data. The violence is steady. It does not pause for holidays or rain. It does not care about age. Children, elders, workers—no one is spared.

      Just this spring, a baby boy was crushed between two SUVs on Linden Boulevard. A 45-year-old woman died crossing 111th Avenue, struck by a driver who did not see her. A 19-year-old was killed on his motorcycle at 90th Avenue and 188th Street. The city records the deaths. The families keep the grief.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the harm. Four dead, 12 seriously hurt, hundreds more injured by these vehicles alone. Trucks and buses add to the toll. Motorcycles and mopeds, too, leave bodies broken. Bikes are not blameless, but the numbers are small—one serious injury, no deaths. The street is a gauntlet, and the biggest machines win.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      Local leaders have taken some steps. Council Member Nantasha Williams voted to legalize jaywalking, ending a law that punished the vulnerable and did nothing to stop cars from killing. She co-sponsored bills for better pedestrian lighting and solar crosswalks, but these remain stuck in committee. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph now. It has not.

      The bills pile up. The deaths do too. “It was unclear what caused the driver to lose control but police said speed may have been an issue,” said the NYPD after a BMW vaulted a median and left six people hurt. The city studies. The families wait.

      The Call

      Every crash is preventable. Every death is a failure. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action, not studies. The street will not wait. Neither should you.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB12 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 27, assembly district AD 29 and state senate district SD 14.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB12?
      It includes the Jamaica, South Jamaica, Baisley Park, Springfield Gardens (North)-Rochdale Village, St. Albans, and Hollis neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 23, District 27, and District 28, Assembly Districts AD 29, AD 32, and AD 33, and State Senate Districts SD 10, SD 11, and SD 14.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB12?
      Cars and SUVs: 4 pedestrian deaths, 12 serious injuries, hundreds more injured. Trucks and Buses: No pedestrian deaths, but 2 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: No pedestrian deaths, 1 serious injury. Bikes: 1 serious injury, no deaths. Cars and SUVs do most of the harm.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents?
      No. Every crash is preventable. Every death is a failure of policy, design, or enforcement. The numbers show a pattern, not chance.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, pass and fund bills for safer crossings and lighting, and demand rapid street redesigns. They can stop delaying and start acting.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
