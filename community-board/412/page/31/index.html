      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB12?

      Queens Streets Bleed: How Many More Must Die Before We Act?

      Written July 2, 2025

      Queens CB12: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 2, 2025

      The Death Count Rises

      In Queens CB12, the numbers do not lie. Twenty people have died on these streets since 2022. Four thousand eight hundred thirty-six have been injured. Thirty-four suffered injuries so severe they will never be the same. The dead do not get a second chance. The living carry scars.

      Just last week, on June 26, 2025, Shaun Lagredelle was killed. He was riding a scooter west on 116th Avenue. A van turned left. Lagredelle was thrown from the scooter and died at the hospital. There were no arrests. The investigation drags on. Police said, “As the van attempted to turn left onto Nashville Boulevard, it collided with the scooter, immediately propelling Lagredelle off the device.”

      Two days earlier, two moped riders collided with a BMW in Jamaica. Both were injured. The street is unforgiving. The NYPD said, “two people were riding the moped when it collided with a BMW.”

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and the Vulnerable

      SUVs and cars do most of the harm. In the last three years, they killed four pedestrians and left hundreds more injured. Trucks and buses injured dozens. Motorcycles and mopeds brought more pain, but the biggest threat rolls on four wheels. The young are not spared. In the last year, 143 people under 18 were hurt. Three people aged 18–24 died.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Local leaders have taken some steps. Council Member Nantasha Williams co-sponsored bills for better lighting and safer crossings. She voted to legalize jaywalking, ending a law that blamed the victim and not the driver. But the pace is slow. Promises pile up. Streets stay dangerous.

      The law now says pedestrians can cross where they need to. But the cars keep coming. The city studies, debates, delays. The dead wait for nothing.

      Act Now: Demand Real Change

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected crossings, daylighted corners, and real enforcement against reckless drivers. Every day of delay is another family broken.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB12 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 27, assembly district AD 29 and state senate district SD 14.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB12?
      It includes the Jamaica, South Jamaica, Baisley Park, Springfield Gardens (North)-Rochdale Village, St. Albans, and Hollis neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 23, District 27, and District 28, Assembly Districts AD 29, AD 32, and AD 33, and State Senate Districts SD 10, SD 11, and SD 14.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB12?
      Cars and SUVs: 4 deaths, 12 serious injuries, and hundreds of injuries. Trucks and buses: 0 deaths, 2 serious injuries, and dozens of injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 1 serious injury, and several injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 1 serious injury, and minor injuries. Cars and SUVs are the main killers.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The pattern is clear. Most deaths and injuries come from cars and trucks. These are preventable with lower speeds, safer street design, and real enforcement.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can set a 20 mph speed limit, build protected crossings, daylight corners, and pass laws that put safety first. They can stop blaming victims and start holding dangerous drivers accountable.
