      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan CB10?

      Another Child Dead on Lenox. How Many More Before We Act?

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Manhattan CB10: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Blood on Lenox and 135th

      A child crossed with the signal. She did not make it to the other side. A 3-year-old girl was killed by an SUV making a left turn at Lenox and 135th. Her mother was hurt beside her. The street was busy. The driver kept going east. The girl’s name is not in the data. The loss is written in numbers: one dead, one injured. NYC Open Data.

      In the past year, Manhattan CB10 saw 1 death and 11 serious injuries from crashes. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians are hit again and again. In the last 12 months, 449 people were hurt. The numbers do not heal. They do not bring back the child.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and Broken Promises

      SUVs and cars did most of the harm. They killed, they maimed, they left families with empty beds. In three years, 5 people died. Pedestrians and cyclists take the blows.

      A cyclist was killed by a bus at Lenox and 138th. A pedestrian was crushed by a turning sedan. A moped rider lost an arm. The stories repeat. The city counts the bodies. The streets do not change fast enough.

      What Leaders Have Done — and Not Done

      Some leaders have moved. Some have not. State Senator Cordell Cleare voted yes to curb repeat speeders, backing a bill to force speed limiters on the worst offenders. She also voted to extend school speed zones. State Senator Cleare voted yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.

      Council Member Yusef Salaam sponsored a bill to pilot high-visibility pavement markings at deadly corners. Council Member Salaam sponsored a bill to pilot high-visibility pavement markings, boosting street safety.

      But the pace is slow. The streets are not safe. Every week, another crash. Another family left to grieve.

      The Call: No More Waiting

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected crossings. Demand action for the dead and the living. Do not wait for another child’s name to be lost in the data.
