      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB7?

      Queens Streets Bleed While Leaders Stall

      Written July 9, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens CB7: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 9, 2025

      The Toll in Queens CB7

      Two people are dead. Over a thousand are hurt. In the past year alone, Queens CB7 saw 1,022 injuries and 38 serious injuries from 1,715 crashes. Two lives ended. The numbers do not bleed, but the streets do.

      The old and the young are not spared. In the last twelve months, 88 children were injured. Two people over 75 died. The pain is not spread evenly. It finds the vulnerable first.

      Recent Crashes: No End in Sight

      Just months ago, a 78-year-old woman was killed by an SUV on Parsons Boulevard. She was not at an intersection. She did not make it home. NYC Open Data

      A year before, a 63-year-old cyclist was struck and killed by a left-turning SUV on Bowne Street. The cause: driver inattention. There was no second chance. NYC Open Data

      Leadership: Small Steps, Slow Change

      Local leaders have moved, but not fast enough. Council Member Sandra Ung co-sponsored bills for speed humps and raised speed reducers. She backed more lighting for step streets and solar crosswalks. These are steps, not leaps.

      But when the Council voted to end jaywalking enforcement—a law that stops blaming the victim—Council Member Vickie Paladino voted no. She stood against a proven safety improvement.

      Assembly Member Ron Kim called out the danger on College Point Boulevard after a toddler was killed: “We’ve always recognized there was a problem on this block.” Streetsblog NYC

      The Road Ahead: Demand More

      The disaster is slow, but it is not fate. Every crash is a policy failure. Every delay is another family broken. Call your council member. Call your assembly member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people walking and biking.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      John Liu Supports Exempting Dollar Vans From Congestion Pricing
      ▸

      Council Member Marte and others push to exempt dollar vans from Manhattan congestion tolls. They argue the vans are vital for Asian communities. Without exemption, fares rise. Commutes get harder. The MTA has not taken a side.

      On September 24, 2023, Council Member Christopher Marte joined eight other elected officials in urging the MTA to exempt dollar vans from the upcoming congestion pricing plan. The letter, signed by Marte and state legislators, was sent to the Traffic Mobility Review Board. The officials wrote, 'Commuter vans offer the Chinese communities in Manhattan, Queens, and Brooklyn a much-needed service that is not being provided by other forms of public transportation: safe, language-accessible, and culturally competent means for people to access jobs, resources, and family.' They argue that tolling these vans—serving Flatbush Avenue, eastern Queens, and the city’s Chinatowns—would raise fares and cut off vital connections for Asian New Yorkers. Assembly Member Grace Lee said, 'They should be exempt from tolls under the congestion pricing plan.' The MTA has not taken a position on the exemption.

      Int 1142-2023
      Ung co-sponsors bill boosting tow pound capacity, improving street safety.
      ▸

      Council filed a bill to force NYPD tow pounds to match enforcement needs. The law demanded public reports on towing and storage. The session ended before action. Streets stay clogged. Danger remains for those on foot and bike.

      Int 1142-2023 was introduced in the Committee on Public Safety on August 3, 2023. The bill required the NYPD to run tow pound facilities with enough capacity to deter illegal parking and traffic violations. It also mandated annual public reports on tow pound use and enforcement, disaggregated by precinct. The matter title reads: 'A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to police department tow pound capacity.' Council Members Kamillah Hanks (primary sponsor), Oswald Feliz, Sandra Ung, Lincoln Restler, Shaun Abreu, Farah N. Louis, Mercedes Narcisse, and Amanda Farías sponsored the bill. The bill was filed at the end of session on December 31, 2023, with no further action. No safety analyst assessed the impact for vulnerable road users. The city’s failure to expand towing leaves illegal cars on sidewalks and crosswalks. Pedestrians and cyclists pay the price.
