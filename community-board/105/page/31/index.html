      No More Excuses: Manhattan’s Streets Are Killing Us

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 6, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Manhattan CB5: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      A man steps off the curb. An SUV turns left. The man does not make it to the other side. In the last twelve months, seven people died on the streets of Manhattan CB5. Twenty-two more were left with serious injuries. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care about age. Three of the dead were over 65. Two were under 35. The sidewalk does not forgive.

      Cars, SUVs, and trucks did most of the killing. In the last three years, SUVs alone took six lives and left dozens more broken. Buses, taxis, and bikes added to the count. The city records the wounds. It does not record the empty chairs at dinner tables.

      Leadership: Promises and Pressure

      The city talks about Vision Zero. It talks about safety. It talks about the future. But the present is still full of pain. Mayor Adams says, “One life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” The words are right. The pace is slow.

      The NYPD cracks down on cyclists and delivery workers. They write criminal summonses for riding on sidewalks or having the wrong bike. Commissioner Jessica Tisch states, “When it comes to traffic safety, compliance is not optional.” The crackdown does not stop the cars. It does not slow the SUVs. It does not protect the old woman in the crosswalk.

      The Fight for Safer Streets

      The city has started to act. Fifth Avenue will get wider sidewalks. The mayor promises $400 million for people on foot. But the work is not done. The blood is not dry.

      Advocates must not wait. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for the most vulnerable. Every day of delay is another day of loss.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Manhattan CB5 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 4, assembly district AD 75 and state senate district SD 28.
      ▸ Which areas are in Manhattan CB5?
      It includes the Midtown South-Flatiron-Union Square and Midtown-Times Square neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 2, District 3, District 4, and District 6, Assembly Districts AD 73, AD 74, and AD 75, and State Senate Districts SD 28 and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Manhattan CB5?
      Cars and Trucks: 6 deaths, 155 moderate injuries, 12 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 19 moderate injuries, 2 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 39 moderate injuries, 7 serious injuries. (Source: NYC Open Data)
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The patterns are clear. Speed, distraction, and dangerous streets kill. These deaths are preventable.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign streets for safety, and enforce laws that protect people walking and biking. They can stop blaming the vulnerable and start protecting them.
