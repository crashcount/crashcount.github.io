      Blood on the Asphalt: Manhattan Streets Still Kill

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      Manhattan CB5: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      Fourteen dead. Fifty-four left with wounds that will not heal. That is the count in Manhattan CB5 since 2022. The numbers do not flinch. In the last year alone, seven people died. Three were over 65. No children died. Six hundred eighty-four injured.

      A 34-year-old man tried to cross 7th Avenue at 36th Street. An SUV hit him. He died at the intersection. The cause: driver inattention. The street did not change. The driver kept going. The city kept counting. NYC Open Data

      The Human Cost

      A cyclist in the West Village was thrown from his bike. “All my body was blood and the sweater,” he said from his hospital bed. “All my memory is gone. When I woke up I was in the ambulance” ABC7. He needed sixteen hours of surgery. The driver ran a red light and fled in reverse. The city called it a hit-and-run. The cyclist called it survival.

      On 34th Street, the city debates whether to give the street to buses and people or keep it for cars. “It’s time to prioritize pedestrians and transit users, and not the minority of residents who use their personal cars to get around,” said one advocate at a public meeting West Side Spirit. The board voted yes. The cars keep coming.

      Leadership: Action and Delay

      Local leaders have made moves. The community board backed the 34th Street busway. The city passed Sammy’s Law, giving itself the power to lower speed limits. But the limit is not yet lowered. The cameras that catch speeders are at risk of going dark unless Albany acts. The city says it is committed. The streets say otherwise.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand streets that do not kill. Every day of delay is another name on the list. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Manhattan CB5 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 4, assembly district AD 75 and state senate district SD 28. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in Manhattan CB5?
      It includes the Midtown South-Flatiron-Union Square and Midtown-Times Square neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 2, District 3, District 4, and District 6, Assembly Districts AD 73, AD 74, and AD 75, and State Senate Districts SD 28 and SD 59. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Manhattan CB5?
      Cars and Trucks: 6 deaths, 156 moderate injuries, 12 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 19 moderate injuries, 2 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 41 moderate injuries, 7 serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data shows patterns: speeding, red-light running, driver inattention. These are preventable. They are not random.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits, keep speed cameras running, redesign streets for safety, and back policies that protect people walking and biking.
