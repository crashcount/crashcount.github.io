      Twelve Dead, No Excuses: Queens Streets Still Kill

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations.)

      Queens CB2: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In Queens CB2, the numbers do not lie. Twelve people dead. Thirty-four left with serious injuries. More than 2,500 hurt since 2022. The youngest killed was sixteen. The oldest, seventy-five. A cyclist, a pedestrian, a child in a car—none spared. The names fade, but the pain stays. A bolt falls from the sky, glass explodes, and a man thinks he’s been shot. “It’s mind-boggling. I can’t believe that this has happened multiple times and it hasn’t been fixed yet,” said Rahimi.

      Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. Trucks, taxis, mopeds, bikes—they all draw blood, but the heaviest toll comes from the biggest machines. In the last three years, SUVs and sedans caused the most deaths and injuries to pedestrians. Children and elders are struck down. Cyclists are crushed by turning trucks. Riders are thrown from mopeds. The street is a gauntlet, not a promise.

      What Leaders Have Done—And Not Done

      The city touts Vision Zero. Speed cameras run all day now. The law says the speed limit can drop to 20 mph. But in Queens CB2, the carnage continues. Local leaders have not moved fast enough. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program lapsed. Cameras need Albany’s blessing to keep running. The city has the power to lower speeds, but waits. Each day of delay is another risk, another family broken.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every crash is a choice made by someone in power. The city can lower the speed limit. The council can demand more cameras, more street redesigns, more enforcement against reckless drivers. Residents can call, write, show up, and refuse to let the dead be forgotten.

      Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand the cameras stay on. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Take action now.
