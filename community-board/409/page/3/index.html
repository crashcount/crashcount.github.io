      Queens Streets Run Red—Lower the Speed or Count More Dead

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Queens CB9: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      In Queens CB9, the toll does not let up. Ten dead. Fourteen left with serious wounds. In three and a half years, 1,711 people have been injured on these streets. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care if you are young or old. In the last year alone, two people died, three more were seriously hurt, and 555 were injured.

      On a Friday morning in Woodhaven, a van veered out of control. Three men were stepping out of a double-parked car. The van slammed a parked car, which slammed into them. Zhihong Shi, 31, died at Jamaica Hospital. The others, 67 and 48, survived. The van driver had a medical episode. No charges. No answers. “Victim Zhihong Shi was exiting a double parked car with the two other victims… when the driver of a passing Ford Econoline van lost control of his wheel.”

      A parked car is not safe. A sidewalk is not safe. A crosswalk is not safe. A delivery truck hits a parked car and flees. The owner, Jose DeAguiar, watched the video. “He rams right into the side of the car,” he said. “He comes out, looks at it, shrugs his shoulders, and leaves. No note, nothing. No care for anyone else’s property.” ABC7

      Leadership: Words, Laws, and Waiting

      City Hall talks of Vision Zero. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can now lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the limit still stands at 25. Speed cameras work, but their future is always up for debate.

      Local leaders say the right words. They promise safer streets. But the dead do not hear promises. They do not wait for another press release. Every day of delay is another day of risk.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the 20 mph limit. Demand speed cameras that never go dark. Join the fight. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB9 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 29, assembly district AD 38 and state senate district SD 15.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB9?
      It includes the Kew Gardens, Richmond Hill, South Richmond Hill, Ozone Park (North), and Woodhaven neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 29 and District 32, Assembly Districts AD 24, AD 28, and AD 38, and State Senate Districts SD 14 and SD 15.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB9?
      Cars and Trucks: 6 deaths, 52 moderate injuries, 2 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 3 moderate injuries, 0 serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 1 moderate injury, 0 serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      They are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and enforcement save lives. Policy choices—not chance—decide who lives and who dies.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, expand speed cameras, redesign dangerous streets, and push for laws that protect people, not just cars. Every delay costs lives.
