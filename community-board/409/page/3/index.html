      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB9?

      Queens Streets: Blood on Their Hands Until Speeds Drop

      Written June 19, 2025 (Revised June 28, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      Queens CB9: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      Two people dead. Over 550 injured. Three left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. That is the toll of traffic violence in Queens Community Board 9 in the last year alone, according to NYC Open Data. The names are not here. But the numbers are. They do not flinch.

      The Crashes Keep Coming

      A man in his 50s lies in a hospital bed, fighting for his life after a black SUV struck him and kept going. It happened just before midnight on 101st Avenue in Ozone Park. The driver did not stop. The man was found unresponsive on the road. He is not a statistic to his family. But to the city, he is one more number in a long line, as reported by ABC7.

      On Atlantic Avenue, a van driver lost control and killed Zhihong Shi, 31, as he exited a double-parked car. Two others were hurt. The van driver had a medical episode. The street did not forgive the mistake. “Shi and the other victims, ages 67 and 48, were all rushed to Jamaica Hospital, but Shi could not be saved.”

      Leadership: Words, Laws, and Waiting

      Local leaders have tools. They have power. They have words. Mayor Adams calls traffic violence “the serious crime that it is” and says, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” Speed cameras now run 24/7. The city can set speed limits lower, thanks to Sammy’s Law. But the pace is slow. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program has lapsed. Cameras need reauthorization. The city can lower speeds to 20 mph but has not done so citywide.

      What Comes Next

      Every day of delay is another risk. The city can act. The council can act. Albany can act. But the dead cannot wait. The injured cannot wait. The families cannot wait.

      Call to Action

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras stay on. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Addabbo co-sponsors e-scooter bill that likely reduces street safety.
      ▸

      Senate bill S 5801 would force e-scooter riders to get licensed, carry insurance, and read a safety manual. It bars unlicensed sales. Sponsors say it targets reckless riding. The bill aims to control scooters, but its effect on street danger is unclear.

      Senate bill S 5801, now in the sponsorship stage, was introduced on March 3, 2025. The bill sits with the Senate, awaiting committee action. Its matter title reads: 'Establishes a bicycle and electric scooter operator's safety manual; provides for the issuance of a license...; requires liability insurance for electric scooters in cities having a population of one million or more.' Primary sponsor Luis R. Sepúlveda (District 32) and co-sponsor Joseph P. Addabbo Jr. (District 15) back the measure. S 5801 would require e-scooter riders in New York City to pass a safety manual, obtain a license, and buy insurance. It also bans sales to unlicensed, uninsured buyers. The bill's impact on vulnerable road users is not assessed. Lawmakers claim it will curb reckless riding, but the real effect on street safety remains unmeasured.
