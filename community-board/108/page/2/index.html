      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan CB8?

      York Avenue Bleeds: How Many More Must Die Before City Hall Acts?

      Written June 20, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Manhattan CB8: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      A 90-year-old woman tried to cross York Avenue. A yellow cab made a U-turn and struck her. Moments later, a second car hit her. She died at the hospital. Both drivers stayed. No charges. Frances Rickard was crossing at York Avenue and East 72nd Street around 5:40 p.m. when the 68-year-old man driving the cab made a U-turn and hit her, authorities said.

      A Tesla sped down the FDR, hit the guardrail, flipped, and burned. The driver died. The passenger survived. A witness described the speed and destruction: “At least 120, 130 [mph]. At least. The damage was just so much that it split in half. And that just started going on fire.”

      In three and a half years, 11 people have died on these streets. 44 more suffered serious injuries. The numbers keep rising.

      The Most Vulnerable Pay the Price

      Pedestrians and cyclists are the first to bleed. In the last year, two people over 75 died. Children were hurt. The old, the young, the ones who walk—these are the bodies left behind. Cars and SUVs killed four. Trucks killed two. Bikes and mopeds left their mark, but the weight of steel does most of the damage.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      City Hall talks of Vision Zero. They say, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” But the dead keep coming. The city has the power now to lower the speed limit to 20 mph. They have not used it. Speed cameras work, but Albany lets their future hang by a thread. The law could expire. The cameras could go dark. The city builds new bike lanes and claims progress, but the blood on the crosswalks says otherwise.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the city use its power. Lower the speed limit. Keep the cameras on. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Take action now.
