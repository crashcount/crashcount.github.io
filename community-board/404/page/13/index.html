      Queens Bleeds While City Sleeps on Speed Limits

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and ensured citation formatting.)

      Queens CB4: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll in Flesh and Bone

      A woman steps into the crosswalk at 80th Street and 57th Avenue. A truck turns. She does not make it across. In Queens CB4, the dead do not speak, but the numbers do. Eleven people killed. Seventeen left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. In the last twelve months alone, two more lives ended, five more bodies broken beyond repair. NYC Open Data

      Children limp home from school. Cyclists bleed on Roosevelt Avenue. A 75-year-old man, struck by a bike, dies at Broadway and Roosevelt. A 78-year-old woman, crossing at Grand Avenue, is hit by a moped and does not get up. The street remembers every name, even when the city forgets.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did most of the damage. They killed two, left 67 with moderate injuries, and eight with wounds called “serious.” Trucks and buses killed one, bikes killed one, motorcycles and mopeds killed one more. The numbers do not care about excuses. They only count the bodies.

      Leadership: Promises and Pauses

      The city talks of Vision Zero. Bills pass in Albany. Sammy’s Law gives New York City the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the limit stands. Speed cameras work, but their future hangs on another vote. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program expired. No word yet on its return. The city says one death is too many. The street says it is not enough.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every day the speed limit stays high, every day a camera goes dark, another family risks a knock at the door. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the 20 mph limit. Demand cameras that never sleep. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB4 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 25, assembly district AD 39 and state senate district SD 13.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB4?
      It includes the Elmhurst and Corona neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 21, District 25, and District 30, Assembly Districts AD 30, AD 35, and AD 39, and State Senate Districts SD 12 and SD 13.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB4?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 67 moderate injuries, 8 serious injuries. Trucks and buses: 1 death, 5 moderate injuries. Bikes: 1 death, 6 moderate injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 1 death, 4 moderate injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents?
      No. The numbers show patterns. Speed, heavy vehicles, and street design decide who lives and who dies. These are preventable deaths, not random acts.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower the speed limit to 20 mph, restore and expand speed cameras, and redesign streets to protect people, not cars. They can bring back the Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
