      Brooklyn Bleeds: Lower Speeds or Count More Dead

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Brooklyn CB17: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Brooklyn CB17, the numbers do not lie. Nine people killed. Forty seriously hurt. In just the last twelve months, four lives ended and twelve more were left with wounds that do not heal. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, elders, children. They are the woman struck crossing with the signal at Avenue D and New York Avenue. She was 74. She had the light. The car turned left and took everything. NYC Open Data

      A 68-year-old woman died on Rutland Road, crossing with the signal. The driver was distracted. She never made it to the other side. NYC Open Data

      Most victims are on foot. Most drivers keep driving.

      The Blunt Edge of Policy

      Local leaders have moved, but not fast enough. Council Member Farah N. Louis has backed bills for senior and student bike share discounts and rules for micromobility safety. She also sponsored a bill to lower e-bike speeds to 15 mph. But slowing bikes will not stop cars from killing. The blood on the street is not from e-bikes. It is from sedans, SUVs, trucks. Cars and trucks caused the deaths of six people here.

      State Senator Kevin Parker voted yes to extend school speed zones and to require speed-limiting tech for repeat speeders. These are steps. But the streets remain dangerous. The city can set a 20 mph limit now. It has not. The silence is loud.

      The Human Cost

      A sister-in-law said, “It’s devastating. It’s affecting everyone in our family, especially (Ruiz’s) mom. Maddy was her only daughter.”

      A neighbor said, “She was a nice and kind girl, always smiling. They went out to take her nephews to eat and play at Dave & Busters.”

      The pain does not fade. The tire marks stay.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against reckless drivers.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. The street will not forgive our delay.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Brooklyn CB17 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 45, assembly district AD 58 and state senate district SD 21.
      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn CB17?
      It includes the East Flatbush-Erasmus, East Flatbush-Farragut, East Flatbush-Rugby, East Flatbush-Remsen Village, and Holy Cross Cemetery neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 40, District 41, District 42, and District 45, Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 42, AD 43, and AD 58, and State Senate Districts SD 19, SD 20, and SD 21.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn CB17?
      Cars and Trucks: 6 deaths, 551 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 8 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 5 injuries. The vast majority of deaths and injuries come from cars and trucks. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Most deaths and serious injuries come from cars and trucks. Lower speed limits, better street design, and real enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can set a 20 mph speed limit, expand speed cameras, redesign dangerous intersections, and pass laws that target reckless drivers—not just cyclists or pedestrians.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
