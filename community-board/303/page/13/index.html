      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Bed-Stuy’s corners keep bleeding. The fixes keep waiting.

      Brooklyn CB3: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 20, 2025

      Just past the middle of the week, at Malcolm X Boulevard and Jefferson Avenue, a van driver going straight hit a man in the intersection crosswalk. Police recorded him as injured. Source.

      This Week

      • On Fulton Street at Tompkins Avenue, a driver in a sedan and a person on a bike collided; the cyclist was injured. Source
      • On Throop Avenue at Lafayette Avenue, a driver backing an SUV hit a man walking; police recorded distraction and unsafe backing. He was injured. Source
      • On Albany Avenue at Decatur Street, a driver turning right on a bike collided with a sedan; the person on the bike was injured. Source

      The toll doesn’t stop

      Since 2022, Brooklyn Community Board 3 has recorded 18 deaths and 3,694 injuries, including 39 serious injuries. People walking account for 13 of those deaths; people on bikes for 1. Source.

      The danger clusters at known corners. Bedford Avenue shows 2 deaths and 158 injuries. Broadway shows 2 deaths and 239 injuries. Source.

      Nights cut deep. The 11 PM hour shows 4 deaths. 4 AM shows 3. Police logs also mark crashes tied to speed, like a serious injury at Ralph Avenue and Gates Avenue where unsafe speed was recorded. Source.

      The pattern is written in the police notes

      At Malcolm X and Jefferson, police logged “view obstructed/limited” for the van driver. At Throop and Lafayette, they wrote “backing unsafely” and “driver inattention/distraction.” At Ralph and Gates, they wrote “unsafe speed.” Each line is short. Each line comes after someone hits the ground. Source.

      Promises on Bedford Avenue

      The city promised a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue. It stalled. Council Member Chi A. Ossé called the delay “unacceptable” and demanded a date. Advocates noted protected lanes cut pedestrian injuries by 21% where installed. Read the record.

      Assembly Member Stefani Zinerman publicly questioned the safety gains of that Bedford Avenue design when DOT presented it in 2023. DOT staff answered with data in support of protection. The lane still isn’t built. Source.

      What leaders can do today

      Local fixes are plain:

      • Daylight crosswalks at Bedford and Broadway. Harden the turns. Give walkers a head start. Police have recorded failure-to-yield, distraction, backing, and speed in this district’s injuries and deaths. Source
      • Finish the protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue. The city has heard the demand; it needs to deliver. Source

      Citywide tools exist to cut the risk from repeat dangerous drivers. State Senator Jabari Brisport co-sponsored and voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045) in 2025, a bill to require intelligent speed assistance after repeated violations. The Senate moved it. The Assembly has a matching push. What will our Assembly do? Bill file.

      Brisport also co-sponsored a bill to enforce protected bike lanes with automated cameras (S3304). Keeping lanes clear keeps people alive. Bill file.

      The line back to Jefferson and Malcolm X

      One corner. One man down. Then another block. Another person on a bike. The ledger adds entries while plans wait.

      The ask is simple: build the protection the city promised on Bedford. Use state tools to rein in the worst drivers. And slow the cars where people cross.

      Take one step now. Tell your council member and state reps to move these fixes. Start here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What area does this cover?
      Brooklyn Community Board 3, which includes Bedford‑Stuyvesant (West) and (East). The period runs from Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 20, 2025.
      ▸ How many people have been hurt or killed here since 2022?
      According to NYC Open Data, there have been 18 deaths and 3,694 injuries in CB3, including 39 recorded as serious injuries. People walking account for 13 of the deaths; people on bikes for 1. Source: NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Where are the worst trouble spots?
      Bedford Avenue (2 deaths, 158 injuries) and Broadway (2 deaths, 239 injuries) top the local list. Late‑night hours are especially deadly: 11 PM shows 4 deaths, and 4 AM shows 3. Source: NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Which elected officials represent this area and what have they done?
      Council Member Chi A. Ossé publicly pressed DOT to deliver the promised Bedford Avenue protected bike lane. State Sen. Jabari Brisport co‑sponsored and voted yes on S4045 to require speed limiters for repeat speeders, and co‑sponsored S3304 for automated bike‑lane enforcement. Sources: Streetsblog NYC; Open States.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We analyzed NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi‑nx95, Persons f55k‑p6yu, Vehicles bm4k‑52h4), filtered to Brooklyn Community Board 3 from Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 20, 2025. We counted deaths, injuries, and serious injuries, and tallied locations by on‑street names. You can start from the crash dataset here and apply the same filters. Data as of Sep 19, 2025.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
