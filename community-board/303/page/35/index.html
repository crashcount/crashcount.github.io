      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn CB3?

      Brooklyn Bleeds While City Stalls

      Written July 31, 2025

      Brooklyn CB3: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 31, 2025

      The Death Count Rises

      Five people are dead. Sixteen more are marked with serious injuries. In the last twelve months alone, Brooklyn CB3 saw 1,514 crashes. These are not just numbers—they are bodies on the street and families left with empty chairs.

      A 79-year-old woman was killed crossing with the signal at Herkimer and New York Avenue. She was hit by two SUVs. She did not make it home, according to NYC Open Data.

      A 32-year-old woman died on Van Buren Street, crushed by an SUV. She was not at an intersection. She was not given a chance.

      The Usual Suspects

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. Of the deaths and injuries to pedestrians, SUVs and sedans are the main weapons. Trucks and buses add to the toll. Bikes and mopeds are in the mix, but the carnage is driven by heavy, fast vehicles.

      Leaders Speak, Streets Wait

      Council Member Chi Ossé called out the city for stalling on the Bedford Avenue protected bike lane. “The Bedford Avenue bike lane has been unacceptably dangerous since it was first laid, and for years, the people of my district have been promised that it would be protected,” he said. The lane is still not built. The danger remains.

      Assembly Member Stefani Zinerman questioned the safety benefits of protected bike lanes. DOT officials answered with numbers: a 21% drop in pedestrian injuries where protected lanes exist. Still, the lanes are delayed, as Streetsblog NYC reports.

      The Cost of Delay

      Every day of delay is another roll of the dice. The city has the power to lower speed limits, build real protection, and take the side of people walking and biking. Instead, the streets stay the same. The bodies keep coming.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand action. The dead cannot speak. The living must.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Brooklyn CB3 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 36, assembly district AD 56 and state senate district SD 25. See NYC Open Data for details.
      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn CB3?
      It includes the Bedford-Stuyvesant (West) and Bedford-Stuyvesant (East) neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 36, and District 41, Assembly Districts AD 55, AD 56, and AD 57, and State Senate Districts SD 20 and SD 25. See NYC Open Data for more.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn CB3?
      Cars and Trucks: SUVs and sedans caused the most deaths and injuries, with trucks and buses also involved. Motorcycles and Mopeds: Fewer incidents, but present. Bikes: Involved in some injuries, but not deaths. Source: NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Delays in building protected lanes and lowering speed limits keep the danger alive.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can push for protected bike lanes, lower speed limits, and redesign streets to protect people, not cars. They can demand action from DOT and back it up with votes and public pressure.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
