      Brooklyn Bleeds While Leaders Stall

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 6, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Brooklyn CB3: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll on Our Streets

      A woman steps out of her car on Van Buren Street. An SUV driver guns the engine. She is struck, dragged, and left dying in the street. Her name was Tiffany Cifuni. She was pregnant. Her husband said, “I lost my whole family tonight and I don’t think I’ll ever be the same.”

      In the last twelve months, three people have been killed on the streets of Brooklyn CB3. Eleven more suffered serious injuries. In total, 715 people were hurt in 1,228 crashes. These are not numbers. They are lives, broken and ended.

      Patterns of Harm

      SUVs are the main weapon. In this district, SUVs and cars caused six deaths and 122 moderate or serious injuries to pedestrians since 2022. Trucks and buses killed one. Bikes injured ten. The violence is relentless. It does not care about age. Children, elders, parents—no one is spared.

      A witness described the aftermath on Van Buren: “They dragged her like to the middle of the street, and then they turned wrong up Lafayette and she was just left there.”

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can act. But the limit remains unchanged. Speed cameras work, but their future is always in doubt. Each delay is another risk, another family shattered.

      Local leaders talk of Vision Zero. They say, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” But the bodies keep coming. The silence after each death is loud.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every day leaders wait, someone else pays. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand streets that put people before cars.

      Do not wait for another family to be broken. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Brooklyn CB3 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 36, assembly district AD 56 and state senate district SD 25.
      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn CB3?
      It includes the Bedford-Stuyvesant (West) and Bedford-Stuyvesant (East) neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 36, and District 41, Assembly Districts AD 55, AD 56, and AD 57, and State Senate Districts SD 20 and SD 25.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn CB3?
      SUVs and Cars: 6 deaths, 122 moderate or serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 1 death, 5 moderate or serious injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 10 moderate injuries. (2022–2025)
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The patterns are clear. Speed, reckless driving, and policy choices make these crashes predictable and preventable.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, keep speed cameras active, and redesign streets to protect people, not cars. Every delay costs lives.
