      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Manhattan CB11?

      Manhattan Bleeds While Leaders Stall: Six Dead, Hundreds Broken, Zero Accountability

      Written June 20, 2025 (Revised June 20, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Manhattan CB11: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      A man steps into the street. A car does not stop. In the last twelve months, six people died on the roads of Manhattan CB11. Nine more were left with injuries so grave they may never walk the same. The numbers do not flinch: 1,275 crashes, 687 hurt, 6 dead—all in a single year. The dead do not speak. The living limp on.

      The Faces Behind the Numbers

      A 43-year-old cyclist, Salvador Nico-Garcia, swerved to avoid a pedestrian in Central Park. He fell. His head struck the curb. He died at the hospital. The pedestrian walked away with a scraped hand. There is no law that would have made him wear a helmet. There is no law that brought him home. Officials say Nico-Garcia was on an e-bike and was not wearing a helmet at the time he was thrown.

      On Randall’s Island, Diana Agudela rode her e-bike home from work. She never made it. She was beaten and left with brain injuries. Her daughter called for more protection, more lights, and criticized the lack of cameras and lighting in the park.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      Local leaders talk of Vision Zero. They tout new laws and cameras. They say, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” But the blood dries on the crosswalks while the city waits. Sammy’s Law passed. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph. The power is there. The will is not. Speed cameras work, but their future is always in question. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program lapsed. The streets do not wait for paperwork.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by someone in power. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the 20 mph speed limit. Demand working speed cameras. Demand lights and safe crossings.

      If you do nothing, the numbers will keep counting. The dead will keep coming. Take action now.
