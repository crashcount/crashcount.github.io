      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx CB9?

      Bronx CB9: Nights Kill, Policy Lags

      Written August 16, 2025 (Revised August 16, 2025 – Finisher pass to tighten prose, correct unsupported claims, and focus on local fixes and policy solutions.)

      Bronx CB9: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 16, 2025

      Overview

      Since 2022, Bronx Community Board 9 has recorded 23 people killed and 42 people seriously injured in crashes, with thousands more hurt (city data). This year, through mid‑August, at least 3 people were killed and 11 were seriously injured in CB9 (city data).

      This week

      Two riders died on the Bronx River Parkway after a driver allegedly struck multiple vehicles and mopeds. Police and local reporting identified the victims and say the driver was arrested on vehicular manslaughter and DWI charges. A victim’s sister asked in court: “He’s just walking freely? Two people were killed. He was drunk. Think about how he took two lives. That’s not fair.” (NY Daily News).

      When and where we break

      Nights are deadliest. Midnight to 4 a.m. saw nine deaths in CB9 since 2022 (city data). The district’s repeat hotspots include Westchester Avenue and the Bruckner/Cross‑Bronx corridors, with White Plains Road also ranking high. These are repeat scenes, not one‑offs.

      Why it keeps happening

      City data point to common causes: alcohol involvement, driver inattention/distraction, and unsafe speed are recurring factors. Cars and SUVs do most of the killing and serious harm in CB9. The pattern is metal and speed.

      Local fixes we can build now

      • Daylight crossings and install Leading Pedestrian Intervals (LPIs) at Westchester Avenue and White Plains Road.
      • Harden turning radii and add curb extensions on expressway service roads to slow entries and exits.
      • Improve night lighting and run targeted DWI saturation patrols on Bruckner ramps and Bronx River Parkway approaches.

      Political solutions — local to citywide

      Local design and enforcement will save lives, but citywide policy must change too. New York must lower its default speed limit and require speed‑limiting technology for habitual speeders. State legislation pushing intelligent speed assistance devices — S4045 in the Senate and A2299 in the Assembly — would force repeat offenders to drive cars that cannot exceed the limit by large margins (see S4045, A2299). Those measures pair with local daylighting and LPIs to cut deaths now.

      Who’s acting — and who isn’t

      State Sen. Nathalia Fernández voted yes in committee on S4045, a step toward speed limiters for repeat offenders (S4045). Assembly Member Karines Reyes is a co‑sponsor of A2299 and supports related bills on owner liability for camera violations (A2299, A5440). The state has also discussed a car‑free connector alongside the Cross‑Bronx, an idea that would pull people off the highway edge and give them safe space to walk and bike (Streetsblog).

      What to demand

      Call your council member in District 18, your assembly member in AD 85, and your state senator in SD 34. Demand: a 20 mph default across NYC; passage of S4045/A2299 to require speed limiters for repeat offenders; and local fixes — daylighting, LPIs, hardened turns, better night lighting, and targeted DWI enforcement — at Westchester Ave, White Plains Rd, and Bruckner approaches.

      Don’t wait for the next body to cool on the asphalt.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Which areas are in Bronx CB9?
      It includes the Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River, Soundview-Clason Point, Castle Hill-Unionport, Parkchester, Soundview Park, and Westchester Square neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 17 and District 18, Assembly Districts AD 85 and AD 87, and State Senate Districts SD 29, SD 32, and SD 34.
      ▸ Where does Bronx CB9 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 18, assembly district AD 85 and state senate district SD 34.
      ▸ How bad is it in CB9 right now?
      Since 2022, at least 23 people have been killed and 42 seriously injured in Bronx CB9 crashes city data. Year‑to‑date, another 3 killed and 11 seriously hurt city data.
      ▸ Are these just “accidents”?
      No. Hotspots repeat, and nights kill. In CB9, midnight to 4 a.m. saw nine deaths since 2022 city data. Westchester Ave, White Plains Rd, and the Bruckner/Cross Bronx corridors are persistent trouble spots city data.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx CB9?
      Cars and Trucks: SUVs killed 5 pedestrians and sedans killed 1; “unspecified” vehicles killed 2 more. Trucks and buses caused injuries but 0 pedestrian deaths in this period. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, with small numbers of injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths and 10 injuries. All figures come from recent city data for CB9 source.
      ▸ What can local politicians do right now?
      Back and pass speed limiters for repeat offenders — S4045 in the Senate and A2299 in the Assembly (S4045, A2299). Push a 20 mph default speed limit citywide. On our streets, deliver daylighting + LPIs on Westchester Ave and White Plains Rd, and sustain night DWI operations on the Bruckner approaches.
