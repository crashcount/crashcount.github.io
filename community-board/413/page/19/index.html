      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB13?

      Twelve Dead, No Answers: Queens Streets Still Bleed

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens CB13: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Toll in Queens CB13

      The numbers do not lie. In the last twelve months, 12 people died and 1,392 were injured in crashes across Queens CB13. Eight suffered serious injuries. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, mothers, sons. In the past year, four people over 75 died. One was only 18. The violence does not stop for age.

      Just last week, July 5, a 76-year-old woman died in the back seat of a sedan on Cross Island Parkway. Two days earlier, a 39-year-old man was killed, ejected from his scooter on Nashville Boulevard. The week before, a 26-year-old man died behind the wheel on Hillside Avenue. The list does not end. It only grows.

      The Human Cost

      The stories are sharp. A BMW vaults the divider on Belt Parkway. The car catches fire. Passengers are thrown onto the road. “They went airborne and into the barrier,” a survivor said. “Jewel was already dead when she flew out the car,” the survivor added.

      A bus turns on Springfield Boulevard. A crane slams into its side. Windows shatter. Riders cry out. “We didn’t see the crane coming… then the crane just hits us,” a passenger told reporters. “The bus driver didn’t do anything wrong,” the passenger said.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      Local leaders have passed bills. Council Member Selvena Brooks-Powers voted to legalize jaywalking, ending a law that blamed the dead for their own deaths. She has called out the Department of Transportation: “DOT gives us their word every hearing and we are not getting results,” Brooks-Powers said.

      But the streets are not safe. The city has not met its own promises. The bodies keep coming. Most deaths are caused by cars and trucks. The old, the young, the careful, the unlucky. All are at risk.

      Act Now—Demand Action

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected crossings. Demand that the city stop blaming the dead and start protecting the living. Every day of delay is another family broken.
