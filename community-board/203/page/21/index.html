      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx CB3?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—Leaders Stall

      Written August 11, 2025 (Revised August 12, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned citations list.)

      Bronx CB3: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 11, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      Five dead. Thirteen seriously hurt. That’s just the last twelve months in Bronx CB3. The numbers do not flinch. In the same year, 517 people were injured on these streets. The names fade, but the pain does not.

      Just days ago, a woman was killed crossing 174th Street in Morris Heights. The driver fled. Police searched an alleyway for her body. She died at St. Barnabas Hospital. The car did not stop. The city did not stop. A 44-year-old woman was fatally struck in a hit-and-run crash in the Bronx.

      A neighbor saw it. “I looked over, and he just rolled over that woman and killed her,” she said. The driver kept going. The street stayed the same.

      The Pattern: No One Is Safe

      Pedestrians, cyclists, children. No one is spared. In the last year, 62 children were injured. No children were killed. Cars and SUVs did most of the damage—over 290 crashes. Trucks and buses hit too. Even bikes and mopeds left people bleeding. The violence is not random. It is built into the road.

      Leadership: Votes, Bills, and Silence

      Local leaders have moved, but not fast enough. Senator Sepúlveda voted yes to extend school speed zones and to curb repeat speeders with mandatory speed limiters. He backed the bill that would force the worst drivers to slow down. Assembly Member Jackson voted to extend speed cameras near schools. These are steps. But the blood on the street says it is not enough.

      The Call: Demand More, Demand It Now

      Every day of delay is another life at risk. Call your council member. Call your senator. Tell them the deaths are not accidents. Tell them to lower the speed limit, fix the streets, and end the silence. The dead cannot speak. You can. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Bronx CB3 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 17, assembly district AD 79 and state senate district SD 32.
      ▸ Which areas are in Bronx CB3?
      It includes the Morrisania, Claremont Village-Claremont (East), Crotona Park East, and Crotona Park neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 15, District 16, and District 17, Assembly Districts AD 79 and AD 85, and State Senate District SD 32.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx CB3?
      Cars and Trucks: 3 deaths, 293 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 11 injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 2 injuries. Cars and trucks are the main killers.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The pattern is clear. Speed, reckless driving, and unsafe streets make these deaths predictable. They can be prevented.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign dangerous streets, and pass laws to rein in repeat speeders. They can fund enforcement and demand accountability. Every delay costs lives.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
