      Bronx Streets Bleed—Leaders Stall, Lives Shatter

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, ensured correct citation formatting and order.)

      Bronx CB6: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      A child hit on Southern Boulevard. A cyclist thrown from his bike. A pedestrian crushed by a truck on Fordham Road. In Bronx CB6, the street does not forgive. Nine people have died since 2022. Thirteen more suffered injuries so grave they may never heal. In the last twelve months, 488 people were hurt in crashes. Five were left with serious injuries. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie. NYC Open Data

      The Sound of Gunfire, the Silence of Leaders

      On a spring day, two drivers sped down Southern Boulevard, guns blazing. The bullets flew. A man crashed his car and bled out before help could come. “There were a whole lot of rounds. It was crazy. This doesn’t happen around here during the day time,” said a deli worker. The other driver vanished. No arrests. No answers. The street swallowed the story and moved on.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      The city says it is fighting for safety. Speed cameras now run all day and night. The law lets New York lower speed limits to 20 mph. But in the Bronx, the pace of change is slow. One life lost is too many, but the work drags on.

      The numbers show who pays the price. Cars and SUVs killed and maimed most. Trucks and mopeds followed. The dead do not get a second chance.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day leaders wait, another family is broken. Call your council member. Demand the 20 mph limit. Demand more cameras. Demand streets that do not kill. Do not wait for another siren.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Bronx CB6 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 15, assembly district AD 78 and state senate district SD 32.
      ▸ Which areas are in Bronx CB6?
      It includes the West Farms, Tremont, and Belmont neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council District District 15, Assembly Districts AD 78, AD 79, AD 86, and AD 87, and State Senate Districts SD 32 and SD 33.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Bronx CB6?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 73 moderate or serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 0 deaths, 3 moderate injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 6 moderate injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 1 moderate, 1 serious injury.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The numbers show a pattern. Most deaths and injuries are preventable with safer streets, lower speeds, and better enforcement.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, expand speed camera coverage, redesign dangerous intersections, and push for laws that protect people walking and biking.
