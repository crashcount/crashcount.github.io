      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn CB5?

      1,602 Crashes, 5 Dead — Why Are Crosswalks Still Parking Spots?

      Written August 15, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Copy-edited body, converted raw references to descriptive inline links, and cleaned/reordered citations to match first appearance.)

      Brooklyn CB5: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 15, 2025

      Five dead. Corners still blind with parked cars. A woman had the light; a turning SUV took her. Call Council: pass the daylighting bill. Call Albany: pass S4045 speed limiters. Demand fixes at Blake and Pennsylvania before another body.

      Just Weeks Ago

      A woman crossed with the signal at Pennsylvania and Blake. A left-turning SUV hit her. She died. The record says failure to yield and distraction. It happened January 24. The city moved on. The street did not.

      Early July, near Gateway Drive, a 21-year-old pedestrian was struck and killed. Unsafe speed. Driver inattention. Two lines in a log. A family undone.

      Late July, at Jamaica and Richmond, a moped met a turning sedan. One rider never got up.

      On the subway, the toll continued. Just last Sunday at Sutter Ave in East New York, “Around 6 p.m., a man fell onto the southbound tracks … and was struck by a train,” police said. “EMS pronounced the victim dead at the scene,” an FDNY spokesman said.

      The Pattern

      This year in CB5: 1,602 crashes, 1,062 injuries, 12 serious injuries, 5 deaths — all up from last year to date. Crashes rose 14%, injuries 29%, deaths 400%. These are people. They are also proof; city data show.

      Cars and trucks do most of the harm to people on foot. Since 2022, they account for 759 pedestrian impacts, including 7 deaths. Motorcycles and mopeds: 18. Bikes: 10.

      Gothamist reported another local crash: “Police said both vehicles were heading westbound on Pitkin Avenue” when a driver hit and killed a moped rider; charges were pending.

      What Leaders Did — And Didn’t

      Council Member Sandy Nurse co-sponsored a bill to daylight 1,000 intersections a year — ban parking near crosswalks so people can see and be seen. It was laid over in committee.

      On zoning and parking mandates, her office said the compromise “wasn’t something that was pushed for specifically by Council Member Nurse.”

      State Sen. Roxanne Persaud voted yes in committee to require speed-limiters for repeat speeders on S 4045. That targets the worst drivers. It does not fix the street at Blake and Penn.

      Do Something Now

      Call your council members. Tell them to pass daylighting. Call your state reps. Tell them to pass S 4045. Demand redesigns where people keep dying. Do not wait for the next body to make the case.
