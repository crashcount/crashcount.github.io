      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Pennsylvania and Hegeman: a man down, a city on notice

      Brooklyn CB5: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 19, 2025

      Just before 10 AM on Sep 14, a driver hit a 71-year-old man at Pennsylvania and Hegeman in East New York. Police logs cite unsafe speed and lane changing; the driver was unlicensed. The pedestrian bled and stayed conscious at the scene (NYC Open Data; ABC7).

      In the past month, two people riding a motorcycle were killed at Atlantic and Elton. The SUV driver was turning left; both on the bike were ejected and died at the scene (NYC Open Data).

      This Week

      • A 71-year-old pedestrian was struck at Pennsylvania and Hegeman; police recorded unsafe speed and lane changing by the driver (NYC Open Data).
      • Two people on a motorcycle died after colliding with a left-turning SUV at Atlantic and Elton (NYC Open Data).

      The toll does not let up

      Since 2022 in Brooklyn Community Board 5, 17 people have been killed and 6,720 injured in traffic crashes (NYC Open Data). Evening hours are the worst; deaths cluster around the commute, with the heaviest at about 8 PM (same source).

      Police have logged fatal crashes tied to specific driver actions here. On Jan 24, 2025, a driver turning left at Pennsylvania and Blake failed to yield and killed a 57-year-old woman in the crosswalk (NYC Open Data). On Jul 13, 2025, a driver speeding on Gateway Drive killed a 21-year-old woman walking outside the intersection (NYC Open Data).

      Pennsylvania Avenue and Atlantic Avenue keep showing up in the records for injuries and deaths. The corners pile up the harm (same source).

      A chase, a teen, a body on the curb

      News outlets reported Sunday’s strike happened during a police pursuit and that the driver was 15 years old (AMNY; ABC7). “15-year-old driver accused of striking pedestrian in Brooklyn,” read one headline (CBS New York).

      What leaders have done — and not done

      State Senator Roxanne Persaud voted yes to move the Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045) through committee, a bill to require speed limiters for repeat violators (Open States). Assembly Member Nikki Lucas voted yes to extend school speed zones in 2025 (Open States).

      Council Member Chris Banks co-sponsored a crackdown on unlicensed commuter vans (Int 1347-2025), now in committee, and backed cleanup of abandoned vehicles (NYC Council – Legistar). These steps do not slow turning drivers at Pennsylvania and Blake or calm speeds on Atlantic.

      The fixes are on the table

      • Daylight corners so people can see and be seen. The Council’s Progressive Caucus is pushing a universal daylighting bill; “a proven, effective way to make our streets safer,” said a member of the caucus (City & State NY).
      • Harden left turns and add leading pedestrian intervals at recurring crash corners like Pennsylvania Avenue and Atlantic Avenue (NYC Open Data).
      • Pass the state’s speed-limiter bill for repeat offenders. Persaud has voted yes in committee; the Assembly should move the companion bill (Open States).

      Slower speeds save lives. Albany renewed the city’s 24/7 school-zone camera program through 2030; with cameras in place, the next step is to lower speeds and rein in repeat offenders. “Your calls are working,” advocates say. “Don’t stop” (Take Action).

We counted deaths, injuries, and serious injuries from the Persons table and matched them to crash locations in the Crashes table. Data were accessed Sep 19, 2025.
