      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB3?

      Queens CB3: The Corner That Keeps Bleeding

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens CB3: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another driver. Same ending.

      • In this district, pedestrians were hit 415 times since 2022. Four died. Cyclists were hit 210 times. One died. Occupants died eight times. These are the city’s own numbers. NYC Open Data.
      • The city counts crashes piling up on Northern Boulevard and 37 Avenue as top hotspots.

      Bold nights. Quiet morgue.

      • The worst hours stack after dark. Deaths spike at 1 a.m., 4–6 a.m., 8 a.m., and the evening rush into 10 p.m. Injuries peak from late afternoon through night. NYC Open Data.
      • Speed and red lights keep showing up. At 37th Ave and 90th St on Feb. 1, 2024, a woman was struck and killed. The data lists “Unsafe Speed” and “Traffic Control Disregarded.” CrashID 4699492.

      One corner. Two bodies.

      • On Mar. 13, 2024, an 8‑year‑old boy, crossing with the signal at 31st Ave and 100th St, was killed by a turning pickup. The record cites “Failure to Yield” and “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” CrashID 4709403.
      • On Jun. 10, 2024, at 37th Ave near 90th St, a 44‑year‑old woman was killed. CrashID 4731762.

      The pattern doesn’t blink.

      • Since 2022, SUVs and cars lead the harm to people on foot here: at least 3 pedestrian deaths and 4 serious injuries in those crashes alone. Trucks killed once. Period stats.
      • Contributing factors in this district’s toll: “other” leads. Then vulnerable road user error. Then disregarded signals. Inattention and alcohol appear too. District rollup.

      “Speed kills,” they said. Then left the limit.

      • After a senior driver in Astoria killed two men and himself, officials called for lower speeds. “Slower speeds are proven to reduce crashes dramatically,” advocates said. Streetsblog NYC.
      • City law now allows lower limits. The power exists. Use it. Take Action.

      The worst 1.5 percent.

      • A tiny group of repeat violators drives a large share of death. Vehicles with 16 camera tickets in 12 months are twice as likely to kill or seriously injure; 30+ tickets multiplies the risk fifty‑fold. One driver with 29 tickets killed a mother and two daughters in April. Streetsblog NYC.
      • Albany’s answer is on the table: require intelligent speed limiters for repeat violators. In the Senate, S4045 advanced with yes votes and co‑sponsors on record. Open States.

      Three corners. One fix.

      • Northern Boulevard. 37th Avenue. Grand Central Parkway frontage. These are named among the top injury sites here. Open Data hotspots.
      • Fixes that match the failures: daylight every corner; add leading pedestrian intervals; harden left turns where drivers keep cutting; narrow lanes where speed shows up in the body count. Target night hours with automated enforcement and repeat‑offender sweeps tied to tickets. Open Data.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      • DOT says protected lanes and traffic calming cut injuries, and they’re defending an Astoria redesign in court while local lawmakers back it. Streetsblog NYC.
      • The Council has moved to speed up long‑delayed greenway work. “The infrastructure projects… need to be progressing at a much much faster rate,” Council Member Shekar Krishnan said. Streetsblog.

      What now.

      • Lower the city default to 20 mph. Install speed limiters on the cars of repeat offenders. The tools are in hand. The deaths are in our data. Take Action.

      ▸

      Bill S 3897 cleared the Senate committee on March 2, 2022, with a final vote on May 25, 2022.       ▸

      Mar 2 - Senate passed S 5130. The bill pushes complete street design. It aims for safe access for all. Pedestrians and cyclists get a shot at safer roads. The vote was split, but the bill moved forward.

The matter, titled 'Is It Time For Free Buses In New York City?', highlights calls from TWU Local 100 Vice-President JP Patafio and Assembly Member Jessica González-Rojas to end fare enforcement and make buses free. Advocates and officials agree: bus improvements and fare-free service are not mutually exclusive. The debate continues in committee, with no formal bill yet.

      S 1078 Ramos votes yes, boosting driver education and improving street safety.
      ▸

      Feb 1 - Senate and Assembly passed S 1078. New drivers must now learn how to avoid hitting people on foot or bike. Law aims to cut crashes at the root—before drivers get the keys.

      Bill S 1078, introduced in the Senate on April 27, 2021, requires pedestrian and bicyclist safety instruction in the drivers pre-licensing course. The bill moved through committee and passed the Senate and Assembly, with key votes on May 20, 2021, February 1, 2022, May 16, 2022, and May 23, 2022. The matter summary reads: 'Requires instruction in pedestrian and bicyclist safety as part of the drivers pre-licensing course.' Primary sponsor: Senator Gounardes, joined by Bailey, Biaggi, Brisport, Cleare, Comrie, and others. The measure targets driver ignorance, a root cause of deadly crashes, by putting vulnerable road users at the center of driver education.
