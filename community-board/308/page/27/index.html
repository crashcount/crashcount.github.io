      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn CB8?

      Atlantic Avenue takes the hits. People pay the price.

      Written August 25, 2025 (Revised August 25, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Brooklyn CB8: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      Atlantic Avenue runs hard through Brooklyn CB8. It leaves a trail. Since 2022, it’s the top killer here, with four deaths and 244 injuries on that corridor alone, per city crash data. NYC Open Data.

      Atlantic at Kingston: dead on arrival

      • March 16, 2024, just after midnight. A moped rider was killed at Atlantic and Kingston. The record lists “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” CrashID 4710249.
      • Nov. 22, 2023, late morning. A truck and an SUV collided on Atlantic near Clinton. An 88‑year‑old passenger died. CrashID 4681770.
      • May 25, 2024, night. A 39‑year‑old man walking outside a crosswalk zone on Atlantic was struck and killed in a crash involving an SUV and a box truck. CrashID 4727579.

      Heavy vehicles show up in the worst files: a tractor truck, a box truck, a refrigerated van. Across CB8’s pedestrian injuries, trucks and buses are tied to deaths this period, with one pedestrian killed and serious harm in others. NYC crash rollup.

      The clock doesn’t care

      In this board area, pain spreads across the day. Injuries spike at the commute and dinner hours. The 5 p.m. hour is the worst: 144 injuries. At 6 p.m., 138 injuries. Deaths hit at many hours: 8 a.m., late morning, noon, 2 p.m., 5 p.m., 6 p.m., 9 p.m., 10 p.m., 11 p.m., 9 p.m., and 10 p.m. The danger does not sleep. Hourly distribution.

      Riders and walkers bear the load: since 2022, CB8 logged 209 injured cyclists (one killed) and 331 injured pedestrians (two killed). People inside vehicles are hurt most by count, but those on foot or bike are exposed. Mode split.

      Rogers, Pacific, Bedford: the side streets bleed too

      • Rogers Avenue: one death, 21 injuries. A 37‑year‑old on an e‑bike died after striking a parked box truck in 2023. The city record cites “Pavement Defective.” Top intersections; CrashID 4692318.
      • Pacific Street: one death, 45 injuries. Top intersections.
      • Bedford Avenue and Eastern Parkway each show dozens of injuries. Top intersections.

      The city’s own factor table for CB8 lists “other” as the largest bucket tied to deaths (three), with smaller tallies for vulnerable‑road‑user error (one) and traces of distraction, failure to yield, and unsafe speed across injuries. The pattern is messy, but the harm is plain. Contributing factors.

      Trucks in the kill zone

      On Atlantic, the death files keep pairing people with big rigs. A 2022 tractor truck crash at Atlantic. A 2024 box truck in a fatal on Atlantic. A refrigerated van in a fatal motorcycle strike nearby. The trucks and buses share a small slice of total pedestrian cases but carry outsize harm, including one pedestrian death in CB8’s rollup. Vehicles and persons tables.

      What would stop the next call to next of kin?

      Local fixes that match the map:

      • Daylight Atlantic, Rogers, and Pacific. Ban parking at crosswalks and harden corners. Council bill Int 1138-2024 would scale this up citywide with barriers at 1,000 intersections a year. Council Member Chi Ossé is a co‑sponsor, per council records.
      • Harden turns and add leading walk signals and turn‑calming on Atlantic at Kingston, Bedford, and Clinton. Protect bike moves where cyclists keep getting hit.
      • Target trucks on Atlantic and Rogers. Set truck‑safe routing and frequent checks where the fatalities cluster.

      Citywide moves that cut the body count

      Albany gave the city power to set lower speeds. The law is on the books. Our city can drop residential limits. The choice is political. See our action guide.

      There’s also a state bill to stop the worst repeat drivers by force‑limiting their cars. The Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045) advanced in June. Senator Zellnor Myrie co‑sponsored it, but he missed two committee votes on June 11 and 12. Open States: S 4045. The bill would require speed limiters for people who rack up heavy violations. This is aimed at the few who do the most harm.

      Two dead in the trains’ shadow

      This board spans subway stations where lives ended this summer. At Prospect Park, a fight spilled to the rails. “He landed on the train tracks and was struck by an oncoming train,” police said. He died at the scene. NY Daily News. Days later, two more people died on the tracks in separate incidents citywide; police said, “No criminality is suspected.” NY Daily News.

      Keep the pressure on

      • Lower speeds on local streets. Twenty saves.
      • Lock repeat speeders to the limit with mandated speed tech.
      • Daylight and harden the corners where people keep falling.

      These are not theories. They are choices. The death files on Atlantic are already written. The next one is not.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where are the worst crash locations in Brooklyn CB8?
      Atlantic Avenue is the top corridor, with four deaths and 244 injuries since 2022. Other hotspots include Rogers Avenue (one death, 21 injuries), Pacific Street (one death, 45 injuries), Bedford Avenue, and Eastern Parkway, per NYC Open Data.
      ▸ When are crashes most common here?
      Injuries peak during the evening rush. The 5 p.m. hour has 144 injuries; 6 p.m. has 138. Deaths occur across many hours, showing all‑day risk, per the hourly distribution in NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Do heavy vehicles play a role?
      Yes. Multiple CB8 fatalities involve trucks on Atlantic and nearby streets. In the pedestrian rollup, trucks and buses account for a death and serious harm despite a smaller share of total cases.
      ▸ What can the city change right now?
      Daylight crosswalks and harden turns at Atlantic, Rogers, and Pacific; add LPIs and turn‑calming at key Atlantic junctions; and target truck routing and enforcement where deaths cluster.
