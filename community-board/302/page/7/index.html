      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn CB2?

      Twelve Dead, Forty-Two Broken: Brooklyn’s Streets Need Justice, Not More Excuses

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Brooklyn CB2: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A woman steps off the curb at Fulton and Washington. An SUV crushes her. She dies on the street. Her name is not in the headlines. She is one of twelve killed in Brooklyn CB2 since 2022. Forty-two more have been left with serious injuries. Some will never walk again. Some will never speak. The numbers are not just numbers. They are lives cut short, families broken, futures erased.

      The Pattern Is Relentless

      Cars and SUVs do the most harm. They killed two, left 12 with serious injuries, and hurt hundreds more. Trucks, buses, motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes all add to the toll. No one is safe. Not the child on a bike. Not the old man crossing the street. Not the woman waiting for the light. The disaster moves slow, but it never stops.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      Local leaders have talked about safety. They have sponsored bills to ban parking near crosswalks, to speed up protected bike lanes, to require daylighting at intersections. But the pace is slow. The bills sit in committee. The danger does not wait.

      “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said an eyewitness after a recent hit-and-run. The driver fled. The city keeps counting the dead.

      Council Member Crystal Hudson and Assembly Member Phara Souffrant Forrest have backed safety bills. But the streets are still deadly. The city has the power to lower speed limits. It has not done so. “We want to make sure what happened doesn’t happen again,” said a neighbor. The city has not listened.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every death is a choice made by those in power. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes. Demand daylight at every corner. Do not wait for another body in the street. Do not let them wait, either.

      Gounardes Condemns Trump Blocking Safety Boosting Congestion Pricing
      ▸

      Trump killed congestion pricing. The MTA sued. Advocates warn: more cars, more crashes, dirtier air. Transit funding gutted. Disabled riders lose elevators. Streets grow deadlier. Politicians vow to fight. The city braces for gridlock and loss.

      ""By blocking this successful policy, Trump will be directly responsible for more traffic, more crashes, more polluted air, slower buses and less funding for our transit system," he continued. "This means no new station elevators for elderly and disabled riders, and no new subway signals to speed up commutes for working New Yorkers."" -- Andrew Gounardes

      On February 19, 2025, the Trump administration withdrew federal approval for New York City's congestion pricing program. U.S. DOT Secretary Sean Duffy cited burdens on working-class drivers and questioned the legality of using toll revenue for transit. The MTA immediately filed a lawsuit to challenge the move, arguing the program is settled law and already reduces congestion. State Senators Andrew Gounardes and Zellnor Myrie condemned the action, warning, 'Trump will be directly responsible for more traffic, more crashes, more polluted air, slower buses and less funding for our transit system.' The withdrawal aligns with New Jersey's opposition and threatens transit upgrades like elevators for elderly and disabled riders. Local officials and advocates say ending congestion pricing will harm vulnerable road users and worsen street danger.

      A 5623
      Simon sponsors bill boosting pedestrian safety by deterring vehicle right-of-way violations.
      ▸

      Assembly bill A 5623 lands in Albany. It targets drivers who block pedestrian paths. Stopping, standing, or parking in crosswalks becomes a crime. Lawmakers aim to clear the way for people on foot. The bill puts walkers first. Enforcement could follow.

      Assembly bill A 5623, titled the "New York State pedestrian right-of-way protection act," was introduced on February 18, 2025. The bill sits at the sponsorship stage in the Assembly. Its summary reads: "makes it an unclassified misdemeanor to stop, stand or park a vehicle in a pedestrian right-of-way." Jo Anne Simon (District 52) leads as primary sponsor, joined by Scott Gray (District 116) and Joe DeStefano (District 3) as co-sponsors. No committee or vote events are listed yet. The bill cracks down on drivers who block crosswalks and sidewalks, making such actions a misdemeanor. By targeting illegal parking in pedestrian zones, lawmakers seek to protect those most at risk on city streets.
