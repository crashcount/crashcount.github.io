      Blood on the Sidewalk—City Hall Still Sleeps

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Queens CB1: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      Children, Elders, and the Relentless Toll

      A 7-year-old girl lay on the sidewalk outside her school, her femur broken, her head bleeding. A 14-year-old girl and a 58-year-old man were hit too. The driver did not have a license. She reversed into a box truck, spun out, and the car mounted the curb. The crash happened at 5:30 p.m., just as families were heading home. Police said, “Bah was arrested and charged with reckless endangerment, reckless driving and driving without a license.”

      A month earlier, a 94-year-old woman was crossing Broadway at 48th Street. A USPS van turned, drove over her, and pinned her underneath. She survived, barely. “The van drove completely over the woman, who fell to the ground when hit, before coming to an abrupt stop with the victim trapped under it.”

      The Numbers: Four Dead, Hundreds Hurt

      In the past year, four people died in crashes in Queens CB1. Over 800 were injured. Five suffered injuries so severe they may never walk the same. Children, teens, elders—no one is spared. Cars and SUVs did most of the damage, but trucks, bikes, and mopeds left their mark too. The disaster is slow, but it does not stop.

      Leaders Talk, Streets Stay Deadly

      City leaders say they want zero deaths. They passed Sammy’s Law. They talk about speed cameras and lower limits. But the sidewalk outside the school is still a danger zone. The crosswalk is still a trap for the old. Residents are left to beg for basics: “increased safety measures, like speed bumps.”

      What Next? Demand Action Now

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand 20 mph limits. Demand real protection for every crosswalk and school. Do not wait for another child’s blood on the sidewalk. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB1 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 22, assembly district AD 36 and state senate district SD 59.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB1?
      It includes the Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Rikers Island, Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, and Astoria Park neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 22 and District 26, Assembly Districts AD 34, AD 36, and AD 85, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 12, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB1?
      Cars and SUVs were involved in 2 deaths, 108 moderate injuries, and 4 serious injuries to pedestrians. Trucks and buses caused 0 deaths, 8 moderate injuries, and 2 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 0 deaths, 9 moderate injuries, and 0 serious injuries. Bikes caused 0 deaths, 4 moderate injuries, and 1 serious injury. (Source: NYC Open Data)
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Speed limits, street design, and enforcement can save lives. The numbers show the danger is constant, not random.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign dangerous intersections, expand speed cameras, and fund real protections for people walking and biking. They can act now, not after another tragedy.
      ▸ How many people were killed or seriously injured in Queens CB1 in the last year?
      In the last 12 months, 4 people died and 5 suffered serious injuries in traffic crashes.
