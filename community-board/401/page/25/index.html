      Thirteen Dead in Queens: Speed Kills, Leaders Stall, Families Shatter

      Written June 24, 2025

      Queens CB1: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 24, 2025

      The Toll in Queens CB1

      Thirteen dead. Twenty seriously hurt. In the last three and a half years, the streets of Queens CB1 have not been quiet. The numbers are blunt: 5,068 crashes, 2,694 injuries, and 13 lives ended. The dead include a cyclist thrown from her bike, a 7-year-old girl with a broken femur outside her school, and a teenager who never made it home. The city counts bodies. Families count empty chairs.

      The Faces Behind the Numbers

      Amanda Servedio, 36, was riding her bike on 34th Avenue. A pickup, fleeing police, hit her. She flew thirty feet. She did not scream. “She went airborne. She flew like 30 feet. It was a lot of force,” said a witness. Her father wanted justice. “We wanted him to be arrested and we want him to serve time,” he told the press.

      On 35th Avenue, an unlicensed driver lost control outside a charter school. A 7-year-old girl was left with a broken leg and a head injury. A 14-year-old and a 58-year-old man were also hit. The driver was charged with reckless endangerment and driving without a license. “An out-of-control unlicensed driver rammed into two kids and one adult,” police said.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      Local leaders talk about Vision Zero. They tout speed cameras and intersection redesigns. But the carnage continues. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not done so. Cameras catch speeders, but the most dangerous drivers keep driving. Laws to stop repeat offenders stall in Albany. Each delay is another risk, another family changed forever.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. These are not accidents. Every crash is a policy failure. Every injury is a warning. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand action against repeat offenders. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Queens CB1 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 22, assembly district AD 36 and state senate district SD 59.
      ▸ Which areas are in Queens CB1?
      It includes the Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Rikers Island, Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, and Astoria Park neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 22 and District 26, Assembly Districts AD 34, AD 36, and AD 85, and State Senate Districts SD 11, SD 12, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Queens CB1?
      Cars and Trucks: 2 deaths, 4 serious injuries, 391 total injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 0 deaths, 1 serious injury, 27 total injuries. Bikes: 0 deaths, 1 serious injury, 15 total injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data shows patterns—speed, repeat offenders, unlicensed drivers. These are preventable crashes, not random accidents.
      ▸ What can local politicians do?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, push for enforcement against repeat offenders, and redesign streets to protect people, not cars. Delay costs lives.
