      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Queens CB1?

      Streets Bleed While Leaders Stall

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Queens CB1: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A cyclist, age 36, killed at 34th Avenue and 37th Street. A 15-year-old moped rider, dead on 43rd Street. A 17-year-old, gone after a crash on Astoria Boulevard. In the last twelve months, two people have died and five have been seriously injured on the streets of Queens CB1. In that same year, 786 people were hurt in 1,363 crashes (NYC Open Data).

      The numbers do not bleed, but the streets do. Cars and SUVs led the body count—two deaths, four serious injuries. Trucks and buses, motorcycles, mopeds, bikes: all have left their mark. The most vulnerable—children, cyclists, pedestrians—pay the highest price.

      “We Didn’t See the Crane Coming”

      The stories pile up. A crane slams into a bus in Queens Village. Emily McKinnon, a passenger, recalled, “We didn’t see the crane coming… then the crane just hits us.” Four people hurt. The bus driver did nothing wrong. The street did not forgive.

      A BMW vaults the divider on the Belt Parkway. Passengers thrown from the car. Fire. Death. “They went airborne and into the barrier,” said a survivor. No seatbelts. No second chances.

      Leaders Move—But Not Fast Enough

      Council Member Tiffany Cabán has co-sponsored bills to ban parking near crosswalks, speed up protected bike lanes, and back the SAFE Streets Act. Assembly Member Zohran Mamdani has pushed for lower speed limits and more bus lanes. Senator Kristen Gonzalez has backed speed-limiting tech for repeat offenders.

      But the deaths keep coming. Every delay is another name on a slab.

      Act Now—Before the Next Siren

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes. Demand daylight at every crosswalk.

      Do not wait for another child’s name in the news. The city has the power. Make them use it.
