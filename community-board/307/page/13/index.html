      Brooklyn Streets Kill—City Stalls. Demand Action Now.

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, ensured proper citation formatting, and cleaned citations.)

      Brooklyn CB7: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll: Lives Lost, Bodies Broken

      In Brooklyn CB7, the street does not forgive. Since 2022, 13 people have died and 2,148 have been injured in crashes. Twenty of those injuries were serious—lives changed in a blink, families left with empty chairs and hospital bills. In the last year alone, 2 people died and 664 were hurt. The dead are not numbers. A 70-year-old woman, crossing with the signal, struck by a turning sedan on 7th Avenue. A 30-year-old man, crushed by a truck on 60th Street. A 49-year-old cyclist, thrown and killed by a pickup at 53rd and 4th. The street is a wound that never closes.

      Who Pays the Price

      Pedestrians and cyclists bleed first. Trucks, sedans, SUVs—they do the killing. In the last three years, cars and trucks took 5 lives, motorcycles and mopeds 1, bikes none. The rest are left with broken bones, crushed limbs, and memories that do not heal. The numbers are steady. The pain is not.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city talks about Vision Zero. They promise safer streets, lower speed limits, more cameras. But in CB7, the pace is slow. The deaths keep coming. The city says, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” The words are right. The action is not enough. Sammy’s Law passed. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph. They have not done it here. Cameras catch speeders, but the law that keeps them running is always at risk. Every delay is another risk, another family waiting for a call that never should come.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand the 20 mph limit. Demand more cameras, more street redesigns, more action. Do not wait for another obituary.

      The street will not change itself. You have to make it happen.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Brooklyn CB7 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 38, assembly district AD 51 and state senate district SD 17.
      ▸ Which areas are in Brooklyn CB7?
      It includes the Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Sunset Park (West), Sunset Park (Central), and Green-Wood Cemetery neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 38, District 39, and District 43, Assembly Districts AD 44, AD 49, and AD 51, and State Senate Districts SD 17, SD 20, and SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Brooklyn CB7?
      In the last three years, Cars and Trucks caused 5 deaths and 57 moderate or serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds caused 1 death and 3 moderate injuries. Bikes caused no deaths but 6 moderate injuries. Data source
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or can they be prevented?
      Crashes are not just accidents. Lower speed limits, protected bike lanes, and enforcement all reduce deaths and injuries. Policy and design save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits to 20 mph, expand speed camera programs, redesign dangerous intersections, and fund more protected bike lanes. They can act now, not later.
