      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Bronx CB4?

      Hit-and-Run City: Blood on Bronx Streets, Silence at City Hall

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Bronx CB4: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 26, 2025

      The Bodies on the Road

      A boy on a scooter, fifteen years old, struck down at Jerome and 167th. The motorcyclist ran the red and kept going. The boy was left in the street, fighting for breath. Police asked for help to find the driver. The boy’s family waits by his hospital bed. Police said a 15-year-old boy riding his scooter was critically hurt when a Bronx motorcyclist blew through a red light, slammed into him and sped away.

      A father, Kelvin Mitchell, crossing Webster Avenue. A black Mercedes, speeding in the bus lane, hit him and kept going. His sister said, “God is going to make sure that my brother sees justice… Everybody is missing Kelvin. It’s not gonna be the same over here without him.”

      In the last twelve months, 1 person died and 526 were injured in crashes in Bronx CB4. Children, elders, workers. One serious injury. Hundreds of broken bodies. The numbers do not heal.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs killed 3 pedestrians here. Sedans killed 1. Buses, bikes, mopeds, taxis, motorcycles—each left someone hurt. The streets are not safe for the old or the young. The danger is not random. It is built into the road.

      The Leaders and the Law

      The city talks about Vision Zero. They say, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” They promise more cameras, lower speed limits, safer crossings. But the pain does not wait for press releases. Sammy’s Law lets the city lower speed limits to 20 mph. The law is there. The will is not. The cameras that catch speeders are at risk of going dark. The city can act. It does not.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand justice for the dead and the living.

      Do not wait for another child’s blood on the street. Take action now.

      Int 0606-2024
      Stevens co-sponsors e-bike and scooter registration bill, reducing overall street safety.
      ▸

      Council wants plates on every e-bike and scooter. The bill sits in committee. Riders face new rules. Lawmakers push for registration. Streets wait. No change yet. The city debates control. Vulnerable users watch. The system holds its breath.

      Int 0606-2024 was introduced on March 7, 2024, in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure. The bill, titled "A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to requiring that every bicycle with electric assist, electric scooter and other legal motorized vehicle be licensed and registered," would force all e-bikes, e-scooters, and similar vehicles not already covered by DMV rules to register with the Department of Transportation and display a visible plate. Council Member Inna Vernikov led as primary sponsor, joined by Holden, Farías, Riley, Brannan, and many others. The bill was laid over in committee on December 11, 2024. The measure aims to track these vehicles, but its impact on vulnerable road users remains unassessed. The city’s debate continues, with no law yet in force.
