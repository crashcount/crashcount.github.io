      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 59?

      Blood on the Asphalt, Silence in Albany

      Written July 14, 2025

      SD 59: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 14, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Silence

      In Senate District 59, the street is a wound that never closes. Nineteen people have died since 2022. Forty-one have been seriously injured. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care if you are young or old. In the last twelve months alone, six more lives ended. Over a thousand were hurt. The dead do not get a second chance.

      A man, 49, was killed by a bus at 3rd Avenue and East 28th. A cyclist, 36, died on 34th Avenue. A pedestrian, 49, was struck down by an e-bike on India Street. A 15-year-old boy fell from a subway train at Queensboro Plaza. The city moves on. The families do not.

      The System Fails, Again and Again

      Most of these deaths could have been stopped. The drivers who kill are often repeat offenders. The streets are built for speed, not safety. The city knows this. The state knows it too. “This was as avoidable as it is tragic,” said NYC Transit President Demetrius Crichlow after the boy died at Queensboro Plaza. The words are cold. The loss is not.

      Leadership: Action and Delay

      Senator Kristen Gonzalez has not been silent. She backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst repeat offenders to slow down. She co-sponsored a bill for automated bike lane enforcement, and another to mandate safety tech in all vehicles. She called for lower speed limits and better street design. But the pace is slow. The bills crawl. The bodies pile up.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Every day of delay is another day someone dies. Call Senator Gonzalez. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand the city use its new power to slow the streets. Demand that repeat speeders are taken off the road. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and oversees state policy for all New Yorkers.
      ▸ Where does SD 59 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 26 and assembly district AD 37.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 59?
      It includes the Greenpoint, Williamsburg, Stuyvesant Town-Peter Cooper Village, Gramercy, Murray Hill-Kips Bay, United Nations, Old Astoria-Hallets Point, Astoria (Central), Queensbridge-Ravenswood-Dutch Kills, Astoria Park, and Queens CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 2, District 4, District 22, District 26, and District 33, and Assembly Districts AD 36, AD 37, AD 50, and AD 74.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 59?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm: 1 death and 628 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 42 injuries. Bikes caused 2 deaths and 53 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Most crashes are preventable. Repeat offenders and unsafe street design are known risks that can be fixed with stronger laws and better infrastructure.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws to slow down traffic, hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable, and redesign streets to protect people walking and biking.
