      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 59?

      Second Avenue, FDR, Franklin Street: One District, Deadly Pattern

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 59: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another driver. Same ending.

      On Second Avenue near 15th Street, a 21-year-old in a 2024 Nissan Rogue hit a 65-year-old man on an Arrow 9 e‑bike around 6 p.m. The driver fled. Police later arrested him and said he confessed to driving without a license. The cyclist suffered severe head trauma and was found unconscious as EMS worked on him. He was rushed to Bellevue. The NYPD Collision Investigation Squad is on the case, and the charges include leaving the scene and unlicensed driving, according to Streetsblog NYC and the West Side Spirit. City open data lists the crash with the cyclist later listed as killed and the SUV driver cited for traffic control disregard in the dataset.

      A few days earlier on the FDR, a 31-year-old FDNY firefighter fell from his motorcycle and was then struck by a vehicle that did not remain. Police said he died at the scene before 4 a.m. near E. 25th Street. “He was struck by an unknown vehicle that did not remain on the scene,” police said, as reported by ABC7 and the West Side Spirit. The city’s crash file records his death and notes “Driver Inattention/Distraction” in the sequence of events for the motorcycle, with an unspecified striking vehicle in the dataset.

      On Franklin and India in Greenpoint, a 49-year-old man walking outside an intersection was hit by an e‑bike and died. The operator was unlicensed, per the city’s files. The record lists “severe bleeding” and death at the scene in the dataset.

      Three corners. One fix.

      Deaths in SD 59 did not stop with these names. In the last 12 months, this district saw 2,673 crashes, 9 deaths, and 1,388 injuries, with 23 serious injuries, according to the city’s open data rollup. Year to date, crashes are up 16.5% over last year, deaths are up to 7 from 2, and serious injuries have nearly doubled. The toll hits across ages, with the 45–54 band logging four deaths so far this year in the stats.

      Pedestrians here are struck by cars, SUVs, trucks, buses, and bikes. The district’s current totals show pedestrians killed by bikes (2), trucks (1), and buses (1), with hundreds more injured by sedans and SUVs in the stats. One mode does not own this crisis. Speed and mass do.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      Albany passed tools. City Hall drifts. The City can lower speeds under Sammy’s Law, and slow zones are on the table. Brooklyn CB1 asked for a 20 mph area-wide limit in Greenpoint and Williamsburg, backed by Sen. Kristen Gonzalez and others, who cited CB1’s high fatality rate. DOT said more info was coming on outer‑borough slow zones, reported by Streetsblog NYC. And the Queensboro Bridge path, promised, stayed shut until lawmakers pressed the Mayor. “The reasons given for this delay are not satisfactory,” they wrote, warning further delay would “unnecessarily put at risk” people forced to share a cramped lane, per Streetsblog NYC.

      The state Senate moved on the worst repeat drivers. Sen. Gonzalez co‑sponsored and voted “yes” in committee for S 4045 to require intelligent speed limiters for repeat camera violators and high‑point drivers. The bill advanced through committee votes on June 11 and 12, 2025, per the Senate file. Streets safety groups say a tiny pool of drivers cause outsized harm; camera repeaters are far more likely to kill, as documented by Streetsblog NYC. The city’s speed cameras were renewed to run 24/7 through 2030, as covered in our Take Action brief.

      Street by street, the work crawls. On 31st Street in Astoria, DOT vowed to defend protected bike lanes and traffic calming in court. “We stand firmly behind this project and will defend our work in court,” a DOT spokesman said, as noted by Streetsblog NYC. Electeds including Sen. Gonzalez backed the plan earlier in June, calling for designs that “protect pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers,” per Streetsblog NYC.

      Relentless math.

      • In this district: 8,490 crashes since 2022, with 22 deaths and 4,186 injuries.
      • In the last 12 months: 2,673 crashes, 9 deaths, 1,388 injuries.
      • Year to date vs. last year: crashes up 16.5%, deaths up 250%, serious injuries up 78% in the stats.

      One more body on Second Avenue. One more siren on the FDR. One more sheet on Franklin Street. The city has the levers.

      What now.

      • Drop speeds. Use Sammy’s Law. Make 20 mph the rule on local streets.
      • Stop repeat speeders. Pass and enforce intelligent speed assistance for the worst offenders.

      These are not theories. They are tools on the desk. Use them. Then check back here. And if you’re done waiting, act now — here’s how to take action.
