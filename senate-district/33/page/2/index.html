      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 33?

      SD 33: Streets That Keep Taking

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 33: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Recent killings

      On Aug. 11, police said a Mercedes hit two men on scooters on the Bronx River Parkway; both suffered fatal injuries “struck two men on scooters, who then suffered fatal injuries”. On June 28, a multivehicle crash sent five to the hospital; police said the driver fled “injured several pedestrians”. On May 10, police searched for a driver who hit and killed a pedestrian and left “after striking and killing a pedestrian”.

      A road rage case turned deadly on June 16. “An arrest was made after Keino Campbell, 27, was shot and killed over the weekend in New York City,” said Erica Lunsford.

      Open data logs another loss: Jan. 2, 2024, a moped and sedan collision at Mosholu Parkway and Bainbridge Ave left one person dead (CrashID 4692380).

      The toll

      Since 2022 in SD 33, 19 people are dead, 68 suffered serious injuries, and 4,632 were hurt in 7,839 crashes (district totals). The bodies and the broken bones say what policy has not.

      Rivera’s record

      State Sen. Gustavo Rivera co‑sponsored the Stop Super Speeders Act (S 4045) and voted yes in committee in June 2025 (bill page). The bill would require speed‑limiting tech for drivers with repeated violations.

      He also co‑sponsored S 1675 to create a pedestrian safety rating for vehicles (bill page). In 2023, powerful institutions tried to halt Fordham Road bus fixes; the pressure to delay safety was real (Fordham Rd letter).

      What to push now

      The pattern is clear. Lower the NYC default speed limit. Pass and enforce speed limiters for repeat speeders. Reward the yes votes. Demand passage and rollout that sticks.

      Act

      Tell City Hall and Albany to slow the streets and stop the worst drivers. Start here: take action. Do it now. The road won’t wait.
