      SD 33: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt: Recent Crashes in SD 33

      A man lies dead on the Deegan. His name was Darryl Mathis. He was hit by a Mercedes while stranded in his car. The driver ran. Police said, they were looking for a man who ran away after crashing his car. No one has been charged. Five more people, including pedestrians, landed in the hospital after a multivehicle crash in June. The driver fled. The suspect allegedly fled the scene after the crash, which injured several pedestrians.

      In the last 12 months, SD 33 saw 1,365 injuries, 19 serious injuries, and 1 death. The dead do not come back. The injured carry scars. The numbers do not stop. In three and a half years, 19 people have died and 4,295 have been hurt in 7,328 crashes. Children, elders, cyclists, and walkers are all in the count.

      Who Pays the Price?

      Cars and SUVs did the most harm. They killed 5, seriously hurt 17, and injured 718 more. Trucks and buses killed 2. Motorcycles and mopeds left 1 seriously injured and 47 more hurt. Bikes, too, are in the tally, but the weight of steel and speed does most of the killing.

      The faces change. The story does not. A pedestrian is struck. A moped rider is thrown. A driver flees. The street is washed clean, but the loss stays.

      Senator Rivera: Action and Accountability

      State Senator Gustavo Rivera has voted yes on bills to create safety zones and curb repeat speeders. He co-sponsored the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst offenders to install speed limiters. He also backed a bill to create a pedestrian safety rating system for vehicles. These are steps. But the carnage continues. The laws are not yet enough.

      The Next Step: Demand More

      Every crash is preventable. Every delay is a choice. Call Senator Rivera. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against repeat offenders. Stand with the families who have lost. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, oversees state agencies, and represents districts like SD 33.
      ▸ Where does SD 33 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 11 and assembly district AD 80.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 33?
      It includes the Mount Hope, Fordham Heights, Belmont, Bedford Park, Norwood, Riverdale-Spuyten Duyvil, Pelham Parkway-Van Nest, Morris Park, Allerton, Woodlawn Cemetery, and Bronx CB6 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 11, District 13, District 14, and District 15, and Assembly Districts AD 78, AD 80, AD 81, and AD 86.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 33?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm: 5 deaths, 17 serious injuries, and 718 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 1 serious injury and 47 injuries. Bikes caused 22 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Every crash is preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and real enforcement. Calling them ‘accidents’ hides the choices that lead to loss.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, pass laws to rein in repeat offenders, and fund safer street designs. They can push for enforcement that targets the most dangerous driving, not the most vulnerable road users.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
