      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 33?

      No More Dead Kids: Streets Are Broken, Leaders Must Act

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 33: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      Broken Streets, Broken Lives

      In Senate District 33, the toll does not let up. Since 2022, 19 people are dead. Over 4,100 have been injured. Sixty-two suffered wounds so grave they will not forget them. The dead do not get a second chance. The injured carry scars—some seen, most not.

      Just last month, a man in the Bronx slammed his car into others, then ran. Five people, including pedestrians, went to the hospital. The driver fled the scene. The news said it plain: “The suspect allegedly fled the scene after the crash, which injured several pedestrians.”

      A week later, a truck overturned and spilled liquid asphalt across a Bronx road. No one died, but the street was left slick and dangerous. Liquid asphalt poured onto a road in the Bronx after a truck overturned Wednesday.

      Who Pays the Price?

      The numbers are not just numbers. Children, elders, cyclists, and walkers are hit hardest. In the last twelve months, 131 people under 18 were hurt. Cars and SUVs did most of the damage—5 deaths, 17 serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed 2. Motorcycles and mopeds, 1 serious injury. Bikes, none. The street is not safe for the ones who do not drive.

      Leadership: Progress and the Long Road Ahead

      Senator Gustavo Rivera has taken some steps. He voted yes on a bill to force safer street design for all users, not just drivers. The bill passed. He also co-sponsored a measure to create a public safety rating for cars, so people can see which vehicles are most dangerous. But the rating bill sits idle. The streets do not wait.

      When business leaders tried to block bus improvements on Fordham Road, Rivera spoke for transit equity but called for more study. The buses still crawl. The danger remains.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day the city delays, another family pays. Call Senator Rivera. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real street redesigns. Demand action for the living, not excuses for the dead.
