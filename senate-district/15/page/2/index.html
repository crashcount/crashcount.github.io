      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 15?

      Nine Dead, Thousands Hurt: Addabbo’s District Bleeds While Streets Stay Deadly

      Written July 11, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 15: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      The Toll in SD 15

      The numbers do not lie. In the last twelve months, nine people died and 1,232 were injured on the streets of Senate District 15. Thirteen suffered injuries so severe they may never recover. The dead do not get second chances. The injured carry scars.

      Just weeks ago, a man in his 50s was left unresponsive on 101st Avenue in Ozone Park. A black SUV hit him and kept going. Police found him on the pavement, fighting for his life. He was rushed to Jamaica Hospital in critical condition. The driver vanished into the night. Police say the victim was struck by a black SUV traveling eastbound on 101st Avenue that kept going.

      A week before, a fire truck turned onto Juniper Boulevard and killed a cyclist in Middle Village. The man died at the scene. The NYPD is still investigating. The truck was turning onto Juniper Boulevard from 80th Street in Middle Village when the collision occurred.

      These are not accidents. They are the result of choices, policies, and priorities.

      What Has Senator Addabbo Done?

      Senator Joe Addabbo has taken some steps. He voted yes on a bill to require complete street design, aiming to protect everyone on the road, not just drivers. He backed a measure to redesign streets for safety. He also co-sponsored a bill to require e-scooter riders to get licenses and insurance, a move that puts more burden on those outside cars, not on the drivers who do the killing. He co-sponsored a bill that could burden e-scooter riders.

      Addabbo has supported studies and funding for better transit options, but the carnage on the streets continues. The bills are slow. The deaths are fast.

      What Comes Next

      The crisis is relentless. The bodies pile up. The policies crawl.

      Call Senator Addabbo. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real street redesigns that protect people, not just cars.

      Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home. The time for waiting is over.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 15.
      ▸ Where does SD 15 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 32 and assembly district AD 38.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 15?
      It includes the Glendale, Middle Village, Mount Olivet & All Faiths Cemeteries, Middle Village Cemetery, St. John Cemetery, Rego Park, Forest Hills, Richmond Hill, South Richmond Hill, Ozone Park (North), and Queens CB10 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 28, District 29, District 30, and District 32, and Assembly Districts AD 23, AD 24, AD 28, and AD 38.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 15?
      Most injuries and deaths were caused by Cars and Trucks (7 deaths, 704 incidents), followed by Motorcycles and Mopeds (3 deaths, 14 incidents), and Bikes (no deaths, 15 incidents). NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These crashes are preventable. They happen because of choices, policies, and street design—not fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign dangerous streets, expand automated enforcement, and pass laws that protect people walking and biking.

      Addabbo votes yes, boosting street safety with complete street design.
      ▸

      Senate Bill S 9718, titled "Enables safe access to public roads for all users by utilizing complete street design principles," advanced through the Senate with committee votes on May 28 and June 3, 2024. The bill, sponsored by Sean Ryan (District 61) and co-sponsored by Liz Krueger, Monica Martinez, Jack M. Martins, Anthony H. Palumbo, and Julia Salazar, passed both votes despite opposition. The measure calls for redesigning streets to protect all users, not just drivers. The vote was not unanimous—several senators voted no, but the majority carried it through. The bill’s focus is clear: safer streets for people on foot, on bikes, and in vehicles. No safety analyst note was provided, but the intent is to cut danger at the curb and crosswalk.
