      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 15?

      Blood in the Crosswalk, Silence in Albany

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      SD 15: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      Blood on the Boulevard

      In Senate District 15, the bodies keep coming. Since 2022, 28 people have died in traffic crashes. Forty-eight more suffered serious injuries. The wounds are not numbers. They are crushed bones, stopped hearts, empty chairs at dinner tables. In the last year alone, deaths nearly doubled—from 4 to 7—while crashes and injuries climbed higher. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians all pay the price.

      Just weeks ago, a man in his 50s was left fighting for his life after a hit-and-run in Ozone Park. The SUV kept going. The man did not. Police say the victim was struck by a black SUV traveling eastbound on 101st Avenue that kept going.

      A cyclist was killed by an FDNY truck turning onto Juniper Boulevard. The driver turned. The cyclist did not get up. The truck was turning onto Juniper Boulevard from 80th Street in Middle Village when the collision occurred.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did most of the damage. They killed 7, injured 538, and left 11 with serious wounds. Trucks and buses killed 4. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 3. Bikes killed none. The numbers are clear. The danger is not spread evenly. The bigger the machine, the more it takes.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      State Senator Joe Addabbo has voted for bills to curb repeat speeders and create safety zones. He backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst offenders to slow down. He co-sponsored bills to protect highway workers and first responders. These are steps. They are not enough.

      The carnage continues. The streets do not wait for laws to catch up. Every delay is another body.

      Call to Action: Demand More, Demand Now

      Call Senator Addabbo. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against repeat offenders. Demand streets where children can cross without fear.

      The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a choice. Make them choose life.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 15?
      Cars and SUVs killed 7 and injured 538; trucks and buses killed 4; motorcycles and mopeds killed 3; bikes killed none. The largest vehicles did the most harm. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and enforcement against repeat offenders can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, target repeat dangerous drivers, and redesign streets to protect people walking and biking.
