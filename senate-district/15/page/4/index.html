      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 15?

      Addabbo Votes; Streets Bleed. Demand Real Safety Now.

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 15: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 15, the road takes what it wants. Since 2022, 28 people have died and 48 have been left with serious injuries in crashes. The numbers do not flinch. In the last year alone, nine more lives ended. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians—none are spared.

      On April 19, a fire truck turned onto Juniper Boulevard and killed a cyclist in Middle Village. The man died at the scene. Police said, “the truck was turning onto Juniper Boulevard from 80th Street in Middle Village when the collision occurred.” The NYPD is still investigating. A witness saw firefighters, “shaken, like shocked,” standing in the road after the crash. “I just feel so bad for everyone. For the people in the fire truck, the families involved, everyone,” he said.

      Days later, a BMW driver killed a motorcyclist in a burst of rage. The Queens DA said, “As alleged, this defendant plowed his BMW SUV into a motorcyclist shortly after the two had a minor collision.” The bike caught fire. The rider died on the street.

      The Vehicles That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did most of the harm: 7 deaths, 11 serious injuries. Trucks and buses: 4 deaths, 4 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds: 3 deaths, 2 serious injuries. Bikes: no deaths, no serious injuries. The weight of steel and speed decides who lives and who dies.

      What Has Senator Addabbo Done?

      Senator Joe Addabbo has voted yes on bills to curb repeat speeders, extend school speed zones, and create safety zones for first responders. He co-sponsored bills to raise penalties for endangering highway workers and to lower speed limits in safety zones. These votes matter. But the deaths keep coming. The policies are not enough. The streets are still not safe for the most vulnerable.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This crisis is not fate. It is policy. Call Senator Addabbo. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people outside cars. Every day of delay is another name on a slab.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 15.
      ▸ Where does SD 15 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 32 and assembly district AD 38.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 15?
      It includes the Glendale, Middle Village, Mount Olivet & All Faiths Cemeteries, Middle Village Cemetery, St. John Cemetery, Rego Park, Forest Hills, Richmond Hill, South Richmond Hill, Ozone Park (North), and Queens CB10 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 28, District 29, District 30, and District 32, and Assembly Districts AD 23, AD 24, AD 28, and AD 38.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 15?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm to pedestrians in SD 15: 7 deaths and 11 serious injuries. Trucks and buses caused 4 deaths and 4 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 3 deaths and 2 serious injuries. Bikes caused no deaths and no serious injuries. Data source
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and stronger enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund safer street redesigns, and support laws that hold dangerous drivers accountable.
