      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 32?

      Drag the Street, Kill a Man—Webster Avenue’s Toll Demands Real Action

      Written July 13, 2025

      SD 32: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 13, 2025

      The Blood on Webster Avenue

      A man stood in the crosswalk. A black Mercedes came fast, did not stop, and hit him. He flew, then fell. The car dragged him half a block. The driver kept going. His mother said, “That car deliberately went straight speeding, didn’t stop, hit my son all the way up into the air and came down, dragged him half a block, they killed my son” (told ABC7).

      This was not the only loss. In the last year, six people died in crashes in SD 32. 1,636 were injured. Twenty-five suffered serious injuries. Most were walking. Some were children. The street does not care who you are. The numbers do not lie.

      The Pattern: Speed, Steel, and Silence

      The dead do not speak. Their families do. “Great father, great person—everybody in the community loved him… Everybody is missing Kelvin. It’s not gonna be the same over here without him” (his sister told the NY Daily News).

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. In three years, cars and SUVs caused nine deaths and over 850 injuries to people on foot or bike. Trucks and buses hit too. The sidewalk is not safe. The crosswalk is not safe. The numbers rise, the bodies fall.

      What Has Senator Sepúlveda Done?

      Senator Luis Sepúlveda voted yes on bills for school speed cameras (S 8117), safer street design (S 3897), and funding for complete streets (S 840). He backed the Stop Super Speeders Act (S 4045), which would force repeat dangerous drivers to slow down. He co-sponsored a bill to expand camera enforcement (S 7336).

      But he also sponsored a bill to require licenses and insurance for e-scooter riders (S 5801). Paperwork for cyclists and delivery workers. No such burden for the drivers who kill.

      The Next Step: Demand More

      The crisis is not over. The deaths keep coming. The laws are not enough. Call Senator Sepúlveda. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people, not paperwork for the vulnerable.

      Every day of delay is another family broken.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 32.
      ▸ Where does SD 32 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 15 and assembly district AD 79.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 32?
      It includes the Melrose, Longwood, Morrisania, Claremont Village-Claremont (East), Crotona Park East, Crotona Park, Concourse-Concourse Village, Mount Eden-Claremont (West), Claremont Park, Mount Hope, and Bronx CB6 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 15, District 16, and District 17, and Assembly Districts AD 77, AD 79, AD 85, AD 86, and AD 87.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 32?
      Cars and SUVs caused 9 deaths and over 850 injuries to people on foot or bike in SD 32. Trucks and buses caused 0 deaths and 62 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 0 deaths and 53 injuries. Bikes caused 0 deaths and 21 injuries. (Source: NYC Open Data)
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not random. Most deaths and injuries in SD 32 come from speeding, driver inattention, and unsafe street design—problems that can be fixed.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, expand camera enforcement, and pass laws that hold dangerous drivers accountable—not just burden cyclists and pedestrians.
