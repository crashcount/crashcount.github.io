      SD 32: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      EAST 167TH AND WASHINGTON: A WOMAN KILLED WITH THE WALK

      Late afternoon. East 167th Street at Washington Avenue. Three SUVs, one parked. A 31‑year‑old woman crossed with the signal. A Toyota SUV going west hit her. The file says driver inattention and a blown traffic control. She died at the intersection. The parked Volkswagen took the impact after. It was May 28, 2024. The state calls it CrashID 4728165. The paper calls it a death.

      WEBSTER AND EAST 169TH: A FATHER DRAGGED HALF A BLOCK

      After midnight, May 10, 2025. A southbound Mercedes on Webster. A man in the crosswalk on East 169th. The car hit him and dragged him down the block. The driver fled. Family stood on the corner and named the loss. “They killed my son. I need justice for my son,” his mother said. “They will be liable for my brother’s death,” his sister said. A friend added, “He’s a basketball player, the best basketball player.” The city data lists unsafe speed. The press shows a police van in chase before the hit. The name was Kelvin Mitchell, 43.

      RIGHT TURN, FLATBED, EAST 169TH AND FRANKLIN

      Morning rush. February 3, 2025. A 65‑year‑old woman in the intersection. A 2017 flatbed truck turned right from E 169th to Franklin. Right front bumper impact. She died. The record lists “Crossing Against Signal.” The truck kept its plates. The corner kept her life.

      THE NUMBERS DON’T LET UP

      From 2022 to now, this senate district logged 9,181 crashes, 5,689 injuries, and 26 deaths. SUVs and sedans hit most pedestrians. Trucks and buses still break bodies. In the last 12 months alone: 2,749 crashes, 1,905 injuries, six dead. Year to date, crashes and injuries are up over last year. The district bleeds in small hours and bright afternoons alike.

      • District rollup and trends — CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data.

      HIT, RUN, AND YEARS LATER A KNOCK AT THE DOOR

      Two Bronx workers died on duty. A livery driver, Robert Godwin, was T‑boned at 77 mph in a 25 mph zone. The SUV pushed his cab seventy feet. The driver fled, cops say, and was later arrested. A car wash worker, Felix Thomas Bontia, was pinned between two SUVs in Morrisania. The driver ran. Two years passed. Then the Warrants Squad came with cuffs. Cases like these show what speed and flight do to a body, and to a family’s sleep.

      WHAT ALBANY DID. WHAT’S STILL LEFT.

      The Senate advanced a bill to force speed limiters on repeat offenders. Senators voted yes in committee. The thresholds are in the bill text. This district’s senator voted yes. The chamber also renewed the city’s 24‑hour school speed cameras through 2030. These are moves on paper. The street waits.

      • Committee action and language — S 4045 bill page
      • School speed zones extension — S 8344 actions (see timeline entries) and district vote records.

      SLOW THE CARS. STOP THE WORST.

      The city now has the power to set safer speeds. It can drop the default limit on local streets. The state bill would fit speed limiters to the small group who keep breaking the law. These are blunt tools for a blunt problem: bodies in crosswalks and steel that does not slow. Demand both. Start here: take action.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where is this happening?
      These crashes occurred across Bronx Senate District 32, including East 167th at Washington, Webster at East 169th, East 169th at Franklin, East 163rd at Westchester, and Claremont Parkway at Park, per NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ How many people have been hurt here recently?
      From Jan 1, 2022 to Aug 26, 2025, the district logged 9,181 crashes, 5,689 injuries, and 26 deaths. In the last 12 months: 2,749 crashes, 1,905 injuries, and six deaths, according to CrashCount’s rollup of NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What do we know about the Webster Avenue hit-and-run?
      City data lists unsafe speed in a fatal strike by a Mercedes sedan near E 168th on May 10, 2025. Reporting names the victim as Kelvin Mitchell, 43, and shows a police van in pursuit before the crash. Quotes from his family are reported by Streetsblog and ABC7.
      ▸ What policies are on the table?
      The Senate advanced S 4045 to require intelligent speed assistance for repeat violators and extended 24‑hour school speed‑zone cameras via S 8344. These steps target the worst drivers and protect school zones.
