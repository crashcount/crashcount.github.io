      Webster Runs Red—Hold Politicians Accountable for Every Lost Life

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed grammar, tightened prose, cleaned citations, and ensured proper link formatting.)

      SD 32: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Blood on Webster Avenue

      A man stands in a crosswalk. A black Mercedes comes fast. The car does not stop. It hits him, drags him half a block. His name is Kelvin Mitchell. He was a father. He is gone. His mother said, “That car deliberately went straight speeding, didn’t stop, hit my son all the way up into the air and came down, dragged him half a block, they killed my son.”

      In the last twelve months, five people have died on the streets of Senate District 32. Over 1,500 have been injured. Twenty-six suffered wounds so grave they may never walk the same. The dead are not numbers. They are fathers, mothers, children. They are missed. “Everybody is missing Kelvin. It’s not gonna be the same over here without him,” said his sister.

      The Record of Leadership

      Senator Luis Sepúlveda has voted for safer street design. He co-sponsored a bill to rate cars for pedestrian safety. But he also pushed a bill that would slow down new bike lanes, making it harder to build the infrastructure that saves lives. He is now sponsoring a bill to require licenses and insurance for e-scooter riders. These rules do not stop speeding cars. They do not protect the child in the crosswalk.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      The crisis is not fate. It is policy. Every day, another family risks loss. Call Sepúlveda. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand more protected crossings. Demand action that protects people, not just drivers. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 32 in Albany.
      ▸ Where does SD 32 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 15 and assembly district AD 79.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 32?
      It includes the Longwood, Morrisania, Claremont Village-Claremont (East), Crotona Park East, Crotona Park, Claremont Park, West Farms, Tremont, and Bronx CB3 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 15, District 16, and District 17, and Assembly Districts AD 77, AD 79, AD 85, AD 86, and AD 87.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 32?
      Cars and trucks were responsible for 8 deaths, 239 moderate injuries, and 11 serious injuries to pedestrians in SD 32. Motorcycles and mopeds caused no deaths but 17 moderate and 2 serious injuries. Bikes caused no deaths, 4 moderate injuries, and 2 serious injuries. See NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and better enforcement. They are not random—they follow patterns that policy can change.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and support laws for lower speed limits, fund protected bike lanes and crosswalks, and oppose bills that delay safety upgrades.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
