      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Bronx SD 32: Crosswalks, sirens, and a trail of bodies

      SD 32: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      At East 169th and Webster, Kelvin Mitchell died under a black Mercedes. Video shows the hit, the drag, the flight. His mother’s words are a blade: “They killed my son.” He was 43. The crash came just before 1 a.m. on May 10. Police records list unsafe speed and a sedan going south on Webster.

      —

      Two blocks over, another crosswalk

      On East 167th at Washington, a 31‑year‑old woman stepped off the curb with the signal. A Toyota SUV hit her. She died there. Three SUVs are logged in the case file; one was parked, one had Arizona plates. The entry is dry: “Crossing With Signal.” “Apparent Death.” CrashID 4728165.

      At East 163rd and Westchester, dawn. A Ford SUV with Texas plates struck a 56‑year‑old man in the marked crosswalk. The note says “No Signal.” The result is the same. Apparent death.

      —

      Night chase, silent street

      Back at Webster and 168th, a police van chased a speeding Mercedes, witnesses said. The car hit Mitchell and kept going. His family stood on the sidewalk and spoke for him. “They will be liable for my brother’s death,” his sister said. “He’s a basketball player, the best,” a friend said. ABC7 taped his mother again: “Dragged him half a block.”

      The dataset marks the cause as “Unsafe Speed.” The impact point: center front. CrashID 4811637.

      —

      Bronx numbers don’t blink

      In this Senate district since 2022: 26 people killed and 5,689 injured across 9,181 crashes, with 86 listed as serious injuries. SUVs and sedans account for the largest share of pedestrian harm: 955 pedestrian hits tied to cars and SUVs, with 9 deaths in that slice alone. NYC Open Data.

      Year to date, reported crashes are up 17% over last year’s pace; injuries up 24%. Deaths are down on paper, but the funerals are real. District stats.

      —

      Work shift, pinned and gone

      A car wash worker in Morrisania was crushed between two SUVs. Police say the driver fled on foot. Two years passed. An arrest came. The charge sheet is long: manslaughter, vehicular manslaughter, criminally negligent homicide, leaving the scene. New York Post.

      A cab driver caught a red‑light runner doing 77 in a 25. The SUV shoved his car 70 feet. He died in the seat. The driver walked away, cops said, until DNA closed the gap. NY Daily News.

      —

      Who moves, who acts

      Albany put tools on the table. The Senate advanced the Stop Super Speeders Act to force repeat violators to use speed limiters. Senator Sepúlveda voted yes in committee and co‑sponsored it. Open States. NYC can also set safer speeds under Sammy’s Law. The city has started to lower limits on some blocks, but the decision for a wider drop sits with City Hall and the Council. Take action.

      —

      What ends this

      Lower the default speed. Fit the worst drivers with governors. Fewer bodies on Webster. Fewer vigils on 167th. Start now. Here’s how.

      S 8344 Sepúlveda votes yes to extend school speed zones, improving child pedestrian safety.
      ▸

      Jun 13 - Senate passes S 8344. School speed zone rules in New York City get extended. Lawmakers make technical fixes. The bill keeps pressure on drivers near schools. Streets stay a little safer for kids.

      Bill S 8344, titled 'Extends provisions and makes technical corrections to school speed zones in NYC; repealer,' moved through the Senate and Assembly in June 2025. Sponsored by Senator Andrew Gounardes, the bill passed Senate votes on June 12 and June 13, and cleared the Assembly on June 17. The measure extends and corrects school speed zone laws in New York City, repealing outdated provisions. Gounardes led the push, with strong support from most Senate Democrats and a split Assembly.       S 4045 Sepúlveda votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Jun 12 - Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
