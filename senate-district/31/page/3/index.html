      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 31?

      Major Deegan, Minor Consequences: Blood on the Streets, Silence in City Hall

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Updated period stats)

      SD 31: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 20, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Silence

      A man stranded on the Major Deegan called his friends for help. The battery was dead. A black Mercedes slammed into him from behind. He called again, this time to say he could not breathe. He died before help arrived. The driver ran. Police have not found him. “He called to get a jump, and after that, he got hit from behind, a hit-and-run. They were on their way to come give him a jump and he got hit waiting for them. And then he called to let them know, I’ve been hit. I can’t breathe.”

      In the last twelve months, eight people died in Senate District 31. Twenty-four were seriously injured. Over a thousand were hurt. The dead include the old and the young. A 65-year-old man was killed crossing with the signal. A 24-year-old woman was struck in a crosswalk. The street does not care who you are.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did most of the damage. In three years, they killed seven pedestrians, left twenty with serious injuries, and caused 190 moderate injuries. Trucks and buses killed three. Motorcycles and mopeds caused one serious injury and sixteen moderate injuries. Bikes caused one serious injury and eight moderate injuries. The numbers do not lie. The street is a battlefield, and the weapons are heavy and fast.

      What Has Been Done, What Has Not

      Senator Robert Jackson has voted for safer streets. He backed the law to force complete street design, to protect people on foot and on bikes. He voted yes. He co-sponsored bills for fare-free buses and for public safety ratings on cars. He supported 24/7 speed cameras. “Cars speeding in New York kill New Yorkers, injure New Yorkers.”

      But the blood keeps coming. The cameras do not cover every street. The laws do not slow every car. The city has the power to lower the speed limit to 20 mph. It has not done so. The dead wait for action.

      Call to Action: No More Waiting

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand 20 mph on every street. Demand cameras that never sleep. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. The street will not wait for you.
