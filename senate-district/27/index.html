      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Mercer and Houston, noon heat, a bike and a taxi

      SD 27: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 18, 2025

      Around noon on Aug 20, 2025, at Mercer Street and W Houston Street, a taxi and a person on a bike collided. A 31‑year‑old woman suffered severe lacerations. Source.

      She is one of 4,998 people injured here since Jan 1, 2022. In that time, this district logged 11,035 crashes and 32 deaths. Source.

      The numbers do not let up

      In the past 12 months, SD 27 saw 2,780 crashes, 1,402 injuries, and 3 deaths. Source.

      Year to date, crashes are down 7.0% and deaths are down 85.7% compared with last year, while injuries are up 4.8%. Source.

      Where responsibility sits

      This is Senate District 27. The State Senator is Brian Kavanagh. He voted yes in committee for S 4045, a bill to require intelligent speed assistance for repeat speeders. Record. The Council Member is Christopher Marte. The Assembly Member is Grace Lee.

      The city now has the power to lower speeds. Sammy’s Law allows a lower default. A citywide 20 MPH limit is on the table. Details.

      What must change to stop this

      • Habitual speeders can be stopped. The Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045C/A2299C) would force repeat violators to use speed limiters. Kavanagh has voted yes on the Senate bill. Will the Assembly move the companion? Bill info and background.
      • The city can set a safer default speed. Use the power in Sammy’s Law to drop the limit on residential streets to 20 MPH. How.

      No more should hinge on the luck of a light at Mercer and Houston.

      Take one step. Ask City Hall to lower the limit and Albany to pass the limiter bill. Act now at CrashCount Take Action.

      ▸
