      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 27?

      Canal and Bowery: two deaths, a promise, and a corridor still lethal

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 27: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      At Bowery and Canal, speed met flesh. On July 19, a stolen Chevrolet Malibu, pushed past 100 mph off the Manhattan Bridge, hit Kevin Cruickshank, 55, and May Kwok, 63. Both died at the scene, according to NY1 and the Daily News. A grand jury indicted two women days later, said CBS New York. The city rushed out barriers, narrower lanes, and lower limits at the corner. “We are taking immediate steps to fortify this intersection,” said DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez, quoted by Gothamist. Advocates warned the rest of Canal stays dangerous.

      NY1 reported Kwok sat on a bench. Cruickshank rode his bike on a sidewalk near the bridge mouth. Both were thrown and did not get up, the Daily News wrote. The city scheduled redesign meetings. The blood stays on the corridor.

      The deaths don’t stop at the corner

      On Spring and Crosby, a 54‑year‑old woman crossed with the signal. A Jeep making a left struck her. She died. The city’s crash file lists “failure to yield” for the driver. That was October 28, 2024, at 8:26 a.m., per NYC Open Data.

      On June 29, 2024, just after midnight, a 31‑year‑old woman was killed walking on the FDR Drive. The driver’s contributing factor reads “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” The crash record says she died at the scene. Source: NYC Open Data.

      On July 4, 2024, a pickup going straight killed four pedestrians and injured a child on Water and Jackson. The database records crush injuries for the dead. Five bodies in one entry. The pickup was registered in New Jersey. Source: NYC Open Data.

      A bike, a truck, a body in the road

      A 44‑year‑old bicyclist died at Broome and Centre on May 1, 2025. The record shows an e‑bike, a box truck, and a parked SUV in the crash. The cyclist was ejected. The file lists “Apparent Death.” Source: NYC Open Data.

      These are not outliers. Since 2022, SD27 has logged 8,588 crashes, with 27 people dead and 3,903 injured, including 61 listed as serious, according to district rollups in NYC Open Data. SUVs and sedans lead the body count for pedestrians. Trucks follow. Bikes hurt people too, but cars kill more. See the breakdown in the same dataset.

      City vows fixes. The ledger keeps growing.

      After the Chinatown crash, DOT promised quick work at Canal and Bowery. “Immediate steps,” Rodriguez said, per Gothamist. Ben Furnas put it bluntly: “Canal Street is only as safe as its most dangerous block, and even after these changes, the vast majority of the corridor will remain deadly.” Same report.

      Albany moved on the worst drivers. The Senate advanced the Stop Super Speeders bill in June. It would force speed‑limiting devices on drivers who rack up violations. Senator Brian Kavanagh voted yes in committee, according to the bill page for S 4045.

      Slow the cars. Stop the repeat offenders.

      Lower speeds save lives. The city now has the power to set safer limits and expand 20 mph zones, as laid out in our own call to action. Cameras run 24/7 through 2030. The worst repeat speeders need limiters. These are the tools at hand. Use them.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where did the Chinatown crash happen and who was killed?
      At Canal Street and Bowery near the Manhattan Bridge. Kevin Cruickshank, 55, and May Kwok, 63, were killed, according to NY1 and the Daily News.
      ▸ What did the city say it would do after the crash?
      DOT said it would add barriers, narrow lanes, and lower speed limits at the intersection. Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez said, “We are taking immediate steps to fortify this intersection,” per Gothamist.
      ▸ What do crash records show elsewhere in SD27?
      A pedestrian killed by a left‑turning Jeep at Spring and Crosby (failure to yield). A pedestrian killed on the FDR by a straight‑moving sedan (driver inattention). A pickup killing four pedestrians on Water and Jackson. A bicyclist killed at Broome and Centre involving a box truck and e‑bike. All from NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What policies could reduce this violence?
      Lower default speeds and mandate intelligent speed assistance for repeat speeders. The Senate bill S 4045 to require speed limiters for habitual violators advanced in June 2025.

      Kavanagh Supports Safety Boosting FDR Drive Removal Plan
      ▸

      Manhattan Borough President Mark Levine wants to tear down FDR Drive south of the Brooklyn Bridge. He calls for federal funds to turn the highway into a bike and pedestrian haven. The plan faces legal and political hurdles but aims to reclaim space for people.

      On September 20, 2023, Manhattan Borough President Mark Levine revived a proposal to remove the FDR Drive viaduct south of the Brooklyn Bridge. The plan, not yet in committee or assigned a bill number, seeks federal support through the Reconnecting Communities and Neighborhoods grant. Levine urges Mayor Adams to apply for funding, stating, 'We’re a perfect candidate for Reconnecting Communities.' State Senator Brian Kavanagh voiced support, saying, 'We will work with the borough president.' The proposal would transform the underused highway into a waterfront space for cyclists and pedestrians, echoing successful removals in cities like Seattle and Seoul. The project faces major legal, environmental, and jurisdictional obstacles, as the FDR is state-owned and exempt from congestion pricing. The city previously considered this removal for coastal resiliency, but dropped it from the final plan. No formal safety analysis was provided, but the plan centers vulnerable road users by reclaiming space from cars.
