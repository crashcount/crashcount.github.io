      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 30?

      No More Names on Asphalt: End the Killing Streets Now

      Written July 11, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 30: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Senate District 30, the numbers do not lie. Since 2022, 22 people are dead. Seventy-one more are seriously hurt. These are not just numbers—they are neighbors, children, elders. A 73-year-old man, crossing at W 135th Street, struck down by an SUV. A 43-year-old cyclist, killed on the 97th Street Transverse. A baby, crushed on the RFK Bridge exit. The road does not care who you are. It takes without mercy.

      The Latest Crashes: No End in Sight

      The violence does not stop. In June, a cyclist was killed in Central Park. In May, a police car swerved to avoid a taxi and plowed into two people eating outside on Broadway. The cab driver got a summons for failure to yield. The diners went to the hospital. The police described the chaos: “The chaos erupted as the police cruiser, with lights flashing and sirens blaring, was traveling north on Broadway… when a cab heading south attempted a leisurely left turn onto West 112th Street.” The investigation is ongoing. The street is unchanged.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Miles to Go

      Senator Cordell Cleare has voted for safer streets. In June 2024, she voted yes on a bill to require complete street design, putting people before cars. The bill calls for roads that protect everyone, not just drivers. But the blood on the asphalt says the work is not done. Every week brings new names to the list.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. The dead and injured are the cost of delay. The city can lower speed limits. The state can stop repeat offenders. The council can redesign streets. The time for half-measures is over.

      Call your leaders. Demand action. Every day they wait, another family waits for a knock at the door.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York, including SD 30.
      ▸ Where does SD 30 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 9 and assembly district AD 70.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 30?
      It includes the Upper West Side-Manhattan Valley, Morningside Heights, Manhattanville-West Harlem, Hamilton Heights-Sugar Hill, Harlem (South), Harlem (North), East Harlem (North), Central Park, Manhattan CB10, and Manhattan CB64. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 6, District 7, District 8, and District 9, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 69, AD 70, and AD 71.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 30?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm to pedestrians in SD 30, with 9 deaths and 625 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds were involved in 30 crashes, causing 30 injuries. Bikes were involved in 39 crashes, causing 39 injuries. These figures are based on NYC Open Data from 2022 to July 2025.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and stronger enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign streets for safety, and pass laws to stop repeat dangerous drivers.
