      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      W 125th: a boy on a bike, a driver, and the cost of waiting

      SD 30: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 18, 2025

      A 15-year-old on a bike went down on W 125 St. A driver in a Toyota sedan, going straight, hit him. Police recorded driver inattention and improper lane use. He bled from the head. He survived, badly hurt. Source.

      This Week

      • Aug 29 at E 125 St and 2 Ave, a driver in a box truck and a man on a bike collided; the cyclist suffered severe bleeding. Police noted driver inattention. Source
      • Aug 15, a 62-year-old driver died after crashing into a parked SUV on W 127 St; police recorded distraction and following too closely. Source

      The toll does not let up

      In the past 12 months, Senate District 30 recorded 11 deaths and 1,385 injuries from 2,594 crashes. Another 25 people were seriously injured. Source.

      This year, deaths are up. Year to date, 6 people have been killed, compared to 3 over the same span last year. Crashes are slightly lower; injuries are roughly flat. The bodies are not. Source.

      Who answers for this

      This district’s State Senator is Cordell Cleare. She co‑sponsored and voted yes on the Senate’s speed‑limiter bill, S 4045, aimed at repeat speeders. Record.

      The Assembly Member here is Jordan Wright. The Council Member is Yusef Salaam. The record shown here does not document Assembly sponsorship. What moves next, and when?

      Slow the cars. Stop the repeats.

      Two fixes are on the table. Albany advanced the repeat‑speeder bill in the Senate (S 4045). City Hall has the power to lower speeds citywide under Sammy’s Law; lower default limits cut the force of every crash. The pattern on 125th Street is not an act of God. It is policy, or the lack of it.

      Act now. Tell your officials to set safer speeds and back speed limiters for the worst repeaters. Start here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What area does this report cover?
      New York State Senate District 30, covering parts of the Upper West Side–Manhattan Valley, Morningside Heights, Manhattanville–West Harlem, Hamilton Heights–Sugar Hill, Harlem (North and South), East Harlem (North), and Central Park.
      ▸ How many people were harmed recently?
      In the past 12 months, 11 people were killed and 1,385 were injured across 2,594 crashes in Senate District 30. Another 25 people suffered serious injuries. Source: NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What changed this year?
      Year to date, 6 people have been killed in crashes in Senate District 30, compared to 3 over the same span last year. Crashes are slightly down; injuries are roughly flat. Source: NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We analyzed NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95, Persons f55k-p6yu, Vehicles bm4k-52h4) for 2022-01-01 to 2025-09-18, filtered to Senate District 30 using our site’s geography filters. We counted deaths, injuries, serious injuries, and total crashes, and compared the same YTD window year over year. Data was accessed Sep 18, 2025. You can explore the base datasets here.
