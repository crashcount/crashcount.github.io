      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 30?

      Bodies in the Crosswalk—Hold Them Accountable Before More Die

      Written July 9, 2025

      SD 30: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 9, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Senate District 30, the bodies keep coming. Twenty people have died on these streets since 2022. Nearly 4,000 more have been injured. Sixty-seven were hurt so badly they may never walk the same again. Children, elders, cyclists, pedestrians—no one is spared.

      Just last month, a man was killed crossing the RFK Bridge exit at 2nd Avenue. In January, a 59-year-old cyclist died under the wheels of a bus on Lenox Avenue. In October, a 13-year-old girl was struck and killed by an SUV on Manhattan Avenue. The numbers are cold. The loss is not.

      The Usual Weapons: Cars, SUVs, Trucks

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. They have taken eight lives and left hundreds bleeding. Trucks and buses killed two more. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes have left their own scars, but the carnage is driven by heavy metal and speed.

      Leadership: Progress and the Long Road Ahead

      State Senator Cordell Cleare has voted for safer streets. She backed a bill to require ‘complete street’ design, putting people before traffic (see her vote). She co-sponsored another to force every new project to consider the lives of those on foot or bike (see sponsorship). But laws on paper do not slow a speeding SUV.

      The city has started to act. Speed cameras now run 24/7. Some speed limits have dropped. But the blood still runs. As Councilmember Keith Powers said, ‘Fires from unregulated e-bike batteries have already caused too much harm, including tragic loss of life.’

      The Call: Demand More Than Words

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every death is a choice made by those in power. Call Senator Cleare. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real street redesign. Demand an end to the killing.

      Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 30.
      ▸ Where does SD 30 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 9 and assembly district AD 70.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 30?
      It includes the Upper West Side-Manhattan Valley, Morningside Heights, Manhattanville-West Harlem, Hamilton Heights-Sugar Hill, Harlem (South), Harlem (North), East Harlem (North), Central Park, Manhattan CB10, and Manhattan CB64 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 6, District 7, District 8, and District 9, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 69, AD 70, and AD 71.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 30?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm to pedestrians: 8 deaths and hundreds of injuries. Trucks and buses caused 2 deaths. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 28 injuries. Bikes were involved in 36 injuries but no deaths. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Policies like lower speed limits, street redesign, and better enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws for lower speed limits, safer street design, and stronger penalties for dangerous driving. They can also push for more speed cameras and prioritize vulnerable road users.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
