      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 22?

      Sutton Sleeps, Brooklyn Bleeds

      Written July 13, 2025

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 13, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 22, the streets do not forgive. In the last twelve months, 11 people died and 22 suffered serious injuries in crashes. Three children never made it home. These are not just numbers. They are families torn open, lives ended in the crosswalk, on the curb, at the corner store.

      Last week, a 90-year-old man was crossing Avenue U at East 14th Street. A blue moped came fast. The rider wore a mask. He hit the old man and left him bleeding in the street. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said a witness. The killer did not stop. Police are still looking.

      The day before, a 70-year-old man was crossing Avenue I. A Smart Car driver plowed into him. The man died at Maimonides. The driver stayed. No charges were filed. The street was quiet again by morning. Source

      The Patterns That Kill

      Cars and SUVs do most of the killing. In this district since 2022, they have taken 18 lives and left hundreds more broken. Trucks and buses killed four. Motorcycles and mopeds killed none, but left many hurt. Bikes have not killed anyone, but have caused injuries. The pattern is clear. The danger comes on four wheels, heavy and fast.

      Leadership: Action or Absence?

      State Senator Sam Sutton missed two key votes this June. One bill would have forced repeat speeders to install devices that keep them from breaking the limit (S4045). Another would have expanded speed cameras near schools (S8117). Both passed without his support. The silence is loud. The streets stay deadly.

      What Now?

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every day without action is another day someone does not come home. Call Senator Sutton. Demand he show up for the living and the dead. Demand real change. The blood on the street is not washed away by words.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, including those that affect traffic safety, for all of New York State.
      ▸ Where does SD 22 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 48 and assembly district AD 41. Source
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 22?
      It includes the Borough Park, Mapleton-Midwood (West), Midwood, Gravesend (East)-Homecrest, Madison, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, Marine Park-Plumb Island, Brooklyn CB12, Brooklyn CB14, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 44, District 46, and District 48, and Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 45, AD 48, and AD 59. Source
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 22?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm, with 18 deaths and hundreds of injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused injuries but no deaths; bikes caused injuries but no deaths.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Policies like lower speed limits, speed cameras, and holding repeat offenders accountable can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and support laws for lower speed limits, expand speed and red-light cameras, and require technology to stop repeat speeders. Showing up and voting for safety bills matters.
