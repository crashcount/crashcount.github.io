      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 22?

      Senator Sutton Sleeps While Blood Runs in SD22 Streets

      Written July 18, 2025 (Revised July 18, 2025 – Fixed grammar, tightened prose, removed headline/body duplication, cleaned citations, and ensured proper inline links.)

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 18, 2025

      The Death Toll Grows, Week by Week

      In Senate District 22, the streets do not forgive. In the last twelve months, 11 people died and 22 suffered serious injuries in crashes. Three children never made it home. The numbers do not flinch: 1,357 injured in just one year. Each number is a body, a family, a life split open.

      Last week, a 90-year-old man was crossing East 14th Street at Avenue U. A blue moped hit him and kept going. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said an eyewitness. The driver wore a mask. He did not stop. The old man died in the hospital. His family waits for answers. “We hope the authorities can find the person who did this. We are also concerned about the motorcycles running rampant in crowded residential areas,” said the victim’s grandson.

      The day before, a 70-year-old man was struck and killed by a Smart Car while crossing Avenue I. The driver stayed. The man died anyway. No charges were filed. The street is quiet again, but the blood is still there.

      Patterns of Harm: Who Pays the Price

      Cars and SUVs do most of the killing. Since 2022, they have taken 18 lives and left 872 injured. Trucks and buses killed 4. Motorcycles and mopeds killed none, but left dozens hurt. Bikes injured 22. The old, the young, the ones who walk—these are the bodies left behind.

      Leadership: Missing in Action

      State Senator Sam Sutton missed the vote on the Stop Super Speeders Act—a bill that would force repeat speeders to install devices that keep them from breaking the limit according to Open States. He missed votes on other school zone safety bills too. There is no record of him speaking out after these deaths. There is no sign he is fighting for the dead or the living.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Senator Sutton. Demand he show up. Demand he fight for the living. Every day of silence is another day of blood on the street.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York.
      ▸ Where does SD 22 sit politically?
      It belongs to Brooklyn, city council district 48, and assembly district 41. See NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 22?
      It includes the Borough Park, Mapleton-Midwood (West), Midwood, Gravesend (East)-Homecrest, Madison, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, Marine Park-Plumb Island, Brooklyn CB12, Brooklyn CB14, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts 44, 46, and 48, and Assembly Districts 41, 45, 48, and 59. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 22?
      Cars and SUVs caused 18 deaths and 872 injuries. Trucks and buses caused 4 deaths. Motorcycles and mopeds caused no deaths but dozens of injuries; bikes caused 22 injuries and no deaths. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Policy, street design, and enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and support laws that slow cars, hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable, and fund safer street designs.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
