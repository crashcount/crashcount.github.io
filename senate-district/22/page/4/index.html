      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 22?

      How Many Dead Kids Before Sutton Acts?

      Written July 8, 2025

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 8, 2025

      The Death Toll on Local Streets

      In Senate District 22, the numbers do not lie. Thirty-five people killed. Forty-one seriously injured. Over 4,000 hurt. These are not just numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. In the last year alone, ten people died and eighteen suffered life-changing wounds. Three of the dead were under eighteen. The youngest was eight. A mother and her two daughters were killed crossing with the light on Ocean Parkway. Their names are gone from the headlines, but the loss remains.

      Recent Crashes: No End in Sight

      The carnage does not stop. On June 23, a 17-year-old boy died after his moped collided with a car in Midwood. His passenger, just fourteen, was left with broken bones and a fractured life. Police said, “Jhoan later died at the hospital.” Days before, a three-car crash at Avenue U and Coney Island Avenue sent four people to the hospital, including two NYPD officers. Police said, “a 25-year-old man was traveling north on Coney Island Avenue in a Chevrolet Suburban when he collided with the driver of a Volvo traveling west on Avenue U.”

      Who Bears the Brunt?

      Pedestrians and cyclists pay the highest price. In this district, SUVs and sedans killed seventeen people and injured hundreds more. Trucks and buses took four more lives. Motorcycles and mopeds left families shattered. The old and the young are not spared. The street does not forgive.

      Leadership: Words, Action, and Silence

      State Senator Sam Sutton represents this district. The record shows no public statement, no bill, no vote on the latest carnage. The silence is loud. The need is plain. Every day without action is another day of risk.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. These are not accidents. Every crash is a policy failure. Call Senator Sutton. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action on repeat dangerous drivers. Demand streets where children can cross and live.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. The toll is already too high.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature, responsible for passing laws and overseeing statewide policy. Senators represent districts like SD 22 and vote on bills that affect traffic safety and more.
      ▸ Where does SD 22 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 48 and assembly district AD 41. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 22?
      It includes the Borough Park, Mapleton-Midwood (West), Midwood, Gravesend (East)-Homecrest, Madison, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, Marine Park-Plumb Island, Brooklyn CB12, Brooklyn CB14, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 44, District 46, and District 48, and Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 45, AD 48, and AD 59. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 22?
      Cars and trucks (including SUVs, sedans, trucks, and buses) were responsible for most deaths and injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds also caused serious harm, while bikes were involved in a smaller number of incidents.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not random accidents—they are the result of policy choices, street design, and enforcement. Every death and injury is preventable with the right action.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to reduce traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, support automated enforcement, redesign dangerous streets, and hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable.
