      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 22?

      Senator Sutton Misses Votes While Brooklyn Bleeds

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 22, the streets do not forgive. Since 2022, 36 people have died in crashes here. Over 4,100 have been injured. Forty-five were left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same again. The dead are not numbers. They are elders, children, neighbors. In the last month alone, two elderly men were killed while crossing the street. One was struck by a moped that fled the scene. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said an eyewitness. The other was hit by a Smart Car near his home. The driver stayed. The man died anyway. No charges were filed. NY Daily News.

      The Vehicles That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage—18 deaths, 973 injuries. Trucks and buses killed 4, injured 367. Motorcycles and mopeds killed none in the last period, but left dozens hurt. Bikes injured 22. The streets are not safe for the old, the young, or anyone on foot. “We want to make sure what happened doesn’t happen again,” said a neighbor. But it does, week after week.

      What Has Senator Sutton Done?

      Senator Sam Sutton missed key votes on bills that would have made streets safer. On June 12, he was absent for a committee vote on the Stop Super Speeders Act, a bill to force repeat dangerous drivers to install speed-limiting devices. The bill passed committee anyway. Sutton also missed votes on school zone safety and speed camera bills. When he did vote, it was for a bill boosting street safety and access, but the record shows more absence than action. See the record.

      The Call: No More Waiting

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call Senator Sutton. Demand he show up, vote, and fight for real change. Tell him the dead cannot wait. The living are counting on him.

      Contact your leaders. Demand action. Do not let them hide behind silence.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, including those affecting traffic safety, for all New Yorkers.
      ▸ Where does SD 22 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 48 and assembly district AD 41.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 22?
      It includes the Borough Park, Mapleton-Midwood (West), Midwood, Gravesend (East)-Homecrest, Madison, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, Marine Park-Plumb Island, Brooklyn CB12, Brooklyn CB14, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 44, District 46, and District 48, and Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 45, AD 48, and AD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 22?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 18 deaths and 973 injuries. Trucks and buses killed 4 and injured 367. Motorcycles and mopeds caused dozens of injuries. Bikes injured 22 people. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are not inevitable. Policies like lower speed limits, speed cameras, and holding repeat offenders accountable can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can vote for and champion bills that lower speed limits, expand automated enforcement, and require speed-limiting devices for repeat offenders. They must show up, vote, and push for real change.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
