      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Belt Parkway, a fall, and the numbers that don’t stop

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 19, 2025

      Just before 4 AM on Sep 6, 2025, at Flatbush Ave and the Belt Parkway, a 29-year-old on a motorcycle was hurt in a crash. Police records list severe lacerations; he was conscious at the scene (NYC Open Data).

      This Month

      • Aug 28: A 12-year-old riding a motorized device was ejected and left unconscious after a collision with a 2017 Honda sedan at Avenue N and E 19th St (NYC Open Data).
      • Aug 18: A 27-year-old on a motorized device was ejected and bleeding after a crash near 2328 Brigham St with a Toyota making a right turn (NYC Open Data).
      • Aug 5: A 22-year-old driver was ejected and killed on the Belt Parkway; police recorded unsafe speed (NYC Open Data).

      The toll here

      Since Jan 1, 2022, this district has recorded 8,772 crashes, with 5,699 people injured and 47 killed. Of those walking, SUVs alone have been tied to 544 injuries and 11 deaths in these crashes (NYC Open Data).

      The pattern is current, not history. In the year to date, 13 people have been killed and 1,115 injured in crashes here, compared to 11 killed and 1,200 injured at this point last year (NYC Open Data).

      A child, a street, a voice

      On Avenue U at E 14th St, a 90-year-old man crossing the street was hit by a moped and died. The rider stopped, then fled. “We hope the authorities can find the person who did this,” his grandson said (CBS New York).

      Across the district, people riding bikes reported hitting a near-invisible line strung across the Marine Parkway Bridge path. “I felt pain, but I didn’t know what it was,” one rider said (Gothamist).

      The record, the votes, the gap

      State Senator Sam Sutton missed committee votes on two road‑safety bills this summer: S 4045, which would require intelligent speed assistance for repeat violators, and S 8344, a school‑zone safety measure (Open States). The S 4045 summary says it would mandate speed limiters for anyone with eleven or more DMV points in 24 months, or six camera tickets in a year (Open States). What gives?

      Citywide measures exist. After Albany passed Sammy’s Law, the city began lowering limits on select streets. Advocates say the next steps are simple: make 20 MPH the norm and rein in repeat speeders. The city can act, and the legislature can force the worst offenders to slow down (Take Action).

      One man bleeding by the Belt Parkway before dawn. A child crumpled on Avenue N. The question is the same on every block. Who slows the cars now?

      Take one step today. Push for a citywide 20 MPH limit and speed limiters for repeat offenders: act here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where is this?
      New York State Senate District 22 in Brooklyn. It includes Borough Park, Mapleton–Midwood (West), Midwood, Gravesend (East)–Homecrest, Madison, Sheepshead Bay–Manhattan Beach–Gerritsen Beach, and Marine Park–Plumb Island.
      ▸ What’s happened here since 2022?
      From Jan 1, 2022 to Sep 19, 2025, police reported 8,772 crashes in this district, injuring 5,699 people and killing 47, according to NYC Open Data.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We queried NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets: Crashes (h9gi-nx95), Persons (f55k-p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k-52h4). We filtered for crashes between 2022-01-01 and 2025-09-19 within Senate District 22. We then counted crashes, injuries, and deaths, and summarized pedestrian harm by vehicle type. Data was accessed Sep 19, 2025. Reproduce our counts here.
      ▸ Who represents this area?
      City Council District 48 is represented by Inna Vernikov, and Assembly District 41 by Kalman Yeger. The State Senator is Sam Sutton.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ What policies could cut repeat speeding?
      Senate bill S 4045 would require intelligent speed assistance for repeat violators — defined in its summary as eleven or more points in 24 months, or six camera tickets in a year. Senator Sam Sutton missed committee votes on S 4045 and on a school‑zone safety bill, S 8344.
