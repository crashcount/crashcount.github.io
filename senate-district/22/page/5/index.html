      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 22?

      Sutton Sleeps, Brooklyn Bleeds: Streets Run Red While Leaders Duck Responsibility

      Written July 14, 2025

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 14, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 22, the street is a killing field. Since 2022, 36 people have died and 45 have been seriously injured in crashes. The dead are not numbers. They are elders, children, neighbors. In the last twelve months alone, 11 people lost their lives and 22 suffered serious injuries. Three of the dead were children. Two were over 65. The violence does not stop.

      Last week, a 90-year-old man was crossing East 14th Street at Avenue U. A blue moped hit him and fled. “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open,” said a witness. The driver did not look back. The family waits for justice. The street remains the same.

      The day before, a 70-year-old man was killed crossing Avenue I at East 5th Street. The driver stayed. No charges were filed. The city moves on. The blood dries. The crosswalk is still there.

      The Vehicles That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the harm. Since 2022, they have killed 18 people and injured hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed four. Motorcycles and mopeds killed none, but left dozens injured. Bikes injured, but did not kill. The pattern is clear. The bigger the vehicle, the greater the damage. The street does not forgive.

      Leadership: Missing in Action

      State Senator Sam Sutton missed the vote on the Stop Super Speeders Act, a bill that would have forced the worst repeat offenders to slow down. He also missed the vote on a bill to expand school speed cameras. The silence is loud. “We hope the authorities can find the person who did this. We are also concerned about the motorcycles running rampant in crowded residential areas,” said the victim’s grandson. The community waits. The senator does not answer.

      What Now?

      Every day of delay is another day of blood on the street. Call Senator Sutton. Demand action. Demand votes. Demand streets where children and elders can cross and come home alive. The dead cannot speak. You must.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 22.
      ▸ Where does SD 22 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 48 and assembly district AD 41. See NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 22?
      It includes the Borough Park, Mapleton-Midwood (West), Midwood, Gravesend (East)-Homecrest, Madison, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, Marine Park-Plumb Island, Brooklyn CB12, Brooklyn CB14, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 44, District 46, and District 48, and Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 45, AD 48, and AD 59. See NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 22?
      SUVs and cars killed 18 people and injured hundreds. Trucks and buses killed four. Motorcycles and mopeds caused injuries but no deaths. Bikes injured 14 pedestrians but caused no deaths. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and stronger enforcement. They are not accidents—they are the result of policy choices.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and support bills for lower speed limits, more speed cameras, and stricter penalties for repeat dangerous drivers. They can also push for safer street design and hold agencies accountable.

No driver errors were listed in the data, and the only contributing factor recorded was the pedestrian crossing against the signal. No mention of helmet or signal use appears in the report.       Bus Turns, Strikes Girl Crossing Avenue P
      ▸

      A bus turned right at Avenue P and Bedford. A 15-year-old girl crossed with the light. The bumper hit her head. She died on the street. The bus showed no damage. Her walk to school ended in blood and silence.

      A 15-year-old girl was killed at the corner of Avenue P and Bedford Avenue in Brooklyn. According to the police report, a bus turned right while the girl crossed with the signal. The bus’s right front bumper struck her head. She suffered severe lacerations and died at the scene. The police report lists 'Failure to Yield Right-of-Way' as the contributing factor. The bus sustained no damage. The girl was walking with the light, in the crosswalk. No other injuries were reported. The crash ended her life before she reached her classroom.
