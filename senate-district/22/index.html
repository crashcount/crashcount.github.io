      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 22?

      Senator Sutton: Your Silence Is Leaving Bodies in the Street

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 22: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 22, the street is a gauntlet. Ten people have died in crashes here in the last year. Over 1,200 have been hurt. The old and the young are not spared. Three children died. Eight seniors over 65 died since 2022. The numbers do not lie. They do not comfort.

      Just days ago, a 90-year-old man was killed crossing Avenue U at East 14th Street. A blue moped hit him and fled. His grandson expressed concern about rampant motorcycles in residential areas. The street was left empty. The man did not come home.

      Last week, Dov Broyde, 70, was struck and killed by a Smart Car while crossing Avenue I. The driver stayed. No charges were filed. The city moves on. The families do not.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage. In three years, they killed 17 people and hurt 846 more on these streets. Trucks and buses killed 4. Motorcycles and mopeds killed none, but left 28 injured. Bikes caused no deaths and 22 injuries. The machines are heavy. The bodies are not.

      Leadership: Words and Silence

      State Senator Sam Sutton represents this district. The record shows no recent public action, bill, or statement from Sutton on traffic violence. The silence is loud. The families wait. The streets do not change.

      The Voices in the Aftermath

      A witness to the moped crash described the scene: “He was laid out on the floor. His head was wide open. It was a blue scooter. The driver had a mask. He hit the guy and he left.” The words are plain. The horror is not.

      What Now

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day of delay is another risk. Call Senator Sutton. Demand action. Demand a street where you can cross and come home alive. The dead cannot speak. The living must.
