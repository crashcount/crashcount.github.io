      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 11?

      Astoria’s stop sign. Three dead. Queens keeps bleeding.

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 11: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      At 19th Avenue and 42nd Street, a Toyota rushed the stop. It hit a parked coffee truck. It hit two men. The driver died too. Witness George Giakoumis said the car was “going at least 60+ miles an hour just right through the stop sign.” Two pedestrians, ages 41 and 70, were killed. The 84-year-old driver also died. Three families went home without their men. ABC7 | CBS New York

      The city’s own file marks the hour: 8:36 a.m., August 12, 2025. Two pedestrians listed “Not at Intersection.” The Toyota is a sedan. A Volvo is turning. A parked Chevy and VW take blows. Three entries end the same: “Apparent Death.” NYC Open Data crash 4834594

      In January, 18 miles away on Hillside Avenue, a left‑turning box truck struck a 29‑year‑old man in the crosswalk. He had the signal. The truck had size and a blind side. He died. The file lists “Driver Inattention/Distraction” and “Oversized Vehicle.” NYC Open Data crash 4789587

      In this Senate district, ten people have died in the last 12 months. Crashes climbed to 3,409. Injuries: 2,176. The count rises like a metronome. Period stats

      The numbers don’t look away

      • In the past year here: 10 deaths, 2,176 injuries, 3,409 crashes. Year‑to‑date: 2,254 crashes, 1,421 injuries, 8 deaths. Last year by this time: 1,590 crashes, 978 injuries, 2 deaths. The increase in deaths is 300%. Period stats
      • Pedestrians are hit most by sedans and SUVs. SUVs killed six pedestrians in this span; trucks killed one. Period stats

      A man on a motorcycle, 19, died in April. Helmet on. Listed cause: “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” The bike is “Demolished.” The body is “Ejected.” NYC Open Data crash 4805902

      A 26‑year‑old driver died on Hillside in June. The file is thin. Death is not. NYC Open Data crash 4818924

      Speed is the blade

      At 19th and 42nd, video showed the car “speeding down the street and slamming into the truck.” A witness said he stepped back one step and felt the wind of it graze his leg. “My truck is totaled but I still have my life.” The brother of one victim said: “He’s the best brother. The best person you can find in the world.” CBS New York | ABC7

      A small group of drivers does the most harm. Vehicles with 16 camera tickets in 12 months are twice as likely to kill or seriously injure. 30+ tickets makes the risk fifty‑fold. One such driver ran a red in Brooklyn this spring and killed a mother and her two daughters. Streetsblog | Daily News

      Albany moved a bill that would force the worst repeat speeders to install devices that cap speed. The Senate’s S 4045 advanced in June. It targets drivers with heavy point totals or many camera tickets. Open States S 4045

      Wrong way, no way out

      On the Clearview Expressway, a Bayside man drove into oncoming traffic and hit five cars. Two motorists were badly hurt. In court he said he entered “in the wrong direction because I wanted to hurt people.” The judge gave him eight years. amNY

      In July 2024, an e‑bike rider died on 164th Street. In May 2024, a pedestrian died on Hillside. In November 2023, a flatbed killed an older walker. The list runs through this district like a crack in glass. Timeline

      What breaks the chain

      • The city can lower speeds on local streets. The state gave that power. Officials have not used it citywide. Streetsblog
      • The Senate bill to fit repeat speeders with limiters moved forward. The city needs it to pass and be enforced. Open States S 4045

      Lower the limit. Stop the repeat offenders. The dead already paid.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What happened at 19th Avenue and 42nd Street?
      On August 12, 2025, an 84-year-old driver sped through a stop sign and struck a parked coffee truck and two men. The driver and both pedestrians died. Sources: ABC7 and CBS New York.
      ▸ How many people have died in this area over the last year?
      Ten people died in the last 12 months within this Senate district, with 3,409 crashes and 2,176 injuries. Source: Period stats in context.
      ▸ What is the Stop Super Speeders bill?
      Senate bill S 4045 would require speed-limiting devices for drivers with repeated violations. It advanced in June 2025. Source: Open States S 4045.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ What can reduce deadly speeding now?
      NYC can lower local speed limits under state law, and Albany can pass S 4045 to force limiters on repeat speeders. Sources: Streetsblog NYC and Open States S 4045.

      S 4045 Stavisky votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Jun 12 - Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
