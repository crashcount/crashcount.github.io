      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 11?

      Two Killed at Astoria Food Truck. SD 11 Must Act.

      Written August 16, 2025 (Revised August 16, 2025 – Finisher pass to tighten prose, fact‑check, add local analysis and political fixes.)

      SD 11: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 16, 2025

      This week in SD 11

      On 2025-08-12 a Toyota blew a stop at 19th Avenue and 42nd Street in Astoria. Three people died: the 84‑year‑old driver and two men near a food truck. Witness George Giakoumis said the car “must have been going at least 60+ miles an hour.” Abelardo Venancio mourned his brother as “the best person you can find in the world.” (ABC7, CBS News, Streetsblog NYC).

      On 2025-08-15 a Queens judge sentenced Joseph Lee for driving the wrong way on the Clearview Expressway and striking multiple cars. Queens DA Melinda Katz said, “Joseph Lee terrorized other drivers.” Two motorists were badly hurt. (amNY).

      The pattern

      Deaths are up 300% year‑to‑date — 8 this year versus 2 at this point last year (NYC Open Data). In the last 12 months SD 11 recorded 3,349 crashes, 10 deaths and 21 serious injuries (NYC Open Data).

      In January (2025-01-31) a 29‑year‑old crossing with the signal at Hillside Avenue and 212th Street was crushed by a turning box truck. The crash report lists driver inattention and an oversized vehicle as contributing factors (NYC Open Data).

      Cars and SUVs account for the bulk of pedestrian impacts here; trucks are less frequent but can be deadly. The common contributing factors in recent fatal crashes are speeding and driver inattention.

      Hotspots: 19th Avenue & 42nd Street and Hillside Avenue & 212th Street keep appearing in the data and in reporting. Fix those corners first.

      What your senator did

      State Senator Toby Stavisky voted yes on S 4045, a bill to require speed‑limiting technology for repeat violators, and she co‑sponsored S 1675 to create a pedestrian safety rating for vehicles (Open States: S 4045, S 1675). Those votes matter. They are not enough.

      What must change now (local and concrete)

      Lower speeds save lives. City Hall can make local streets safer now. On these blocks we need:

      • Signal timing and Leading Pedestrian Intervals (LPIs) at crosswalks on 19th Ave & 42nd St and Hillside Ave & 212th St.
      • Daylighting: clear sightlines by banning sidewalk parking near corners and removing visual clutter.
      • Hardened turns and curb extensions to slow approaches, plus targeted speed‑enforcement at peak hours.

      Citywide fixes must follow. Make 20 mph the default on residential streets. Require intelligent speed assistance for habitual speeders. S 4045 would force speed‑limiting tech on repeat offenders — pass it. These two actions together cut speed, cut crashes, save lives.

      The cost of delay

      SD 11 keeps bleeding because officials move too slowly. “Traffic violence is not inevitable,” says Council Member Tiffany Caban. The data shows it: choices and policies, not fate, shape who lives and who dies.

      Call. Write. Show up. Demand a 20 mph default and mandatory speed limiters for repeat speeders now. Start with this resource: Take Action.

      Year‑to‑date, deaths are up 300% — 8 this year vs. 2 last year. Over the last 12 months, 10 killed, 21 seriously injured, 3,349 crashes (NYC crash data).
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 11?
      Cars and trucks: 7 deaths, 23 serious injuries, 659 total pedestrian impacts. Motorcycles and mopeds: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 4 total. Bikes: 0 deaths, 0 serious injuries, 7 total (NYC crash data).
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just “accidents”?
      On Tuesday, a car sped through a stop and hit people on the corner (ABC7). In January, a turning box truck killed a man who was crossing with the signal (NYC crash data). The patterns are choices and conditions, not fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do now?
      Move S 4045 to the finish line so repeat speeders get speed‑limiting tech (S 4045). Push the city to make 20 mph the default on local streets. Start here: Take Action.

      S 4045
      Stavisky votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
