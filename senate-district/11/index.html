      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 11?

      Astoria’s stop sign. Two men. No time.

      Written August 31, 2025 (Revised August 31, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 11: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 31, 2025

      At 19th Avenue and 42nd Street in Astoria on Aug 12, 2025, a Toyota blew the stop and hit two men by a coffee truck. “Must have been going at least 60+ miles an hour,” a witness said. All three died. ABC7 | CBS New York | Streetsblog NYC

      They are among 25 people killed in crashes in Senate District 11 since Jan 1, 2022, alongside 5,593 injuries in 9,706 crashes. NYC Open Data

      The toll is climbing now

      This year isn’t easing. Through Aug 31, SD 11 recorded 2,287 crashes, 1,455 injuries, and 9 deaths, up from 1,633 crashes, 1,010 injuries, and 3 deaths over the same stretch last year. That’s a 40.0% jump in crashes and deaths that tripled. NYC Open Data

      The bodies are local. On Jan 31, 2025, a 29‑year‑old crossing with the signal at Hillside Avenue and 212 Street was hit and killed by a left‑turning box truck; police listed driver distraction. NYC Open Data

      On May 31, 2024, a 56‑year‑old man was struck and killed on Hillside Avenue near 171 Street. NYC Open Data

      Speed on the Cross Island

      Just after 2 AM on Aug 26, 2025, a BMW on the Cross Island Parkway near Bell Boulevard lost control. A 24‑year‑old passenger died. Police cited unsafe speed. Patch | NYC Open Data

      Two years earlier, a pedestrian died on the parkway. Three vehicles were involved. Another reminder that highways cut through this district and take lives off‑ramps too. NYC Open Data

      The record shows who must act

      The city has the tools to slow cars and save lives. A citywide 20 MPH default and real consequences for repeat speeders are on the table now. See details and scripts here.

      In Albany, Senator Toby Stavisky voted yes in committee on S 4045 to require intelligent speed assistance for repeat violators. Open States

      She also co‑sponsored S 1675 and earlier S 343 to create a pedestrian‑safety rating system for motor vehicles. Open States (S 1675) | Open States (S 343)

      City Hall can lower speeds. Albany can curb repeat speeders. The bodies are here. The levers are there. Use them.

      What you can do now

      Tell your electeds to slow our streets and stop repeat speeders. It takes three minutes. Start here.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What area does this cover?
      New York State Senate District 11 in Queens. It includes parts of Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, College Point, Whitestone-Beechhurst, Bay Terrace-Clearview, Douglaston-Little Neck, and more listed in the district profile.
      ▸ How many crashes and deaths are we talking about?
      From Jan 1, 2022 through Aug 31, 2025, SD 11 recorded 9,706 crashes, 5,593 injuries, and 25 deaths, according to NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What recent crashes show the pattern?
      Aug 12, 2025: three killed at 19th Ave and 42nd St in Astoria; Jan 31, 2025: a 29-year-old pedestrian killed at Hillside Ave and 212 St; Aug 26, 2025: one killed on the Cross Island Parkway near Bell Blvd. All are documented in NYC Open Data and local press.
      ▸ Who represents this area?
      State Senator Toby Stavisky represents SD 11. She voted yes on S 4045 and co‑sponsored S 1675 and S 343. City Council District 19 is represented by Vickie Paladino; Assembly District 26 by Ed Braunstein.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95, Persons f55k-p6yu, Vehicles bm4k-52h4). Filters: date between 2022‑01‑01 and 2025‑08‑31; geography limited to Senate District 11; all modes. We counted crashes, injuries, and fatalities from the filtered records as of Aug 31, 2025. You can start from the datasets here and apply the same filters.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.

      S 4045 Stavisky votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Jun 12 - Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
