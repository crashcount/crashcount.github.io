      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 34?

      No More Excuses: End the Slaughter on Pelham Parkway

      Written August 9, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, deduped and cleaned citations.)

      SD 34: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 9, 2025

      The Blood on the Parkway

      Just weeks ago, a 31-year-old man rode his motorcycle west on Pelham Parkway. A Chevrolet Equinox turned left. The crash killed him. The driver stayed. No arrest. The highway squad investigates. The story runs cold, but the loss does not make headlines.

      On the Hutchinson River Parkway, seven people were hurt before dawn. A scooter, two cars, a chain of metal and bodies. An 18-year-old woman was thrown from the back of a moped. She clings to life. The driver left her in the road and sped off. “The moped driver sped away… leaving the young woman to die on the road,” police said.

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      In the last 12 months, 14 people died on SD 34 streets. Over 1,300 were hurt. Twenty-two suffered injuries so serious they may never walk the same. Children, elders, riders, walkers. The toll does not spare. Cars and SUVs did the most harm—10 deaths, 366 injuries. Trucks and buses killed one, hurt dozens more. Motorcycles and mopeds, bikes, all part of the carnage according to city data.

      Leadership: Action and the Waiting

      State Senator Nathalia Fernández voted yes on bills to curb repeat speeders and extend school speed zones. The bills target the worst drivers—those who rack up violations, those who kill again and again. But the streets do not wait for laws to pass. Each vote is a start, not an end. “This defendant was allegedly driving three times the speed limit when her SUV slammed into a livery cab,” said District Attorney Darcel Clark.

      The Call

      Every day of delay costs lives. Call your council member. Call the senator. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real action against repeat speeders. The blood on the street is not an accident. It is a choice. Make them choose life.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 34.
      ▸ Where does SD 34 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 13 and assembly district AD 82.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 34?
      It includes the Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River, Soundview-Clason Point, Castle Hill-Unionport, Parkchester, Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Morris Park, and Bronx CB10 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 13 and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 82, AD 85, AD 87, AD 88, and AD 91.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 34?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 10 deaths and 366 injuries. Trucks and buses were involved in 1 death and 39 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds were involved in 13 injuries, while bikes caused 11 injuries. See official crash data.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not random accidents. Most are preventable with lower speeds, better street design, and enforcement against repeat dangerous drivers.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws to lower speed limits, target repeat offenders, and redesign streets to protect people walking and biking.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
