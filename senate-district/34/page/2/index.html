      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 34?

      Blood on the Crosswalk, Silence in Albany

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised June 27, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 34: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      A man walks home from mosque. A BMW turns left. The man signals, tries to stop the car with his hand. The car does not stop. He dies in the crosswalk. “The accident is so terrible,” said his neighbor. “I’ve never seen anything like this in my life.”

      On the parkway before dawn, a moped is struck from behind. An 18-year-old woman is thrown onto the asphalt. The driver flees. She is left fighting for her life. Seven are hurt. The road is slick with fear and silence. “The moped driver sped away… leaving the young woman to die on the road,” reported police.

      In the last twelve months, SD 34 saw 11 deaths, 18 serious injuries, and 1,074 injuries. Children, elders, workers. The numbers do not flinch. The street does not care.

      Who Pays, Who Acts

      Most deaths come from cars and SUVs. The drivers keep moving. The city keeps counting. In three years: 26 dead, 51 seriously hurt, 3,468 injured. The dead do not get a second chance.

      Senator Fernández: Votes and Silence

      Senator Nathalia Fernández has voted for bills that could slow the carnage. She said yes to speed limiters for repeat speeders. She backed complete street design, and school speed cameras. She co-sponsored a bill to force safer street design on every project that takes public money. The votes are there. The bodies keep coming.

      What Next: The Road Demands More

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Senator Fernández. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand the city use every tool to stop repeat killers behind the wheel. Demand streets where a child can cross and live. The road will not change itself. Only you can force their hand.

      Senate Bill S 9718, titled "Enables safe access to public roads for all users by utilizing complete street design principles," advanced through the Senate with committee votes on May 28 and June 3, 2024. The bill, sponsored by Sean Ryan (District 61) and co-sponsored by Liz Krueger, Monica Martinez, Jack M. Martins, Anthony H. Palumbo, and Julia Salazar, passed both votes despite opposition. The measure calls for redesigning streets to protect all users, not just drivers. The vote was not unanimous—several senators voted no, but the majority carried it through. The bill’s focus is clear: safer streets for people on foot, on bikes, and in vehicles. No safety analyst note was provided, but the intent is to cut danger at the curb and crosswalk.
