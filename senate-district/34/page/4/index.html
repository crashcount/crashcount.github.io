      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 34?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—How Many More Will Die Before We Act?

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 20, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      SD 34: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Senate District 34, the road takes its due. Twenty-six people killed. Forty-seven left with serious wounds. In three and a half years, more than 6,000 crashes tore through these Bronx streets. Children, elders, riders, walkers—none spared. In the last year alone, two children died. Two more under 24. One man, 75, was crossing with the light when an SUV turned and crushed him. He never made it home. The driver was distracted. The man is gone. NYC Open Data.

      A moped crash on Metcalf Avenue left a 17-year-old dead, a 14-year-old girl thrown to the pavement. His family waited for a call that never came. “He was here yesterday morning, sitting on the bed and watching TV,” his mother said. The girl survived. He did not.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage. Nine killed by cars and SUVs. Trucks and buses, too, leave bodies and broken bones. Motorbikes and mopeds add to the toll. The streets are not safe for the young or the old. The numbers do not lie. They do not comfort.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      Senator Nathalia Fernández has voted for safer streets. She backed bills to force speed limiters on repeat offenders, to redesign roads for people, not just cars, and to expand speed cameras near schools. She co-sponsored a law to require “complete street” design on every project that takes public money. These are steps. They are not enough. The dead do not come back. The wounded do not heal fast.

      The Call

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day without action is another day the street claims a life. Call Senator Fernández. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras that never sleep. Demand streets built for people, not for speed. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York. Open States
      ▸ Where does SD 34 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx and city council district District 13. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 34?
      It includes the Soundview-Clason Point, Castle Hill-Unionport, Parkchester, Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Hutchinson Metro Center, Pelham Bay Park, and Bronx CB10 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 13 and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 82, AD 85, AD 87, AD 88, and AD 91. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 34?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm, with 9 deaths and 117 serious or moderate injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 0 deaths and 3 moderate injuries. Bikes caused 0 deaths and 3 moderate injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are not random. They are the result of choices—speed, street design, and enforcement. Prevention is possible.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, redesign streets for safety, and pass laws that hold dangerous drivers accountable.
