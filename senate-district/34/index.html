      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 34?

      Left for Dead: Blood on Bronx Streets, and Still No Safe Passage

      Written June 22, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Fixed grammar, tightened prose, removed duplicate headline, ensured proper citation formatting and deduplication.)

      SD 34: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 22, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      A girl lies in the road before dawn. She is eighteen. The moped that carried her is gone. The driver fled. Seven people are hurt, one clings to life. This is the Bronx, Hutchinson River Parkway, June 2025. The police say, “An 18-year-old was in critical condition after being thrown from a moped in the hit-and-run” (CBS New York).

      In the last twelve months, 11 people died on SD 34 streets. 1,067 were injured. Fourteen suffered injuries so grave they may never walk the same. Two children are dead. The numbers do not stop. They do not care how careful you are. They do not care if you are old or young, walking or riding, crossing with the light or against it.

      Who Pays the Price

      The dead are not just numbers. A 75-year-old man, crossing with the signal, is struck by a BMW SUV making a left turn. He tries to wave the driver off. The car does not stop. His neighbor described the accident as “so terrible. I’ve never seen anything like this in my life”.

      SUVs and sedans do most of the damage. In three years, they killed nine, injured over 400. Trucks and buses hurt dozens more. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes are not spared. No street is safe. No hour is safe.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      Senator Nathalia Fernández has voted for bills to force safer street design and speed cameras in school zones. She backed laws to require speed limiters for repeat offenders and co-sponsored a bill to put complete street design at the heart of every project. These are steps. They are not enough. The blood on the road says so.

      The Call

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call Senator Fernández. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras that never go dark. Demand streets where a child can cross and come home alive. Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and oversees state policy, including traffic safety.
      ▸ Where does SD 34 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx and city council district District 13.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 34?
      It includes the Soundview-Clason Point, Castle Hill-Unionport, Parkchester, Westchester Square, Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Hutchinson Metro Center, Pelham Bay Park, and Bronx CB10 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 13 and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 82, AD 85, AD 87, AD 88, and AD 91.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 34?
      Most injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 34 were caused by cars and trucks (9 deaths, 445 injuries), followed by motorcycles and mopeds (no deaths, 12 injuries), and bikes (no deaths, 10 injuries). NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and enforcement save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, redesign streets for safety, and pass laws that hold dangerous drivers accountable.

      S 4045
      Fernandez votes yes to require speed limiters, boosting street safety.
      ▸

      Senate passes S 4045. Drivers with too many points or camera tickets must install speed assistance devices. Lawmakers move to curb reckless driving. The bill targets repeat offenders. The aim: fewer crashes, fewer deaths. Streets demand action.

      Bill S 4045, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' advanced in the Senate on May 20, 2025, following a committee vote. The bill requires drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red-light camera tickets in twelve months, to install intelligent speed assistance devices. Primary sponsor Andrew Gounardes led the push, joined by co-sponsors Samra Brouk, Cordell Cleare, and others. The vote passed with support from senators including Jeremy Cooney, Pete Harckham, and Jessica Ramos. The measure aims to rein in repeat offenders and reduce deadly speeding. The bill’s language is blunt: 'Requires the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices if a driver accumulates eleven or more points on their driving record during a 24 month period, or receives 6 speed camera or red light camera tickets during a twelve month period.' No safety analyst note was provided.
