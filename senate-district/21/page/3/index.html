      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 21?

      Ocean Parkway to Kings Highway: seven dead, and the year isn’t over

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 21: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Ocean Parkway, 1:02 a.m.

      • A 45‑year‑old woman was struck on Ocean Parkway at Avenue C. The SUV’s center front hit her. She died at the scene. The state crash log lists her as a pedestrian, killed off the corner, “crossing, no signal or crosswalk.” The driver was in a 2023 Toyota, heading south, “merging.” The city records the death as August 9, 2025. See the official entry: CrashID 4833650.

      Kings Highway and Church

      • A 30‑year‑old man died in January at Kings Highway and Church Avenue. The record calls out “unsafe speed.” The sedan kept straight; the impact was front‑end. He is listed as a pedestrian at the intersection. The date is January 25, 2025. Source: CrashID 4788144.

      More bodies, same streets

      • May 28: a 38‑year‑old driver died near 319 Ditmas Avenue. The entry flags “driver inattention.” Vehicle listed: 2021 Mercedes SUV parked Westbound; details are sparse. Source: CrashID 4816179.
      • April 16: an 89‑year‑old man died at Arkansas Drive and 56th Drive. A Toyota sedan struck a parked Honda SUV’s rear. The older driver is marked as killed. Source: CrashID 4806223.
      • July 5: a 34‑year‑old moped rider died on Clarendon Road at E. 35th Street. The file lists “passing or lane usage improper” and ejection. An Alfa SUV was changing lanes. Source: CrashID 4825305.

      What the numbers say

      • Since January 1, 2025, this district logged 1,609 crashes, 1,184 injuries, and 6 deaths year‑to‑date, up from 3 deaths at this point last year. Serious injuries nearly doubled. That’s a 15.6% rise in crashes and a 22.9% rise in injuries year‑over‑year. Source: district stats through August 26, 2025 (open data).
      • Over the full coverage window, SUVs and sedans dominate pedestrian harm here. SUVs alone are tied to 405 pedestrian injuries and 4 deaths; sedans to 386 injuries and 5 deaths. Trucks and buses add more bodies. Source: local rollup, 2022–2025 (open data).

      Two men gone in a flash

      • In July, two men were killed crossing in Brooklyn. The driver fled. Police later took a suspect into custody, reporters said. “Two people died when a car drove through a Brooklyn intersection… hitting and killing them as they crossed the street,” CBS New York reported. Neighbors said “drivers speed on that stretch of roadway” (CBS New York).

      Repeat danger, weak consequences

      • Citywide, as of mid‑July, 60 pedestrians had been killed this year, the Daily News reported in a roundup of prosecutions. One driver faced vehicular manslaughter after striking a man in a crosswalk in East Flatbush. Another was charged months after hitting a 95‑year‑old. Both had stayed at the scene, the paper said. Source: NY Daily News.

      What Albany moved — and what’s left

      • The Senate voted yes in June to require intelligent speed assistance for drivers with a record of repeated violations. The bill is S 4045. Committee votes advanced it on June 11 and 12. See the file: S 4045 via Open States.
      • Albany also renewed 24‑hour school‑zone speed cameras through 2030, according to our site’s coverage. Thank them for that. Now lower speeds on every block.

      Slow it down, or count the dead

      • Lower the default city speed limit to 20 mph. The city has the power under Sammy’s Law. Use it. See our action guide: Take action.
      • Force chronic speeders to install limiters. Back S 4045’s goal. Call your lawmakers. One hard rule for the 1.5% who do the most harm.

      One last note

      • A woman dies on Ocean Parkway. A man dies at Kings Highway. The tape and chalk get washed away. The risk stays. Until we turn the screw on speed, the names will keep coming.
