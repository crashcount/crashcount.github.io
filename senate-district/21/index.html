      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 21?

      Brooklyn Bleeds, Politicians Stall: Demand 20 MPH Now

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      SD 21: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Brooklyn’s Senate District 21, the numbers do not lie. Eighteen people have died on these streets since 2022. Over 5,300 have been injured. Seventy-one suffered wounds so grave they may never walk the same. The dead include children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians. Each one had a name. Each one is gone.

      Just days ago, two men were killed crossing a Brooklyn street. A car tore through the intersection. The driver fled. Neighbors did not sound surprised. “Drivers speed on that stretch of roadway,” one told CBS New York. The bodies are fresh. The danger is old.

      A 90-year-old man died after a moped hit him and kept going. Security video showed the moment of impact. The man died at the hospital. The driver did not stop. CBS New York reported the facts. No one in power came to the scene.

      The Patterns That Kill

      SUVs and sedans do most of the damage. Since 2022, cars and SUVs killed eight people and injured 870 more in SD 21. Trucks and buses killed four. Motorcycles and mopeds, bikes, and vans left dozens more hurt. The violence is not random. It is a pattern. It is policy made flesh.

      What Has Senator Parker Done?

      State Senator Kevin Parker has voted yes on bills to curb repeat speeders and expand school speed zones. He backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst offenders to install speed-limiting devices. He supported laws to create safety zones and expand ignition interlock monitoring. These are steps. But the blood still runs.

      The streets remain deadly. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not done so. The cameras catch speeders, but the worst keep driving. The laws are not enough. The dead do not come back.

      The Call

      Call Senator Parker. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand action against repeat offenders. Do not wait for another name on the list. The time for patience is over. The time for mourning is every day. The time for action is now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 21.
      ▸ Where does SD 21 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 45 and assembly district AD 41.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 21?
      It includes the Kensington, Flatbush, Flatbush (West)-Ditmas Park-Parkville, East Flatbush-Erasmus, East Flatbush-Farragut, East Flatbush-Rugby, Holy Cross Cemetery, Flatlands, Marine Park-Mill Basin-Bergen Beach, and Brooklyn CB17 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 40, District 45, and District 46, and Assembly Districts AD 41, AD 42, AD 44, AD 58, and AD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 21?
      Cars and SUVs killed 8 people and injured 870 more in SD 21 since 2022. Trucks and buses killed 4. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 18 injuries but no deaths. Bikes were involved in 10 injuries, but no deaths.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable, or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries follow patterns—speeding, dangerous vehicles, and poor street design. They are preventable with better laws, enforcement, and infrastructure.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, pass laws against repeat dangerous drivers, fund safer street designs, and ensure enforcement targets the most dangerous behavior.

      Parker votes yes to extend school speed zones, improving child pedestrian safety.
      ▸

      Senate passes S 8344. School speed zone rules in New York City get extended. Lawmakers make technical fixes. The bill keeps pressure on drivers near schools. Streets stay a little safer for kids.

      Bill S 8344, titled 'Extends provisions and makes technical corrections to school speed zones in NYC; repealer,' moved through the Senate and Assembly in June 2025. Sponsored by Senator Andrew Gounardes, the bill passed Senate votes on June 12 and June 13, and cleared the Assembly on June 17. The measure extends and corrects school speed zone laws in New York City, repealing outdated provisions. Gounardes led the push, with strong support from most Senate Democrats and a split Assembly. The bill's technical fixes aim to keep protections in place for children and other vulnerable road users near schools. No safety analyst note was provided.

      S 4045
      Parker votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
