      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      No More Names on the Asphalt

      Written June 22, 2025 (Revised June 21, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, ensured proper citation formatting, and cleaned citations.)

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 22, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      A man lies dead in the crosswalk. A boy on a scooter is struck, left bleeding on Jerome Avenue. In the last twelve months, 12 people have died on the streets of Senate District 29. Twenty-five more suffered serious injuries. The numbers do not flinch: over 1,500 injured, 2,560 crashes. Each one a life changed, or ended, in a moment.

      Children are not spared. Sixty-three kids were hurt. The old are broken too—no age is safe. Cars, trucks, bikes, buses: all take their share. SUVs alone killed eight pedestrians in this district since 2022. The city calls them accidents. The families know better.

      The Stories That Stay

      A 15-year-old boy rides his scooter north on Jerome. A motorcycle runs the red. The boy is hit, left in critical condition. The driver flees. Police ask for help, but the street stays the same. Police reported that “a 15-year-old boy riding his scooter was critically hurt when a Bronx motorcyclist blew through a red light, slammed into him and sped away.”

      A man sleeps under a box truck in Hunts Point. The driver pulls away, not knowing. The man dies. A worker said, “Of course it wasn’t his fault, but you know it’s really hard knowing that by your accident a man died. It could happen with anybody. Anybody.”

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Miles to Go

      Senator Jose Serrano voted yes on a bill to require safer street design for all users, not just drivers. He backed S 9718, a measure to build streets that protect people on foot and on bikes. This is a start. But the deaths keep coming. The bill passed, but the streets do not change overnight. There are no public quotes from Serrano demanding faster action or more funding. No press conference at the crash site. The silence is loud.

      What Next: No More Waiting

      Every crash is preventable. The bodies in the street are not the price of doing business. They are the cost of delay. Call Senator Serrano. Demand he fight for lower speed limits, more protected crossings, and real accountability for drivers who kill and flee.

      Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 29.
      ▸ Where does SD 29 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and assembly district AD 84.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 29?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, North & South Brother Islands, Highbridge, Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Soundview Park, East Harlem (South), Randall’S Island, and Bronx CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, District 17, and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 77, AD 84, and AD 85.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 29?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm to pedestrians, with 8 deaths and 741 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds were involved in 3 deaths and 37 injuries. Bikes were involved in 1 death and 58 injuries. Trucks and buses were involved in 3 deaths and 74 injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Every crash is preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and real accountability.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and fund laws for safer street design, lower speed limits, and stronger enforcement against reckless driving.
