      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      Blood on the Sidewalk, Silence in City Hall

      Written July 7, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 7, 2025

      The Curb Is No Refuge

      On July 3rd, a Ford Mustang turned left at East 149th and Courtlandt. It hit a woman in the crosswalk, then mounted the curb and struck five more under the scaffolding. The driver and passenger ran, leaving the car behind. Six people went to Lincoln Hospital. They were alive. They were lucky. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. The youngest victim was 30. The oldest was 79. The city called it minor. The pain was not minor.

      The Numbers Behind the Noise

      In the last 12 months, SD 29 saw 11 deaths and 1,532 injuries from crashes. Twenty-two were serious injuries. Pedestrians, cyclists, children—no one is spared. Cars and SUVs did the most harm: 8 deaths, 739 injuries. Trucks and buses killed 3, bikes killed 1, motorcycles and mopeds killed 2. The toll does not slow. The street does not forgive.

      Leadership: A Vote, But Not Enough

      State Senator Jose Serrano voted yes on S 9718, a bill to push for safer street design. It passed. It is a start. But the blood on the curb is not washed away by a vote. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh. The fear is real. The danger is daily.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Call Senator Serrano. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people on foot and on bikes. Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home. The street will not change itself. Only pressure will.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, including those that affect street safety, alongside the Assembly.
      ▸ Where does SD 29 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and assembly district AD 84.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 29?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, North & South Brother Islands, Concourse-Concourse Village, Highbridge, Mount Eden-Claremont (West), Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Soundview Park, Upper East Side-Yorkville, and Bronx CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, District 17, and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 77, AD 84, and AD 85.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 29?
      Cars and trucks caused 8 deaths and 747 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 2 deaths and 38 injuries. Bikes caused 1 death and 60 injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and support laws that protect people walking and biking.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
