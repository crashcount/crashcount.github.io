      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—Who Will Stop the Killing?

      Written July 3, 2025

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 3, 2025

      The Toll in SD 29: No End in Sight

      The streets of Senate District 29 do not forgive. Since 2022, 36 people have died and 80 have been seriously injured in crashes here. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, workers, elders. In the last twelve months alone, 11 killed, 25 left with life-altering wounds—and the year is only half done.

      Just last week, three pedestrians were mowed down on a Bronx sidewalk. One man, 33, is fighting for his life. Police say the driver “slammed on the gas and sped off east on Hunts Point Ave., blowing through a red light” before the car spun onto the curb and crushed them. The unlicensed 28-year-old driver is facing multiple counts of vehicular assault, cops said. He ran. The street did not let the victims run.

      A few days before, a cyclist in Central Park hit a pedestrian and died after striking his head on the curb. The pedestrian walked away with a minor injury. The cyclist did not walk away at all. He ran into the unidentified 41-year-old pedestrian. Nico-Garcia was then flung from his bike and struck his head on the curb, the NYPD said.

      Patterns in Metal and Flesh

      SUVs and cars did the most harm—8 deaths, 15 serious injuries, hundreds hurt. Trucks and buses killed 3, left 6 with broken bodies. Motorcycles and mopeds, 2 dead, 1 maimed. Bikes, too, can kill: 1 death, 3 serious injuries. The numbers do not lie. The pain does not fade.

      Leadership: A Vote, But Not Enough

      State Senator Jose Serrano voted yes on a bill to force safer street design. The bill passed. It is a start. But the blood keeps flowing. The dead keep coming. The law is not yet the street. The curb is still a killing ground.

      Call to Action: Make Them Hear You

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Senator Serrano. Call your council member. Demand a street where you can walk and live. Demand action, not words. The next name could be yours.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 29.
      ▸ Where does SD 29 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and assembly district AD 84.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 29?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, North & South Brother Islands, Concourse-Concourse Village, Highbridge, Mount Eden-Claremont (West), Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Soundview Park, Upper East Side-Yorkville, and Bronx CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, District 17, and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 77, AD 84, and AD 85.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 29?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm: 8 deaths and 15 serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed 3 and seriously injured 6. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 2 deaths and 1 serious injury. Bikes caused 1 death and 3 serious injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Safer street design, lower speeds, and enforcement can stop the killing.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws for safer street design, lower speed limits, and hold dangerous drivers accountable. They can also fund and demand rapid changes to infrastructure.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
