      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      Mustang Madness: Six Down, Zero Safe—Bronx Streets Bleed While Leaders Stall

      Written July 11, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      Blood on the Crosswalks

      Six people went to the hospital on July 3. A Ford Mustang turned, hit a woman in the crosswalk, then jumped the curb and struck five more before smashing into scaffolding at East 149th and Courtlandt. The driver and passenger ran. The car stayed. The pain stayed. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. The youngest victim was 30. The oldest, 79. All were walking. None were safe.

      This is not rare. In the last twelve months, 11 people have died and 1,732 have been injured in crashes in SD 29. Twenty-three suffered serious injuries. The dead include cyclists, pedestrians, and children. The numbers do not heal. They only grow.

      The Slow Disaster

      The violence does not slow. Cars and SUVs did the most harm—8 deaths and 770 injuries. Trucks and buses killed three more. Bikes and mopeds add to the toll. Every week brings new names. Every week, families wait in hospital halls.

      The Mustang crash was not the first. It will not be the last. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh. The city calls these accidents. But the pattern is clear. The danger is built in.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      State Senator Jose Serrano voted yes on a bill to require safer street designs for all users, not just drivers. The bill passed. It is a start. But the bodies keep coming. The law is not yet the street. The street is still blood and broken glass.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy.

      Call your council member. Call Senator Serrano. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand enforcement against reckless drivers, not crackdowns on cyclists and pedestrians. Join Families for Safe Streets. Stand with the living. Remember the dead. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, including those that affect street safety, for all of New York State.
      ▸ Where does SD 29 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and assembly district AD 84.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 29?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, North & South Brother Islands, Concourse-Concourse Village, Highbridge, Mount Eden-Claremont (West), Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Soundview Park, Upper East Side-Yorkville, and Bronx CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, District 17, and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 77, AD 84, and AD 85.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 29?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm, with 8 deaths and 770 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 2 deaths and 39 injuries. Bikes were involved in 1 death and 61 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The pattern is clear and preventable. Policy and street design can stop the deaths.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws for lower speed limits, safer street designs, and stronger penalties for reckless drivers. They can also push for more automated enforcement and invest in infrastructure that protects people walking and biking.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
