      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      Six Down, No Arrests: The Street Still Bleeds

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      On July 3, a Ford Mustang turned left at East 149th and Courtlandt. Pedestrians crossed with the light. The car surged forward. One woman fell. The Mustang kept going, smashing into scaffolding and dragging five more people down. The driver and passenger ran. Six neighbors went to Lincoln Hospital. The oldest was 79. The youngest, 30. None died, but pain echoed on the sidewalk. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. The Mustang was left behind, battered and stripped of plates. No one has been arrested. The street is unchanged.

      The Toll: Numbers That Do Not Bleed

      In the last 12 months, SD 29 saw 11 deaths and 22 serious injuries. There were 1,532 injuries in 2,520 crashes. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. The most common killers are cars and SUVs—8 deaths and 15 serious injuries—but trucks, bikes, and mopeds also take lives. The violence is steady. It does not stop for rain or sun. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh.

      Leadership: One Vote, Many Silences

      State Senator Jose Serrano voted yes on a bill to make streets safer for everyone, not just drivers. The bill passed. It calls for streets designed to protect people on foot and on bikes. But the blood on the curb says the work is not done. No law has yet stopped the Mustang. No law has yet made the crosswalk safe.

      What Next: The Call That Cannot Wait

      Contact Senator Serrano. Demand more. Ask for real speed limits, real enforcement, real redesigns. Do not wait for another car to jump the curb. Do not wait for another neighbor to fall. The street will not forgive delay. The dead do not get a second chance.
