      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      No Arrests, No Justice: Melrose Bleeds, Politicians Dither

      Written July 13, 2025

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 13, 2025

      The Crash That Shook Melrose

      On July 3, a Ford Mustang mounted the curb at East 149th and Courtlandt. Six people walking in the crosswalk were hit. The driver and passenger ran. The car was left behind, battered and stripped of plates. The youngest victim was 30. The oldest, 79. All were sent to Lincoln Hospital. None died, but pain echoed through the street. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. The police are still looking for the driver. No one has been held to account.

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      In the past 12 months, SD 29 saw 11 deaths and 1,734 injuries from crashes. Twenty-three people were seriously hurt. The toll is highest for those on foot or bike. SUVs and sedans did most of the damage. Trucks, buses, motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes all left bodies broken. The violence is not rare. It is routine. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh.

      What Has Senator Serrano Done?

      Senator Jose Serrano has voted for bills to curb repeat speeders and expand school speed cameras. He backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst offenders to install speed-limiting tech. He voted yes on school speed cameras and driver education reforms. These are steps forward. But the blood on the sidewalk says more is needed. Cameras and laws do not stop a Mustang with no plates. They do not heal a broken leg or erase a child’s fear.

      The Call: Demand More Than Words

      Six people hit. No arrests. Eleven dead in a year. This is not fate. It is policy. Call Senator Serrano. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand action against repeat offenders. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 29.
      ▸ Where does SD 29 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and assembly district AD 84.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 29?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, North & South Brother Islands, Concourse-Concourse Village, Highbridge, Mount Eden-Claremont (West), Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Soundview Park, Upper East Side-Yorkville, and Bronx CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, District 17, and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 77, AD 84, and AD 85.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 29?
      SUVs and Cars: 8 deaths, 778 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 2 deaths, 39 injuries. Bikes: 1 death, 61 injuries. All numbers from 2022–2025. Source
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      These crashes are preventable. Policy, street design, and enforcement choices shape the risk on every block.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, target repeat dangerous drivers, fund safer street designs, and ensure laws protect people walking and biking.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
