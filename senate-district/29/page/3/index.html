      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      Crosswalk at Bruckner and Leggett. A mother down. The driver gone.

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 3, 2025

      Just after midnight on Aug 26, 2025, at Bruckner and Leggett, 32-year-old Dilmania Lopez de Rodriguez crossed in the crosswalk. A driver hit her and kept going. She died at Lincoln Hospital. Police said the driver was heading north on Bruckner Boulevard.

      She was one of 38 people killed on the streets of this Senate district since Jan 1, 2022, in data through Sept 3, 2025. The same period shows 6,084 injuries and 10,995 crashes. NYC Open Data.

      The corners keep taking people

      On Feb 25, 2025, a 57-year-old man on an e-bike was killed when an MTA bus made a left at East 149th and Brook. The rider was ejected and died. Crash data.

      On Apr 2, 2025, a 52-year-old pedestrian died near East 149th and Morris. Three SUVs were involved. Crash data.

      On Aug 26, 2025, Lopez de Rodriguez was struck at Bruckner and Leggett. Police say video shows a truck turning as a scooter waited on its right. She never made it across. ABC7.

      A year that’s getting worse

      Year to date, compared with last year at this time, crashes are up 31.7% (2,324 vs. 1,764). Injuries are up 28.5% (1,370 vs. 1,066). Serious injuries are up 41.2% (24 vs. 17). Deaths are down to 7 from 8. District rollup.

      In the last 12 months alone: 3,382 crashes, 2,014 injuries, 9 deaths in these same blocks and boulevards. NYC Open Data.

      What people saw on 149th Street

      On July 3, 2025, a Ford Mustang swung left at East 149th and Courtlandt, jumped the curb, and hit six people under scaffolding. The driver ran. “People were yelling, were in pain,” a witness said. ABC7.

      SUVs figure heavily in harm to people on foot here. In this period’s pedestrian cases, SUVs are tied to 338 injuries and 5 deaths; sedans to 304 injuries and 2 deaths; trucks to 45 injuries and 3 deaths. District rollup.

      The levers on the table

      Albany’s Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045) would force repeat dangerous drivers to use speed limiters. State Senator Jose Serrano backed it: he co-sponsored and voted yes in committee. Open States. The Assembly seat here is held by Amanda Septimo. Our record shows no listed stance from her on the Assembly companion in this dataset. What gives? Open States.

      Citywide, we can also lower the default speed limit and expand enforcement against repeat speeders. The steps are laid out here.

      The mother in the crosswalk did everything right. The street did not. The tools exist. Use them. Take action.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where did these crashes happen?
      Within New York State Senate District 29, which covers parts of the South Bronx and East Harlem shown on this page’s district map. Figures and examples in this article come from incidents inside this district during Jan 1, 2022–Sept 3, 2025, per NYC Open Data.
      ▸ How many people have been killed here since 2022?
      CrashCount’s district rollup shows 38 people killed in traffic crashes from Jan 1, 2022 through Sept 3, 2025, with 6,084 injured and 10,995 crashes. Source: NYC Open Data motor-vehicle crash datasets.
      ▸ What bills or policies could cut this harm?
      The Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045) would require speed limiters for repeat dangerous drivers. Sen. Jose Serrano co-sponsored and voted yes in committee, per Open States. City leaders can also lower default speeds and target repeat speeders; see our steps at /take_action/.
      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets (Crashes h9gi-nx95, Persons f55k-p6yu, Vehicles bm4k-52h4), filtered to crashes within Senate District 29, from 2022-01-01 through 2025-09-03. We aggregated counts of crashes, injuries, serious injuries, and fatalities and compared year-to-date periods. Data were accessed Sept 3, 2025. You can view the source datasets here.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.

      Jan 13 - A Chevy SUV hit a 77-year-old woman at dawn on York Avenue. The front end struck her full. She bled from the head, conscious on the street. The driver’s view was blocked. Steel met flesh in the cold morning light.

      According to the police report, a 2022 Chevy SUV traveling north on York Avenue near 92nd Street struck a 77-year-old woman who was crossing alone at dawn. The report states, 'The front end hit her full. She lay bleeding from the head. The driver’s view was blocked. She was conscious.' The pedestrian suffered a head injury with severe bleeding, but remained conscious at the scene. The official contributing factor listed is 'View Obstructed/Limited,' indicating the driver’s line of sight was compromised. The data does not cite any pedestrian behavior as a contributing factor. The crash underscores the lethal risk when drivers proceed with limited visibility, especially in areas where vulnerable road users cross.
