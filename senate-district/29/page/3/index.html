      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 29?

      Blood on the Sidewalk, Silence in Albany

      Written July 4, 2025

      SD 29: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 4, 2025

      The Street Does Not Forgive

      Six people lay on the sidewalk at East 149th and Courtlandt. A Mustang hit one, then five more, then the scaffolding. The driver and passenger ran. The car stayed behind, battered and empty. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. This was July 3rd. The blood is still fresh.

      Just days before, a driver ran a red at Hunts Point, jumped the curb, and struck three more people. One man is still fighting for his life. The driver tried to run too, but police caught him. The city keeps counting. The bodies keep coming.

      The Toll: Numbers That Do Not Lie

      In the last year, SD 29 saw 11 deaths and 23 serious injuries. Over 1,500 people were hurt. Most were walking or biking. Cars and SUVs did the worst damage, but trucks, bikes, and mopeds all left their mark. The names fade. The pain does not.

      Leadership: What Has Been Done, What Has Not

      State Senator Jose Serrano voted yes on a bill to force safer street designs in June 2024. It passed. The law says streets must protect everyone, not just drivers. But the pace is slow. The danger is not. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh. The panic is real. The fixes are not.

      The Call: No More Waiting

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call Senator Serrano. Call your council member. Demand streets that do not kill. Demand action before the next siren.

      Do not let them wait you out.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature, responsible for passing laws and shaping statewide policy. Senators represent districts and vote on bills that affect all New Yorkers.
      ▸ Where does SD 29 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, city council district District 8 and assembly district AD 84.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 29?
      It includes the Mott Haven-Port Morris, Melrose, Hunts Point, North & South Brother Islands, Concourse-Concourse Village, Highbridge, Mount Eden-Claremont (West), Yankee Stadium-Macombs Dam Park, Soundview Park, Upper East Side-Yorkville, and Bronx CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 8, District 16, District 17, and District 18, and Assembly Districts AD 68, AD 77, AD 84, and AD 85.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 29?
      Cars and SUVs were responsible for the most harm to pedestrians in SD 29, with 8 deaths and over 700 injuries. Trucks and buses caused 3 deaths and dozens of injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 2 deaths and dozens of injuries. Bikes were involved in 1 death and 59 injuries. (Source: NYC Open Data, 2022–2025)
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are not random. Most could be prevented with safer street design, lower speeds, and stronger enforcement.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws for safer street design, lower speed limits, and hold dangerous drivers accountable. They can also push city agencies to act faster and fund real safety improvements.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up-to-date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
