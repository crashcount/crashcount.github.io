      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 10?

      Blood on the Parkway, Silence in Albany

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 10: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Bodies on the Parkway

      Thirteen dead. Nearly 1,800 injured. That is the toll in Senate District 10 over the last year. These are not just numbers. They are mothers, sons, neighbors—the sound of glass breaking at dawn, the smell of burning fuel on the Belt Parkway.

      On July 5, a BMW tore across the divider near Cross Bay Boulevard and went airborne. Two people died. Three more were thrown from the wreck. A survivor described how “they went airborne and into the barrier.” The car caught fire. Police confirmed that no one in the BMW wore a seat belt. The crash closed the parkway, but not the wound.

      The Pattern: Speed, Steel, and Silence

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. In three years, cars and SUVs in SD 10 have killed 11 people and injured more than 650 on foot. Trucks and buses killed one. Motorcycles and mopeds killed none. This violence is not random. It is a pattern—speed, distraction, and divided highways. The Belt Parkway, North Conduit, Rockaway Boulevard: names that mean danger to anyone not behind the wheel.

      What Has Senator Sanders Done?

      Senator James Sanders has voted for safer street design. In June 2024, he voted yes on a bill to require complete streets that protect everyone, not just drivers. He has supported more transit for Queens and pushed for the QueensLink rail project. But when the governor paused congestion pricing—cutting funds for transit and safety—Sanders supported the pause. The silence on lowering speed limits to 20 mph is loud. The dead do not care about politics. They care about action.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every day without change is another day for another crash. Call Senator Sanders. Demand a default 20 mph speed limit. Demand real investment in safe streets, not just studies and speeches. The bodies are piling up. Act before the next one hits the ground.

      Sanders Supports Safety Boosting Traffic Laws and Lower Speeds
      ▸

      New York rolls out sweeping traffic laws for 2025. Charter bus seatbelts, lower speed limits, and congestion pricing hit the streets. Senator Sanders and Governor Hochul push for safer roads. School zones, bridges, and bus riders see new protections. Change comes fast.

      On December 31, 2024, New York announced new transportation laws for 2025. Senator James Sanders sponsored a ban on car lease turn-in fees, stating, "The legislation prohibits charging a turn-in fee at the expiration of the term of a vehicle lease, the basis of which is solely for administrative, handling or clerical charges." Governor Kathy Hochul signed this and other safety bills into law. Charter bus passengers ages 8 to 16 must now wear seatbelts, a response to a fatal crash in Orange County. Sammy's Law, enacted in 2024, lets the city lower speed limits to 20 mph, and even 10 mph on redesigned streets. The Department of Transportation will target 250 locations, focusing on school zones. The MTA launches congestion pricing in Manhattan, with funds set for transit upgrades. Hochul said, "These enhancements to our roads and bridges will improve mobility for motorists, pedestrians and cyclists alike."
