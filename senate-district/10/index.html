      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 10?

      Wrong-Way Streets, Dead-End Promises: Demand Action Now

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 29, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 10: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt: The Human Cost

      A man on a moped, Antonio Smith-Ortiz, was struck and killed by a wrong-way driver on 149th Avenue. The driver sped off into the night. Police said, “The driver, who was going against traffic in the eastbound lane, then struck an unoccupied parked…van before speeding off” (NY Daily News).

      A cyclist was left unconscious and critical after a hit-and-run on 115th Avenue. The driver did not stop. Police are “now looking for evidence to help them track down the driver” (ABC7).

      A minivan carrying seniors veered off Brookville Boulevard and hit a tree. One woman died. Three others were rushed to the hospital (NY Daily News).

      In the past 12 months, SD 10 saw 12 deaths and 1,834 injuries from 2,781 crashes. The dead include children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians. The numbers do not bleed, but the streets do.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Senator James Sanders has voted for safer street design. He backed a bill to enable safe access to public roads for all users by utilizing complete street design principles. He has pushed for more transit and supported the QueensLink rail plan over a park-only option.

      But Sanders also supported the pause on congestion pricing, a move that cuts funding for transit and leaves vulnerable road users at risk. He has called for more express bus service, but the trains and buses still do not reach many in his district.

      Laws have changed. Streets have not changed fast enough. The dead do not wait.

      What Comes Next: No More Waiting

      Speed kills. Lower speed limits save lives. Cameras catch reckless drivers. But the city drags its feet, and Albany threatens to let the cameras go dark. Every day of delay is another day of risk.

      Call your leaders. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand more cameras. Demand streets that protect the people who walk, bike, and wait for the bus.

      The numbers are not just numbers. They are names, faces, and empty chairs at dinner tables. The time for patience is over. Take action now.

      Sanders Supports Safety Boosting Traffic Laws and Lower Speeds
      ▸

      New York rolls out sweeping traffic laws for 2025. Charter bus seatbelts, lower speed limits, and congestion pricing hit the streets. Senator Sanders and Governor Hochul push for safer roads. School zones, bridges, and bus riders see new protections. Change comes fast.

      On December 31, 2024, New York announced new transportation laws for 2025. Senator James Sanders sponsored a ban on car lease turn-in fees, stating, "The legislation prohibits charging a turn-in fee at the expiration of the term of a vehicle lease, the basis of which is solely for administrative, handling or clerical charges." Governor Kathy Hochul signed this and other safety bills into law. Charter bus passengers ages 8 to 16 must now wear seatbelts, a response to a fatal crash in Orange County. Sammy's Law, enacted in 2024, lets the city lower speed limits to 20 mph, and even 10 mph on redesigned streets. The Department of Transportation will target 250 locations, focusing on school zones. The MTA launches congestion pricing in Manhattan, with funds set for transit upgrades. Hochul said, "These enhancements to our roads and bridges will improve mobility for motorists, pedestrians and cyclists alike."
