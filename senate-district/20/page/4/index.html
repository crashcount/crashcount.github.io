      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 20?

      Another Child Dead. What Will It Take to Stop the Killing?

      Written July 6, 2025 (Revised July 6, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, ensured all citations are correct and deduped.)

      SD 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 6, 2025

      Blood on the Parkway

      On June 28, an eight-year-old boy was killed crossing Eastern Parkway at Albany Avenue. He was walking with his sister when a black Honda SUV struck him. The driver stayed. The boy did not. A witness said, “I just saw a lot of blood gushing out of his ears, his mouth” (NY Daily News). Neighbors called the street dangerous. The city called it an accident. The family called the undertaker.

      In the last twelve months, three people have died in crashes in SD 20. One was a child. One was over 75. Over 1,200 people have been injured (NYC Open Data). Thirteen were seriously hurt. The dead and wounded are not numbers. They are children, elders, neighbors. They are the cost of delay.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and the Young

      Cars and SUVs do most of the killing. In the last three years, SUVs and sedans caused five pedestrian deaths and hundreds of injuries. Trucks and buses killed three more. Bikes and mopeds did not kill a single pedestrian. The pattern is clear. The danger is heavy and fast (NYC Open Data).

      What Has Senator Myrie Done?

      Senator Zellnor Myrie has voted for safer streets. He backed the 2024 bill to redesign roads for all users, not just drivers (supported the 2024 bill). He has called for more protected bike lanes, busways, and daylighting. He rode a bike with a reporter and said, “We should be making this as easy as possible and as safe as possible for as many people as possible” (said Myrie). He supports congestion pricing and enforcement against reckless drivers. But the deaths keep coming. The pace of change is slow. The streets are not safe yet.

      The Call: No More Waiting

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call Senator Myrie. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people on foot and bike. Do not wait for the next child to die.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York (Open States).
      ▸ Where does SD 20 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 35 and assembly district AD 57. (NYC Open Data)
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 20?
      It includes the Park Slope, Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Prospect Heights, Crown Heights (North), Lincoln Terrace Park, Crown Heights (South), Prospect Lefferts Gardens-Wingate, East Flatbush-Erasmus, East Flatbush-Rugby, Prospect Park, and Brooklyn CB8 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 35, District 36, District 39, District 40, and District 41, and Assembly Districts AD 43, AD 44, AD 52, AD 56, AD 57, and AD 58. (NYC Open Data)
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 20?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm, with SUVs and sedans responsible for five pedestrian deaths and hundreds of injuries. Trucks and buses killed three more. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes caused injuries but no pedestrian deaths (NYC Open Data).
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and better enforcement. They are not random acts of fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, redesign streets for safety, expand protected bike lanes, and hold reckless drivers accountable.
