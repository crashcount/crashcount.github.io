      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 20?

      No More Dead Kids in the Crosswalks

      Written June 30, 2025

      SD 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 30, 2025

      Children Bleed in the Crosswalk

      On June 28, an eight-year-old boy tried to cross Eastern Parkway with his sister. A black Honda Pilot hit him. He died in the street. A witness described the scene: “I saw the kid being dragged from underneath the car by a woman.” Another neighbor put it plainly: “Yeah, this street is dangerous.”

      This is not rare. In the last twelve months, SD 20 saw 1,289 injuries and 2 deaths from crashes. Fifteen people were seriously hurt. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians are broken on these roads. The numbers do not stop. The pain does not fade.

      The Toll of Cars and Trucks

      SUVs and cars do the most harm. In this district, they caused 4 deaths and 18 serious injuries to people on foot or bike. Trucks and buses killed 3 more. Bikes, mopeds, and motorcycles add to the toll, but the big machines do the killing. Every week, another family is left to grieve.

      What Has Senator Myrie Done?

      Senator Zellnor Myrie has voted for safer streets. In June 2024, he voted yes on a bill to redesign roads for all users, not just drivers. He has called for more protected bike lanes, busways, and enforcement. He said, “We should be making this as easy as possible and as safe as possible for as many people as possible.”

      But the deaths keep coming. Promises and plans are not enough. Every delay is another body on the pavement.

      Call to Action: Demand Relentless Change

      Do not wait for another child to die. Call Senator Myrie. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes and daylighted corners. Demand action, not words. The blood on the street is real. The time to act is now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, including those that affect street safety and traffic policy. See the legislative record.
      ▸ Where does SD 20 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 35 and assembly district AD 57. See NYC crash data.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 20?
      It includes the Park Slope, Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Prospect Heights, Crown Heights (North), Lincoln Terrace Park, Crown Heights (South), Prospect Lefferts Gardens-Wingate, East Flatbush-Erasmus, East Flatbush-Rugby, Prospect Park, and Brooklyn CB8 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 35, District 36, District 39, District 40, and District 41, and Assembly Districts AD 43, AD 44, AD 52, AD 56, AD 57, and AD 58. See NYC crash data.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 20?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm, with 4 deaths and 18 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds were involved in 27 crashes, and bikes in 37, but the majority of deaths came from larger vehicles. See NYC crash data.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable with safer street design, lower speeds, and better enforcement. They are not random acts of fate.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws for lower speed limits, protected bike lanes, daylighted intersections, and redesign streets to protect people, not just cars.
