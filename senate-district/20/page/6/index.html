      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 20?

      Crown Heights Crossings, Hard Deaths, and a Senate Seat That Talks Safety but Misses Votes

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Brooklyn’s SD 20 has a list of dead. It keeps growing.

      Eastern Parkway took a boy

      At Eastern Parkway and Albany, a southbound Honda Pilot hit an eight‑year‑old crossing with his sister at 5:33 p.m. The driver stayed. No arrest that day. Police said they were looking at speed. Witnesses saw the child dragged from under the SUV. “I just saw a lot of blood,” one said. NYC crash data confirms the fatal strike on June 28, 2025. The scene and voices are in the Daily News and ABC7: “Police are looking at whether speed was a factor.”

      A century of life ended in a turn

      On April 8, 2025, at Montgomery and Brooklyn, a GMC SUV making a left failed to yield and hit a woman, 101, who was crossing with the signal. The driver was unlicensed, the record says. She died of head injuries. This is all in the city dataset for CrashID 4804451.

      Caton and Flatbush broke a man

      New Year’s Eve, 2024. Flatbush at Caton. An Audi sedan struck a 64‑year‑old man outside an intersection. He suffered crush injuries and died. The city crash file shows the death at 5:40 p.m., CrashID 4783016.

      Prospect Park’s quiet morning, then a body

      June 5, 2025. West Drive at Center Drive. A 71‑year‑old bicyclist going straight ahead lost consciousness and died. No second vehicle listed. The bike shows “no damage” in the official record, CrashID 4824644.

      Church and E 55: mid‑day, one more death

      July 17, 2025. Church Avenue at East 55th Street. A Ford SUV hit a 36‑year‑old man at the intersection. The file lists “alcohol involvement” and “crossing against signal.” He died at the scene. The SUV was going straight. The NYPD dataset is blunt, CrashID 4828979. Only one person is dead, and it isn’t the driver.

      The count won’t stop

      From 2022 to now, SD 20 logged 9,752 crashes, 5,768 injuries, and 22 deaths. In the last 12 months alone, there were 2,936 crashes, 1,861 injuries, and 5 deaths. Year‑to‑date crashes and injuries are up sharply over last year. These figures come from our area roll‑up of NYC Open Data. SUVs and cars are the main source of harm to people on foot: 868 pedestrian injuries, including 7 deaths in the period covered, tied to those vehicles. Trucks and buses add more. See the full breakdown in our statistics sourced from the same dataset.

      The senator rides, then misses votes

      State Sen. Zellnor Myrie says streets must be safe for riders and walkers. “We should be making this as easy as possible and as safe as possible for as many people as possible,” he told Streetsblog on a June 16, 2025 ride. He co‑sponsored the speed‑limiter bill, S 4045, which targets repeat violators. But on June 11 and 12, committee votes moved that bill forward while he was marked “excused.” The records are on the Senate site via Open States.

      What stops the next one

      The tools sit on the table. Albany passed Sammy’s Law to let the city set lower speed limits. City Hall can drop residential streets to 20 mph. The state bill S 4045 would force speed limiters on repeat offenders. Both are covered in our Take Action page and the Senate file. Use them.

      One task today: tell City Hall to set a default 20 mph and back speed limiters for chronic offenders. Start here: Take action.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where is SD 20?
      It covers parts of Park Slope, Windsor Terrace–South Slope, Prospect Heights, Crown Heights (North and South), Prospect Lefferts Gardens–Wingate, East Flatbush (Erasmus and Rugby), Prospect Park, and CB8/CB9/CB55. See our map links in this page’s header.
      ▸ How many crashes and injuries has SD 20 had during the covered period?
      Since 2022-01-01, SD 20 recorded 9,752 crashes, 5,768 injuries, and 22 deaths, based on NYC Open Data.
      ▸ What recent fatal crashes stand out in SD 20?
      An 8‑year‑old killed at Eastern Pkwy/Albany Ave (6/28/25), a 101‑year‑old killed while crossing with the signal at Montgomery St/Brooklyn Ave (4/8/25), a 64‑year‑old killed at Flatbush/Caton (12/31/24), a 71‑year‑old bicyclist who died on West Dr at Center Dr (6/5/25), and a 36‑year‑old killed at Church Ave/E 55 St (7/17/25). All are in NYC Open Data and contemporaneous press where noted.
      ▸ What policies could reduce repeat dangerous driving?
      Senate bill S 4045 would require intelligent speed assistance devices for drivers with certain repeat violations. The bill advanced in committees on June 11 and 12, 2025; Sen. Myrie was marked “excused” on those votes. Source: NY Senate/Open States.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.

      Senator Zellnor Myrie told a city panel how NYPD officers used bikes as weapons during a protest. He described tires on his back, threats of pepper spray, and trauma that lingers. The Civilian Complaint Review Board found the officer abused his authority.

      On June 30, 2022, State Senator Zellnor Myrie testified at an NYPD disciplinary hearing for Officer Michael Kovalik, a member of the Strategic Response Group. The hearing followed a Civilian Complaint Review Board (CCRB) investigation, which found Kovalik abused his authority by threatening Myrie and others with pepper spray during a protest on May 29, 2020. The matter centered on 'excessive use of force with department-issued bikes' and the officer's actions at a protest outside Barclays Center after George Floyd's murder. Myrie, detained with zip ties, said, 'I felt what felt like bike tires on my back and on my arms.' He described lasting trauma from the incident. The CCRB prosecutor, Andre Applewhite, argued video evidence confirmed the threats. Myrie and another lawmaker have filed a federal lawsuit against the NYPD. The case highlights the dangers faced by peaceful protesters and the use of police bicycles as tools of force.
