      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 20?

      Crown Heights, Eastern Parkway, and the long walk home

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Eastern Parkway took a child at Albany Avenue. A black Honda Pilot hit him as he crossed with his sister. He was eight. The 69‑year‑old driver stayed. No arrest. Neighbors called the street dangerous. “Police are looking at whether speed was a factor.” A witness said, “I saw the kid being dragged from underneath the car.”

      At Montgomery and Brooklyn Avenue, a driver turned left and struck a woman in the crosswalk. She was 101. The SUV driver was unlicensed, police records say. She had the walk. The report lists “Failure to Yield Right‑of‑Way” as a factor.

      On Church and East 55th, a Ford SUV hit a 36‑year‑old man at the intersection. He died at the scene, the city dataset shows. The car went straight. He didn’t get up.

      On Flatbush at Caton, an Audi sedan struck a 64‑year‑old man away from the crosswalk. The entry says “Crush Injuries.” No more words.

      Where the bodies are

      These deaths sit inside Senate District 20. The roll call is short and brutal:

      • Eastern Parkway at Albany Ave: boy, 8, killed by SUV, driver remained, investigation ongoing (NYPD crash database; ABC7; NY Daily News).
      • Montgomery St at Brooklyn Ave: woman, 101, crossing with the signal, struck and killed by unlicensed left‑turning SUV driver (NYC Open Data, CrashID 4804451).
      • Church Ave at E 55 St: man, 36, pedestrian at the intersection, struck by Ford SUV, killed (CrashID 4828979).
      • Flatbush Ave at Caton Ave: man, 64, pedestrian outside an intersection, killed by sedan (CrashID 4783016).
      • Prospect Park West Drive at Center Drive: cyclist, 71, died after losing consciousness while riding, no motor vehicle involved (CrashID 4824644).

      This district logged 9,752 crashes since 2022. 22 people are dead. 5,768 are hurt. SUVs and cars account for most injuries to people on foot (PeriodStats).

      The numbers don’t blink

      Year to date, crashes are up. Compared to last year’s span, SD 20 has 21.7% more crashes and more people hurt. Deaths rose from 3 to 4 in the same period (PeriodStats). Children are not spared: one killed this year; 98 injured so far. Seniors too: deaths in the 65–74 and 75+ bands, injuries in every decade (PeriodStats).

      Pedestrians are hit most by sedans and SUVs. The dataset lists 398 pedestrian casualties tied to sedans and 302 tied to SUVs, including deaths (PeriodStats). Trucks and buses add to the toll. No corridor carries it all. Many do their part.

      A child, a century, and a light that stayed green

      Witnesses at Eastern Parkway spoke in fragments. “They were walking, he was crossing the street with his sister,” a neighbor said. “I just saw a lot of blood,” another said. The driver stood by the car. The NYPD towed the SUV. No charges announced in the press reports.

      On Montgomery Street, the form fields are stark. “Crossing With Signal.” “Failure to Yield.” “Unlicensed.” Center front end. Left turn. That is all the city gives you. It is enough.

      Power sits on the switch

      Albany gave the city the power to drop speeds. The city can act. A 20 mph default on local streets is on the table. Speed cameras now run around the clock through 2030. The tools to slow cars exist. The city has not used all of them. The state can press repeat offenders.

      The Senate is moving a bill to stop the worst drivers. The Stop Super Speeders Act (S4045) would force drivers with heavy records to install speed limiters. The measure advanced in June; SD 20’s senator is listed as a co‑sponsor. Two committee votes passed while he was marked excused (bill file).

      Slow the streets. Box in the worst speeders. That is the work.

      Take one step now. Tell City Hall and Albany to use the tools we already have. Start here: Take Action.

      Within Brooklyn’s Senate District 20. Sites include Eastern Pkwy at Albany Ave, Montgomery St at Brooklyn Ave, Church Ave at E 55 St, Flatbush Ave at Caton Ave, and West Drive at Center Drive.
      ▸ How many crashes and victims are in SD 20 since 2022?
      Since 2022, SD 20 recorded 9,752 crashes, 22 deaths, and 5,768 injuries. SUVs and cars cause most pedestrian injuries, per the dataset.
      ▸ What do witnesses say about the Eastern Parkway crash?
      Press reports quote neighbors: “They were walking, he was crossing the street with his sister” (New York Post) and “I saw the kid being dragged from underneath the car” (NY Daily News).
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
      ▸ What can fix this pattern?
      Lower the default city speed limit using Sammy’s Law and pass S4045 to require speed limiters for repeat offenders. Both actions are referenced in our Take Action page and the S4045 bill file.
