      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 20?

      Eastern Parkway: Three Dead, a Thousand Hurt—How Many More?

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      Blood on the Parkway

      Three lives lost. Over 1,200 injured. In the last year and a half, Senate District 20 has seen the slow grind of traffic violence. The numbers do not flinch: three deaths, thirteen serious injuries, and more than two thousand crashes in just twelve months (NYC Open Data). Children, elders, and working people—none are spared. On June 28, an eight-year-old boy tried to cross Eastern Parkway with his sister. A black SUV hit him. Witnesses saw him dragged from under the car. “I just saw a lot of blood gushing out of his ears, his mouth,” said one witness. The driver stayed. The boy did not.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs, sedans, trucks. These are the weapons. In SD 20, cars and SUVs caused the most harm: five deaths, 18 serious injuries, hundreds wounded. Trucks and buses killed three more. Motorcycles and mopeds, none. Bikes, none. The streets are not safe for the old or the young. “He was a boy who loved life,” said Baila Krainer. The street took him anyway.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      State Senator Zellnor Myrie has voted for safer street design, backing the Complete Streets bill that forces roads to be built for people, not just cars. He has called for physically separated bus lanes and more automated enforcement. He rode a bike through Brooklyn, saw the blocked lanes, and said, “We should be making this as easy as possible and as safe as possible for as many people as possible.” He said it himself.

      But the blood keeps coming. Promises and plans do not stop a speeding SUV. Every day without action is another day someone does not come home.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      Call Senator Myrie. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes. Demand action, not condolences. The dead cannot speak. You must.
