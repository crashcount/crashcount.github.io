      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 20?

      Another Child Dead—How Many Bodies Before We Fix the Parkway?

      Written July 4, 2025

      SD 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 4, 2025

      Blood on the Parkway

      On June 28, an eight-year-old boy was killed by a Honda SUV while crossing Eastern Parkway with his sister. The street was busy. The driver stayed at the scene. There was no arrest. A witness described the horror: “I saw the kid being dragged from underneath the car by a woman.” Another neighbor said, “Yeah, this street is dangerous.”

      This is not rare. In the past year, SD 20 saw 3 deaths and 13 serious injuries. Children, elders, and working people. SUVs and cars did most of the killing.

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      In the last 12 months:

      • 1 child killed, 70 children injured
      • 1 elder killed, 39 elders injured
      • 1 person over 55 killed, 152 injured
      • Total: 3 dead, 1,293 injured, 13 seriously hurt

      SUVs and cars caused the most harm: 5 deaths, 18 serious injuries since 2022. Trucks and buses killed 3. Motorcycles and mopeds, none. Bikes, none. The bodies pile up. The city moves on.

      What Has Senator Myrie Done?

      Senator Zellnor Myrie has voted for safer streets. In June 2024, he voted yes on a bill to require “complete street design”—redesigning roads to protect everyone, not just drivers. He has called for more protected bike lanes, busways, and congestion pricing. He said, “We should be making this as easy as possible and as safe as possible for as many people as possible.”

      But the deaths keep coming. Promises and plans do not stop a speeding SUV. Every day without change is another day someone dies.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy.

      Call Senator Myrie. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real street redesigns—protected bike lanes, daylighted corners, busways. Do not wait for another child’s blood on the street.

      Act now. The next victim is only a day away.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 20 in Albany.
      ▸ Where does SD 20 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 35 and assembly district AD 57.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 20?
      It includes the Park Slope, Windsor Terrace-South Slope, Prospect Heights, Crown Heights (North), Lincoln Terrace Park, Crown Heights (South), Prospect Lefferts Gardens-Wingate, East Flatbush-Erasmus, East Flatbush-Rugby, Prospect Park, and Brooklyn CB8 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 35, District 36, District 39, District 40, and District 41, and Assembly Districts AD 43, AD 44, AD 52, AD 56, AD 57, and AD 58.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 20?
      Since 2022, SUVs and cars caused 5 deaths and 18 serious injuries to pedestrians in SD 20. Trucks and buses killed 3. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes caused none.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data shows patterns—speed, street design, and vehicle size all play a role. These deaths are preventable with better laws and safer streets.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund street redesigns, expand protected bike lanes and busways, and hold dangerous drivers accountable.
