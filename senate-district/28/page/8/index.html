      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 28?

      Eleven Dead, Thousands Hurt—How Many More Before SD 28 Streets Change?

      Written June 24, 2025

      SD 28: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 24, 2025

      The Death Count in SD 28

      The streets of Senate District 28 do not forgive. In the last twelve months, 11 people died and 34 more were left with serious injuries. The old and the young fall alike. Five people over 75 are dead. Three more between 65 and 74. A 90-year-old woman was crushed crossing York Avenue. She never made it to the other side. NYC Open Data

      The numbers do not stop. 1,257 injured. 2,285 crashes. Each one a story cut short or a body broken. The city calls them accidents. The families know better.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and cars did the most harm. In three years, they killed 11, left 21 with serious injuries, and hurt hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed three. Motorcycles and mopeds, six serious injuries. Bikes, too, left their mark—eight serious injuries, no deaths, but pain all the same. NYC Open Data

      The Next Fight

      Every day of delay is another body in the street. The law now lets New York City drop the speed limit to 20 mph. The city has not done it. The council and the mayor need to hear from you. Call. Write. Demand safer speeds. Demand streets built for people, not machines.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature, responsible for making and passing state laws. Senators represent districts across New York and vote on bills that affect the entire state.
      ▸ Where does SD 28 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 4 and assembly district AD 73.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 28?
      It includes the Midtown South-Flatiron-Union Square, Midtown-Times Square, East Midtown-Turtle Bay, Upper East Side-Lenox Hill-Roosevelt Island, Upper East Side-Carnegie Hill, Manhattan CB5, and Manhattan CB8 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 3, District 4, District 5, and District 6, and Assembly Districts AD 73, AD 75, and AD 76.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 28?
      Cars and SUVs killed 11, caused 21 serious injuries, and hundreds of minor injuries. Trucks and buses killed 3. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 6 serious injuries. Bikes caused 8 serious injuries and no deaths. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The data show patterns—speed, turning, and street design kill again and again. These deaths are preventable.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund safer street designs, and support automated enforcement that targets dangerous driving—not vulnerable road users.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
