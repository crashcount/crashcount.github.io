      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 16?

      Queens SD 16: A summer of sirens on Northern, Hollis Court, and the expressways

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 16: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      Northern Boulevard. Hollis Court Boulevard. The Long Island Expressway. The names repeat. So do the funerals.

      Three deaths, three corridors

      • On July 31, a 62‑year‑old Nissan Rogue driver hit 55‑year‑old Zhao Feng Zhen on Hollis Court Blvd at 50th Ave. She died at the scene. Police said the driver stayed. They are investigating the crash. City data shows an SUV making a left and an e‑bike going straight at that time and place, with the bicyclist ejected and killed in the official log.
      • On June 11 at Northern Blvd and 217 St, a 74‑year‑old man crossing at the corner was struck by a 2017 Chevy SUV heading east. He died at the hospital. Patch named him Eric Wexler and reported no charges as of that afternoon here. The city’s file lists a pedestrian killed at that corner by an eastbound SUV in the dataset.
      • On December 18, a 65‑year‑old man was struck and killed on the Long Island Expressway by a westbound flatbed truck. The driver action is recorded as going straight. The file lists “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” The pedestrian died at the scene in the city log.

      The numbers don’t blink

      • In the last 12 months in this district: 11 deaths, 1,834 injuries, 3,021 crashes. Serious injuries: 42. SUVs are the leading vehicles in pedestrian harm, tied to 7 pedestrian deaths and 415 pedestrian casualties districtwide by the numbers.
      • Year to date: crashes up nearly 40% over last year’s pace; serious injuries up 71%. Deaths are flat at 5, but flat means five families torn open district stats.

      Buses on the curb, people on the edge

      • On July 11 at Main St and 57th Rd, an MTA Q20 bus jumped the curb, hit a pole and a ticket machine. Seven or eight injured, depending on the outlet. ABC7 reported investigators now believe the 25‑year‑old driver “had fallen asleep at the wheel.” The MTA pulled him from service pending the probe ABC7, CBS New York.
      • “The 25-year-old driver of the Q20 bus said he had a green light and misjudged the curb,” ABC7 reported, before the video review. “It is now believed he had fallen asleep at the wheel.” The injuries were described as minor by authorities ABC7, CBS New York.

      Northern Boulevard keeps taking

      • Eric Wexler, 74, crossing at 217 St. A Chevy SUV. Head and body trauma. Pronounced dead at the hospital. “No charges have been filed,” Patch wrote; both drivers in the two crashes it covered remained at the scene Patch.
      • Another case on Northern at Parsons Blvd in March: a 78‑year‑old woman killed by a westbound SUV, not at an intersection. The city’s record lists her as crossing with no signal or crosswalk official record. She died there.

      Policy on paper, blood on asphalt

      • Albany’s cameras run all day. School‑zone speed cameras are extended through 2030, legislators say. But the worst repeat offenders keep racking up tickets. The Senate moved a bill to force speed limiters on cars of chronic violators. Senator John Liu voted yes in committee on S 4045, which would require intelligent speed assistance for drivers with repeated violations Senate file. As advocates have shown, a small share of drivers cause a large share of deaths Streetsblog.
      • The city already has the power to lower speeds. It has not done it citywide. New Yorkers are asking for a 20 mph default and enforcement that stops the worst repeat speeders. Our own call to action lays out the steps and contacts Take Action.

      What breaks first: the habit or the body?

      • Zhao Feng Zhen died where a neighborhood street meets a turn. Eric Wexler died at a corner many know by the car lots and the gas fumes. A man died on the expressway under the hum of trucks.
      • The district’s count keeps climbing. 8,651 crashes since 2022. 36 dead. 5,014 injured. The toll sits in the open data. It does not look away district rollup.

      Bold fixes exist. Lower the default speed to 20 mph. Pass and enforce speed limiters for repeat violators. Start here, now. Then keep going. Act today.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where did the recent fatal crashes happen in SD 16?
      Hollis Court Blvd at 50th Ave (July 31, 2025), Northern Blvd at 217 St (June 11, 2025), and the Long Island Expressway (Dec. 18, 2024), per NYC Open Data and linked press.
      ▸ How bad is the crash trend right now?
      In the last 12 months: 3,021 crashes, 1,834 injuries, 11 deaths, 42 serious injuries. Year‑to‑date crashes are up ~40% over last year’s pace, with serious injuries up ~71%, per NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Which vehicles are most often hurting pedestrians here?
      SUVs lead: 7 pedestrian deaths and 415 pedestrian casualties districtwide in the current period, according to NYC Open Data’s rollup.
      ▸ What policies could reduce repeat dangerous driving?
      The Senate advanced S 4045 to require intelligent speed assistance for drivers with repeated violations. Sen. John Liu voted yes in committee. See the bill page linked in the story.

      S 8344
      Liu votes yes to extend school speed zones, improving child pedestrian safety.
      ▸

      Senate passes S 8344. School speed zone rules in New York City get extended. Lawmakers make technical fixes. The bill keeps pressure on drivers near schools. Streets stay a little safer for kids.

      Bill S 8344, titled 'Extends provisions and makes technical corrections to school speed zones in NYC; repealer,' moved through the Senate and Assembly in June 2025. Sponsored by Senator Andrew Gounardes, the bill passed Senate votes on June 12 and June 13, and cleared the Assembly on June 17. The measure extends and corrects school speed zone laws in New York City, repealing outdated provisions. Gounardes led the push, with strong support from most Senate Democrats and a split Assembly. The bill's technical fixes aim to keep protections in place for children and other vulnerable road users near schools. No safety analyst note was provided.

      S 4045
      Liu votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
