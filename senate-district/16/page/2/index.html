      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 16?

      Northern Boulevard: Nine Dead, No Excuses—It’s Time to Take the Streets Back

      Written July 12, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, deduped and reordered citations, ensured correct inline links.)

      SD 16: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      Blood on Northern Boulevard

      In Senate District 16, the slow disaster does not pause. In the last twelve months, nine people died in crashes. Thirty-four suffered serious injuries. The bodies are not numbers. They are neighbors, elders, children.

      On June 11, Eric Wexler, age 74, tried to cross 217th Street and Northern Boulevard. A Chevy Tahoe hit him. He died at the hospital. The driver kept going east. The street stayed open. The city moved on. “Wexler was found with head and body trauma. He was transported to North Shore Manhasset Hospital, where he was pronounced dead.”

      Just days ago, an MTA bus in Flushing jumped the curb and smashed into a pole. Seven people were hurt. The driver, only 25, told investigators he “misjudged the curb.” Video showed more. Officials now believe he fell asleep at the wheel. The MTA pulled him from service. “I was all the way in the back and all of a sudden the bus hit the curb, I guess, jumped the curb, I went this way and that way and banged into the side of the bus.”

      The Pattern: Who Pays the Price

      SUVs and cars do most of the killing. In three years, they took thirteen lives and left hundreds broken. Trucks and buses followed. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes are in the numbers, but the real threat is heavy and fast. The old and the young are hit hardest. In the last year, two people over 75 died. One child did not make it home.

      What Has Senator John Liu Done?

      Senator John Liu has voted for safer streets. He backed the bill to require speed limiters for repeat speeders. He voted for complete street design. He co-sponsored a fare-free bus pilot. He stood with those who want car-free streets and congestion pricing. He has not been silent. But the deaths keep coming. The work is not done.

      What Next?

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day without change is a choice. Call Senator Liu. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed limiters for repeat offenders. Demand streets built for people, not cars. Do not wait for another name on the list. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature, responsible for passing laws and overseeing state policy. Senators represent districts across New York and vote on bills that affect every resident.
      ▸ Where does SD 16 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 20 and assembly district AD 25.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 16?
      It includes the Murray Hill-Broadway Flushing, East Flushing, Queensboro Hill, Flushing-Willets Point, Kissena Park, Kew Gardens Hills, Pomonok-Electchester-Hillcrest, Fresh Meadows-Utopia, Mount Hebron & Cedar Grove Cemeteries, Cunningham Park, and Queens CB81 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 19, District 20, District 23, and District 24, and Assembly Districts AD 25, AD 26, AD 27, and AD 40.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 16?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm to pedestrians in SD 16, with 13 deaths and hundreds of injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds were involved in 19 pedestrian injury incidents, while bikes were involved in 22.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable, or are they just accidents?
      Crashes are not fate. They are the result of policy, street design, and enforcement choices. Every death and injury can be prevented.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to reduce traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, require speed limiters for repeat offenders, redesign streets for safety, and support enforcement that targets dangerous driving—not vulnerable road users.
