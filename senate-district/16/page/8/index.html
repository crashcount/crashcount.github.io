      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 16?

      Northern Boulevard takes and takes: three dead in SD 16

      Written August 27, 2025 (Revised August 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 16: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 27, 2025

      Aug 23, 2025, late afternoon. A Ford sedan going west on United Nations Ave S hit a person in the roadway. The city file lists cause as driver distraction. The pedestrian died at the scene. The data is bare and cold. One entry says “Apparent Death.” The code calls it CrashID 4836979.

      Northern keeps killing

      June 11, 74‑year‑old Eric Wexler tried to cross at 217 St and Northern Boulevard. An eastbound Chevy Tahoe struck him. He had head and body trauma. He was taken to North Shore Manhasset and died, police told Patch. The crash file shows a pedestrian at the intersection, hit by an SUV going straight. The state lists the injury as “Apparent Death” under CrashID 4819696.

      Mar 14, westbound SUV, Parsons at Northern. A 78‑year‑old woman crossing outside a crosswalk. The database shows “Entire Body” injured. “Apparent Death.” No driver name. No charges. Just another point on the line. CrashID 4799008.

      A left turn, a rider down

      July 31 in Auburndale, a 62‑year‑old Nissan SUV driver turned left on Hollis Court Blvd at 50 Ave and struck Zhao Feng Zhen, 55, on an e‑bike. She died. Police said the driver stayed at the scene and the case is under investigation, reported the Daily News. City data lists a female bicyclist, ejected, “Apparent Death,” hit by an SUV making a left. CrashID 4832001.

      The bus that jumped the curb

      July 11 on Main Street at 57th Road, a Q20 bus came up over the curb, smashed a pole and a ticket machine, and nearly hit two people waiting, ABC7 reported. Eight were hurt. Investigators later believed the operator had fallen asleep; the MTA pulled him from service pending review, said ABC7. “I was all the way in the back and all of a sudden the bus hit the curb,” passenger Ken Baur said to ABC7. CBS said seven had minor injuries and the probe was ongoing (CBS New York).

      The toll of big cars

      In this district since 2022, SUVs and sedans dominate pedestrian harm. They account for most of the deaths and injuries recorded in crashes with people on foot: SUVs are tied to 7 pedestrian deaths; sedans to 8, in incidents counted in the dataset through Aug 27, 2025. The rollup is clear in the city’s files on crashes, persons, and vehicles. The numbers are not abstract. They are bodies.

      In the last 12 months, SD 16 saw 3,025 crashes, with 12 people killed and 1,833 injured. Serious injuries: 42. Year‑to‑date crashes are up 40% over last year’s pace; serious injuries up 71%. This comes from our district cut of the city collision data.

      What Albany and City Hall have on the table

      The Senate moved a bill to stop repeat speeders. It would make drivers who rack up violations install intelligent speed assistance. Senator John Liu co‑sponsored and voted yes in committee on S 4045 on June 11 and June 12. The aim is simple: keep cars from going more than 5 mph over the limit after too many tickets, as summarized in the bill file noted in our timeline.

      Albany also extended 24‑hour school‑zone cameras through 2030, according to our timeline’s Senate votes on S 8344. Advocates say cameras cut speeding. City officials have also pushed congestion pricing to cut car trips and fund transit, as documented in coverage we track, while local leaders call for safer, people‑first streets.

      Slow the streets, save the living

      We know what works. Lower the default speed limit citywide. Force repeat offenders to install speed limiters. Call it policy, or call it mercy. Either way, the numbers will move. The names will stop piling up. Start here: take action.

      Audi Turns Left, Strikes Pedestrian on Utopia Parkway
      ▸

      Jan 22 - A 19-year-old crossed with the signal. An Audi turned left. Steel hit flesh. His hip broke. The driver kept going. The street stayed quiet. The wound did not.

      A 2007 Audi sedan, driven by a 49-year-old woman, struck a 19-year-old man as he crossed Utopia Parkway with the signal. According to the police report, the driver was making a left turn when she failed to yield the right-of-way. The impact shattered the pedestrian’s hip and left him in pain. The report lists 'Failure to Yield Right-of-Way' as the primary contributing factor. The driver remained licensed and uninjured. The pedestrian was crossing legally at the intersection. No other contributing factors were noted in the report.

      Liu Supports Enhanced Subway Safety and Mental Health Services
      ▸

      Jan 16 - A woman died after being shoved onto Times Square subway tracks. The train struck her. Police arrested the suspect. Council Member Julie Won joined officials at the scene. Leaders promised action. The system failed a vulnerable New Yorker. Riders remain exposed.

      On January 16, 2022, Council Member Julie Won (District 26) joined city and state officials in response to a fatal subway incident. The matter, titled "Suspect Charged With Murder In 'Unprovoked' Fatal Shoving Of Woman Onto Times Square Subway Tracks," details the killing of Michelle Go, who was pushed onto the tracks and struck by a train. Mayor Eric Adams and Governor Kathy Hochul pledged to improve subway safety and address mental health and homelessness in transit. Won stood with other lawmakers, highlighting the impact on Asian-American communities. The council has not advanced specific legislation, but the event underscores the urgent need for systemic protections for vulnerable riders. No safety analyst assessment was provided.
