      Eight Dead, Dozens Broken—No More Waiting for Safe Streets

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 19, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 16: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Eight dead. Twenty-seven seriously hurt. In the last year alone, Senate District 16 has seen 2,342 crashes. The bodies are not numbers. They are neighbors. A 78-year-old woman, crossing Northern Boulevard near Parsons, struck down by a minivan that did not stop. She died in the hospital. The driver kept going. “A 78-year-old woman was fatally struck by a hit-and-run driver as she crossed a Queens street, police said.”

      A child pinned under a car at 32nd Avenue and 138th Street. The man beside him, critical. The driver stayed. The street did not forgive. Police responded and found an adult man pinned under a vehicle.

      The old, the young, the careful, the hurried. All at risk.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs and sedans do most of the damage. In three years, cars and SUVs killed 12, left 27 with serious wounds, and hurt hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed one. Motorcycles and mopeds, none. Bikes, none. The street is not equal. The bigger the machine, the deeper the wound.

      What Has John Liu Done?

      Senator John Liu has voted for safety. He backed bills to require speed limiters for repeat offenders and to redesign streets for all users. He pushed for lower drunk driving limits, saying, “The 0.05 BAC simply says, don’t drink and drive.” Liu said. He called for more speed cameras near schools. He fought for a greenway expansion, but Albany said no.

      But the deaths keep coming. The votes are not enough. The street does not wait for laws to catch up.

      What Next?

      Every day of delay is another risk. Call Senator Liu. Call your council member. Demand lower speed limits. Demand more cameras. Demand streets built for people, not just cars. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Take action now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York.
      ▸ Where does SD 16 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 20 and assembly district AD 25.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 16?
      It includes the Murray Hill-Broadway Flushing, East Flushing, Queensboro Hill, Flushing-Willets Point, Kissena Park, Pomonok-Electchester-Hillcrest, Fresh Meadows-Utopia, Mount Hebron & Cedar Grove Cemeteries, Auburndale, and Bayside neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 19, District 20, District 23, and District 24, and Assembly Districts AD 25, AD 26, AD 27, and AD 40.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 16?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 12 deaths, 27 serious injuries, and 194 moderate injuries. Trucks and buses caused 1 death, 1 serious injury, and 18 moderate injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused no deaths, 1 serious injury, and 6 moderate injuries. Bikes caused no deaths or serious injuries, but 8 moderate injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not random accidents. The data shows patterns—speed, vehicle size, and street design all play a role. Prevention is possible.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, redesign streets for safety, and pass laws that hold dangerous drivers accountable.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
