      About these crash totals

      Counts come from NYC police crash reports (NYC Open Data). We sum all crashes, injuries, and deaths for this area across the selected time window shown on the card. Injury severity follows the official definitions in the NYPD dataset.

      • Crashes: number of police‑reported collisions (all road users).
      • All injuries: total injured people in those crashes.
      • Moderate / Serious: subcategories reported by officers (e.g., broken bones vs. life‑threatening trauma).
      • Deaths: people who died due to a crash.

      Notes: Police reports can be corrected after initial publication. Minor incidents without a police report are not included.

      United Nations Ave South, 4:30 PM

      SD 16: Jan 1, 2022 - Sep 10, 2025

      Just after 4:30 PM on Aug 23, on United Nations Avenue South in Flushing Meadows–Corona Park, a driver in a Ford sedan going straight hit a man who was not at an intersection. Police records list driver inattention. He died (city crash data).

      He was one of 38 people killed on the streets of Senate District 16 since Jan 1, 2022 (city data). Through Sep 10 this year, the district has 7 deaths, up from 5 at this point last year — a 40% jump. Crashes are up too: 2,072 this year to date, compared with 1,511 last year to date (district stats).

      “This is what police said about that park road death: ‘A man died after a police officer in an NYPD car ran over him as he lay on a road in Flushing Meadows Corona Park,’” reported Gothamist, noting it happened on United Nations Avenue South around 4:30 PM (Gothamist).

      This week on our streets

      • Aug 23 (United Nations Ave South): a driver in a Ford sedan going straight hit a man not at an intersection; he died (city crash data).
      • Jul 31 (Hollis Court Blvd at 50 Ave): an SUV driver making a left turn hit a 55‑year‑old woman riding an e‑bike going straight; she died (city crash data).

      The pattern does not lift

      Since 2022, police reports here show pedestrians bear the brunt. Records list harm to people on foot involving SUV drivers 416 times, and sedan drivers 330 times. These counts include injuries and deaths (open data).

      Year to date, injuries are 1,244, up from 986 at this point last year. Serious injuries climbed from 21 to 36 (district stats). The numbers move in one direction, and it is not safety.

      The bills that could slow the cars

      State Sen. John Liu co‑sponsored and voted yes in committee for S 4045, a bill to require speed limiters for repeat speeders (bill file). The Senate took steps. The Assembly still has to pass its version.

      City Hall has the tool to lower speeds on local streets. Use it. Our action guide explains how to press leaders to set 20 MPH on residential streets and back speed limiters for repeat offenders (Take Action).

      Hold the line

      The man in the park. The woman on Hollis Court. Two lives in two weeks. The fixes are on the table. Push them across.
