      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 12?

      Astoria SD 12 traffic accidents: Pass S4045, protect lives

      Written August 15, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Edited for clarity and formatting; converted citations to inline links, cleaned and reordered sources, tightened prose, and updated FAQ with linked attributions.)

      SD 12: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 15, 2025

      This week in Astoria

      On Tuesday, three people died at a food truck on 19th Avenue and 42nd Street. “Three people died Tuesday after a driver struck two pedestrians and another car near a food truck in Queens,” police said. A food vendor watched it happen. “The car must have been going 50, 60 miles an hour,” he said. “It was catastrophic. It just wiped out everything,” said Giancarlo Caruso.

      Families learned the names. Police said the pedestrians were 42 and 70. The driver, 84, also died, police said. The corner is quiet again. The gap is not.

      A summer of loss

      The toll in this district since 2022: 35 dead, 6,008 injured, 83 seriously hurt. The numbers keep climbing.

      Last month, a 39-year-old moped rider was killed at 23rd Avenue and 37th Street. In June, a bus killed a 70-year-old man on Woodhaven Boulevard at Hoffman Drive. In January, a 53-year-old woman died at Ditmars Boulevard and 31st Street, struck by a bus. Different vehicles. Same result.

      What leaders did — and didn’t

      State Senator Michael Gianaris co-sponsored and voted yes in committee on S 4045 in June 2025. He pushed the city to open the Queensboro Bridge pedestrian path in April and welcomed its completion in May. Meanwhile, the 114th Precinct area continues to see dangerous high-speed chases, as reported by Streetsblog.

      But the bodies keep coming. Cameras ticket. Tickets pile up. The worst drivers keep driving.

      What needs to happen now

      • Pass S 4045 without delay to stop the super speeders.
      • Lock in permanent walking and biking space like the Queensboro Bridge fix — on every dangerous corridor.

      Call Sen. Gianaris. Call City Hall. Demand safer speeds and protected space. Do it today. See how to act here: Take Action.

      Gianaris Opposes DOT Process Backs Safety Boosting Signals
      ▸

      A hit-and-run killed Be Tran in Queens. State Sen. Michael Gianaris and advocates demand swift action. They want signals, not studies. The city waits for blood before it moves. Residents, tired of carnage, rally for change. The toll mounts.

      On August 17, 2022, State Senator Michael Gianaris called for urgent pedestrian safety improvements after a deadly hit-and-run in Queens. The incident, which killed 74-year-old Be Tran at Myrtle and Seneca Avenues, triggered public outcry. Gianaris criticized the city’s process, saying, 'They have to wait until that happens to get the score high enough to realize that an intersection needs protection.' He joined activists from the Ridgewood Tenants Union and Transportation Alternatives, demanding new signals and better street design. Gianaris said he contacted the Department of Transportation a year earlier with no result. The city claims over 20 traffic control approvals in Ridgewood, but residents and advocates say it’s not enough. The call comes as pedestrian deaths and injuries continue to climb citywide.
