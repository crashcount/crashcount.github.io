      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 12?

      Astoria’s Streets Run Red—Speed Kills, Leaders Stall

      Written August 13, 2025

      SD 12: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 13, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt: The Recent Toll

      Three men died in Astoria last Tuesday. An 84-year-old driver sped through a stop sign at 42nd Street and 19th Avenue, hitting two men standing by a food truck. The car was going about 60 miles an hour. “It was catastrophic. It just wiped out everything. He must have been gassed to the floor.” The force severed a foot. The truck owner survived by a step. “My truck is totaled, but I still have my life. I’m just grateful that something moved me out of the way.”

      In the last 12 months, SD 12 saw 5 deaths and 27 serious injuries on its streets. Over 2,000 people were hurt. The dead include the old and the young. The numbers keep coming. The pain does not end.

      Patterns of Harm: Who Pays the Price

      Cars and SUVs do the most damage. In this district, they killed 5, seriously hurt 12, and injured hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed 5. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1. Bikes killed 1. The bodies pile up, but the story is the same: speed, steel, and a moment’s loss.

      Leadership: What Gianaris Has Done—and What Comes Next

      State Senator Michael Gianaris has moved. He co-sponsored and voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, a bill to force repeat speeders to install speed-limiting devices. He backed dedicated bike and pedestrian paths on the Queensboro Bridge after years of delay. He called for stronger penalties for reckless drivers. But the carnage continues. Promises and votes are not enough. Streets remain deadly.

      What You Can Do

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Senator Gianaris. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph default speed limit. Demand the city and state act now, not after the next body. Every day of delay is another family broken.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature, responsible for passing laws and overseeing state policy. Senators represent districts across New York and vote on bills that affect every resident.
      ▸ Where does SD 12 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Queens, city council district District 26 and assembly district AD 30. Source
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 12?
      It includes the Astoria (North)-Ditmars-Steinway, Astoria (East)-Woodside (North), Sunnyside Yards (North), St. Michael’S Cemetery, Sunnyside, Woodside, Sunnyside Yards (South), Calvary & Mount Zion Cemeteries, Elmhurst, Maspeth, and Queens CB1 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 22, District 25, District 26, and District 30, and Assembly Districts AD 30, AD 34, AD 37, and AD 38. Source
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 12?
      Cars and SUVs killed 5 and seriously injured 12. Trucks and buses killed 5 and seriously injured 2. Motorcycles and mopeds killed 1 and seriously injured 1. Bikes killed 1 and seriously injured 2. Source
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and holding repeat dangerous drivers accountable can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws to lower speed limits, require speed-limiting devices for repeat offenders, and redesign streets to protect people walking and biking.
