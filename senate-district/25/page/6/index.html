      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 25?

      No More Empty Seats: End the Killing on Our Streets

      Written July 18, 2025 (Revised July 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, tightened prose, deduped and sorted citations.)

      SD 25: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 18, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A man tried to cross Broadway at East New York Avenue. A gray Ford hit him and kept going. He died at Brookdale Hospital. The driver vanished into the night. This was July 3rd. It was not the first time. It will not be the last. A hit-and-run driver struck and killed a 36-year-old man crossing a Brooklyn street, officials said Friday.

      Just hours before, a motorcyclist lost control on the Brooklyn-Queens Expressway. He hit the median and died on the road. Police said he was “zipping west” when it happened. A 55-year-old motorcyclist died after crashing on the Brooklyn-Queens Expressway early Thursday, police said.

      In the last 12 months, 12 people have died and 1,582 have been injured in SD 25. Twenty-four were seriously hurt. The dead do not get a second chance.

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Since 2022, there have been 26 deaths and 61 serious injuries on these streets. Most of the dead were walking. Most of the vehicles were cars, SUVs, or trucks. The numbers are not just numbers. They are mothers, sons, neighbors. They are the empty seat at the table.

      Leadership: What Has Been Done

      State Senator Jabari Brisport has not been silent. He co-sponsored the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force repeat speeders to install devices that keep them from breaking the limit. He voted yes to extend school speed zones, making it safer for children to cross the street. He backed automated bike lane enforcement to keep drivers out of the space meant for cyclists. These are steps. They are not enough. The bodies keep coming.

      The Call

      Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call Senator Brisport. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against repeat offenders. Demand streets where a walk to the store is not a gamble with death.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Act now.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 25 in Albany.
      ▸ Where does SD 25 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 36 and assembly district AD 56.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 25?
      It includes the Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Brooklyn Navy Yard, Bedford-Stuyvesant (West), Bedford-Stuyvesant (East), Prospect Heights, Ocean Hill, Brownsville, Brooklyn CB16, and Brooklyn CB2 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 35, District 36, District 37, and District 41, and Assembly Districts AD 52, AD 55, AD 56, and AD 57.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 25?
      Most injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 25 were caused by cars and trucks (10 deaths, 14 serious injuries), followed by motorcycles and mopeds (no pedestrian deaths, 3 serious injuries), and bikes (no pedestrian deaths, 1 serious injury). NYC Open Data crash records
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are not random. Policies like lower speed limits, better street design, and enforcement against repeat offenders can prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and enforce laws for lower speed limits, expand automated enforcement, and invest in safer street designs that protect people walking and biking.

      Head-On Crash Shatters Two Drivers on Blake Avenue
      ▸

      Steel met steel on Blake Avenue. A van and a box truck collided head-on. Two men, both drivers, left injured and in shock. One’s shoulder crushed, the other’s chest battered. The street fell silent. No answers, only pain and twisted metal.

      A van and a box truck collided head-on near Blake Avenue and Strauss Street in Brooklyn. According to the police report, both vehicles were going straight when they struck each other. The 51-year-old van driver suffered a shattered shoulder and crush injuries. The 38-year-old box truck driver sustained internal chest injuries. Both men were strapped in and left in shock. The report lists the contributing factors as 'Unspecified.' No pedestrians or cyclists were involved. The crash left both vehicles with heavy front-end damage. The police report offers no further details on the cause.
