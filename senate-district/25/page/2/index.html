      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 25?

      Blood in Brisport’s District: Streets Still Kill, Action Still Lags

      Written July 12, 2025

      SD 25: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt

      In Senate District 25, the killing does not stop. In the past year, 12 people died in crashes. Another 1,592 were hurt. Twenty-four were left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors. A 36-year-old man, struck crossing Broadway and East New York Avenue, died as the driver sped away. “The driver of the Ford sped off, never stopping, cops said. He remained at large on Saturday.”

      Pedestrians and cyclists pay the price. SUVs and sedans do most of the damage. In three years, cars and trucks killed 10, injured 840, and left 14 with life-altering wounds. Bikes and mopeds are not blameless, but their toll is a fraction. The street is a gauntlet. No one is safe.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      State Senator Jabari Brisport has taken steps. He voted yes on a bill to force safer street design for all users. “The bill’s focus is clear: safer streets for people on foot, on bikes, and in vehicles.” He co-sponsored a bill to use cameras to keep bike lanes clear. He signed on to require every new project with public money to put people first, not cars. These are steps, not leaps. The blood still runs.

      The Cost of Delay

      Every day without change is another day of loss. The numbers do not lie. In the last year, deaths in the district jumped from 1 to 12. Injuries rose by nearly 22%. The city talks of Vision Zero. The street tells another story.

      Call to Action

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call Senator Brisport. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for every person on foot or bike. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 25.
      ▸ Where does SD 25 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 36 and assembly district AD 56.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 25?
      It includes the Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Brooklyn Navy Yard, Bedford-Stuyvesant (West), Bedford-Stuyvesant (East), Prospect Heights, Ocean Hill, Brownsville, Brooklyn CB16, and Brooklyn CB2 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 33, District 35, District 36, District 37, and District 41, and Assembly Districts AD 52, AD 55, AD 56, and AD 57.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 25?
      Cars and trucks killed 10 and injured 887 pedestrians in SD 25 over the past three years. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 3 serious injuries and 0 deaths. Bikes caused 1 serious injury and 0 deaths.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are preventable. Policies like lower speed limits, safer street design, and enforcement save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to reduce traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce laws for safer street design, lower speed limits, and automated enforcement. They can also push for funding and oversight to protect people walking and biking.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
