      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 25?

      Blood on Bedford: The Killings Won’t Stop Themselves

      Written June 19, 2025 (Revised June 20, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      SD 25: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 19, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 25, the numbers do not lie. Eight people killed. Twenty-two left with serious injuries. Over 1,400 hurt. In the last twelve months alone, there were 2,500 crashes. The dead include a pregnant woman, a 55-year-old pedestrian, a 26-year-old passenger, and a 27-year-old motorcyclist. They are not numbers. They are lives ended on the street.

      On May 24, Tiffany Cifuni stepped out of her car in Bedford-Stuyvesant after a minor crash. She never made it home. The driver hit the gas, dragged her under the car, and left her to die. “The defendant floored the gas, instantly striking Tiffany, causing her body to become lodged beneath the vehicle,” said Assistant District Attorney Tara Kelly. The street was quiet again. But the blood stayed.

      The System Responds—Or Doesn’t

      SUVs, sedans, trucks, and buses do most of the killing. In SD 25, cars and trucks caused seven pedestrian deaths. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes added more injuries. The city talks about Vision Zero. The state passes laws. But the carnage does not stop.

      Jabari Brisport has taken steps. He voted yes on a bill to force safer street design for all users, not just drivers. He co-sponsored a bill to use cameras to keep bike lanes clear, so cyclists do not have to swerve into traffic. He backed another bill to require complete street design for any project using public money. These are not empty gestures. They are votes and signatures on the record. But the streets are still not safe.

      The Fight Over Infrastructure

      The city removed a protected bike lane on Bedford Avenue after crashes involving children. The mayor called it a response to community concerns. Council Member Lincoln Restler called it “a purely political decision to rip out a bike lane with no alternative” in an interview. The lawsuits came fast. The danger remains.

      What Now? Demand More

      The bodies keep coming. The promises keep coming. The only thing that stops a car is concrete, not talk. Call your council member. Call your senator. Demand protected bike lanes, lower speed limits, and enforcement that does not blame the dead. Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.
