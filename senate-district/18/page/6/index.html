      End the Excuses—Build the Barriers, Stop the Bloodshed

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and cleaned up citations.)

      SD 18: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll: Lives Lost, Streets Unforgiving

      A man steps into the crosswalk. A truck turns. He does not make it home. In Senate District 18, 20 people have died on the streets since 2022. Seventy more suffered serious injuries—bodies broken, lives changed forever. In the last year alone, three deaths, thirteen left with wounds that will not heal. The youngest lost was a child. The oldest, someone’s grandmother. Cars and trucks did most of the killing—SUVs, vans, dump trucks, box trucks. Each crash leaves behind a pair of shoes, a pool of blood, a family waiting for a call that never comes.

      Broken Promises, Slow Progress

      The city paints bike lanes. Drivers park in them. Cyclists swerve into traffic and die. On Grand Street, the so-called protected lane is a joke. Plastic sticks do not stop a two-ton car. Advocates and electeds, including Julia Salazar, demanded real barriers. “We knew the omission of protective jersey barriers would lead to dangerous conditions back in 2019 and we have witnessed that prediction come tragically true throughout 2021” said local activists and officials. Still, the city drags its feet. The carnage continues.

      What Has Julia Salazar Done?

      Salazar has backed bills for safer streets. She voted yes on a law to force “complete street design”—redesigning roads for people, not just cars (see bill S 9718). She co-sponsored a bill for automated bike lane enforcement, to keep drivers out of the lanes meant for cyclists (see S 5008). She signed on for fare-free buses, a move that could shift more people out of cars (see S 4421). But the deaths keep coming. The bills are not yet law. The barriers are not yet built. The blood is still on the street.

      The Next Step: Demand More, Demand Now

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every delay is another body. Call Julia Salazar. Call the Mayor. Call DOT. Demand real barriers, lower speed limits, and enforcement that protects people, not just paint on pavement. Do not wait for another child’s shoes to be left in the road.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 18 in Albany. NYC Council – Legistar
      ▸ Where does SD 18 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 37 and assembly district AD 53. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 18?
      It includes the South Williamsburg, East Williamsburg, Bushwick (West), Bushwick (East), The Evergreens Cemetery, Cypress Hills, Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (South), Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (North), and Brooklyn CB4 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 32, District 33, District 34, and District 37, and Assembly Districts AD 38, AD 50, AD 53, and AD 54. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 18?
      Most injuries and deaths came from cars and trucks (3 deaths, 164 injuries), followed by motorcycles and mopeds (no deaths, 16 injuries), and bikes (no deaths, 12 injuries). NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These crashes are preventable. Better street design, lower speeds, and real enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and fund laws for safer street design, lower speed limits, real barriers, and automated enforcement that protects people walking and cycling.

      A sedan traveling north on Morgan Avenue near Division Place in Brooklyn struck the rear of a dump truck. According to the police report, the sedan was moving at unsafe speed and disregarded traffic control. The front passenger, age 22, suffered a fatal head injury and died at the scene. Another passenger, age 21, sustained a fractured arm. The sedan was demolished. The police report lists 'Unsafe Speed' and 'Traffic Control Disregarded' as contributing factors. The front passenger’s airbag deployed during the crash. No driver or passenger actions beyond these errors are cited in the report.
