      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 18?

      Broadway Bleeds. Albany Waits.

      Written August 5, 2025 (Revised August 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      SD 18: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 5, 2025

      Blood on Broadway

      Just days ago, a 47-year-old man tried to cross Broadway at Suydam Street. A car hit him, dragged him fifty feet, and left him to die. The driver did not stop. Police are still looking for the car. The man’s name is not yet known. The street is the same as it was the day before. “A driver struck and killed a 47-year-old pedestrian… then left the scene,” police said.

      This is not rare. In the last twelve months, SD 18 saw 12 deaths and 39 serious injuries from crashes. 1886 people were hurt. The dead are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. The street does not care who you are.

      The Toll Grows, the Promises Pile Up

      The violence is steady. Trucks, cars, and SUVs do most of the killing. In three years, cars and SUVs caused 4 deaths and 7 serious injuries to pedestrians. Trucks and buses killed 5. Motorcycles and mopeds, 2 serious injuries, no deaths. Bikes, none. See the city’s own data.

      On Bedford Avenue, the city tried to fix a bike lane. Crashes rose. Officials gave up and put it back the old way. “The redesign was initially intended to improve safety for cyclists, but recent incidents have prompted city officials to revert to the previous layout.”

      What Has Senator Salazar Done?

      State Senator Julia Salazar has backed bills to slow the worst drivers. She co-sponsored and voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force repeat speeders to install devices that keep them from going too fast. She also supported extending school speed zones. She signed on to bills for real bike lane protection and e-bike access. She stood with other lawmakers to defend congestion pricing, a policy proven to cut traffic and save lives. But the killing has not stopped.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Twelve dead. Thirty-nine with life-changing wounds. The street is not safe. The laws are not enough. The time for waiting is over. Call Senator Salazar. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real barriers, not paint. Demand action, not words.

      If you do nothing, nothing will change.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 18 in Albany. See official NY Senate info.
      ▸ Where does SD 18 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 37 and assembly district AD 53. [Source: CrashCount district data]
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 18?
      It includes the Williamsburg, South Williamsburg, East Williamsburg, Bushwick (West), Bushwick (East), The Evergreens Cemetery, Cypress Hills, Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (South), Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (North), and Brooklyn CB4. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 32, District 33, District 34, and District 37, and Assembly Districts AD 38, AD 50, AD 53, and AD 54. [Source: CrashCount district data]
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 18?
      Cars and SUVs killed 4 pedestrians and caused 7 serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed 5. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 2 serious injuries, no deaths. Bikes caused no deaths or serious injuries. See the city’s own data.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or can they be prevented?
      Crashes are not fate. Lower speeds, real barriers, and strict rules for repeat offenders can prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, fund real street redesigns, and support laws that target repeat dangerous drivers.
