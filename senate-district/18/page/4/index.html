      Blood on the Asphalt, Silence in Albany

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed citation formatting, deduped citations.)

      SD 18: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Eight dead. Thirty-two seriously injured. In the past year, Senate District 18 has seen 2,775 crashes. These are not just numbers—they are neighbors, children, elders. A 26-year-old cyclist killed on Evergreen Avenue. A pedestrian crushed by a dump truck on Withers Street. A toddler struck by an e-bike on Bedford Avenue, rushed to Bellevue with internal injuries. The cyclist died. The child survived. The street stayed the same.

      The Human Cost

      “Broken ankle, broken humorous, bunch of fractures in my skull.” That’s how Ruari Fay-Handebeaux described his injuries after a hit-and-run in Bushwick. He was thrown ten feet by a car and left in the intersection. His friend said he “gets absolutely launched, maybe 10 to 15 feet right into the middle of the intersection, and just left there,” as reported by ABC7. The driver fled. The intersection remains unchanged.

      Leadership: Action and Silence

      Senator Julia Salazar has backed bills for safer streets. She voted yes on S 9718, pushing for complete street design. She co-sponsored S 5008, calling for automated bike lane enforcement. She joined calls for concrete barriers on Grand Street and supported rebates for e-bike access. But the deaths keep coming. The bills are not enough until the streets change. Every delay is another body on the pavement.

      What Next: No More Waiting

      The crisis is not fate. It is policy. Call Senator Salazar. Call your council member. Demand hardened bike lanes, lower speed limits, and real enforcement. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York.
      ▸ Where does SD 18 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 37 and assembly district AD 53.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 18?
      It includes the South Williamsburg, East Williamsburg, Bushwick (West), Bushwick (East), The Evergreens Cemetery, Cypress Hills, Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (South), Highland Park-Cypress Hills Cemeteries (North), and Brooklyn CB4 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 32, District 33, District 34, and District 37, and Assembly Districts AD 38, AD 50, AD 53, and AD 54.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 18?
      Cars and trucks were involved in 7 deaths and 164 injuries; motorcycles and mopeds in 0 deaths and 16 injuries; bikes in 0 deaths and 11 injuries. See NYC Open Data.
      ▸ Are crashes just 'accidents' or are they preventable?
      Crashes are preventable. Street design, speed limits, and enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and fund laws for safer street design, lower speed limits, and real enforcement. They can push for hardened bike lanes and automated safety cameras.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
