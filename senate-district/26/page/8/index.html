      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 26?

      Third Avenue took two lives. The count keeps climbing.

      Written August 31, 2025 (Revised August 31, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 26: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 31, 2025

      Just after midnight on Jul 11, 2025, a BMW going south on 3rd Avenue hit two men in the crosswalk at 52nd Street. Both died at the scene. The police noted a red light run and unsafe speed. They had the walk signal. NYC Open Data (CrashID 4826750).

      On Aug 27, an off-duty NYPD officer riding on the BQE near the Atlantic Avenue exit was struck by a box truck. His motorcycle hit a wall and burned. The truck driver left. Police later arrested Carlos Almanzar Toribio and charged him with leaving the scene of a crash that caused death. NY Daily News, ABC7.

      These are two names in a longer list. Since Jan 1, 2022, SD 26 has logged 11,689 crashes, 5,963 injuries, and 36 deaths. Year to date, crashes are up 23.1% over last year; six people are dead so far, compared with seven at this point in 2024. Source: CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data.

      Third Avenue isn’t a highway

      Two dead at 52nd Street is not an outlier. The corridor has a record of high-speed harm. After another deadly hit-and-run, local officials and neighbors begged City Hall to stop delaying a DOT safety plan for Third Avenue. “This is not meant to be a highway. This is where people walk. This is where people live. This is where kids go to school,” said State Sen. Andrew Gounardes at 60th Street. Brooklyn Paper.

      A day later, they gathered again. “Here we are, once again gathering to mourn another preventable tragedy on our streets. But it doesn’t have to be this way,” Gounardes said. The plan has been stalled for two years. BKReader.

      The pattern holds

      The July 11 double fatal crash lists “Traffic Control Disregarded” and “Unsafe Speed.” The victims had the signal. NYC Open Data (CrashID 4826750).

      Speed shows up elsewhere in the record. In SD 26 since 2022, cars and SUVs striking people on foot account for the largest share of pedestrian harm counted in the dataset, with 320 injuries and 4 deaths from sedans and 284 injuries and 3 deaths from SUVs. Trucks add 74 injuries and 3 deaths. Source: CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data and Vehicles.

      The numbers climb. Year to date, SD 26 has 2,430 crashes, up from 1,974 at this point last year. Injuries are up too, 1,319 this year versus 1,089 last year. Source: CrashCount analysis of NYC Open Data.

      The levers on the table

      Albany renewed the school‑zone speed camera program through 2030. Sen. Gounardes sponsored the bill. Streetsblog, AMNY.

      Gounardes also sponsors the Stop Super Speeders Act (S 4045), which would force repeat speeders to install speed‑limiting devices. He voted yes in committee in June. Open States.

      Citywide, there is another tool: lower the default speed limit using home‑rule authority already in place. The city can set safer speeds on residential streets. Our action guide lays out the steps to press City Hall. CrashCount: Take Action.

      The Third Avenue plan waits. The bodies do not. “This is where people walk.” Brooklyn Paper.

      ▸ How were these numbers calculated?
      We used NYC Open Data’s Motor Vehicle Collisions datasets: Crashes (h9gi‑nx95), Persons (f55k‑p6yu), and Vehicles (bm4k‑52h4). We filtered records to Senate District 26 and the period Jan 1, 2022–Aug 31, 2025, then counted crashes, injuries, deaths, and year‑to‑date changes. Pedestrian harm by vehicle type was computed by joining Persons (role = pedestrian; injury severity) to Vehicles (normalized vehicle categories). Data were accessed Aug 31, 2025. A reproducible filtered query is provided here.

The bill does not mandate, but allows, lower limits where carnage is highest. The council’s action centers the lives of pedestrians and cyclists, demanding the city act before more are lost.
