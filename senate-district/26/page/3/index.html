      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 26?

      How Many Empty Chairs Before Albany Acts?

      Written June 26, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 26: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 26, 2025

      The Toll in SD 26: Lives Lost, Families Broken

      The streets do not forgive. In the last twelve months, 8 people died and 1,618 were injured in Senate District 26. Thirteen suffered wounds so grave they may never heal. The dead include children, elders, riders, walkers. No one is spared.

      On June 21, a 22-year-old man rode south on Third Avenue. He never made it home. His moped struck the side of an Acura at 67th Street. The driver was drunk and unlicensed. Joel Mota died in the hospital. His passenger left with broken bones. His brother said, “He was a hardworking man. He had a lot of love for his family. He was always passionate about the things that he did.” NY Daily News.

      Unlicensed, impaired, and still behind the wheel. The city counts the bodies. The rest of us count the empty chairs.

      Who Pays? The Most Vulnerable

      Cars and SUVs killed 5. Trucks killed 2. Bikes and mopeds, too, left their mark. The numbers are cold. The pain is not. In this district, cars and trucks caused 5 deaths and 17 serious injuries to pedestrians. Motorcycles and mopeds: no deaths, but 35 injuries. Bikes: no deaths, 68 injuries. The street is a gauntlet. The weak pay the price.

      Leadership: Action and Delay

      State Senator Andrew Gounardes has fought for speed cameras. He called them “life-saving” and said they “make our streets safer for everyone” Streetsblog NYC. He voted yes on bills to redesign streets and cut driving. He sponsored a bill to crack down on drivers who hide their plates. But the biggest bill—to force repeat speeders to slow down—died in Albany. Gounardes said he would “keep fighting” Streetsblog NYC. The streets do not wait.

      The Call: Demand More, Demand Now

      The blood on the street is not an accident. It is policy, inertia, delay. Call your council member. Call your senator. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement. Demand streets built for people, not for cars. Do not wait for another name to be carved into stone. Take action now.

      Gounardes Supports Safety Boosting Penalties for Obstructed Plates
      ▸

      Council backs tougher fines for drivers who hide plates. Obscured tags let reckless motorists dodge cameras and tickets. The problem grows. Lawmakers want stiffer penalties to stop the evasion and protect people on city streets.

      Resolution 0467-2024 sits in the Committee on Public Safety. Introduced June 20, 2024, it urges Albany to pass S.2447/A.5234. The measure’s title: 'increase the penalties for purposefully obstructed license plates.' Council Members Justin L. Brannan (primary sponsor, District 47) and Robert F. Holden (co-sponsor, District 30) lead the push. The bill targets drivers who cover or deface plates to evade speed, red-light, and bus-lane cameras. Data shows unreadable plates rose from 3.98% to 4.66% in one year. The city lost up to $38.7 million in unissued tickets between 2019 and 2022. The resolution calls for higher fines, confiscation of plate coverings, and registration suspensions. Lawmakers say this crackdown is needed to keep dangerous drivers from slipping through the cracks and to make streets safer for everyone.
