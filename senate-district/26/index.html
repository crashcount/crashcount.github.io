      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 26?

      Seven Dead, Sixteen Hundred Broken—Who’s Slowing the Killers in Senate District 26?

      Written June 30, 2025

      SD 26: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 30, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In Senate District 26, the streets do not forgive. In the last twelve months, 7 people died and 1,611 were injured in 2,900 crashes. Fourteen of those injuries were serious. The dead include the young and the old. A 22-year-old moped rider, Joel Mota, died at Third Avenue and 67th Street. His brother remembered him simply: “He was a hardworking man. He had a lot of love for his family. He was always passionate about the things that he did.” NY Daily News.

      A woman was killed on State Street in February. A 73-year-old man died behind the wheel on the BQE in January. The list goes on. Every week, another family gets the call.

      The Usual Suspects

      The killers are not mysteries. Cars and trucks caused the most harm: 5 deaths and 18 serious injuries to pedestrians. Motorcycles and mopeds left 35 people hurt, 0 dead. Bikes injured 68, with 3 serious injuries, but no deaths. The pattern is clear. The danger comes on four wheels, fast and heavy.

      Leadership: Progress and Delay

      Senator Andrew Gounardes has pushed for change. He sponsored a bill to let speed cameras catch drivers who hide their plates and to extend enforcement in school zones sponsored a bill. He voted yes on a bill to redesign streets for safety voted yes. He backed the renewal of the city’s speed camera program, calling it a way to “save lives and make our streets safer for everyone” backed the renewal.

      But Albany failed to pass the Stop Super Speeders Act. The bill would have forced the worst repeat offenders to slow down. Instead, it died in committee. As one advocate put it, “Our elected officials in Albany have failed the livable streets movement again.”

      The Call

      The bodies keep coming. The policies move slow.

      Call Senator Gounardes. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real consequences for repeat speeders. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Cars and Trucks caused 5 deaths and 18 serious injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds caused 0 deaths and 0 serious injuries. Bikes caused 0 deaths and 3 serious injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The patterns are clear and preventable. Speed, heavy vehicles, and repeat offenders drive the toll.

      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, expand automated enforcement, redesign streets for safety, and ensure real consequences for repeat dangerous drivers.
