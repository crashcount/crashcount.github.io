      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 23?

      Bay Street Bleeds—And Albany Lets It Happen

      Written July 12, 2025

      SD 23: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A man on a motorcycle died on Bay Street last week. He hit the side of a Toyota making a K-turn. The crash happened in the afternoon, in plain sight. The rider, Jeremy Claudio, was thirty-four. He died at the hospital. The driver stayed at the scene. There were no arrests. The NYPD is still investigating. As reported by amNY, “A motorcyclist was killed after colliding with a driver awkwardly turning their car on Staten Island during the Fourth of July holiday weekend.”

      This is not rare. In the past twelve months, SD 23 saw 6 deaths and 1,569 injuries in traffic crashes. Thirteen people were seriously hurt. The old and the young are not spared. Two people over 75 died. One under 18 died. The numbers do not stop. They only grow.

      The Machines That Kill

      Cars and SUVs did most of the damage. They killed 8 people and hurt hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed 3. Motorcycles and mopeds, 0. Bikes, 0. The street is not safe for anyone on foot or on two wheels. The numbers are not just numbers. They are lives. They are families left with empty chairs.

      Leadership: Action and Inaction

      State Senator Jessica Scarcella-Spanton voted for safer street design last year. She backed a bill to use “complete street design principles” to protect all users, not just drivers. The bill passed. But when it mattered most, she voted against renewing New York City’s speed camera program—a tool proven to save lives. She was one of only three city senators to vote no. Cameras catch the drivers who speed past schools and kill. The vote was a choice. The cost is paid by the people outside the cars.

      The Call

      Every crash is preventable. Every delay is a choice. Call Senator Scarcella-Spanton. Tell her to stand with the dead and the living. Demand she support speed cameras, lower speed limits, and real protection for people who walk and ride. The blood on Bay Street is not washed away by silence.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York.
      ▸ Where does SD 23 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Staten Island, city council district District 49 and assembly district AD 63. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 23?
      It includes the Fort Hamilton, Gravesend (South), Coney Island-Sea Gate, Brighton Beach, Calvert Vaux Park, Sheepshead Bay-Manhattan Beach-Gerritsen Beach, St. George-New Brighton, Tompkinsville-Stapleton-Clifton-Fox Hills, Rosebank-Shore Acres-Park Hill, West New Brighton-Silver Lake-Grymes Hill, and Brooklyn CB15 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 47, District 48, District 49, and District 50, and Assembly Districts AD 45, AD 46, AD 61, AD 63, and AD 64. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 23?
      Cars and trucks killed 11 people and caused hundreds of injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused no pedestrian deaths. Bikes caused no pedestrian deaths. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Every crash is preventable. Speed, street design, and enforcement choices decide who lives and who dies.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can vote for speed cameras, lower speed limits, and street designs that protect people outside cars. They can refuse to delay or weaken proven safety measures.
