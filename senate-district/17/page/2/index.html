      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 17?

      Senator Chan: Insurance Won’t Save Pedestrians—Fix the Streets Now

      Written July 7, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 17: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 7, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In Senate District 17, the bodies do not stop. Three people killed and 925 injured in crashes in the last year alone. The dead are not numbers. They are elders, children, workers. In January, a 95-year-old woman, Mayya Gil, was crossing Cropsey Avenue with her home health aide. A van turned left. Gil died. The aide survived. Her daughter described her as “a very active lady.”

      Just days later, another crash. A transport van in Bath Beach swerved and slammed into a tree. Fourteen people hurt, most with special needs. A bystander said, “It’s sad because they definitely need a lot of attention more than anyone else.”

      In the last twelve months, SD 17 has seen 1,450 crashes, 3 deaths, and 7 serious injuries.

      The Vehicles That Kill

      SUVs and cars do most of the damage. In three years, they killed six and injured hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed two. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes killed two more. The pattern is clear. The streets are not safe for the old, the young, or anyone on foot.

      What Has Senator Steve Chan Done?

      Senator Chan has spoken up for some reforms. He supported calls to fix deadly arterial roads and back urban street design standards. But when the Senate passed a bill to require delivery apps to provide insurance for workers and pedestrians, Chan supported it. Insurance pays after the crash. It does not stop the crash. Safety analysts warn: “Focusing on insurance rather than speed limits shifts responsibility away from prevention and system design, failing to reduce crash risk and potentially discouraging mode shift by not addressing the root causes of danger for pedestrians and cyclists.” (Streetsblog NYC)

      The silence is louder than the action. No bill to slow cars. No push for street redesign. No demand for real accountability for repeat offenders.

      The Call: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      Contact Senator Chan. Demand he fight for lower speed limits, street redesign, and enforcement against repeat offenders. Insurance is not enough. The next crash is coming. Do not wait until it is your family.

      Steve Chan Supports Safety Boosting Urban Arterial Reforms
      ▸

      Arterial roads kill. They are wide, fast, and deadly for walkers and cyclists. Most are state-owned. Cities and advocates demand lower speeds, urban design, and local control. Changing these streets is urgent. Lives hang in the balance.

      This policy advocacy statement, published October 18, 2022, by Streetsblog NYC, highlights the urgent need to fix deadly arterial roads. The statement, titled 'Vision Zero Cities: How to Fix Our Most Dangerous Roads,' calls for context-sensitive speed limits, urban street design standards, and transferring state-owned roads to city control. Streetsblog and Transportation Alternatives urge, 'Cities and advocates should push their states to transform arterial streets into safe spaces for all modes.' The statement notes that arterials make up 15 percent of roads but see 67 percent of pedestrian deaths. It stresses that speed is the main factor in fatal crashes and that state DOTs often prioritize fast driving over safety. The call is clear: redesign streets for people, not cars, and give cities the power to act.
