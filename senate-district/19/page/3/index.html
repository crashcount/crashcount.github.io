      Gateway lot, crosswalk, parkway: five dead in SD 19

      Written August 28, 2025 (Revised August 28, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 28, 2025

      SD 19, Jan to Aug 2025. Five dead. No storm. Just cars.

      • Jan 24, a 57‑year‑old woman crossed with the signal at Pennsylvania and Blake. An SUV turned left and hit her. Police cited failure to yield and distraction in the official record. She died. City data shows it plain.
      • Feb 16, a 41‑year‑old driver on the Belt Parkway was ejected and killed. The report lists unsafe speed on a slippery surface. The BMW was “demolished.” The dataset records the words.
      • Apr 29, Seaview at Pennsylvania. A 28‑year‑old driver died in a crash between a pickup and a Porsche SUV. The city log says “Apparent Death.”
      • May 14, 6 PM hour, Rutland and E 95th. A 68‑year‑old woman was crossing with the signal. A Mercedes sedan turned left and struck her. The file marks “Driver Inattention/Distraction.” She died. The record is blunt.
      • Jul 13, about 12:30 AM, Gateway Center lot. A Genesis sedan doing donuts lost control and hit 21‑year‑old Madisyn Ruiz, who was sitting near the curb. She died at the hospital. Police charged the driver, Zachary Cando, with criminally negligent homicide, reckless endangerment and reckless driving, according to both Daily News and the Post. “He lost control when he was doing donuts with the vehicle,” the papers quote from court records. Days later, reporters noted the circular tire marks still on the asphalt. Daily News.

      Speed. Left turns. No second chances.

      In twelve months, SD 19 logged 11 deaths and 2,975 injuries across 4,135 crashes. That is the last year on record, ending Aug 28, 2025. Our pull of city data. Year to date, deaths and serious injuries are up over last year in this district. Crashes are up too. The numbers do not soften.

      Police say “no criminality” in many deaths. They said it after a man fell to the tracks at Sutter Ave on Aug 10. “EMS pronounced the victim dead at the scene,” said FDNY. Daily News.

      The road toll keeps its own kind of time.

      —

      The left turn that ends a life

      Pennsylvania and Blake. Crosswalk. Morning. A woman with the light. The SUV cuts left. The file lists “Failure to Yield.” She never made the far curb. City data.

      Rutland and E 95th. Evening light. Another left. Another woman killed. The note is the same: a turn, and then a body. Collision record.

      The mall lot spinout

      Gateway Center, after midnight. A car loops tight circles. The skid marks are still there days later. The woman on the curb does not stand up. Police make an arrest. Charges follow. Daily News. Post.

      “Criminal charges for him were still pending,” another outlet wrote of a separate moped fatal in Brooklyn that same week. The line fits too many cases. Gothamist.

      The highway that does not forgive

      The Belt at 4 AM hour. The pavement slick. A car at unsafe speed. The driver is thrown and dies. The form says “Demolished.” Crash file.

      Seaview and Pennsylvania. Two big vehicles. Night. A 28‑year‑old does not go home. City record.

      —

      The numbers pile up

      In SD 19 this year, crashes rose more than 21% over last year’s pace. Deaths doubled. Serious injuries nearly tripled. That is the ledger through late August. District stats.

      Citywide policy moved a little. Albany renewed 24‑hour school‑zone speed cameras through 2030. That keeps watch. The Senate also advanced a bill to fit repeat speeders with intelligent speed assistance after a pattern of violations. Senator Roxanne Persaud voted yes in committee on May 20 and Jun 12. Open States S4045.

      The city now has the power, under Sammy’s Law, to lower speeds on local streets. Use it. Slow the default. Break the chain. See how to press your leaders on our Take Action page.
