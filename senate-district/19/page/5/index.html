      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 19?

      Blood on the Asphalt: SD19’s Deadly Speed Crisis Demands Action

      Written August 6, 2025

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 6, 2025

      The Toll in SD 19

      The numbers do not lie. Since January 2022, 28 people have died and 74 have been seriously injured on the streets of Senate District 19. More than 8,300 have been hurt. The dead include children, elders, and people just trying to cross the street. The living carry scars you cannot see.

      Just last month, a 21-year-old woman was killed in the Gateway Center parking lot. She was sitting on a curb. Her boyfriend lost control of his car while doing donuts. Police charged him with negligent homicide. Days later, the tire marks were still there. “Next thing I know, he was into the fence. Absolutely crazy,” said a witness at another crash scene.

      In May, a 68-year-old woman was crossing with the signal at Rutland Road and E 95th Street. A left-turning sedan struck and killed her. She had the light. She did not make it home. The car kept going. The city kept moving.

      Who Pays the Price

      Most victims are not in cars. They are on foot, on bikes, or waiting for the light to change. Cars and SUVs are the main killers—15 deaths and over 1,000 injuries. Trucks and buses killed one, injuring dozens more. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes left their own trail of broken bodies. No helmet, no crosswalk, no prayer can stop a two-ton machine moving too fast.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      State Senator Roxanne Persaud has voted yes on bills to curb repeat speeders and extend school speed zones. She backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force the worst offenders to install speed-limiting devices. She voted to create more safety zones and raise penalties for endangering highway workers. These are steps. But the blood on the street says it is not enough.

      The carnage continues. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not done so. The most dangerous drivers are still behind the wheel. The next crash is already on its way.

      Call to Action

      Call Senator Persaud. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against repeat speeders. Do not wait for another name on the list. Do not wait for another family to grieve. The road will not forgive delay.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts like SD 19 in Albany.
      ▸ Where does SD 19 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 46 and assembly district AD 60.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 19?
      It includes the East New York (North), East New York-New Lots, Spring Creek-Starrett City, East New York-City Line, Brownsville, East Flatbush-Remsen Village, Canarsie, Mcguire Fields, Canarsie Park & Pier, Barren Island-Floyd Bennett Field, and Brooklyn CB16 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 32, District 37, District 42, and District 46, and Assembly Districts AD 23, AD 54, AD 58, AD 59, and AD 60.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 19?
      Cars and SUVs caused 15 deaths and over 1,000 injuries. Trucks and buses caused 1 death and 78 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 16 injuries. Bikes caused 13 injuries. The largest share of harm comes from cars and SUVs.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and real enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, pass laws to rein in repeat speeders, fund safer street designs, and demand real enforcement that protects people outside cars.
