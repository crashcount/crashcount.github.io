      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 19?

      East New York to Canarsie: The killing keeps coming

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another driver. Same ending.

      • On January 24, at Pennsylvania and Blake, a 57‑year‑old woman crossed with the signal. An SUV turned left and killed her. Police logged failure to yield and distraction. City data lists her as killed, the SUV’s right front bumper damaged, the driver licensed. CrashID 4788343.
      • On May 14, at Rutland and E 95th, a 68‑year‑old woman crossed with the signal. A sedan turned left and killed her. Police cited inattention. CrashID 4812813.

      The corner takes the hit. The walker takes the blow. The light is not a shield.

      Speed on the straightaway. Blood on the road.

      • February 16, Belt Parkway: a 41‑year‑old BMW driver was ejected and died. Unsafe speed. Pavement slippery. Vehicle demolished. CrashID 4793436.
      • April 29, Seaview and Pennsylvania: a 28‑year‑old driver died after an SUV and a pickup collided. Police noted inattention and unsafe speed. One driver unlicensed. CrashID 4816372.

      The numbers don’t blink. In the last 12 months in this district: 11 deaths, 2,961 injuries, 4,111 crashes. Year‑to‑date deaths doubled over last year. Serious injuries are up 150%. District stats.

      A parking lot is not a track.

      • July 13, Gateway Center lot: police say a 24‑year‑old driver was “doing donuts,” lost control, and struck 21‑year‑old Madisyn Ruiz, killing her. He was charged with criminally negligent homicide, reckless endangerment, and reckless driving. “He lost control when he was doing donuts with the vehicle,” court documents quote him saying. NY Daily News | New York Post.

      Days later, reporters saw circular tire marks still cut into the asphalt. NY Daily News.

      Three corners. One fix.

      Left turns killed women in the crosswalk on Pennsylvania at Blake and on Rutland at E 95th. They crossed with the light. The vehicles turned and struck them. CrashID 4788343 | CrashID 4812813.

      Drivers speeding killed on the Belt and on Seaview. Police flagged unsafe speed each time. CrashID 4793436 | CrashID 4816372.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      Albany renewed 24‑hour school‑zone speed cameras through 2030. Your senator backed it. Senate votes.

      The Senate also advanced a bill to force speed‑limiting tech on repeat violators. Senator Persaud voted yes in committee. S 4045.

      City Hall can go further. Lower the default speed. Force limiters on repeat speeders. These are not theories. They are tools on the table.

      One more thing.

      If you want this to stop, say so. Tell City Hall and Albany to use the tools they have. Start here: Take Action.
