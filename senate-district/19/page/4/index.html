      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 19?

      Senator Persaud Acts, But the Blood Still Runs

      Written July 16, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Fixed citation formatting, removed duplicate headline, and clarified vehicle fatality data in FAQ.)

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 16, 2025

      The Death Toll Grows

      In Senate District 19, the road does not forgive. Since 2022, 25 people have died in crashes. 71 more suffered serious injuries. The wounded are not numbers. They are children, elders, workers, neighbors. In the last twelve months alone, 8 lives were lost and 26 people were left with injuries that will not heal (NYC crash data).

      Last month, a 21-year-old woman sat by the curb outside Gateway Plaza. Her boyfriend spun his car in circles. He lost control. She died there. Her family mourns. “It’s devastating. It’s affecting everyone in our family, especially (Ruiz’s) mom. Maddy was her only daughter,” said her sister-in-law. Days later, the tire marks still scarred the lot.

      A month before, a man delivering groceries on Lincoln Avenue was beaten with a pipe after a minor collision. He was left bleeding in the street. “The suspect opened his hatchback, pulled out a pipe, and lunged at the victim, striking him repeatedly in the head and body,” reported police.

      The Pattern: Cars and SUVs Kill Most

      Cars and SUVs do the most harm. In this district, they killed 14 people and injured over a thousand. Trucks and buses killed one. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes together killed none, but left some injured. The street is not safe for the unprotected. The numbers do not lie (NYC crash data).

      What Has Senator Persaud Done?

      State Senator Roxanne Persaud has voted for safety. She backed the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force repeat speeders to install devices that keep them from breaking the limit (Stop Super Speeders Act). She voted to extend school speed zones and create more safety zones (school speed zones). These are steps forward. But the deaths keep coming. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not done so. The most dangerous drivers still roam free.

      The Next Step: Demand More

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every death is a choice made by leaders who act or delay. Call Senator Persaud. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action against repeat speeders. Do not wait for another family to grieve. The road will not wait.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper house of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and oversees state policy for all New Yorkers.
      ▸ Where does SD 19 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 46 and assembly district AD 60.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 19?
      It includes the East New York (North), East New York-New Lots, Spring Creek-Starrett City, East New York-City Line, Brownsville, East Flatbush-Remsen Village, Canarsie, Mcguire Fields, Canarsie Park & Pier, Barren Island-Floyd Bennett Field, and Brooklyn CB16 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 32, District 37, District 42, and District 46, and Assembly Districts AD 23, AD 54, AD 58, AD 59, and AD 60.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 19?
      Cars and SUVs killed 14 people and injured hundreds in SD 19. Trucks and buses killed one. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes caused no deaths but did cause injuries (NYC crash data).
      ▸ Are these crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      These deaths and injuries are preventable. Policies like lower speed limits and action against repeat speeders can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can lower speed limits, target repeat dangerous drivers, and invest in safer street design. Every delay costs lives.
