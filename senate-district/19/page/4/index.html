      SD 19 Traffic Accidents Surge: Street Safety SD 19 Needs Action Now

      Written August 15, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Edited body and FAQ for clarity; standardized inline links; cleaned and reordered citations.)

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 15, 2025

      This week in East New York

      A man fell onto the southbound tracks at the Sutter Ave L station and was struck by a train, police said. “EMS pronounced the victim dead at the scene,” an FDNY spokesman said.

      Last month in a parking lot

      After midnight at Gateway Center, a driver did donuts, lost control, and struck 21-year-old Madisyn Ruiz. “He lost control when he was doing donuts with the vehicle,” Zachary Cando said in court papers. A relative said, “I have no idea why he was doing donuts in the parking lot.”

      The toll in SD 19

      The count keeps rising. In the last 12 months: 11 people killed, 27 seriously hurt, 2,920 injured, across 4,066 crashes. Year to date, deaths doubled in SD 19: 6 this year vs. 3 last year.

      What your senator did

      State Senator Roxanne Persaud voted yes in committee on S 4045 on May 20 and June 12. The bill targets repeat speed violations. Credit for the votes. But people are still dying.

      What must change now

      The pattern is consistent block to block: left turns that don’t yield, speed that doesn’t slow, eyes off the road. A woman crossing with the signal. A young woman in a parking lot. A driver on the parkway. The ending is the same. If we slow cars, we save people. Push Albany to finish S 4045. Demand the city lower speeds and fix deadly turns and lots.

      Call today. Then call again. Start here: Take Action.
