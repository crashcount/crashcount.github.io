      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 19?

      Blood on the Asphalt: Speed Kills, Inaction Buries

      Written July 13, 2025

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 13, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Bone

      In Senate District 19, the road takes its due. Since 2022, 25 people have died in crashes. Seventy-one suffered serious injuries. These are not just numbers—they are mothers, sons, neighbors. In the last twelve months alone, eight more lives ended on these streets. Two were seniors. One was a child. The violence does not stop.

      Just weeks ago, a 68-year-old woman was killed crossing Rutland Road at E. 95th Street. She had the walk signal. The driver did not wait. “I heard the van run over her and her bones crunching,” said Lorna Johnson. The city moved on. The family did not.

      On Lincoln Avenue, a grocery delivery worker was beaten with a pipe after a minor crash. He was left on the sidewalk, blood on his shirt, groceries scattered. “The suspect opened his hatchback, pulled out a pipe, and lunged at the victim, striking him repeatedly,” reported the Daily News. The attacker fled. The worker went to the hospital.

      Who Pays the Price?

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. Fourteen deaths and hundreds of injuries came from cars and trucks. Motorcycles, mopeds, bikes, and buses add to the toll. Pedestrians die crossing with the light. Children are struck in crosswalks. The violence is not random. It follows the same lines, the same roads, the same mistakes.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      State Senator Roxanne Persaud has voted for bills to curb repeat speeders, expand speed cameras, and protect highway workers. She voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, which targets drivers who rack up tickets and points in committee. She backed more speed cameras near schools. These are steps. But the blood keeps flowing. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not. The most dangerous drivers still roam free.

      Act Now—Or Count More Dead

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Call Senator Persaud. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action against repeat offenders. Do not wait for another name on the list. The road will not wait.

      Every day of delay is another day of loss.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York.
      ▸ Where does SD 19 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, city council district District 46 and assembly district AD 60.
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 19?
      It includes the East New York (North), East New York-New Lots, Spring Creek-Starrett City, East New York-City Line, Brownsville, East Flatbush-Remsen Village, Canarsie, Mcguire Fields, Canarsie Park & Pier, Barren Island-Floyd Bennett Field, and Brooklyn CB16 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 32, District 37, District 42, and District 46, and Assembly Districts AD 23, AD 54, AD 58, AD 59, and AD 60.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 19?
      Cars and trucks caused 15 deaths and over 1,100 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 1 death and 15 injuries. Bikes caused no deaths and 13 injuries. Buses caused no deaths and 22 injuries.
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, safer street design, and action against repeat offenders can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, expand speed cameras, pass laws targeting repeat dangerous drivers, and push for safer street designs.

      S 4045
      Persaud votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Senate passes S 4045. Drivers with too many points or camera tickets must install speed assistance tech. Law targets repeat speeders. Streets may get safer for people on foot and bike.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on May 20, June 11, and June 12, 2025. The bill requires drivers who rack up eleven or more points in two years, or six speed/red light camera tickets in one year, to install intelligent speed assistance devices. The bill summary reads: 'Requires the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices if a driver accumulates eleven or more points on their driving record during a 24 month period, or receives 6 speed camera or red light camera tickets during a twelve month period.' Gounardes and colleagues pushed for action. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving and protect vulnerable road users.
