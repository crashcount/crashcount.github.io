      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 19?

      Seven Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Persaud’s District Still Bleeds for Safe Streets

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      SD 19: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      Seven dead. Nineteen seriously hurt. Over 2,300 injured. That’s just the last twelve months in Senate District 19. The dead include the old, the young, and the nameless. A 68-year-old woman, Maurette Lafleur, was crossing with the light in East Flatbush. She never made it to the other side. “The lady spin around and sped through,” said a witness. The bones broke. The city moved on.

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. In the last three years, cars and SUVs took 14 lives and left 16 with serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed one. Bikes, none. The numbers are cold. The bodies are not.

      The Crashes Keep Coming

      The streets are not safe. In May, a 55-year-old man was killed crossing Fulton Street in Cypress Hills. The driver fled. The man died in the hospital. The car kept going. Police are still looking. The story is always the same. “Investigators say a burgundy Ford Explorer with Pennsylvania license plates struck and killed the 55-year-old man… The vehicle did not remain on the scene,” reported ABC7.

      What Has Roxanne Persaud Done?

      Senator Persaud has voted for safety. She backed speed limiters for repeat offenders and complete street design. She voted yes on bills for school zone speed cameras and safety zones. These are steps. But the blood on the street says it’s not enough. The deaths keep coming. The injuries keep coming. The work is not done.

      What Next? Demand More

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call Senator Persaud. Call your council member. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand more speed cameras. Demand streets built for people, not for cars. Don’t wait for another name on the list. Take action now.

      S 4045
      Persaud votes yes to require speed limiters, boosting street safety.
      ▸

      Senate passes S 4045. Drivers with too many points or camera tickets must install speed assistance devices. Lawmakers move to curb reckless driving. The bill targets repeat offenders. The aim: fewer crashes, fewer deaths. Streets demand action.

      Bill S 4045, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' advanced in the Senate on May 20, 2025, following a committee vote. The bill requires drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red-light camera tickets in twelve months, to install intelligent speed assistance devices. Primary sponsor Andrew Gounardes led the push, joined by co-sponsors Samra Brouk, Cordell Cleare, and others. The vote passed with support from senators including Jeremy Cooney, Pete Harckham, and Jessica Ramos. The measure aims to rein in repeat offenders and reduce deadly speeding. The bill’s language is blunt: 'Requires the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices if a driver accumulates eleven or more points on their driving record during a 24 month period, or receives 6 speed camera or red light camera tickets during a twelve month period.' No safety analyst note was provided.
