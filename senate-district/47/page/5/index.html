      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 47?

      Six Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Hoylman’s District Bleeds While Streets Stay Deadly

      Written July 12, 2025

      SD 47: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 12, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      Six dead. Twenty seriously hurt. In the last year alone, 881 people in Senate District 47 were injured in crashes. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care about the weather or the hour. They do not care if you are young or old. A 69-year-old woman, crossing with the light at Amsterdam and 96th, was killed by a turning SUV. She had the right of way. She is gone. See the NYC Open Data crash records.

      On April 24, a 74-year-old man riding his bike was killed at West End Avenue and 70th. A bus and a bicycle. The bus was parked. The man died. No warning. No second chance. See the NYC Open Data crash records.

      The System Fails the Vulnerable

      Most of the dead and injured are not behind the wheel. They are walking, riding, crossing, living. The streets are not safe. Trucks, SUVs, taxis, bikes—all can kill. But the biggest toll comes from cars and trucks: six deaths, sixteen serious injuries, 580 crashes in the last three years. Bikes and mopeds hurt too, but the numbers are smaller. The pain is not.

      What Has Been Done—and What Has Not

      Senator Brad Hoylman-Sigal has voted for safer street design and supported bills for lower speed limits and better enforcement. He backed the law that lets New York City set its own speed limits. He voted yes on the complete streets bill. He supported more speed cameras and called for camera enforcement of blocked bike lanes. But the pace is slow. The danger does not wait.

      The city and state talk about Vision Zero. But the bodies keep coming. “One bus made contact with the rear of the other bus,” said an FDNY chief after a crash that injured fourteen at Port Authority. “It required some extrication, meaning they had to use some equipment to free the patient.”

      The Call to Action

      Every day of delay is another day of risk. Call your council member. Call your senator. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand enforcement that targets the real danger—speed, reckless driving, blocked bike lanes. Join the fight. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Take action now.

      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 47?
      Cars and trucks caused 6 deaths and 16 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 3 serious injuries. Bikes caused 5 serious injuries but no deaths. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes are preventable with better street design, lower speeds, and enforcement that targets dangerous driving. NYC Open Data

      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, expand automated enforcement, redesign streets for safety, and ensure laws protect people walking and biking. Open States
