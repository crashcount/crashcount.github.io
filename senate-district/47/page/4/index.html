      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 47?

      Blood on the Asphalt: Seven Dead, Hundreds Hurt—Who Will Stop the Slaughter?

      Written July 28, 2025

      SD 47: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 28, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      In the last twelve months, seven people died and nearly a thousand were hurt in crashes across Senate District 47. The bodies are not numbers. They are neighbors, children, elders. On May 31, a 79-year-old man died in a tangle of SUVs on West 77th Street. In April, a 57-year-old man was crushed crossing Broadway at 86th. A cyclist, age 74, was killed on West End Avenue. The list goes on. No week passes without blood on the asphalt.

      The Human Cost

      The pain is not abstract. It is a neck twisted in a bus seat, a father holding his son’s hand in the crosswalk, a woman struck twice by bikes in Central Park and left with kidney damage. “A lot of them with musculoskeletal injuries, neck pain, back pain,” said FDNY Deputy Assistant Chief Joseph Pataky after a bus slammed into another at Port Authority. The system shrugs. The ramp where it happened is a known bottleneck, set for replacement years from now. The next crash is already on the calendar.

      Leadership: Progress and Pressure

      Senator Brad Hoylman-Sigal has taken real steps. He voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force repeat dangerous drivers to install speed-limiting devices. He co-sponsored bills for automated bike lane enforcement and moped registration. He backed Sammy’s Law, giving the city power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the deaths keep coming. The bills are not yet law. The streets are not yet safe. “This is about trying to eliminate all unnecessary deaths, and one of the ways you do that is by making the speed limit lower,” said Assemblymember Linda Rosenthal, echoing the urgency.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      The crisis is not fate. It is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand action on repeat speeders. Do not wait for another name on the list.
