      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in SD 47?

      Blood on 42nd Street: Speed Kills, Lawmakers Stall

      Written July 17, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      SD 47: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 17, 2025

      The Death Toll Grows

      Six people killed. Eight hundred seventy-nine injured. That is the price of traffic violence in Senate District 47 in the last year alone. Twenty lives shattered by serious injury. The numbers do not flinch. They do not lie.

      Last month, a food cart broke loose on 42nd Street. It slammed into a parked car. Inside: a woman and her ten-year-old son. They went to the hospital. Police found the van packed with 76 propane tanks and 75 gallons of gasoline. The driver was charged with reckless endangerment. Firefighters forced entry. The smell of fuel hung in the air. Firefighters forced an entry into the van, removing 76 20-pound propane cylinders and 15 five-gallon fuel containers.

      A week before, a car fire in the Lincoln Tunnel trapped drivers for an hour. One commuter went to the hospital. Five more were treated at the scene. The tunnel filled with smoke. One commuter was taken to Mount Sinai West to be treated for injuries. The extent of the victim’s injuries was unclear, according to the FDNY.

      Who Pays the Price

      The old, the young, the ones on foot or bike. In three years, 21 people have died on these streets. Nearly 3,000 have been hurt. SUVs and cars did most of the killing—six deaths, 16 serious injuries. Trucks and buses, two deaths. Bikes, five serious injuries. The numbers are cold. The pain is not.

      What Has Been Done—and What Has Not

      Senator Brad Hoylman-Sigal has moved. He voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, a bill to force repeat speeders to install speed limiters. He co-sponsored a bill for automated bike lane enforcement. He backed the law letting New York City lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the streets are not yet safe. The dead do not come back. The injured do not heal with promises.

      Call to Action

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call your senator. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement against repeat speeders. Demand streets that protect the ones who walk and ride. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York State Senate and how does it work?
      The New York State Senate is the upper chamber of the state legislature. It passes laws, approves budgets, and represents districts across New York. Open States
      ▸ Where does SD 47 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, city council district District 6 and assembly district AD 67. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Which areas are in SD 47?
      It includes the West Village, Chelsea-Hudson Yards, Hell’S Kitchen, Upper West Side-Lincoln Square, Upper West Side (Central), Upper West Side-Manhattan Valley, Central Park, Manhattan CB4, Manhattan CB7, and Manhattan CB64 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Council Districts District 3, District 6, and District 7, and Assembly Districts AD 66, AD 67, AD 69, and AD 75. NYC Open Data
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in SD 47?
      Cars and trucks caused 6 deaths and 16 serious injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 3 serious injuries. Bikes caused 5 serious injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Most crashes are preventable. Lower speeds, better street design, and real enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass and enforce lower speed limits, support automated enforcement, and fund safer street redesigns that protect people walking and biking.

      Bill S 8344, titled 'Extends provisions and makes technical corrections to school speed zones in NYC; repealer,' moved through the Senate and Assembly in June 2025. Sponsored by Senator Andrew Gounardes, the bill passed Senate votes on June 12 and June 13, and cleared the Assembly on June 17.       S 4045
      Hoylman-Sigal votes yes in committee, boosting street safety by curbing repeat speeders.
      ▸

      Senate backs S 4045. Repeat speeders face forced installation of speed assistance tech. Eleven points or six camera tickets triggers action. Law targets reckless drivers. Streets may get safer for those outside the car.

      Senate bill S 4045, sponsored by Andrew Gounardes and co-sponsored by over two dozen senators, passed committee votes on June 11 and June 12, 2025. The bill, titled 'Relates to requiring the installation of intelligent speed assistance devices for repeated violation of maximum speed limits,' mandates these devices for drivers who rack up eleven or more points in 24 months, or six speed or red light camera tickets in a year. The measure aims to curb repeat dangerous driving. Senators including Jamaal Bailey, Jabari Brisport, and others voted yes. The bill targets drivers with a pattern of violations, seeking to cut risk for pedestrians and cyclists by limiting repeat speeding.
