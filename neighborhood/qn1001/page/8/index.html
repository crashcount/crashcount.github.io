      Sidewalks Aren’t Safe—Demand Action Before More Lives Are Lost

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Fixed FAQ citation, removed duplicate headline, cleaned citations)

      South Ozone Park: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      Three dead. Thirteen seriously hurt. In the last year alone, South Ozone Park saw 777 crashes, leaving 525 people injured. The bodies tell the story: a 19-year-old woman, neck broken in a sedan on South Conduit Avenue. A 52-year-old woman, struck and killed by a motorcycle at Liberty Avenue and 114th Street. A 37-year-old man, ejected from his moped on Nassau Expressway, never made it home. These are not numbers. They are lives, cut short or changed forever.

      Vulnerable on the Sidewalk

      No one is safe. In April, a car jumped the curb at Liberty Avenue and Lefferts Boulevard. Nine people were hurt—four waiting for the bus, two on the bus, three inside the car. Police said none of the victims suffered life-threatening injuries. All went to Jamaica Hospital. The sidewalk is not a shield. The bus stop is not a refuge.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      The city has the tools. Sammy’s Law lets New York lower speed limits to 20 mph. Speed cameras slash speeding and injuries. But in South Ozone Park, the carnage continues. The council can act. The mayor can act. Albany can keep cameras on. Every day of delay is another day of risk.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. These deaths are not the price of living in Queens. They are the cost of inaction. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras that never go dark. Stand with the families who have lost too much.

      Do not wait for another name to become a number. Act now.
