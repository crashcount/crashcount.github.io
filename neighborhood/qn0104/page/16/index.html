      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Astoria (East)-Woodside (North)?

      Blood on the Crosswalk: How Many More Before They Act?

      Written June 25, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Astoria (East)-Woodside (North): Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 26, 2025

      The Toll: Flesh and Bone on Asphalt

      A cyclist leaves a ride and never comes home. Amanda Servedio, 36, was hit and killed at 37th Street and 34th Avenue. The truck that struck her was fleeing police. She “went airborne. She flew like 30 feet. It was a lot of force,” said a witness. Her father spoke for every parent who has waited for justice: “We were certainly upset that they didn’t stop the chase… It was probably not the place to be doing a high-speed chase, in the residential neighborhood, and we were very upset that that’s what actually happened” he told the Daily News.

      In the last twelve months, 154 people were injured and 3 suffered serious injuries in this district. One person died. The numbers are not just numbers. They are broken legs, split skulls, and families left with silence.

      Children and Sidewalks: No Safe Harbor

      A 7-year-old girl, a 14-year-old, and a man were hit outside a school on 35th Avenue. The driver had no license. The younger girl left with a broken femur and a head injury. The car did not care about the school bell or the crossing guard. The street did not forgive. Police charged the driver with reckless endangerment and reckless driving.

      Leadership: Steps Forward, Steps Back

      Local leaders have not been silent. Council Member Tiffany Cabán has backed bills to ban parking near crosswalks, speed up protected bike lanes, and legalize jaywalking—measures that put people before cars. State Senator Michael Gianaris voted for “complete streets” design and called for stronger penalties for repeat reckless drivers. But the pace is slow. The streets do not wait.

      The Call: Demand More, Demand Now

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every injury, every death, is a choice made by those in power or those who look away. Contact your council member, your senator, your assembly member. Demand protected bike lanes, daylighted crosswalks, and real enforcement against reckless drivers.

      Do not wait for another name to be added to the list.

      Int 0500-2022
      Cabán co-sponsors bill to revoke private parking permits, boosting street safety.
      ▸

      Council bill Int 0500-2022 would kill private vehicle parking permits. All existing permits get revoked. Only disabled drivers and union contracts are spared. The bill targets abuse and privilege. It died in committee. Streets stay dangerous.

      Int 0500-2022 was introduced on June 2, 2022, in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure. The bill sought to "prohibit the issuance of private vehicle parking permits and revoke such existing permits," with exceptions for people with disabilities and collective bargaining agreements. Council Member Christopher Marte led as primary sponsor, joined by Restler, Brewer, Joseph, Nurse, Cabán, Avilés, Bottcher, Won, Ossé, and Richardson Jordan, plus the Brooklyn Borough President. The bill aimed to end a system that let private cars claim curb space, fueling illegal parking and danger for those on foot or bike. It was filed at the end of session on December 31, 2023, without a vote. The status quo remains: permits still shield drivers, while vulnerable road users face the risk.

      González-Rojas Frames MTA Bridge Bike Access as Justice Issue
      ▸

      MTA pledges better access for cyclists and walkers. The agency seeks public input. Sam Schwartz Engineering will draft the plan. New bike parking appears at Grand Central. Permit rules ease on commuter trains. Advocates push for more. History shows slow progress.

      On May 27, 2022, the MTA announced a strategic action plan to improve bicycle, pedestrian, and micromobility access to its trains and facilities. This follows a state bill signed last year mandating better conditions for cyclists. The plan, still in development, aims to create 'equitable access to the transit system and support sustainable transportation modes.' MTA Chairman and CEO Janno Lieber said, 'We want to improve customer access to our environmentally friendly MTA services, however they get to their train or bus.' Queens Assembly Member Jessica González-Rojas called bridge access 'a matter of environmental justice, public health, and labor justice.' The MTA has ended bike permits on LIRR and Metro-North and added bike parking at Grand Central, but its record on bike access remains mixed. The agency is now seeking public comment and has hired Sam Schwartz Engineering to help shape the plan.

      Int 0256-2022
      Cabán co-sponsors bill boosting safety by tracking police vehicle force incidents.
      ▸

      Int 0256-2022, introduced April 28, 2022, in the Committee on Public Safety, sought to amend the city code to require NYPD to report when officers use a motor vehicle as force.
