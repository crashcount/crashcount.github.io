      Parkchester Bleeds. City Delays. Who’s Next?

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed citation formatting and links.)

      Parkchester: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Toll in Parkchester

      One dead. Five left with life-changing wounds. In the last twelve months, Parkchester saw 105 crashes. Fifty-seven people were hurt. One did not come home. One more was marked with a serious injury, the kind that does not heal with time. NYC Open Data

      Children are not spared. Five under 18 were injured in crashes this year. The street does not care about age. The numbers do not lie: the violence is steady, unbroken, and it finds the young as well as the old.

      Who Bears the Brunt

      Pedestrians and cyclists take the hit. In the last year, cars and SUVs caused most of the pain. Motorcycles and mopeds added to the toll. Bikes were not the killers, but their riders bled too. The street is a field of risk for anyone not behind a windshield.

      Leadership: Promises and Silence

      The city talks of Vision Zero. They count the dead and the wounded. They promise safer streets, lower speed limits, more cameras. But in Parkchester, the numbers do not move fast enough. One death is too many. One family waits for a call that never comes. The city has the power to lower speed limits now. They have the tools to slow the cars. The question is not what they can do. The question is why they have not done it yet.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. Every crash is a choice made by the city, by the state, by those who hold the levers. The violence will not stop until they act. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand more cameras. Demand streets where children can walk and live. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      ▸

      A sedan making a U-turn hit an eastbound e-scooter on East Tremont Avenue. The scooter rider was ejected and suffered a fractured leg. The crash left the sedan’s rear damaged. The street saw pain and metal twisted by impact.

      A crash at 1950 East Tremont Avenue in the Bronx involved a sedan and an e-scooter. According to the police report, the sedan was making a U-turn when it collided with the e-scooter, which was traveling straight. The e-scooter rider, a 58-year-old man, was ejected and suffered a fractured lower leg. The sedan’s right rear quarter panel was damaged. The sedan driver, a 76-year-old man, was not ejected and reported no injury. The police report lists all contributing factors as 'Unspecified.' The e-scooter rider was not using any safety equipment, but the report does not cite this as a contributing factor. The crash highlights the danger when turning vehicles cross the path of vulnerable road users.
