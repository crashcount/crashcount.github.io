      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Mott Haven-Port Morris?

      Six Walk, Mustang Strikes—Bronx Bleeds, City Waits

      Written July 4, 2025 (Revised July 10, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Mott Haven-Port Morris: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 4, 2025

      Six Down on 149th Street

      On July 3, a Ford Mustang tore through the crosswalk at East 149th and Courtlandt. Six people walking were hit. The car smashed into scaffolding. The driver and passenger ran. The Mustang sat there, front end ruined, plates gone. The street was left with blood and broken glass. “People were yelling, were in pain, so yelling, crying, it was very upsetting,” said Vivian Cole. Another witness thought it was a bomb. “We thought it was a bomb or something, because we are all panicking around here,” said Christina Sieh.

      Six people went to the hospital. The driver is still gone. No arrests. No answers. The intersection is busy. The risk is constant. This is not rare. It is daily life here.

      The Toll: Deaths and Injuries Stack Up

      In the last year, three people have died on these streets. Over 400 have been hurt. Eleven were left with serious injuries. Most were walking, biking, or just trying to cross. Cars and trucks did the most harm, but motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes all played a part. The numbers do not slow. The pain does not fade.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      Council Member Diana Ayala has backed bills to light step streets, ban parking near crosswalks, and legalize jaywalking. She voted to end penalties for crossing outside the lines, a move meant to stop blaming the dead and the hurt. But the city still waits for real change. The streets are not safe. The laws are not enough. The drivers keep running.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      This is not fate. These deaths are not the weather. Call Ayala. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand daylight at every corner. Demand action, not speeches. Six people hit in one morning is six too many. Tomorrow, it could be you.
