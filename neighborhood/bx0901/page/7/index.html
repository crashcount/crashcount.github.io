      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—Leaders Stall. Demand Action Now.

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised June 27, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Blood on the Asphalt

      In Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River, the numbers do not lie. Three dead. Ten left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. In the last year alone, 432 crashes tore through these streets. One child, one elder, one man over seventy-five—all gone. A 75-year-old man was killed crossing Wood Avenue. He tried to wave the driver to stop. The driver did not. “The accident is so terrible. I’ve never seen anything like this in my life,” said a neighbor.

      An 18-year-old woman was thrown from a moped on the Hutchinson River Parkway. The driver left her in the road. “The moped driver sped away… leaving the young woman to die on the road,” police said. Six others went to the hospital. The street does not forgive. It only takes.

      The Numbers That Haunt

      In three and a half years: 11 dead, 19 seriously injured, over 1,000 hurt. Cars and SUVs did most of the damage—two deaths, 102 minor injuries, 34 moderate, six serious. Trucks and buses left 11 more broken. Motorcycles and mopeds, six more. Bikes, six. The young are not spared. One child killed, 101 injured.

      What Leaders Have Done—And What They Haven’t

      Council Member Amanda Farías has voted for safer truck routes, daylighting, and speed humps. She co-sponsored bills for school signs and speed reducers. She backed the SAFE Streets Act. But the carnage continues.

      State Senator Nathalia Fernández voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force repeat speeders to install limiters. It is a step. But the dead do not come back. The wounded do not heal because a bill passed. No law yet has slowed the cars enough.

      The Call: Demand More Than Words

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by leaders who act or do not act. Call your council member. Call your senator. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement. Demand streets that put people first.

      Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Bronx, community board Bronx CB9, city council district District 18, assembly district AD 85 and state senate district SD 34.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River?
      Cars and SUVs: 2 deaths, 102 minor injuries, 34 moderate injuries, 6 serious injuries. Trucks and Buses: 11 injuries. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 6 injuries. Bikes: 6 injuries. Most deaths and injuries come from cars and SUVs.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. The numbers show a pattern. These are preventable. Policy, design, and enforcement can stop the next crash.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can lower speed limits, redesign streets, enforce laws against reckless drivers, and pass bills that protect people walking and cycling. They can act now, not after another death.
      ▸ What has Council Member Amanda Farías done for street safety?
      She has voted for safer truck routes, daylighting, speed humps, and co-sponsored bills for school signs and speed reducers. She backed the SAFE Streets Act.

      Int 0853-2024
      Farías co-sponsors borough traffic teams bill, boosting citywide street safety.
      ▸

      Council bill Int 0853-2024 calls for DOT teams in every borough. Staff must answer traffic requests fast—within three months if possible. The bill aims to cut delays, bring action, and address dangers that linger on city streets.

      Int 0853-2024 sits in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure after its introduction on April 18, 2024. The bill, titled "A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to creating borough-based traffic request response teams," demands that the Department of Transportation assign staff to each borough, matching staff numbers to the volume of local traffic requests. Council Members Farah N. Louis (primary sponsor), Amanda Farías, and Rafael Salamanca, Jr. back the measure. Their bill requires DOT to respond to each traffic request in three months, if practicable. The goal is clear: end the backlog, speed up fixes, and address hazards that put pedestrians and cyclists at risk. The bill was referred to committee on the day of introduction. No safety analyst note was provided.

      Int 0606-2024
      Farías co-sponsors e-bike and scooter registration bill, reducing overall street safety.
      ▸

      Council wants plates on every e-bike and scooter. The bill sits in committee. Riders face new rules. Lawmakers push for registration. Streets wait. No change yet. The city debates control. Vulnerable users watch. The system holds its breath.

      Int 0606-2024 was introduced on March 7, 2024, in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure. The bill, titled "A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to requiring that every bicycle with electric assist, electric scooter and other legal motorized vehicle be licensed and registered," would force all e-bikes, e-scooters, and similar vehicles not already covered by DMV rules to register with the Department of Transportation and display a visible plate.
