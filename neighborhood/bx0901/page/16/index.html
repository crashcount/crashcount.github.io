      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River?

      Bronx Streets Bleed—Leaders Stall. Demand Action Now.

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Blood on the Asphalt

      In Soundview-Bruckner-Bronx River, the numbers do not lie. Three dead. Ten left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. In the last year alone, 432 crashes tore through these streets. One child, one elder, one man over seventy-five—all gone. A 75-year-old man was killed crossing Wood Avenue. He tried to wave the driver to stop. The driver did not. “The accident is so terrible. I’ve never seen anything like this in my life,” said a neighbor.

      An 18-year-old woman was thrown from a moped on the Hutchinson River Parkway. The driver left her in the road. “The moped driver sped away… leaving the young woman to die on the road,” police said. Six others went to the hospital. The street does not forgive. It only takes.

      The Numbers That Haunt

      In three and a half years: 11 dead, 19 seriously injured, over 1,000 hurt. Cars and SUVs did most of the damage—two deaths, 102 minor injuries, 34 moderate, six serious. Trucks and buses left 11 more broken. Motorcycles and mopeds, six more. Bikes, six. The young are not spared. One child killed, 101 injured.

      What Leaders Have Done—And What They Haven’t

      Council Member Amanda Farías has voted for safer truck routes, daylighting, and speed humps. She co-sponsored bills for school signs and speed reducers. She backed the SAFE Streets Act. But the carnage continues.

      State Senator Nathalia Fernández voted yes on the Stop Super Speeders Act, which would force repeat speeders to install limiters. It is a step. But the dead do not come back. The wounded do not heal because a bill passed. No law yet has slowed the cars enough.

      The Call: Demand More Than Words

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by leaders who act or do not act. Call your council member. Call your senator. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real enforcement. Demand streets that put people first.

      Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Sepulveda Pushes Misguided Bill Slowing Bike Safety Upgrades
      ▸

      Senate Bill S6929, pushed by Sen. Luis Sepulveda, passed committee. It demands more red tape before bike lanes or racks go in. Critics say it slows safety. Advocates rally to block it. The city wants faster, not slower, bike upgrades.

      Senate Bill S6929, sponsored by Sen. Luis Sepulveda, passed the Cities Committee and now faces fierce opposition. The bill requires the NYC Department of Transportation to give extra notifications and presentations to community boards and local officials before installing or removing bike lanes or racks. The matter summary reads: 'require the NYC DOT to provide additional notifications and presentations.' Advocacy groups, including Bike New York, are preparing testimony to stall the bill before it reaches the Senate floor or Assembly Cities Committee. Critics argue the DOT already must notify community boards under the 'Fidler rule,' and that this bill would gum up the process, delaying life-saving bike infrastructure. A legislative source called the bill 'boneheaded.' Public polls show strong support for more bike lanes, with only a small minority opposed. The city is seeking to streamline—not slow—bike safety upgrades.
