      Belt Parkway Bleeds—How Many More Lives Before We Act?

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, tightened prose, ensured all citations are properly linked and deduped.)

      Spring Creek-Starrett City: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      One person dead. Three left with serious injuries. In the last twelve months, 173 people were hurt in 284 crashes across Spring Creek-Starrett City. The dead man was 41. He was thrown from his BMW on the Belt Parkway. The car hit a tree. He died before the medics arrived. No one else was hurt. The crash was over in seconds. The loss will last a lifetime. NYC Open Data

      Highways, High Speeds, Hard Truths

      The Belt Parkway cuts through this place like a wound. In February, Marcus Joseph lost control of his BMW X5 near Exit 14. The SUV skidded off the road and slammed into a tree. He was already dead when help arrived. police said Monday.

      Elsewhere, a 23-year-old woman was struck by an e-bike while crossing Gateway Drive. She left with deep cuts to her leg. A 45-year-old man on a motorcycle was torn up in a crash on Pennsylvania Avenue. The list goes on. The pain does not end.

      Leadership: Words, Laws, and Waiting

      Local leaders have the power to slow the carnage. Albany passed Sammy’s Law. The city can now set speed limits at 20 mph on local streets. But the Belt Parkway is still a speedway. The city has not acted to lower limits here. Speed cameras work, but only where they are installed. The law that keeps them running is again up for renewal. Each day of delay is another day of risk.

      Police chase stolen cars. Sometimes the chase ends in gunfire. Sometimes it ends in a crash. “They observed a suspicious Porsche with suspicious plates,” said Chief John Chell after a deadly police shooting on the Belt Parkway.

      What Now?

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Demand lower speed limits. Demand more cameras. Demand streets that put people first. Do not wait for another name to be added to the list. Take action now.
