      Bellerose Bleeds—City Waits. Demand Safe Streets Now.

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, tightened prose, and cleaned citations.)

      Bellerose: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      One death. 412 injuries. Zero serious injuries. That’s the toll in Bellerose since 2022. The dead: an elder, age 75 or older. The injured: children, teens, adults, seniors. No one is spared. In the last year alone, 150 people were hurt in 232 crashes. Not one month passes without pain. NYC crash data

      The Faces Behind the Numbers

      On December 14, 2024, a 78-year-old man tried to cross Jericho Turnpike at 91st Avenue. He never made it. A driver turned left and struck him. The man died a week later. The driver stayed at the scene. Five months passed before police charged him. The charge: failure to yield, failure to exercise due care. Both are misdemeanors. The driver got a desk ticket. The family got a funeral. Police arrested the driver five months after fatally striking a 78-year-old man crossing a heavily traveled Queens intersection.

      Who Pays and Who Acts?

      Cars and SUVs cause the harm. In three years, not one cyclist or moped killed or seriously hurt a pedestrian here. The violence comes from behind the wheel. The city says it’s making progress. But in Bellerose, the numbers do not move. The pain stays the same.

      Local leaders talk about Vision Zero. They talk about safer streets. But the law moves slow. The dead are buried before the charges come. The cameras that catch speeders need Albany’s blessing to keep running. The city has the power to lower speed limits. It has not used it here.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every crash is a choice made by leaders who wait. Every injury is a call for action. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras that never go dark. Demand streets where crossing does not mean risking your life.
