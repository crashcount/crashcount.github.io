      bk0201: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 25, 2025

      The Toll: Broken Bodies, Silent Streets

      No one was killed in bk0201 in the past year. But the numbers do not comfort. The streets still bleed. In the last three years, people have been crushed, trapped, and left with broken limbs. A 76-year-old woman died in the back seat of an SUV on Furman Street. The driver was speeding. She was trapped, incoherent, and then gone. The man behind the wheel survived, but not unscathed. The street did not forgive either of them. NYC Open Data

      A 27-year-old woman crossing with the signal at Tillary and Cadman Plaza West was hit by a turning sedan. She walked with the light. The car turned anyway. Her knee and leg were crushed. She lived. The pain stays.

      A 23-year-old man, not at an intersection, was struck by a sedan on Court Street. He was emerging from behind a parked car. The city calls it an abrasion. The city does not count the fear.

      Leadership: Promises, Bills, and the Waiting

      Local leaders have written bills. Council Member Restler has sponsored laws to speed up protected bike lanes, ban parking near crosswalks, and mandate speed limiters for repeat offenders. The bills sit in committee. The SAFE Streets Act waits for a vote. The city promises daylight at crossings, but cars still block the view. The law says to wait. The street does not wait.

      State Senator Gounardes has pushed for speed cameras and speed limiters. The Stop Super Speeders Act would force the worst drivers to slow down. It has not passed. The cameras watch, but the dead do not rise.

      The Human Cost: Names Lost, Grief That Stays

      “She was a phenomenon, a force to be reckoned with. She was determined. She was smart. She was funny, and she had everything to look forward to. I’m gonna tell you it’s been awful, but there isn’t a word to describe how awful it’s been. She’s been part of my DNA, and it’s like losing part of me. I lost part of me.” said Kim Harris after her daughter was killed by a drunk driver in Brooklyn. The loss is not just a number. It is a hole in the world.

      What Next: No More Waiting

      The disaster is slow, but it is not silent. The laws are written. The votes are not cast. The streets are not safe. Call your council member. Demand a vote. Demand daylight at every crossing. Demand speed limiters for repeat offenders. Demand protected bike lanes that do not wait for another body.

      Do not wait for the next name.

      Gounardes Supports Safety Boosting Stop Super Speeders Bill
      ▸

      After a crash killed a mother and two daughters in Gravesend, advocates and Council Member Shahana Hanif rallied for the Stop Super Speeders bill. The law would force repeat reckless drivers to use speed-limiting tech. Survivors demand action. Lawmakers promise change.

      On April 1, 2025, Council Member Shahana Hanif joined a rally at Brooklyn Borough Hall demanding passage of the Stop Super Speeders bill. The bill, sponsored in Albany by State Sen. Andrew Gounardes and Assemblymember Emily Gallagher, would require drivers with repeated violations to install intelligent speed assistance (ISA) devices. These devices cap speed at 5 mph over the limit for those with 11 or more license points in 24 months or six camera tickets in a year. The rally followed a fatal Gravesend crash that killed a mother and her two daughters. Hanif and other lawmakers called current enforcement—ticketing, suspensions, fines, jail—ineffective. Assembly Member Jo Anne Simon said, 'The speed limiter technology is available to us. Let’s use it. It will save lives.' The bill is modeled on EU and Virginia laws. Some opposition remains, but supporters say the measure is urgent and practical.

      Gounardes Urges Action on Safety Boosting Speed Limiter Bill
      ▸

      After a Brooklyn crash killed a mother and two children, lawmakers renewed calls for speed limiters on cars of repeat offenders. Assemblymember Emily Gallagher and others demand action. The bill targets drivers with long records of speeding and red-light violations.

      Assemblymember Emily Gallagher, representing District 50, is pushing for the Stop Super Speeders bill, which would require speed limiters on vehicles owned by drivers with repeated speeding or red-light violations.       ▸

      Dumbo’s speed limit drops to 20 mph. Brooklyn’s first Regional Slow Zone targets crowded streets. Ten severe injuries and one death in five years haunt the area. Council Member Restler and DOT push for slower traffic. Lives hang in the balance.

      On March 19, 2025, Council Member Lincoln Restler announced the lowering of Dumbo’s speed limit to 20 mph, making it Brooklyn’s first Regional Slow Zone. The city plans to expand 20 mph limits to over 250 locations by the end of 2025.

      Restler Urges Safety Boosting Extension of Weigh-in-Motion Program
      ▸

      Weigh-in-motion sensors slashed overweight truck crossings on the BQE by 60%. Councilmember Lincoln Restler backs the tech and calls for state lawmakers to extend the program. The city wants expansion before the enabling law expires. DOT hails the results.
