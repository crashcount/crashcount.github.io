      Staten Island Bleeds While Officials Stall: Lower the Speed, Save a Life

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      New Dorp-Midland Beach: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      One dead. Two seriously hurt. 524 injured. That’s the toll on New Dorp-Midland Beach since 2022. The dead: a 55-year-old, struck by a truck while crossing at Hylan and Jefferson. The living: 524 people, each left with pain, scars, or worse. In the last year alone, 183 people were hurt—31 of them children.

      The Human Cost

      On a Friday morning, Chaosheng Wu, 80, tried to cross Hylan Boulevard. He never made it home. A 65-year-old woman in a Ford Edge hit him. Wu died at Staten Island University North Hospital. The driver stayed. No charges. Police said they were “still investigating how fast the woman was driving and what the traffic signals at the intersection were displaying at the time,” reported Gothamist. Wu’s death was the first traffic fatality this year in the 122nd Precinct.

      The Daily News put it plain: “The 65-year-old driver stayed at the scene and was not charged, but police are still investigating the crash,” wrote NY Daily News.

      Who Pays, Who Acts

      Trucks and SUVs do the killing. In this district, trucks killed, SUVs and sedans injured. Buses left people bleeding. Children are not spared. The numbers are not just numbers. They are people, and they are gone or changed forever.

      Leadership: Words and Silence

      City leaders talk about Vision Zero. They say, “one life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” But the street stays the same. The crosswalks stay wide and fast. The signals stay short. The cameras, when they work, work. But the law that keeps them alive is always up for debate. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. It has not used it.

      What Now

      This is not fate. This is policy. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Tell them to lower the speed limit. Tell them to keep the cameras on. Tell them to fix the street. Every day they wait, someone else pays.

      Take action now.
