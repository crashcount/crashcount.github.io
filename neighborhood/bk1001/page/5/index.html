      Bay Ridge Bleeds While City Hall Sleeps

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Cleaned up body text, fixed citation formatting, and ensured FAQ answers use proper inline links.)

      Bay Ridge: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      Eight dead. Over a thousand injured. In Bay Ridge, from 2022 to June 2025, the numbers keep coming. They do not slow down. They do not care who you are. Four deaths in the last year alone. None was a child. One was an elder. One was a cyclist. One was a man on his way home. NYC Open Data

      Crashes do not choose. In the last twelve months, 394 people were hurt. Only one was labeled a “serious injury.” The rest are just numbers in a file. But every number is a body. A family. A life changed.

      The Machines That Kill

      SUVs, sedans, trucks, bikes. In Bay Ridge, cars and trucks killed four. Bikes left one with a serious injury. Trucks and buses, another. The roll call is cold: 48 crashes with cars and SUVs, 5 with trucks and buses, 4 with bikes. The street does not care if you are careful. The street does not care if you are young or old.

      The Leaders Who Wait

      The city has the power. Sammy’s Law passed. The city can lower the speed limit to 20 mph. It has not. Speed cameras work, but their future is always in doubt. The council and the mayor can act. They have not acted fast enough. Each day of delay is another risk, another body on the street.

      The Call That Must Be Made

      This is not fate. These are not accidents. These are choices. Choices made in City Hall, in Albany, on every block where a crosswalk fades and a driver speeds up. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand speed cameras stay on. Demand streets that do not kill.

      Do not wait for another name to become a number. Take action now.
