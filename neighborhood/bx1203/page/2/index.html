      Numbers Rise. Bodies Fall. Wakefield-Woodlawn Needs Action—Now.

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body, tightened prose, ensured correct citation formatting and cleaned citations.)

      Wakefield-Woodlawn: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Never Stop

      One death. Five serious injuries. 576 people hurt. That is the toll in Wakefield-Woodlawn since 2022. The numbers do not flinch. They do not pause for grief. They keep rising, year after year, crash after crash. In the last twelve months alone, 152 people were injured in 256 crashes. One was left with injuries so severe the world changed in an instant, according to NYC Open Data.

      The Most Vulnerable, Still at Risk

      Pedestrians and cyclists do not walk away. In the past three years, SUVs and trucks have struck down walkers at intersections. A 63-year-old man, hit by an SUV, left bleeding on White Plains Road. A 23-year-old, crossing with the signal, thrown by a driver who did not slow down. Children, too, are counted among the injured. The numbers do not show the faces, but the pain is real.

      Leadership: Progress or Delay?

      Local leaders talk of Vision Zero. The city has added protected bike lanes, redesigned intersections, and pushed for lower speed limits. But in Wakefield-Woodlawn, the pace is slow. The law now allows the city to set 20 mph limits, but most streets remain unchanged. Speed cameras work, but only where they are installed. The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program has lapsed. Promises echo, but the street stays the same.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. Every crash is a choice made possible by policy, by design, by delay. Residents can demand more. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand 20 mph limits on every street. Demand speed cameras on every block. Demand action, not words.

      Do not wait for the next name to become a number.

      Take action now.
