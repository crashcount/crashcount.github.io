      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Upper East Side-Carnegie Hill?

      Deadly Streets, Silent Leaders: Demand Action Before Another Life Is Lost

      Written June 20, 2025 (Revised June 26, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Upper East Side-Carnegie Hill: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Numbers Don’t Lie

      One death. Twelve serious injuries. In the last three and a half years, the streets of Upper East Side-Carnegie Hill have not spared the vulnerable. Cyclists, pedestrians, the old, the young. In 959 crashes, 376 people were hurt. Two were left with injuries so severe they may never walk the same. The dead do not speak. The injured carry the story in their bodies.

      The Latest Losses

      On June 18, Salvador Nico Garcia rode his e-bike through Central Park. He collided with a pedestrian in the crosswalk at East Drive and 97th Street. He was thrown from the bike. His head struck the curb. He died at the hospital. The pedestrian walked away with a cut hand. The city counted Garcia as the sixth e-bike rider killed this year. Nico Garcia was thrown from his electric bike by the impact and slammed his head on the curb.
      The NYPD’s Collision Investigation Squad is still reviewing the case. A 43 year-old Bronx resident died on June 18 after flying from an e-bike and striking his head on the curb.

      Who Bears the Cost

      Cars and SUVs caused the most pain. Thirty-two crashes left pedestrians bleeding or broken. Buses and trucks struck two more. Three people were hit by bikes. One by a moped. The numbers are small until you see the faces. A woman crossing with the signal. A cyclist thrown to the pavement. A hand that will never fully heal.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      City leaders talk about Vision Zero. They tout new speed cameras and intersection redesigns. But the carnage continues. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. They have not used it. Cameras that catch speeders are at risk of going dark unless Albany acts. The silence is loud. The clock ticks. Every day without action is another day someone does not come home.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras stay on. Demand streets that do not kill. Take action now.
