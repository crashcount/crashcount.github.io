      Four Dead, 510 Hurt: Mount Eden’s Streets Are Killing Fields

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed and deduped citations, ensured proper link formatting.)

      Mount Eden-Claremont (West): Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Four dead. Eight seriously hurt. In three and a half years, Mount Eden-Claremont (West) has seen 921 crashes. Five hundred ten people have been injured. The dead do not come back. The wounded carry scars you cannot see.

      Faces Behind the Numbers

      Kelvin Mitchell was crossing Webster Avenue. A black Mercedes sped through the bus lane. The car did not stop. Mitchell died in the street. His sister, Justina Lorrious, said “Everybody is missing Kelvin. It’s not gonna be the same over here without him.”

      His mother, Nancy Aguilar, stood at the memorial and said, “They killed my son. I need justice for my son.”

      Who Pays the Price?

      SUVs, sedans, buses, trucks, and mopeds have all left bodies broken. In the last year alone, 161 people were hurt. No one was killed. The youngest victims are children. The oldest are elders. The street does not care.

      What Has Been Done?

      The city has lowered speed limits in some places. Cameras catch some speeders. But the law that keeps those cameras running is always at risk. Local leaders talk about Vision Zero. They say, “One life lost to traffic violence is one life too many.” But the numbers do not change fast enough. The silence after each crash is loud.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. This is policy. Every day leaders wait, another family is broken. Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras that never go dark. Demand streets where children can walk home.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.
