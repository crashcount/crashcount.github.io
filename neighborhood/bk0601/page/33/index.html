      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Carroll Gardens-Cobble Hill-Gowanus-Red Hook?

      Five Dead, Hundreds Hurt: City Leaders Stall While Streets Bleed

      Written June 30, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      Carroll Gardens-Cobble Hill-Gowanus-Red Hook: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 30, 2025

      The Toll on Our Streets

      Five dead. Fourteen seriously hurt. Nearly a thousand injured. That’s the cost of traffic violence in Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Gowanus, and Red Hook since 2022. NYC Open Data. The numbers do not bleed, but the people do. Pedestrians, cyclists, children—none are spared. Cars and trucks do most of the damage. Trucks killed. SUVs broke bones. Bikes and mopeds left their own scars, but the big machines do the worst work.

      Just last year, a truck turned and killed an 83-year-old woman crossing Butler and Bond. NYC Open Data. She died on the street. No arrest. No comfort. Only the cold record: “Apparent Death.” The city moves on. The street stays the same.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      Local leaders talk safety. The streets say otherwise. Council Member Shahana Hanif co-sponsored a bill to ban parking near crosswalks and force the city to clear sightlines at 1,000 intersections a year. NYC Council – Legistar. The bill sits in committee. No vote. No law. Senator Andrew Gounardes sponsored a bill to let speed cameras catch drivers who hide their plates. Open States. It waits in Albany. Another bill to force repeat speeders to slow down is still just talk.

      Advocates push. Leaders promise. The wheels turn slow. “Our elected officials in Albany have failed the livable streets movement again,” said a Streetsblog report.

      The Human Cost

      Every number is a person. In the last 12 months, one person died, four were seriously hurt, and over 300 were injured in crashes here. NYC Open Data. Children, elders, workers. A crash on Atlantic and Court killed an 18-year-old woman. A man died on Hicks Street. The stories pile up. The pain does not fade.

      A witness to one crash said, “I just saw a lot of blood gushing out of his ears, his mouth.” NY Daily News. Another said, “People ran upstairs, got towels, trying to stop the bleeding.” NY Daily News.

      What Next? Demand More

      This is not fate. This is policy. Streets can be made safe. Leaders can act. Call your council member. Demand a vote on daylighting. Call your senator. Demand action on speeders. Join the fight. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ Where does Carroll Gardens-Cobble Hill-Gowanus-Red Hook sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, community board Brooklyn CB6, city council district District 39, assembly district AD 52 and state senate district SD 26.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in Carroll Gardens-Cobble Hill-Gowanus-Red Hook?
      Cars and Trucks: 93 crashes involving cars and SUVs, 17 involving trucks and buses. Motorcycles and Mopeds: 6 crashes. Bikes: 5 crashes. Most deaths and serious injuries came from cars, SUVs, and trucks.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. These crashes are preventable. Safer street design, lower speeds, and better enforcement can stop the deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass laws to daylight intersections, lower speed limits, and hold repeat dangerous drivers accountable. They can fund and build safer streets. They can act now, not just talk.
      ▸ What has been done recently to improve street safety here?
      Council Member Hanif co-sponsored a bill to ban parking near crosswalks and clear sightlines, but it has not passed. NYC Council – Legistar. Senator Gounardes sponsored a bill to expand speed camera enforcement, but it is still waiting in Albany. Open States.
      ▸ How many people have been killed or seriously hurt in this area since 2022?
      Five people have died. Fourteen have been seriously injured. 975 people have been hurt in crashes since 2022.
