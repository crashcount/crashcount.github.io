      Two Dead in Two Days—NYC Lets Streets Kill Again

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 5, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      Upper East Side-Lenox Hill-Roosevelt Island: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Deaths Keep Coming

      A woman, ninety years old, tried to cross York Avenue at dusk. A yellow taxi made a U-turn and hit her. Seconds later, an SUV struck her again. She died at the hospital. Both drivers stayed. No charges were filed. The street was quiet again, but the loss stayed. Frances Rickard was crossing at York Avenue and East 72nd Street around 5:40 p.m. when the 68-year-old man driving the cab made a U-turn and hit her, authorities said.

      A day before, a Tesla hit a guardrail on the FDR. The car flipped and burned. The driver died. Her passenger survived. A witness described the crash: “At least 120, 130 [mph]. At least. The damage was just so much that it split in half. And that just started going on fire.”

      In the last twelve months, three people died and 210 were injured in 418 crashes here. Two of the dead were over 75. One was a driver. Three people suffered serious injuries. The numbers do not stop. They do not slow.

      The Usual Suspects

      SUVs killed three pedestrians. Trucks killed one. Bikes and mopeds injured, but did not kill. Cars and trucks do most of the damage. The old and the young are not spared. The streets are not safe for anyone who walks, rides, or waits for the light to change.

      What Leaders Have Done—and Not Done

      The city says it is working. Speed cameras now run all day. The council passed Sammy’s Law. The city can lower speed limits to 20 mph. But the limit has not changed yet. Cameras need Albany to act again, or they will go dark. The city has built more bike lanes and changed some intersections. But the dead are still dead. The injured still limp. The silence from some leaders is louder than the sirens.

      What You Can Do

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand cameras stay on. Demand streets where the old can cross and the young can ride without fear.

      Do not wait for another name to become a number. Take action now.
