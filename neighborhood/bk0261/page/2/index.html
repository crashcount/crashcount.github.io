      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in Brooklyn Navy Yard?

      Blood on Kent Avenue: City Inaction Costs Lives

      Written June 4, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      Brooklyn Navy Yard: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 4, 2025

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      One person dead. Two seriously hurt. In three and a half years, the Brooklyn Navy Yard has seen 102 crashes. Fifty-four people have been injured. One did not go home. Two were left with wounds that do not heal fast. These are not numbers. They are lives, broken on the road. NYC crash data

      Pedestrians and cyclists take the worst of it. A 59-year-old woman was killed crossing Kent Avenue. The SUV kept going. A cyclist, 24, was thrown from his bike after a car cut him off. Another cyclist, 39, hit by a sedan, left bleeding in the street. A 53-year-old woman, walking, hit by a car at the crosswalk. The pattern is plain. The most vulnerable pay the price.

      Leadership: Promises and Delays

      The city talks about Vision Zero. They say every life matters. They say they are redesigning intersections and lowering speed limits. But in the Navy Yard, the pace is slow. The crashes keep coming. The city has the power to lower speed limits to 20 mph. They have not used it here. Speed cameras work, but only where they are turned on. Laws lapse. Programs expire. The street stays the same.

      What Comes Next

      No one should die crossing the street. The city can act. The council can demand lower speed limits. The mayor can order more cameras and redesigns. Albany can keep the cameras running. But nothing changes unless people demand it.

      Call your council member. Call the mayor. Demand safer speeds. Demand real protection for people who walk and bike.

      Take action now.

      Resolution 0851-2025 sits with the Committee on Public Safety. Introduced April 24, 2025, it urges the State Legislature and Governor to ban police courtesy cards. The resolution states: 'prohibiting the issuance of police courtesy cards.' Council Members Chi A. Ossé (primary sponsor), Crystal Hudson, Lincoln Restler, and Tiffany Cabán back the measure.
