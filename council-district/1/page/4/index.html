      No More Excuses: The Streets Are Killing Us

      Written July 18, 2025 (Revised July 18, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline from body and deduped citations.)

      District 1: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 18, 2025

      Blood on the Streets

      Just last week, a city worker was slashed by an e-bike rider at Broadway and Cedar. He was fixing a street sign when the cyclist nearly hit his truck. Words were exchanged. The cyclist pulled a box cutter and cut the worker’s arm and back before fleeing. The city called it an “abhorrent assault of a NYC DOT employee who performs critical work to keep our city moving”. No arrest. No peace.

      On July 15, a van towing a food cart broke loose near 42nd and 10th. It struck a parked car with a woman and a child inside. Police found 76 propane tanks and 75 gallons of gasoline in the van. The driver was arrested for reckless endangerment. The firefighters forced entry. The woman and child went to the hospital. The street could have gone up in flames.

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      In the last 12 months, District 1 saw 2 deaths, 16 serious injuries, and 736 people hurt in 1,560 crashes.

      Most of the blood comes from cars and trucks. SUVs alone killed 2 people and injured 150 more. Trucks killed 4. Bikes and mopeds are not blameless, but the carnage is driven by heavy metal. The old, the young, the ones just trying to cross the street—no one is spared. The numbers are not just numbers. They are bodies on the pavement.

      What Has Marte Done?

      Council Member Christopher Marte has voted for bills to clear abandoned vehicles, add daylight at crosswalks, and warn taxi passengers about dooring. He co-sponsored laws to ban fake plates, require speed humps near parks, and boost pedestrian lighting. He backed a bill to prohibit standing or parking a vehicle within 20 feet of a crosswalk, aiming to keep sightlines clear. He stood with residents calling for urgent changes on Canal Street, saying the time is “long overdue to take action in response to the dire conditions”.

      But the pace is slow. The streets are not safe. The dead do not get a second chance.

      Call to Action: Demand More, Demand Now

      Call Council Member Marte. Call the Mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected space for people, not cars. Demand action before another name is added to the list.

      The city has the power. The council has the votes. The blood is on their hands until the killing stops.

      District 1 belongs to borough Manhattan, assembly district AD 61 and state senate district SD 26. NYC Open Data

      It includes the Financial District-Battery Park City, Tribeca-Civic Center, The Battery-Governors Island-Ellis Island-Liberty Island, Soho-Little Italy-Hudson Square, Chinatown-Two Bridges, Lower East Side, Manhattan CB2, Manhattan CB3, and Manhattan CB1 neighborhoods.       ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in District 1?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm, with SUVs and trucks together responsible for 7 deaths and hundreds of injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds caused injuries but no deaths. Bikes were involved in 1 death and dozens of injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes preventable or just 'accidents'?
      Crashes are preventable. Safer street design, lower speed limits, and better enforcement can save lives.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and enforce laws for lower speed limits, clear sightlines, protected bike lanes, and crack down on reckless driving and illegal parking.
