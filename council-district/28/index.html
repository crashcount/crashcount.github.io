      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 28?

      Deadly Miles, Empty Promises: Demand Safe Streets Now

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 27, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 28: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 28, 2025

      Blood on the Boulevard

      In District 28, the numbers do not lie. Ten dead. Thirty-six left with serious injuries. More than 3,300 hurt since 2022. The streets are not safe for the old, the young, or anyone on foot or bike. Linden Boulevard, Belt Parkway, Rockaway, Lefferts—names that echo with sirens and loss.

      A man with a cane tried to cross Linden. He never made it home. “Today is a sad day. We lost a brother, father, son, uncle, and cousin. [He] has been snuffed from us by a hit and runner driver,” said his family. The driver kept going. The street kept moving.

      A cyclist was left broken and alone on 115th Avenue. Police found him unconscious. The driver vanished into the night. “They are now looking for evidence to help them track down the driver,” reported ABC7.

      Who Pays the Price?

      Pedestrians and cyclists bear the brunt. Cars and SUVs killed or maimed most. Trucks, buses, motorcycles, and mopeds added to the toll. Children, elders, workers—none are spared. The numbers are relentless: in the last year alone, 8 killed, 16 seriously hurt, over 1,000 injured. Every number is a name erased from a family table.

      What Has Been Done—and What Hasn’t

      Council Member Adrienne Adams has voted for some safety bills. She backed the citywide greenway plan and supported legalizing jaywalking, a move that ends the old habit of blaming the victim. She called for more traffic calming in senior zones: “It’s important that the Council advance equitable policies… to ensure that all New Yorkers can live, work and commute on safer streets.”

      But the council has not brought a citywide 20 mph speed limit to a vote. The streets remain fast, wide, and deadly. Promises are made. The dead stay dead.

      What Next? Demand Action

      This is not fate. It is policy. Call Council Member Adrienne Adams. Demand a vote for a citywide 20 mph limit. Demand protected bike lanes and crosswalks that put people first. Join Families for Safe Streets. Stand up. Do not wait for another name to be added to the count.

      Take action now.

      Half Of NYC Taxis Now Wheelchair Accessible
      ▸

      Half the city’s taxis now take wheelchairs. Ten years of pressure forced change. The fleet stands as the most accessible in the nation. Policy, not luck, drove this. Disabled riders gain ground. Streets shift. Access grows.

      amNY reported on June 5, 2025, that New York City’s Taxi and Limousine Commission (TLC) reached a milestone: 50% of active taxis are now wheelchair accessible. TLC Commissioner David Do said, “We made that today. It is the most accessible fleet in the entire country.” This achievement follows a decade-long push by disability advocates and a 2013 settlement requiring half the medallion fleet to be accessible. The transition faced delays, including the COVID-19 pandemic and medallion owner financial struggles. Policy changes, such as alternating accessible vehicles and funding support, drove the shift. The article highlights systemic progress, not individual driver actions, and underscores the impact of sustained advocacy and regulatory pressure.
