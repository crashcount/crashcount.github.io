      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 20?

      Three Dead, No Answers: Queens Streets Keep Claiming Lives While Leaders Stall

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 20: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 17, 2025

      The Toll on the Ground

      Three dead. Twenty-one left with serious injuries. In the past year, District 20 saw 1,181 crashes. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care about age. Two of the dead were over 75. One was a woman, 78, crossing Northern Boulevard at night. The driver did not stop. Police said, “A 78-year-old woman was fatally struck by a hit-and-run driver as she crossed a Queens street.” Her name was not released. The street remembers her.

      Children are not spared. In March, a car struck a man and a child at 32nd Avenue and 138th Street. The man was pinned under the car. The child, between eight and ten, was also hit. Police found them both. “Police responded…and found an adult man pinned under a vehicle.”

      Who Bears the Weight

      SUVs and sedans do most of the killing. Seven deaths and 19 serious injuries came from cars and SUVs. Trucks and buses hurt more. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes add to the toll. The street is a gauntlet for anyone not behind a wheel.

      Sandra Ung: Steps and Silences

      Council Member Sandra Ung has signed on to bills for more lighting, speed humps, and safer crosswalks. She voted for a citywide greenway plan. She co-sponsored a study for tactile paving to help the blind. But when the Council voted to end jaywalking enforcement—a law that stops blaming the victim—Ung was absent. She backed a bill to ban e-scooter share, despite no deaths or serious injuries from the program, and despite city data showing tens of thousands of safe rides.

      The Road Ahead

      The crisis is not random. It is built into the street. Residents must demand more. Call for a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected crossings and real accountability for drivers. Contact Council Member Ung. Call the Mayor. Join Families for Safe Streets and Transportation Alternatives. Do not wait for another name to become a number.

      Ung Pushes Queens Greenway Funding Despite Harmful Delays
      ▸

      Forty years. No greenway. Queens waits as city stalls. Parks and DOT talk, but no cash. Council Member Sandra Ung vows to fight for funding. Cyclists and families left exposed. The path remains a promise. Danger and delay linger.

      This action concerns the long-delayed Queens Greenway, discussed in a public statement on February 17, 2022. The project, first planned in 1977 and 1988, would connect Flushing Meadows Corona Park to Fort Totten with a safe route for pedestrians and cyclists. The Parks Department and DOT presented a new vision but admitted that none of the $105 million needed is funded. Council Member Sandra Ung, representing parts of the greenway, said, 'I want to finally see it become a reality,' and stressed the need for accessible open spaces. Still, she could not commit any specific funding. Cycling advocates criticized the city for asking residents to pick favorite sub-projects, calling it a tactic to lower expectations. The city offered no timeline. Vulnerable road users remain at risk as the greenway languishes.
