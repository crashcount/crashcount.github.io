The Street Keeps Taking. Give Hanif the Power to Stop It.

Jan 1, 2022 - Apr 2, 2025

From January 2022 to April 2025, eleven people died on the streets of Council District 39. Twenty more were seriously hurt. They were crushed crossing the street, thrown from bikes, struck while walking home. Some were children. Some were elderly. All are gone.

A 16-year-old boy on a Hurley e-bike was killed by a turning box truck on Ditmas Avenue. A 74-year-old woman died crossing against the light in Borough Park. An 83-year-old woman was struck by a dump truck in Gowanus. The truck was undamaged. Her body bore all of it.

Katherine Harris was killed by a drunk driver doing 72 mph through a red light in Cobble Hill. She was steps from home. Her mother said, “I lost part of me.”

Council Member Shahana Hanif has not looked away. She stood at the die-in on 9th Street, where Sarah Schick was killed. She pushed for safety fixes at deadly intersections. She fought for Sammy’s Law, to lower speed limits. She won changes on Atlantic Avenue, where too many have died.

But the killing hasn’t stopped. In the last 12 months alone, three more people died. Four more were seriously injured. The street keeps taking.

Speed kills. Lowering limits saves lives. Council Member Hanif has shown the will. Now she needs the power. Call her. Write her. Demand she push harder. Demand the city act faster.

The dead can’t speak. But we can.
