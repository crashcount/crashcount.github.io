Crashes in District 39
May 04, 2022 7:50 PM
Union Street near Clinton, Brooklyn — A taxi struck an eastbound e-scooter. The rider, 40, flew off. No helmet. Blood streamed from his arm. The brakes had failed. He lay conscious on the pavement, staring up at the sky.

The Street Still Takes. Who Will Stop It?

Council District Council District 39: Jan 1, 2022 - Apr 2, 2025

In Brooklyn’s District 39, the toll keeps rising. Since 2022, eleven people have died. Twenty more were seriously injured. The dead include a 16-year-old crushed by a box truck on Ditmas Avenue, an 83-year-old woman struck by a dump truck at Bond and Butler, and a 74-year-old killed crossing 14th Avenue. Their names are not in the data. But their blood is in the street.

The pain is not abstract. It is a crushed leg on 5th Avenue. A head split open on Union Street. A body broken at Park Circle. The numbers say 4,168 crashes. But the truth is in the silence that follows.

Council Member Shahana Hanif has not looked away. She stood on 9th Street after Sarah Schick was killed by a box truck at a dangerous bike lane gap. She pushed for Sammy’s Law, which passed in 2024, giving the city authority to lower speed limits. She fought for safety upgrades on Atlantic Avenue, where Katherine Harris was killed by a drunk driver going 72 mph. Harris died steps from her home. Her mother said, “I lost part of me.”

The city has tools. Lower the speed limit. Redesign the streets. Enforce the law. But the work is not done. In 2025, crashes and injuries are up compared to last year. The dead are still dying.

Call Council Member Hanif. Tell her the work must go faster. Tell her the street still takes. And we are not done fighting.
