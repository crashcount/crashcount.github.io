      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 48?

      Blood on Avenue U: Vernikov Blocks Safety While Families Grieve

      Written July 10, 2025 (Revised July 17, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 48: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 10, 2025

      The Toll on Our Streets

      Fifteen people are dead. Seventeen more are left with injuries that will not heal. In the last three and a half years, District 48 has seen 3,673 crashes. These are not just numbers. They are bodies on the pavement, families waiting in hospital halls, shoes left in the crosswalk.

      Last week, a 90-year-old man tried to cross Avenue U at East 14th Street. A blue moped hit him. The rider stopped, looked, then left him dying in the street. “He hit the guy and he left,” said a witness. The man died at the hospital. His family waits for answers. “We hope the authorities can find the person who did this. We are also concerned about the motorcycles running rampant in crowded residential areas,” said the victim’s grandson.

      This is not rare. In the last year alone, 431 people have been hurt in crashes here. Six were seriously injured. Last year, six died. The dead are old and young. The killers are cars, trucks, SUVs, mopeds, bikes. The street does not care.

      Leadership: Action and Evasion

      Council Member Inna Vernikov has taken some steps. She co-sponsored a bill to add speed humps near parks. She backed a reward for tips on hit-and-run drivers. But she has also stood in the way. She voted against the law that ended jaywalking tickets, a law that stops blaming the dead for their own deaths. She opposed expanding speed cameras in school zones, even as her own car racked up 23 school-zone speeding tickets. She fought to pause new bike lanes, calling them a “woke agenda” while people bled in the street.

      What Comes Next

      This is not fate. It is policy. Every delay, every excuse, every vote against proven safety measures means another family gets the call. Call Council Member Vernikov. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes. Demand that the city use the power it has to save lives. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Vernikov Mentioned in Critique of Harmful Reckless Driver Program
      ▸

      The Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program barely touches reckless drivers. Over 22,000 cars rack up tickets, but only 630 drivers take the safety course. Just 12 vehicles seized. Most repeat offenders keep driving. The law’s weak language leaves streets exposed.

      Local Law 36 of 2020 created the Dangerous Vehicle Abatement Program, overseen by the Department of Transportation. The law, passed October 26, 2020, aimed to educate repeat traffic offenders. It requires a course for owners with 15 speeding or 5 red-light tickets, but only if DOT chooses. As of November 28, 2022, just 630 drivers completed the course, and only 12 vehicles were seized. The law’s summary promised to 'educate vehicle owners about the dangers resulting from vehicle operators exceeding posted speed limits and failing to comply with traffic signals, including the potential to cause injury or death.' Council Member Inna Vernikov was named as an example of a driver reaching the threshold. Elizabeth Adams of Transportation Alternatives called for more teeth, saying, 'new data shows a clear need for additional investments in the program to get dangerous vehicles off our streets.' The program’s weak enforcement leaves thousands of reckless drivers unpunished, endangering vulnerable road users.
