      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 35?

      No More Blood on Eastern Parkway: Demand Real Safety Now

      Written June 29, 2025

      District 35: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 29, 2025

      Children, Elders, and the Unprotected

      The streets of District 35 do not forgive. In the last year, four people died and 678 were injured in 1,275 crashes. The dead include a child, a centenarian, and a man crushed by a truck. The living carry wounds you cannot see.

      On June 28, an eight-year-old boy was killed crossing Eastern Parkway with his sister. He did not make it to the other side. The driver stayed at the scene. No arrest. “They were walking, he was crossing the street with his sister. She’s 17-years-old,” said a neighbor. The street stayed open. The city moved on.

      Just weeks before, a 101-year-old woman was killed in a crosswalk. The driver failed to yield. She had the signal. He had the car. She is gone.

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      Seventeen people have died in District 35 since 2022. More than 2,700 have been injured. SUVs and cars are the main weapons—three deaths and over 400 injuries. Trucks and buses killed one, motorcycles and mopeds left dozens more hurt. Bikes injured 29. The old, the young, the ones on foot—they pay the price.

      Council Member Hudson: Progress and Gaps

      Council Member Crystal Hudson has backed bills to daylight crosswalks, speed up protected bike lanes, and legalize jaywalking. She co-sponsored the SAFE Streets Act and voted to end jaywalking enforcement, a law that removes a tool often used to blame victims and target the vulnerable. She called for safety on Atlantic Avenue but stopped short of demanding a full redesign. The city delays. The bodies pile up.

      On illegal parking, Hudson supported the crackdown on placard abuse, a move that improves safety and accessibility for pedestrians and cyclists by reclaiming public space.

      But the streets are not safe. The fixes are slow. The deaths keep coming.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      Call Council Member Hudson. Call the Mayor. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes and daylighted crosswalks. Demand action before another child dies.

      The city will not save you unless you force it to move.

      The New York City Council is the city’s legislative body. It passes laws, oversees the budget, and holds city agencies accountable. NYC Council – Legistar

      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, assembly district AD 43 and state senate district SD 20. NYC Open Data

      It includes the Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Prospect Heights, Crown Heights (North), Crown Heights (South), Brooklyn CB8, Brooklyn CB2, and Brooklyn CB9 neighborhoods.       ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in District 35?
      Cars and trucks caused the most harm—three deaths and over 400 injuries. Motorcycles and mopeds injured dozens, while bikes were involved in 29 injuries. NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not random. The same streets see the same tragedies, year after year. Better design and enforcement can prevent deaths.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to make streets safer?
      They can pass and fund laws for lower speed limits, protected bike lanes, daylighted crosswalks, and enforcement against reckless driving and illegal parking.
