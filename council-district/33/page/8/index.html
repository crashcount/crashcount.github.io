      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 33?

      Eight Dead, Still Waiting: Restler’s Promises Don’t Stop Cars

      Written July 31, 2025

      District 33: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 31, 2025

      The Bodies Keep Coming

      Eight dead. Nearly a thousand hurt. In the last year alone, District 33 saw 2,119 crashes. Twenty-five people were left with injuries so grave they may never walk right again. The dead do not get second chances. The living carry scars.

      Just weeks ago, a cyclist was struck on McGuinness Boulevard. The driver turned, the bike lane was only paint. The city had promised protection. The city did not deliver. “The fact is that this wouldn’t be a problem if Mayor Adams had just done what he had promised to do, which was, make all of McGuinness safe,” said Kevin LaCherra. “The road diet works where it’s been installed and it’s needed for the entire corridor before this happens again.” said Kevin LaCherra

      A few blocks away, a 74-year-old man was killed at Tillary and Jay. An e-bike, a bus, a crosswalk. No protection. No margin for error. NYC Open Data

      The Numbers Do Not Lie

      Cars and SUVs did the most harm: 411 crashes, 3 dead, 10 seriously hurt. Trucks and buses killed 3 more. Bikes killed 2. Motorcycles and mopeds left dozens bleeding. The street does not care who you are. It only cares who is bigger, faster, heavier.

      What Has Restler Done?

      Council Member Lincoln Restler has moved on some fronts. He voted to clear abandoned cars from crosswalks and sidewalks, a small step for sightlines and safety. He led a law to put warning decals on taxi doors, a warning for cyclists about to be doored. He co-sponsored bills for better lighting, faster removal of hazards, and more data. He pressed the NYPD on racial bias in traffic stops, calling the disparity “extreme.” pressed the NYPD on racial bias

      But the blood keeps flowing. The bills are slow. The paint fades. The bodies do not.

      Call to Action

      Do not wait for another child to die. Call Council Member Restler. Demand protected bike lanes, daylighted crosswalks, and a 20 mph speed limit. Demand action that saves lives, not just headlines. The dead cannot call. The living must.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York City Council and how does it work?
      The New York City Council is the city’s lawmaking body. It passes local laws, oversees city agencies, and represents the interests of New Yorkers in each district.
      ▸ Where does District 33 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Brooklyn, assembly district AD 50 and state senate district SD 18.
      ▸ Which areas are in District 33?
      It includes the Greenpoint, Williamsburg, South Williamsburg, Brooklyn Heights, Downtown Brooklyn-Dumbo-Boerum Hill, Brooklyn Navy Yard, Brooklyn CB1, and Brooklyn CB2 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Assembly Districts AD 50, AD 52, and AD 57, and State Senate Districts SD 18, SD 25, SD 26, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in District 33?
      Cars and SUVs caused the most harm: 3 deaths and 10 serious injuries. Trucks and buses killed 3 more. Bikes killed 2 people and seriously injured 1. Motorcycles and mopeds caused 15 minor and 8 moderate injuries.
      ▸ Are these crashes just 'accidents'?
      No. Most crashes are preventable with better street design, lower speeds, and enforcement. They are not random—they are the result of choices and policies.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass laws for protected bike lanes, daylighted crosswalks, lower speed limits, and enforce against reckless driving. They can also hold city agencies accountable for slow or incomplete safety projects.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
