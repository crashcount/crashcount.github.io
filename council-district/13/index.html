      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 13?

      Nine Dead, Hundreds Hurt—How Many More Before We Put People Before Cars?

      Written June 22, 2025 (Revised June 22, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      District 13: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 22, 2025

      The Bodies in the Road

      In District 13, the numbers do not lie. Nine people killed. Thirty-five left with injuries so severe they change a life. In the last twelve months alone, two more deaths, fifteen more serious injuries, and over 800 hurt. The streets run through Throgs Neck, Pelham Bay, Morris Park, and the pain runs with them.

      On the Hutchinson River Parkway, before dawn, a Honda Pilot slams into a scooter. An 18-year-old woman is thrown onto the asphalt. She does not get up. “An 18-year-old was in critical condition after being thrown from a moped in the hit-and-run, according to NYPD” (CBS New York). The driver flees. Six others go to the hospital. The road is quiet again, but nothing is the same.

      A year before, a 64-year-old cyclist is killed at Pierce Avenue and Williamsbridge Road. A pedestrian, 66, is crushed on the parkway. A 40-year-old man dies on Westchester Avenue. The list is long. The pain is longer.

      Who Pays the Price

      Most of the dead and maimed are not behind the wheel. Pedestrians and cyclists take the brunt. Cars and SUVs are the main weapons—three killed, six seriously hurt, 292 more injured by these machines in just three years. Trucks and buses take another life. Motorcycles, mopeds, bikes—they hurt too, but the numbers are smaller. The risk is not shared equally.

      What Has Been Done—And What Has Not

      Council Member Kristy Marmorato has signed on to bills for more street lighting and faster utility pole removal. These are steps, but small ones. She voted against safer medians (NYC Council), and against ending jaywalking enforcement (NYC Council), a law that would have protected those on foot from needless tickets and blame. She calls congestion pricing a “cash grab” (amNY), fighting a measure proven to cut traffic and save lives.

      She defends parking mandates, saying, “We live in a transit desert where cars are a necessity for daily activities” (Streetsblog NYC). But every new parking spot is another reason for a car to be on the road, another risk for a child crossing the street.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      The blood on the street is not an act of God. It is a choice. Call your council member. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand real protection for people walking and biking. Demand an end to policies that put cars first and people last. The dead cannot speak. You must.

      Take action now.

      It belongs to borough Bronx. It also overlaps with Assembly District 80, Assembly District 82, State Senate District 33, State Senate District 34, and State Senate District 36. NYC Open Data

      It includes the Throgs Neck-Schuylerville, Pelham Bay-Country Club-City Island, Hart Island, Ferry Point Park-St. Raymond Cemetery, Pelham Parkway-Van Nest, Morris Park, Pelham Gardens, Hutchinson Metro Center, Pelham Bay Park, and Bronx CB10 neighborhoods.       ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in District 13?
      Most injuries and deaths were caused by Cars and Trucks (4 deaths, 7 serious injuries, 316 total injuries), followed by Motorcycles and Mopeds (no deaths, 15 injuries), and Bikes (no deaths, 4 injuries). NYC Open Data
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or are they preventable?
      Crashes are not inevitable. Safer street design, lower speed limits, and enforcement can prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to reduce traffic violence?
      They can pass laws for lower speed limits, safer street design, and prioritize people over cars. They can also support enforcement that targets dangerous driving, not vulnerable road users.

      Int 1142-2024, now in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure, was introduced December 19, 2024.
