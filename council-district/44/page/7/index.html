Ocean Parkway Runs Red—And City Hall Looks Away

Council District 44: Jan 1, 2022 - Apr 2, 2025

In Council District 44, the streets do not forgive. Since 2022, fifteen people have died in crashes. Twenty-one more were seriously hurt. Some bled in the crosswalk. Some never made it to school. Some were children.

The deadliest day came just after Simcha Felder reclaimed his old Council seat. On March 29, 2025, a sedan tore through a red light on Ocean Parkway. A mother and her three children were crossing with the signal. Three died. One, age four, was critically injured. The car had over 90 violations, including 20 speeding tickets. The driver’s license was suspended.

In the past year alone, six people have died in traffic crashes in the district. Nine more were seriously injured. A 73-year-old woman was killed in a crosswalk. A 67-year-old man died beneath a pickup truck’s headlights. A one-year-old boy was struck and left bleeding on 59th Street. The numbers do not lie. They only count the bodies.

Council leadership has not kept pace with the blood on the asphalt. Kalman Yeger, who held the seat until early 2025, opposed speed cameras and voted against daylighting. He called safety reforms a cash grab. Felder, his successor, has remained silent after the Ocean Parkway killings. He once fought to raise speed limits on that very road.

This is not fate. It is policy. Lowering speed limits saves lives. So does redesigning streets. So does daylighting corners. The tools exist. The bodies pile up.

Email Council Member Simcha Felder at District44@council.nyc.gov. Tell him: the dead can’t vote. But the living can.
