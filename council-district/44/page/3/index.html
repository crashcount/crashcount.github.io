      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 44?

      Eighteen Dead, Still No Action: Blood on Ocean Parkway

      Written June 27, 2025 (Revised July 7, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 44: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 27, 2025

      The Toll in Blood and Silence

      Eighteen dead. Nearly two thousand injured. That is the cost of traffic violence in District 44 since 2022 (NYC Open Data). The dead include children, elders, mothers, and sons. These numbers are not just statistics—they are empty chairs at dinner, shoes left by the curb.

      In the last year alone, eight people died and over 600 were hurt. Three of the dead were under 18. A five-year-old girl, her eight-year-old sister, and their mother were killed crossing Ocean Parkway with the light. The driver kept going. The city kept moving (NYC Open Data).

      Recent Crashes: No Mercy for the Young

      On June 23, a 17-year-old boy died on East 8th Street. He was riding a moped with a friend. The moped hit a car turning left. Both boys were thrown. The driver stayed. The boy died at Maimonides. Police said, “Jhoan later died at the hospital.”

      Two days earlier, Joel Mota, 22, was killed riding a moped in Bay Ridge. The driver who hit him was drunk and unlicensed. Joel’s brother said, “He was a hardworking man. He had a lot of love for his family.”

      Leadership: Votes Against the Living

      Council Member Simcha Felder has a record of opposing life-saving safety measures. He voted against 24-hour speed cameras, even as they cut speeding and deaths where installed. After a speeding driver killed a mother and two girls on Ocean Parkway, Felder said, “Some have wasted no time using this tragedy as an opportunity to advance their agenda. There is a time to act but there is also a time to mourn…”

      Felder has blocked lower speed limits, fought street redesigns, and opposed speed limiters for repeat offenders. He has not led on bills to protect children or seniors crossing the street. The silence is loud. The deaths keep coming.

      What Now: Demand Action, Not Excuses

      Every death is preventable. The bodies pile up while leaders drag their feet. Call Council Member Felder. Demand 20 mph speed limits, more speed cameras, and real street redesigns. Join Transportation Alternatives and Families for Safe Streets. Do not wait for another child to die. Act now.

      Int 0346-2024
      Yeger votes no on jaywalking bill, opposing a proven safety improvement.
      ▸

      Council passed a law letting pedestrians cross streets anywhere, at any time. Jaywalking is now legal. No more tickets for crossing outside crosswalks or against signals. The law shifts blame from walkers to drivers. Streets belong to people again.

      Int 0346-2024, now Local Law 98 of 2024, was enacted by the City Council on October 26, 2024, after passing the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure. The bill, titled "A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to pedestrian crossing guidelines and right of way," lets pedestrians cross roadways at any point, even against traffic signals. It erases penalties for so-called jaywalking. Sponsors include Tiffany Cabán (primary), Lincoln Restler, Crystal Hudson, Shahana K. Hanif, Julie Won, Mercedes Narcisse, Darlene Mealy, Erik D. Bottcher, and Farah N. Louis. The Council voted overwhelmingly in favor. The law requires the Department of Transportation to educate all road users about these new rights. By ending jaywalking enforcement, the city removes a tool often used to blame victims and target the vulnerable. The law marks a shift: streets are for people, not just cars.
