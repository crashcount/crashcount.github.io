      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 2?

      No More Body Counts: Make District 2 Streets Safe Now

      Written July 11, 2025 (Revised July 11, 2025 – Fixed broken link, cleaned citations)

      District 2: Jan 1, 2022 - Jul 11, 2025

      Blood on the Asphalt: Recent Crashes in District 2

      The streets of District 2 do not forgive. Since 2022, 15 people have died and 28 have been seriously injured in traffic crashes here (NYC Open Data). In the last year alone, the toll stands at 2 dead, 15 seriously hurt, 524 injured—each number a life changed or ended (NYC Open Data).

      The dead are not just numbers. On June 16, 2025, a pedestrian was killed by a bus at 3rd Avenue and East 28th. The cause: driver inattention (NYC Open Data CrashID 4820937). A 79-year-old woman died at Bowery and East 4th, struck by a taxi. The report lists her as “crossing against signal.” Only one person died that day. It was not the driver (NYC Open Data CrashID 4735570).

      Cars and trucks do the most damage. In three years, they killed 7 and injured nearly 300 pedestrians. Buses and trucks killed 3 more. Bikes, mopeds, and motorcycles injured dozens, but killed none (NYC Open Data).

      Leadership: Progress and Gaps

      Council Member Carlina Rivera has backed bills that matter. She voted to legalize jaywalking, ending a law that punished the vulnerable and left drivers untouched. She co-sponsored the SAFE Streets Act, pushing for lower speed limits and more rights for crash victims. She voted yes on daylighting, a law to clear parked cars from crosswalks and give children and elders a fighting chance to be seen.

      But the crisis is not over. The city can now set a 20 mph speed limit. It has not. The dead keep coming. The numbers do not lie.

      The System Is the Killer

      “Completion of the Queens Boulevard redesign has reduced fatalities by 68% since work began in 2015,” the city said. The lesson is plain: street design saves lives. Enforcement and education are not enough. The city must act, not just talk.

      Call to Action: Demand More, Demand Now

      Every day of delay is another family broken. Call Council Member Rivera. Call the Mayor. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes and daylight at every corner. Join Families for Safe Streets and Transportation Alternatives. Do not wait for another name on the list.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York City Council and how does it work?
      The New York City Council is the city’s legislative body. It passes laws, oversees agencies, and represents neighborhoods across the five boroughs.
      ▸ Where does District 2 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, assembly district AD 65 and state senate district SD 27.
      ▸ Which areas are in District 2?
      It includes the Greenwich Village, Lower East Side, East Village, Gramercy, Manhattan CB2, and Manhattan CB3 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Assembly Districts AD 65, AD 66, and AD 74, and State Senate Districts SD 27, SD 28, and SD 59.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in District 2?
      Cars and SUVs killed 7 and injured 287 pedestrians. Trucks and buses killed 3 and injured 28. Motorcycles and mopeds injured 13. Bikes injured 64 and killed none (NYC Open Data).
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or can they be prevented?
      Crashes are not random. Street redesigns, lower speed limits, and better enforcement have been proven to prevent deaths and injuries.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to reduce traffic violence?
      They can pass laws for lower speed limits, fund protected bike lanes, ban parking near crosswalks, and demand street redesigns that protect people, not just cars.
      ▸ What is CrashCount?
      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
