      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 10?

      No More Excuses: Blood on the Streets, Power in City Hall

      Written June 17, 2025 (Revised June 25, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 10: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 25, 2025

      The Numbers That Don’t Lie

      One death. Sixteen serious injuries. Over 500 hurt. That’s just the last year in District 10. The names are gone from the data, but the wounds remain. A cyclist crushed on West 181st. A 15-year-old thrown from a moped on Saint Nicholas. A woman’s arm torn off by a distracted driver. These are not accidents. They are the cost of inaction.

      When Help Never Comes

      In April, Francisco Guzman Parra burned to death in his car after a police chase ended in flames on Dyckman Street. Officers arrived, saw the fire, and drove away. No help. No call for aid. His stepmother said, “They could’ve helped get him out, get him assistance.” The family waited for answers. The city waited for justice. The officers were suspended, but the damage was done.

      Leadership: Progress and Pitfalls

      Council Member Carmen De La Rosa has backed real safety upgrades. She pushed for protected bike lanes and safer crossings on the Washington Bridge, saying, “I’ve never been so afraid to walk a bridge on foot… it was an accident waiting to happen.” She co-sponsored bills for daylighting crosswalks and speed humps near parks. She voted to legalize jaywalking, ending a law that punished the vulnerable for crossing the street.

      But De La Rosa also sponsored a bill to slash taxi injury insurance, a move that would leave crash victims with less support and more pain. Survivors warned the Council, “The minimum $50,000 no fault insurance was exhausted within the first week or so of my hospitalization.” The bill is still alive. The risk remains.

      What Comes Next

      The crisis is not over. Every week brings new crashes. Every day, another family waits for a call that never should come. The city has the power to lower speed limits, redesign streets, and protect the most vulnerable. It is time to use it.

      Call your council member. Demand safer streets. Demand action, not excuses.

      Take action now.

According to the police report, she was crossing with the signal when the e-bike hit her, causing a severe injury that resulted in amputation. The rider did not stop.       ▸

      A violent crash unfolded at Amsterdam Avenue and West 172nd Street. A 29-year-old man riding an e-bike collided with two sedans. According to the police report, 'Speed, confusion, and failure to yield carved the moment.' The e-bike rider was ejected and suffered a severe head injury with heavy bleeding. The crash data lists 'Failure to Yield Right-of-Way,' 'Passing or Lane Usage Improper,' and 'Unsafe Speed' as contributing factors. The sedan occupants, both men aged 60 and 30, were not reported as injured. The report does not mention helmet use or signaling as factors. The crash left blood on the asphalt and exposed the dangers of speed and improper lane use on city streets.
