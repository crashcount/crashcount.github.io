      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 37?

      District 37: hit-and-run on Broadway, a right turn on Wyckoff, and a map that bleeds

      Written August 26, 2025 (Revised August 30, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 37: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 26, 2025

      A man lay in the road on Broadway at Suydam just before 4 AM. Detectives said a northbound car hit him and dragged him more than 50 feet. The driver kept going. Police said, “A driver struck and killed a 47‑year‑old pedestrian… then left the scene.” He died there. Gothamist. NY Daily News.

      At Wyckoff and DeKalb, a van turned right and a man in the crosswalk never made it across. The record lists “Failure to Yield Right‑of‑Way.” He had the walk. He was 59. NYC Open Data.

      On Pitkin at Pennsylvania, a moped and a sedan met before dawn. The rider was ejected and died. Speed shows up in the file. NYC Open Data.

      Where the bodies are

      Since 2022 in District 37, pedestrians have suffered 4 deaths and 589 injuries. No cyclist deaths in that span. NYC Open Data.

      Trucks and buses are few, but they hit hard. They are tied to 2 pedestrian deaths here. Vans too. Cars and SUVs kill most often. NYC Open Data.

      Deaths spike at the edges of the day. 6 AM and 6 PM each saw two deaths. Single deaths stack at 8–11 AM and again 5–10 PM. The clock keeps time; the street keeps score. NYC Open Data.

      Corners we already know

      The maps circle the same names: Bushwick Ave, Central Ave, Crescent St, Atlantic Ave.

      Failure to yield is in the file at Wyckoff and DeKalb. Right turn, man in the crosswalk, dead. CrashID 4764527.

      City Hall has a bill to clear the sightlines at crosswalks. It would ban parking within 20 feet and order DOT to daylight 1,000 corners a year. The Council laid it over in April. The cars still sit on the corners. Legistar.

      What breaks the pattern

      Start with the corners that blind you. Daylight them. Harden the right turns where walkers die, including Wyckoff at DeKalb. Set truck turns and routes that keep heavy vehicles off the tightest blocks. Target the repeat spots after dark.

      Then set brakes citywide. Lower the default speed limit. Require speed‑limiters for repeat speeders. Do both, and fewer families get the 3 AM call.

      Act here. Take action.

      ▸

      Jul 30 - Council moves to ban parking near corners. Sightlines clear. Crashes drop. Pedestrians and cyclists breathe easier. DOT doubts linger. Advocates press on. Streets could change fast.

      ""Universal daylighting is a proven, effective way to make our streets safer for pedestrians, bikers and drivers."" -- Sandy Nurse

      On July 30, 2025, the NYC Council Progressive Caucus made a major push for Intro 1138, a bill to ban parking within 20 feet of intersections. The measure sits with the Transportation Committee. Council Member Julie Won introduced it after deadly crashes in her district. Eighteen caucus members back the bill, aiming to force a vote this year. The matter: 'eliminate parking within 20 feet of an intersection.' Council Member Sandy Nurse called daylighting 'a proven, effective way to make our streets safer.' DOT raised doubts about non-hardened daylighting, but safety analysts say restricting parking near intersections improves visibility, reduces collisions, and protects people on foot and bike.
