      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 42?

      District 42: Deadly Speed, Silent Streets

      Written August 24, 2025 (Revised August 24, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 42: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 24, 2025

      Another driver. Same ending.

      • A 30-year-old man died at Kings Hwy and Church Ave. The car was going fast. The city crash log lists “Unsafe Speed.” He was hit at the intersection. He died there. NYC Open Data records it as CrashID 4788144.
      • A 21-year-old woman died on Gateway Dr just after midnight. The record lists “Driver Inattention/Distraction” and “Unsafe Speed.” She was not at an intersection. She died. CrashID 4827641, per NYC Open Data.

      These are not outliers. In District 42 since 2022, pedestrians suffered 681 injuries and 11 deaths; cyclists saw 208 injuries and one death. Cars and SUVs did most of the harm. NYC Open Data.

      Belt Parkway, Linden, Pennsylvania

      • The Belt Parkway shows two deaths and 300 injuries tied to crashes in this district slice. A 41-year-old driver died there in February 2025. The log names “Unsafe Speed.” CrashID 4793436.
      • Linden Boulevard racks up 375 injuries. Pennsylvania Avenue shows two deaths and 158 injuries. These are wide roads. Drivers go straight. People bleed at the edges.

      When the clock turns ugly

      Late day and night hit hardest. Deaths stack up at 1 a.m., 4 a.m., the rush from 1–2 p.m., then 6–9 p.m., and again at 9–11 p.m. Injuries peak through the late afternoon into evening. This is our pattern. District 42 hourly data.

      What breaks bodies here

      “Other” factors lead this district’s toll with 17 deaths and 1,191 injuries logged. After that come “Vulnerable road user error,” “Inattention/Distraction,” and “Disregarded traffic control.” “Unsafe Speed” is present in deaths and injuries across multiple fatal files. Contributing factors.

      Two cases, one truth

      • A 26-year-old front-seat passenger died at Blake Ave and Van Sinderen Ave. The record lists “Unsafe Speed” and “Traffic Control Disregarded.” The sedan driver was unlicensed. CrashID 4795300, from NYC Open Data.
      • At Seaview Ave and Pennsylvania Ave, a 28-year-old driver in a Porsche SUV died after hitting a pickup. The file lists “Driver Inattention/Distraction” and “Unsafe Speed.” CrashID 4816372, per NYC Open Data.

      Three corners. One fix.

      Start with daylighting and hardened turns on Linden Boulevard and Pennsylvania Avenue. Add leading pedestrian intervals. Lock in no-turn-on-red. Target late-day and night with speed cameras and plate enforcement. The Council already voted to clear derelict cars fast, a basic sightline fix. Banks voted yes. Council file Int 0857-2024.

      Officials know what works — do they?

      Albany gave New York City the power to set safer speeds under Sammy’s Law. The city can lower residential limits. Do it. Our own published guide lays it out and cites the data on repeat speeders and fatal risk. See our Take Action page.

      • “Sammy’s Law gave NYC the power to set safer speeds.” Take Action.
      • “Repeat speeders keep killing people in our district.” Take Action.

      Stop the worst, now

      A small group of drivers does big harm. Citywide research shows repeat camera offenders pose outsized risk. Mandating intelligent speed assistance for these drivers is on the table in Albany. The path is in our Take Action page. The deaths above show the stakes.

      A hard number to carry home

      • Year-to-date in this district: 1,413 crashes, 1,013 injuries, 8 deaths, up from 2 deaths at this point last year. That is a 300% jump. District stats.

      Do one thing today. Ask the city to set 20 mph. Ask the state to rein in repeat speeders. Start here: Take Action.
