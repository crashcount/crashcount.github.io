      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 47?

      Blood in Brannan’s District: How Many More Must Die?

      Written June 29, 2025 (Revised June 29, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 47: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 29, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      Five dead. Six hundred eighty-four injured. That is the toll in District 47 over the last year. The numbers do not flinch. They do not care about excuses. In these streets, the most vulnerable pay the price. Children, elders, cyclists, and pedestrians—struck down by cars, SUVs, trucks, and mopeds.

      Just this year, a 17-year-old moped rider died after a collision with a turning car in Midwood. Police said, “Jhoan Puga was riding a gas moped with a passenger on the back, traveling north on East Eighth St. in Midwood when it slammed into a Genesis G80 car operated by a 71-year-old man making a left turn off East Eighth St.” (NY Daily News). No arrest. Another family left with a hole that will not heal.

      Patterns of Harm

      SUVs and cars do the most damage. In the last three years, they killed four people and injured over 300 more on these streets. Trucks and buses killed one. Motorcycles and mopeds left dozens more injured or dead. The city calls them accidents. The victims call them life-changing.

      What Has Brannan Done?

      Council Member Justin Brannan has backed bills to make streets safer. He co-sponsored the SAFE Streets Act, pushed for daylighting at crosswalks, and voted to legalize jaywalking—ending a law that punished the vulnerable instead of the reckless. He called a school zone traffic law a “no-brainer,” saying, “Some of our biggest victories have been getting a traffic signal installed near a busy intersection near a school – that shouldn’t be some huge colossal victory.” (Streetsblog NYC).

      But the pace is slow. Promises pile up. The dead do not wait.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      Every day without action is another day of blood on the street. Call Council Member Brannan. Demand a 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes and daylighted crosswalks. Demand that every child, every elder, every neighbor can cross the street and live.

      Do not wait for another name on the list. Take action now.

      Brannan co-sponsors bill to boost step street lighting, improving pedestrian safety.
      ▸

      Council wants lights on city step streets. The bill orders the DOT to brighten at least 25 stairways a year. Dark stairs mean danger. Light means fewer falls, fewer hidden threats. The push comes from a broad coalition. The goal is simple: safer steps.

      Int 0875-2024 sits in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure. Introduced May 16, 2024, it commands the Department of Transportation to install pedestrian lighting on at least 25 step streets each year. Step streets are public staircases linking streets at different heights. The bill aims to ensure these stairs are well-lit, reducing risk for walkers. Once all step streets are lit, the mandate ends. The measure targets a clear hazard: darkness on city stairs.
