      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 17?

      Red Lights, Dead Streets: Demand Action Now

      Written June 29, 2025 (Revised June 29, 2025 – Minor stats refresh)

      District 17: Jan 1, 2022 - Jun 29, 2025

      Blood on the Sidewalks

      Three men lay on the sidewalk at Hunts Point Avenue and Bruckner Boulevard. On June 27, a driver ran a red light, crashed into another car, and plowed into pedestrians. One man, 33, is fighting for his life. The driver ran. Police caught him. He faces charges for vehicular assault and fleeing. “Jenkins slammed on the gas and sped off east on Hunts Point Ave., blowing through a red light,” police said.

      This is not rare. In the last year, District 17 saw 8 deaths and 983 injuries from crashes. Nineteen people were seriously hurt. Most victims were on foot or riding bikes. The numbers do not heal. They do not bury the dead.

      The Pattern: Cars, Trucks, and Broken Promises

      Cars and SUVs do the most harm. In three years, they killed 7 people and injured hundreds more. Trucks and buses killed at least one. Motorcycles, mopeds, and bikes add to the toll, but the weight of death comes on four wheels.

      The pattern is old. A man was crushed by a truck on Oak Point Avenue in April. A 52-year-old was killed by SUVs on Morris Avenue. A cyclist died under a bus at East 149th. The sidewalk is not safe. The crosswalk is not safe. The numbers keep rising.

      Salamanca: Some Steps, Too Much Silence

      Council Member Rafael Salamanca Jr. has taken some steps. He voted to legalize jaywalking, ending a law that blamed victims and targeted the poor. He voted yes to “legalize jaywalking, improving overall pedestrian safety.” He co-sponsored bills to fix NYCHA sidewalks and tow abandoned cars. But he missed the vote on the citywide greenway plan. He backed bills to license e-bikes and scooters—measures that risk shifting blame to the most vulnerable.

      The streets are still killing. The bills are not enough. The dead cannot wait for another study or another press release.

      Call to Action: Demand More Than Words

      Call Salamanca. Call the Mayor. Call DOT. Demand a citywide 20 mph speed limit. Demand protected bike lanes and safe crossings. Demand action for the living, not excuses for the dead. The next victim is already walking your street.

      Int 0591-2022
      Salamanca co-sponsors bill raising ATV and dirt bike fines, minimal safety impact.
      ▸

      Council filed a bill to raise fines for illegal ATV and dirt bike use. First offenses jump to $750. Repeat violations hit $1,500. Lawmakers say the crackdown targets reckless riders. The bill died at session’s end. Streets remain exposed.

      Int 0591-2022, introduced July 14, 2022, in the Committee on Public Safety, aimed to increase fines for unlawful use of all-terrain vehicles and dirt bikes in New York City. The bill summary reads: 'A Local Law to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to increasing the fines for the unlawful use of all-terrain vehicles and dirt bikes.' Council Member Carmen N. De La Rosa led as primary sponsor, joined by Salamanca, Louis, Brewer, Joseph, Yeger, Dinowitz, and Sanchez. The measure would have raised first-offense fines to $750 and subsequent offenses to $1,500. The bill was filed at the end of session on December 31, 2023, and did not advance. No safety analyst assessment was provided. The city’s most vulnerable—pedestrians, cyclists, passengers—remain at risk from illegal vehicle use.
