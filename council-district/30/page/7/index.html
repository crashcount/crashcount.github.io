      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 30?

      District 30 Traffic Accidents: Holden Fails on Street Safety, Vision Zero

      Written August 15, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Copyedited body and FAQ, added and reformatted inline links to match existing sources, and reordered citations for first appearance.)

      District 30: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 15, 2025

      Deaths doubled in Holden’s district. Six are dead; corners wide as runways. Defaced plates don’t stop steel. Demand concrete now: protected bike lanes, daylighted crossings, slower turns, junkers gone. Crashes are up about 12%. Don’t let City Hall stall.

      What broke this summer On Aug. 3, an SUV made a U-turn and hit a moped on Fresh Pond Road.

      On June 13, a 70-year-old man was killed by a bus at Woodhaven Boulevard and Hoffman Drive. On March 19, a 23-year-old cyclist was killed at 63rd Drive and Queens Boulevard. On April 19, another cyclist died at Juniper Boulevard North and 80th Street. Last fall, a motorcyclist died at Eliot Avenue and 69th Street.

      These are the places. These are the dead. The road goes on.

      The count, not the spin In District 30, crashes are up about 12% this year compared with last year to date, and deaths doubled from 3 to 6. Across the period since 2022, the district logged 4,116 crashes, 2,302 injuries, and 35 serious injuries.

      The headlines elsewhere in Queens echo the same story. Near JFK last Wednesday, “The driver sped off without stopping. No arrests have been made.” Police said, “The operator of the vehicle fled the scene after hitting the man.”

      What Holden has done — and hasn’t Council Member Robert F. Holden backed a bill to strip city parking permits for obscured or defaced plates (Int 1358-2025, in committee). He co-sponsored a measure to verify plates and VINs and require NYPD parking data (Int 1252-2025, in committee). He voted yes to speed removal of derelict vehicles (Int 0857-2024, passed).

      Holden also supported protected bike lanes and new sidewalks on Cypress Avenue to the Ridgewood Reservoir. These steps help. But the deaths keep coming. Enforcement on plates won’t slow a turning truck at a wide corner. Reports won’t harden a crossing.

      What must change now Build and protect the lanes. Daylight the corners. Tame the speeds. Clear the junked cars fast. Then do it again, block by block.

      Call to action District 30 cannot wait. Call Council Member Holden. Demand concrete that saves lives: protected bike lanes, daylighted intersections, and real enforcement on plate tampering. Tell him the numbers are up and the names are piling. Do not accept delay.

      ▸ How have crashes and deaths changed year-to-date in District 30?
      Crashes are up about 12% year-to-date compared with last year, and deaths rose from 3 to 6, according to NYC Open Data for District 30 as of Aug. 15, 2025.
      ▸ Since 2022, how many crashes, injuries, and serious injuries has District 30 recorded?
      From 2022 through Aug. 15, 2025, District 30 logged 4,116 crashes, 2,302 injuries, and 35 serious injuries.
      ▸ Who represents City Council District 30?
      Council Member Robert F. Holden represents District 30. He is the prime sponsor of Int 1358-2025 now in the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure.

      Teen Passenger Killed in Moped-SUV Collision on Cooper Avenue
      ▸

      A moped struck a turning SUV on Cooper Avenue. The bike shattered. A 16-year-old girl riding on the back flew off, helmetless. Her head hit the pavement. She died at the scene. Two others suffered injuries. The street stayed cold and silent.

      According to the police report, a moped traveling straight collided with an SUV making a left turn on Cooper Avenue near 88th Street in Queens. The moped carried two teenagers. The 16-year-old rear passenger was ejected and suffered fatal head injuries. The 15-year-old moped driver was also ejected and sustained a fractured leg. The SUV driver, age 30, and a 27-year-old passenger reported neck and leg pain. The crash report lists 'Traffic Control Disregarded' as a contributing factor. The moped driver was unlicensed. The 16-year-old passenger was not wearing a helmet, as noted after the primary driver errors. The impact left the moped demolished and the SUV damaged at the front.
