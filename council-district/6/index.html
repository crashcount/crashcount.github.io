      Who’s Injuring and Killing Pedestrians in District 6?

      Six Dead. Hundreds Hurt. Council Stalls. Blood on District 6 Streets.

      Written August 6, 2025 (Revised August 15, 2025 – Removed duplicate headline, tightened prose, fixed citation formatting, deduped and sorted citations.)

      District 6: Jan 1, 2022 - Aug 6, 2025

      The Death Count Grows

      In District 6, the violence does not stop. Six people have died on these streets in the last twelve months. Four were over 65. One was a child. 485 people were injured. Thirteen were left with injuries so serious they may never walk the same again. These are not just numbers. They are lives cut short, families broken, futures erased.

      Just this spring, a 74-year-old cyclist was killed at West End Avenue and 70th Street. A 57-year-old man died crossing Broadway at 86th. The city logs the deaths. The street forgets. The families do not.

      The Same Streets, The Same Stories

      The pattern is old. SUVs, sedans, trucks, and bikes all leave bodies behind. In three years, cars and trucks killed or injured 286 people walking in District 6. Bikes injured 56. Motorcycles and mopeds, 20. The city calls them accidents. But the pain is not random. It is built into the street.

      Residents see it. “No one stops at these stop signs. We see people go through these red lights all the time,” said Nita. The horror is routine. “I really want there to be speed humps because it’s just terrifying,” said Nina Schmidt. The city moves slow. The cars do not.

      Leadership: Words, Bills, and Waiting

      Council Member Gale A. Brewer has backed bills to ban parking near crosswalks, to force delivery apps to provide safety training, and to demand more protected bike lanes. She has called for higher pay for delivery workers, but also for safer streets to match. She has stood with advocates calling for a lane on the West Side Highway for bikes. But bills sit in committee. Promises pile up. The dead do not wait.

      The Next Step Is Yours

      This is not fate. It is policy. Call Council Member Brewer. Demand action. Demand the city ban parking near crosswalks, build real protected bike lanes, and slow the cars. Every day of delay is another day someone does not come home.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      ▸ What is the New York City Council and how does it work?
      The New York City Council is the city’s legislative body. It passes laws, approves budgets, and oversees city agencies. See the NYC Council’s legislative records.
      ▸ Where does District 6 sit politically?
      It belongs to borough Manhattan, assembly district AD 67 and state senate district SD 28. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ Which areas are in District 6?
      It includes the Upper West Side-Lincoln Square, Upper West Side (Central), Central Park, Manhattan CB7, and Manhattan CB64 neighborhoods. It also overlaps parts of Assembly Districts AD 67, AD 68, AD 69, and AD 75, and State Senate Districts SD 28, SD 30, and SD 47. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ What types of vehicles caused injuries and deaths to pedestrians in District 6?
      Cars and Trucks were involved in 286 pedestrian injuries and deaths. Motorcycles and Mopeds caused 20. Bikes were responsible for 56. See NYC Open Data crash records.
      ▸ Are crashes just accidents, or can they be prevented?
      Crashes are not random. The same streets see the same violence year after year. Better street design and enforcement can prevent them.
      ▸ What can local politicians do to stop traffic violence?
      They can pass laws to ban parking near crosswalks, build protected bike lanes, and lower speed limits. They can push for enforcement that protects people walking and biking.

      We’re a tool for helping hold local politicians and other actors accountable for their failure to protect you when you’re walking or cycling in NYC. We update our site constantly to provide you with up to date information on what’s happening in your neighborhood.
